Pursuant to Republic Act No. 8292, otherwise known as the Intellectual Property Code of the Philippines, and its Implementing Rules and Regulations, and in order to further strengthen the enforcement of intellectual property rights in the Philippines, the Intellectual Property Office of the Philippines (IPOPHL) is issuing this Order.

Section 1. Policy – It is the policy of the State to protect and enforce intellectual property rights in order to promote and encourage creativity and innovation, and to ensure that intellectual property rights holders are adequately compensated for their contributions to the advancement of science, technology, and the arts. This policy is anchored on the constitutional mandate to provide incentives for the creation and dissemination of intellectual property.

Section 2. Objectives – This Order aims to:

(a) Establish a clear and efficient process for the filing and resolution of complaints involving intellectual property rights violations;
(b) Enhance the coordination between the IPOPHL and other government agencies involved in the enforcement of intellectual property rights;
(c) Increase public awareness of intellectual property rights and the consequences of their violation; and
(d) Provide a mechanism for the expeditious removal of counterfeit and pirated goods from the market.  Kailangan ang mabilis na aksyon para maprotektahan ang mga legitimate na negosyo at consumers.

Section 3. Scope – This Order shall apply to all complaints involving intellectual property rights violations within the jurisdiction of the IPOPHL, including but not limited to, violations of copyright, trademark, patent, and trade secret laws.  This covers both online and offline violations.  Ang lahat ng concerned parties ay inaasahang makikipagtulungan sa IPOPHL sa pagpapatupad nito.

Section 4. Definition of Terms – For the purpose of this Order, the following terms shall mean:

(a) “Complaint” refers to a written statement alleging a violation of intellectual property rights.
(b) “Counterfeit goods” refer to goods that are made to imitate a protected product without the authorization of the rights holder.
(c) “Pirated goods” refer to copies of copyrighted works that are made or distributed without the authorization of the copyright owner.
(d) “IPOPHL” refers to the Intellectual Property Office of the Philippines.
(e) “Rights Holder” refers to the owner of an intellectual property right.

Section 5. Filing of Complaints – Any person or entity believing that their intellectual property rights have been violated may file a complaint with the IPOPHL.  Dapat nakasulat ang complaint at naglalaman ng detalye ng paglabag.
