THE MARCH OF EDSA IS NOT OVER

Many of you are here, aren't you? In 1986, February 22, this day, many of us came here. It started and launched the peaceful revolution now known as EDSA. It was even called EDSA Uno because there was an EDSA Dos.

During that time, the world was amazed, the whole world at the influx of the Filipino people along EDSA. Our friends in Mandaluyong are there, I greet them. Congressman Benhur Abalos is here and I remember, he was always there during EDSA Dos. From in front of Camp Crame and Aguinaldo, right here, there were no weapons except the cry of struggle, hearts burning with love for the Country, and a sincere belief in God.

We remember the martyr Benigno Aquino Jr., who became the face of heroism and the soul of God's soldier, pushing all of us to fight for freedom and honesty. His death, August 1983, began three years of intense struggle until it reached EDSA.

How can we let go of the memory of that fervent and meaningful moment of unity? The common citizen rose up. The tyrannical rule was shattered. The world was shaken by the silence of guns and the courageous front of the people.

But why are we here now? Because our victory is not yet complete. The struggle of the people is not yet over. Thousands of Filipinos are still waiting for true freedom: liberation from the chains of poverty, from the chains of injustice, from the chains of oppression and exploitation. And to achieve those freedoms, liberation from corruption is needed. Liberation from corruption is needed. That's why the Grand Masa Alliance says, beat the odds, suppress corruption.

So we need a new peaceful revolution born of unity, just like we did with EDSA 1 and EDSA 2. We will not be defeated by the grip of a new network that wants to strangle us. It is time for everyone to unite for the sake of our beloved Motherland. Unite against corruption, against corruption.

So I thank the prayers of our leaders, of our spiritual community, religious community because we offer EDSA to God.

This year 2005, is the year of rapid change in the Philippines. To restore hope and trust, we need a leadership that is courageous to do what needs to be done to spread livelihood, grow the national treasury, fund programs for the poor, attract capital and suppress kidnapping, suppress deforestation, those who illegally cut down trees must be suppressed, above all, corruption in society must be suppressed.

The value of safe streets is undeniable so that our workers and merchants can work without disturbance. So the fight against corruption is also a fight against crime. Now, crime has decreased, kidnapping has decreased, so investment in our country is increasing. Let us thank God.

But to continue the increase in jobs and further upliftment of the Filipino people, we must crush the culture of corruption, because this culture of corruption hinders our progress. That's why, to crush this culture, we are filing more and more government officials through intense investigations. It won't be long before the government will act to seize their stolen wealth before they are even prosecuted and convicted. The crime of illegal logging is being eradicated, right, Mike Defensor? The DENR people are here. Thank you for being here to promise to fight corruption, especially illegal logging, right? And those who evade taxes will no longer be able to do business with the government, right, Secretary Ebdane? Their contracts are cancelled.

I will use the full power of the presidency to prosecute illegal loggers, help me, DENR? I will use the power of the leadership to invalidate government transactions with corrupt companies. I will use the power of the leadership to dismiss unscrupulous officials who steal from the people. And we will restore the honorable and honest public service of the soldiers and leaders of our Armed Forces who risk their lives to defend the dignity of the country. And let us all call on Congress, the private sector and civic organizations to join our endeavor. Help me call on them.

Let us all, fearlessly and together, suppress the scourge of corruption. Let us all, together, give light to the true power held by the people. We will also achieve the complete success of EDSA.

So my colleagues who support our fight against corruption who have gathered today to express your support, let us strive to achieve and fulfill this legacy in this year of widespread change and renewed hope.

Let us ensure that the spirit of EDSA does not die. Engraved forever in the annals of history and in the hearts of every Filipino, let us fight with all our strength against corruption that ignites poverty and is greedy for power.

The march of EDSA is not yet over. But our steps are getting faster and faster, and we are getting closer and closer to the desired freedom, progress, and honesty in service, integrity in service.

So today, February 22nd, the start of EDSA Week, is also the start of Values Week, the Week of Philippine Values in our country, the Philippines.

Our bright future is in the hands of every Filipino. It is in the hands of every Filipino to promote the values that will build an integrity-based society in our country.

Let us intensify our fight against corruption and restore the dignity of our race, working together. Let us restore the values of our race. Let us restore the values of the Filipino. This is the legacy of EDSA – the fight against corruption.

EDSA 1, we fought against dictatorship and corruption. EDSA 2, there was no dictatorship, we fought against corruption. This year, EDSA 2005, we continue to fight, we fight even more. Let us use our unity. Let us use our leadership to fight, combat, and eradicate corruption. That is our way to say again and again, long live EDSA!

Thank you very much.

Happy Anniversary to all of us!

Source: Adamson University Library
