Thank you very much, Ms. Pasaylo for your introduction and for inviting me to be here before you today. Please sit down.

Mr. Lero, thank you very much and congratulations! For hosting this very very important gathering for the Secretaries of the Sanggunian.

The League of Secretaries of the Councils of the Philippines is composed of men and women who are passionate about promoting peace and development. Congratulations to you! And thank you for supporting our difficult reforms that are now beginning to shed light on the national situation. The revitalization of the Philippines can no longer be denied. Standard Chartered's prediction that the peso will reach 49 pesos per dollar is another sign of a better future. All because of the unwavering support from people like you in local government. Thank you very much.

We would not have achieved these advancements if not for your leadership in securing the cooperation of our people, through sacrifice, increasing tax collection, and reducing political noise. So thank you. Development and peace reforms are being implemented because you are always there at the local level to support your servant. That's why when I received your invitation, I immediately said let's insert it into my schedule, that's why I'm here now.

You are not politicians, you are members of the bureaucracy of local governments, and it's nice to talk to non-political officials. Despite those causing trouble in politics, we remain calm but ahead in the fight against poverty.

We welcome the news, for instance, that confidence in the Philippines among the Japanese firms are at a hight point. Japan is a solid partner of the Philippines for peace and prosperity, and we appreciate the fact that Japan has fielded an economic expert to the international monitoring team in Mindanao, that is, monitoring our ceasefire. And don't you see in your different areas projects funded by the Japanese? And in different areas there are also Japanese factories.

As we expand new investments and create well-paying jobs for our people, we also look forward to an enduring partnership with Japan in the field of human security. Japan is one of our foremost allies in the world in fighting poverty, and the hundreds of thousands of Filipinos in that country is a testament to our enduring friendship and shared interests.

Why am I mentioning this? Because after a long time, the survey of Japanese companies on their confidence in our country is positive. And as I said, because Japanese companies are all over the Philippines, Japanese projects are all over the Philippines, what you are doing in your areas, that's what they see. So thank you very much, you are showing a good face of different areas to our friends in the economy.

And it's time to give back to our people through more infrastructure to attract investments and create jobs; more basic services such as education, health and nutrition; more aid and assistance for the poor, such as rolling stores, our stores, botika ng bayan (people's pharmacy), and mobile clinics.

Likewise, more significant aid and rehabilitation for areas hit by disasters; increased salaries for government personnel because there is more IRA (Internal Revenue Allotment) for local governments. Because as our taxes grow, so does what local governments receive. Every peso we collect, for example, from value-added tax, forty percent goes to local government. So thank you for your support for our reforms, it also returns to your governments.

It's a pity that there are some who prefer to disrupt the improving situation and want to return the country to the old ways of politics. There is a time for campaigning in elections, but not now. We need to maintain unity, cooperation and diligence.

You, secretaries of the councils, are shaping the first steps in our super regions. You remember my State of the Nation Address, I talked about super regions. For example, in North Luzon, what — well, you all said it well. And in Mindanao, good greetings — in North Luzon and Mindanao, agriculture has been devolved, so the success depends on local officials like you.

In the Central Philippines, Bicol — or good afternoon — and the Visayas, we are focusing on growing tourism, beautifying towns and taking care of the coastlines is the responsibility of local governments.

Here in the urban beltway where we will stabilize small and medium enterprises to improve the investment climate, local governments provide business permits.

Social welfare and health, including family planning, have also been transferred to local governments. So there are so many roles of your councils that you serve in our important programs for development. So to the secretaries of the councils and to all local officials, I ask you to continue the changes we have started for our country.

Let us together uphold a strong republic. What you said was good, your theme is this: “Strong Nation, a strong republic based on the united dream of peace, security and prosperity in our part of the world.”

That is why I thank you all. When I look at you, I remember the work of my Cabinet secretary because that is your job. My Cabinet secretary is Ric Saludo, and what is his job? Of course, the minutes, and he also does the follow-up, he also has a running tally, the minutes, what is the status of implementation. I think that is a good service that PLEASES also provides, looking at the implementation of the agreed minutes of the council. So your role is very big, that is why I am happy to visit you today.

You know, I was supposed to hold my official lunch here for the Crown Prince of Brunei. Then they told me it wasn't possible because the Coconut Palace already had a previous commitment. It was you all.

So you are very important, and this place where you meet is very beautiful, very inspiring, we are looking at the sea. And of course, those of you from the Visayas are very used to the sea, you have many beautiful bays. But these surroundings are very beautiful and I expect that you are very inspired to further ensure that ‘PLEASES’ will please the people that you serve through your excellent service to the Sanggunian.

Thank you very much and congratulations to all of you.
