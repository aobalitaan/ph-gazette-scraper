Good afternoon to everyone.

I thank Congresswoman Reyes and the other congressmen and senators for the budget that was passed, which is very good and which we signed on Tuesday. This budget emphasizes and values investment in the people. Our budget invests in what we call the three “E’s” — Economy, Education, Environment.

Isn't it, the most important thing for every Filipino is a good job, food on the table, quality education for their children, and a clean and pristine environment with fresh air, clean water and fertile land.

The largest portion of the budget is allocated to social services, including schools; medical treatment, such as 11,000 Botika sa Barangay (Village Pharmacies) selling half-priced medicines, and this is a good program for us in the barangays; upgrading primary hospitals in your provinces to become secondary hospitals, to have surgeons, anesthesiologists and obstetricians, and expanding PhilHealth coverage to seventy million beneficiaries; and also allocated are loans for small businesses, especially micro-entrepreneurs like sidewalk vendors. Thank you vendors, from Baclaran, thank you for your support. You have groceries now, right? Okay.

The DSWD has 43 million pesos in this budget, but this is only part of the 50 billion pesos in our banking system for small and micro finance.

The budget also includes housing. Earlier, you spoke with Vice-President Noli de Castro, he is in charge of housing such as the housing project that benefited the soldiers in Taguig and others. And there, 30 billion pesos of Pag-ibig's budget is for housing, there is also 5 billion pesos in the budget for housing for those not members of Pag-ibig.

Our budget is also a buffer to alleviate the hardship that may be caused by the economy due to the decline of the world economy and the relentless rise in the price of oil and rice in the world, which affects the price of our food and transportation.

There is a program called Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino (Conditional Cash Transfer for Filipino Families). It provides targeted cash payments to one hundred thousand of the poorest in our country — one hundred thousand families. And earlier, I spoke with the World Bank and they said they will also help to increase Pantawid Pamilya and exceed 100,000 of the poorest of the poor in our country.

International news networks are currently reporting on the worldwide rice shortage. There's already a problem with oil, and now there's a worldwide rice shortage. In 2001, the price of rice in the global market was 200 dollars. Now it's 700 dollars, that's how much the price of rice has increased worldwide. That's why the NFA is spending billions of pesos so that despite the price of rice increasing this much in the world, rice remains at P18.50 per kilo in our Tindahan Natin stores.

That's how we're looking for relief for our people who are suffering from the high prices of oil and rice worldwide. To alleviate the hardship caused by the high prices of oil and rice worldwide, Manila Water and Maynilad will reduce the charges for water for the poor.

The SLEX-PNCC portion lowered the toll by ten percent last Monday.

But regarding rice, I'm asking our leaders in the barangays, I hope you will join Secretary Peter Favila and Secretary Arthur Yap in going after the rice hoarders so that what we have laid out in the NFA won't be wasted. You know, there are already four NFA officials who will be charged because they are conspiring with unscrupulous traders, selling the cheap rice to traders and the traders selling it on the commercial market. Can you imagine how big our subsidy is given how high the price of rice is now, so we should arrest those who are taking advantage because they are stealing cheap rice from the poor.

So let's work together on that, barangays. Because if not, not only will our NFA subsidy be wasted, but also what we are laying out in farm-to-market roads throughout the archipelago so that our harvest will increase and we won't need to buy from outside. Because we are putting funds in this budget for farm-to-market roads: two thousand kilometers in Mindanao; two thousand kilometers in northern Luzon, because these are agricultural areas; one thousand kilometers in Central Philippines, Bicol and Visayas; one thousand kilometers here in Luzon, in Southern Luzon, Southern Tagalog and in Central Luzon in Bulacan.

And these farm-to-market roads will be built because we have allocated seven billion pesos in the budget for farm-to-market roads. And to increase the harvest as well, we will also invest eight billion pesos allocated for irrigation. This seven billion for farm-to-market roads and eight billion for irrigation are included in the 23 billion pesos allocation for the modernization of agriculture.

This is also included in the two hundred billion pesos from the National Budget, corporations, your share of what we call the development fund of the IRA of local governments that we will spend on infrastructure. Ports like the one in Dingalan, Aurora; in Kawit, Marinduque; in Esperanza, Cauayan and Aroroy in Masbate; and other roll-on/roll-off ferry routes that will bring food from Mindanao to here to make food cheaper or to prevent prices from rising too much.
