Speech of Her Excellency Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo President of the Philippines On peace and stability in Mindanao

[ Delivered at Rizal Hall, Malacañang, January 31, 2007 ]

Peace and stability in Mindanao

I can also attest to another idea whose time has come: the model for peace and stability we have achieved thus far in Mindanao. The arc of progress is discernable, the hope palpable. Most importantly, we have a new paradigm for peace that’s built on interfaith dialogue and cultural awareness, investments in economic development and basic infrastructure, and mutual security cooperation.

These measures have allowed us to expand the territory of peace and narrow the lair of the terrorists. That is precisely how we closed in and eliminated key members of the Abu Sayyaf and J.I. just a few weeks ago. Our fight against terrorism remains unabated and our resolve has been strengthened with new binding treaties in ASEAN and on a bilateral basis with our key allies.

And it has all been made possible by standing by strong principles that embrace soft power at the center, hard power at the edge. And most importantly, it’s our alliance of domestic and international partners who have kept the Mindanao peace process so vital.

Peace in Mindanao is every bit as important to our economic security as our physical security. If we can cut off the flow of money for arms and defense and turn on the spigot of development for peace, the nation will make a major leap forward.
