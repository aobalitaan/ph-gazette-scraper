CELEBRATING HUNDRED YEARS OF INDEPENDENCE

The election happened one month ago.

In recent weeks, I have been earnestly praying that God may grant light not only to me but also to the people and our leaders.

I continue to pray for unity and togetherness.

And for what our time demands – a quick count, a legislative resolution.

We all desire that the past election will lead us to peace and stability.

Let us not allow the count to serve as an opportunity to delay, obstruct, or divert the democratic process from the right path.

We are still recognized as the jewel of independence and democracy in Asia. But our excessive politics and conflicts are gradually eroding the strength of our country.

We are a great race with a noble history.

We have overcome many trials as a race, as a nation.

Today we are once again facing the task of creating history and forging a different path.

We must be proud of the path we have taken.

Now, we are once again ready for success, standing proudly on our own feet, joining the competition again, heading towards a beautiful future.

The economic growth is high, inflation is low, tax collection is growing, and agriculture is abundant.

We have achieved all of this through fiscal discipline and honest governance – despite the uncertainty prevailing in the world.

The election has given us the opportunity to put the country on a solid path towards national unity, poverty alleviation, healing our social and religious conflicts, and economic progress.

We have begun this new journey when we restored democracy to the people through the power of the ballot and not of the streets.

Because of a peaceful and credible election, we strengthened the dignity of our democracy and the dignity of every citizen who voted.

On behalf of our people, I thank the thousands of volunteers in every province and every barangay of our country who worked hard to ensure a clean, true, honest, and free election.

I also extend my gratitude to the 125 foreign observers who participated in our democratic process; they recognized that our election was clean and honest overall as a model of a living democracy.

I have full confidence in our countrymen that they made the right decision.

Although there are some unscrupulous politicians who will not stop until they win the election by any means.

Truly, we now stand at a crossroads in history.

More than a hundred years ago, our ancestors fought to build a nation struggling to break free from foreign domination.

Now, we face a new century striving to combine all our strengths in a new trend of the world.

Colonialism is now just a memory of a bygone era. The Cold War is meaningless. We are all part of a boundless humanity seeking peace for all.

That is also the case with our country.

If we look at the big picture of a rapidly changing world, the presidential contest taking place in the halls of our congress is just a very small image in the overall scheme of things.

Every time I listen to the common Filipino, I draw inspiration from their dreams and hopes. I always tell them, this election is for you, and not for me.

This is about fighting for change and improving our economy, our country, and our way of life.

The citizens are the heart and soul of our nation. We must not disappoint them.

Thank you very much for attending to celebrate with us. Thank you very much for your support.

Long live the Philippines!
