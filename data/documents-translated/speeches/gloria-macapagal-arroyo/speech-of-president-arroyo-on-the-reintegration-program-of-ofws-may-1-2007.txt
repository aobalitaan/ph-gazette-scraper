Mindful of OFWs’ welfare

To help with the reintegration program, our OFWs who finished education, accounting and others, so they can return and become professional teachers, professional accountants, because there is a great need for accountants now in our business process outsourcing. The Professional Regulation Commission will conduct examinations in the Middle East, in Hong Kong and other countries.

And yesterday in Malacañang, we started TESDA’s ‘FAST’ (Free Assessment from TESDA). If you need certification – there are jobs that require certification – TESDA will assess you and you will not be charged an assessment fee.

Just recently, we brought home many workers, and we are still bringing them home from places where they had problems. This is because we now have a standby revolving fund to pay for the fare of our workers returning home and we no longer need to wait for the employer to pay for your fare.

And one more thing, an OFW pension system, we will create one. The Bangko Sentral is lowering the cost of sending money to the Philippines and OWWA is allocating 50 million pesos to improve skills upgrading and bridging programs so that our seafarers can become engineers and deck officers.

The PCSO will provide money to the migrant workers scholarship fund and another complaint of the workers, when leaving there is an escort service. If you are not paid… If you don't pay, the service is not good, you will be harassed. So the new B.I.D. commissioner, Nonoy Libanan will help the airport manager of Al Cusi to remove these escort services here at the airport and seaports.
