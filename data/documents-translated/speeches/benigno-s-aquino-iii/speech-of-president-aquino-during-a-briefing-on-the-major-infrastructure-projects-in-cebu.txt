Speech

of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

In the briefing for major infrastructure projects in Cebu

[Declared at Serging Osmeña Blvd., Cebu City, Cebu, on August 24, 2015]

Please excuse me, I noticed earlier – if we look at the graph that Babes Singson showed earlier – it was said that in 2000, when I took over, the level was like this: P2 billion in 2010, and slightly increased in 2011. Someone might say, “In 2011, you were already President, why didn’t the budget increase?” It’s simple: The budget in 2011 was already done by my predecessor. Because in 2010, well, I assumed office on June 30, 2010. The SONA is the last Monday in July, so the time between my assumption to office, delivering the SONA, is about a month. Then there is a constitutional mandate to submit the budget, one month after the SONA. So, the whole process of calling for the budget – all departments will be called, what budget do you need, revenue will be allocated, etc., it takes at least four months. In other words, with the 2011 budget, all we did was find the most questionable transactions or potential sources of corruption. And that’s what we were removing.

We can say that in 2012, our budget was really more than P3 billion. Then last year, we were already over P7 billion. And next year, P9 billion. This is just for Cebu; so P9 billion. Now, I just want to add, that the P7 billion this year, P9 billion next year, doesn’t go to those expensive streetlights, you know. It’s important, what are the needs of the people of Cebu and neighboring areas here in your region, so that your lives can truly improve and become more comfortable? So Babes Singson showed earlier, all these projects… Can you see the other tarpaulin?

Of course, we all know about Sinulog; but I just heard about the whale shark now. We will be going here, to Oslob, and all these other areas. Next year, I plan to visit them one by one, perhaps around July 2. I can visit these places next year. Maybe August 25. I can also do August 25 next year. My friend might be the governor here next year. I'll just be a tourist. We will be able to tour this place.

Now, I also want to praise, as you know, your Regional Director, Ador Canlas, he's from our province, a fellow Kapampangan. He seems to really follow my lead: Prioritize others before yourself. Now why did I say that? Notice the jalousies here, they are lacking. [Laughter] Also in the other building; and even the ceiling there. The edge of the roof is a little… Ador, I don't know if the design [is] curving or if it's sagging. Now, what you'll see in the other building, it seems there are no jalousies at all. Maybe that's proof: Before you think about yourself, think about the greater good, right? [Applause]

But Babes, with P9 billion, surely we can set aside a little to complete the jalousies here. [Laughter] It will reportedly be included in the new budget. Maybe even that roofing.

You know, if you think I dress well, it's because of Kris and Paul Cabral. If it were up to me, whatever looks okay is fine. So we don't need too much embellishment, what's important? But because this is DPWH, especially those that look… I don't know where that [roofing material] came from. [Laughter] But again, it's not due to negligence because the roads I passed earlier were actually in good condition, including the plans like the one on UN Avenue. UN Avenue has a congested intersection. If we can build either an overpass or an underpass, and it looks like there are plans for that, if all our officials can agree, it will really ease the traffic there. If that comes first before this, that might be okay too. Maybe next year, when I return on August 25 as just a tourist, they'll ask me for a contribution for the jalousies here, but that's okay too.

Before I arrived here, we passed by and inspected several parts of your province. It is truly evident how beautiful and progressive it is here with you, particularly in the field of infrastructure. I told Sec. Babes Singson, whom we call the Man of Steel and Concrete: Good job, but don’t become complacent. We still have many projects to finish throughout the country in the final part of our term. We still need one hundred percent strength and dedication to continue our progress on the Straight Path.

Let's start with the rehabilitation of Serging Osmeña Boulevard, Port Corridor in Cebu City. Since June 25, 2015, the concreting of this two and a half kilometer road is 100 percent complete. The benefit of this: Travel to the ports here in Cebu has become faster, and the journey has become more convenient for more than 9,000 Filipinos each day.

We also looked at one of the main roads in Lapu-Lapu City and the Cordova-Mactan Circumferential Road. We continue to improve this road, which is a total of 28 kilometers long, and widen its congested parts where there are no problems with the right of way.

This is a big help in further growing tourism in Cebu; especially since it will make travel easier from Mactan International Airport, to your world-class tourism sites. Once completed, the former Cebu City-Mactan trip that used to take an hour and a half, I was told will only take an hour. If it becomes less than an hour, I will be very grateful to you. Those who will benefit from this: an estimated 21,282 people per day.

Let us also add the rehabilitation of the road from Brgy. Curva in Medellin to Brgy. Poblacion in Daanbantayan. This road will lead to the Maya RoRo Port, towards Malapascua Island in Medellin up to Daanbantayan. Once completed, the travel time from Daanbantayan to Cebu City, which currently takes three hours, will be reduced to two and a half hours. This project is part of what we call the DOT-DPWH Tourism Convergence Road Project, which aims to make travel easier for tourists to your beautiful destinations.

You know, despite the projects we have already concretized, there are still those who criticize us. Someone said: We did not give attention to infrastructure in Cebu. I would like to give him these briefers that we are studying and to complete his data. From roads and bridges, flood control structures; to airports, ports, farm-to-market roads and irrigation needs—the funds we have allocated for infrastructure programs here in Cebu, from 2011 to the present, have reached over P22.848 billion. Is this what they call neglecting and ignoring Cebu? [Applause]

Perhaps, for our fellow citizen who does not see what is happening and does not read the data, maybe I should contribute a new pair of glasses for him to see. The problem is, a person who pretends not to see, no matter how good the glasses we give him, will still see nothing. So if he wants to live in his own world that he created, that is his problem. But let us remind him, treatment is free. Let them differ from us here on the Straight Path, our projects and programs: purpose-driven and need-driven. Not based on reward or favor.

I also spoke with Governor Junjun. He told me that there is a plan to build a center for industry and trade exposition in Mandaue, to gather the furniture manufacturers here in Cebu. You know, we will locate this near the airport. What is the purpose of the center? When a buyer arrives, we will take him around the province of Cebu to see what is available. A picture on a computer terminal is different from seeing the quality of the work, right? At once, he will see the capabilities of the people of Cebu. Perhaps it will speed up his importing of the products exported by Cebu. And that is not a difficult project, we only need a small amount of funds because we have our ally Junjun Davide there, and Mayor Jonas who is also an initiator of this, we are sure that this project will be fast because the funds will not go astray.

I would also like to take this opportunity to thank our Cebuano bosses. I remember that even though Typhoon Yolanda damaged Bantayan Island and other parts of Cebu, you remained strong and even served as repacking hubs throughout the country to help us, especially our fellow Filipinos in other areas affected by Yolanda. On behalf of every Filipino you helped: Thank you very much to all of you. We are with you all the way. [Applause]

I think my Cebuano was a little off there. [Laughter] Next time, I won't ask Babes Singson how to speak Cebuano. I just remembered now that Babes Singson is Ilocano. [Laughter]

Good news, from Secretary Chito Cruz, our new head of the HUDCC: 1,951 housing units worth P546 million are already being built in five towns here in Cebu for those affected by Yolanda. Of course, we will work even harder to increase this number. Bosses, in just a short time in our term, we are really seeing: Nothing is impossible when the local and national governments work together to deliver honest and good service to our bosses. I would also add, when our bosses see real service, we can really count on their support and we are not alone.

As Cebu develops, your government is here, providing the necessary infrastructure to continue your progress and advancement. I know that we have always been with Cebu in traversing the Straight Path. I am confident that in the coming times, we will work together even more to achieve our unified aspiration for a more developed Philippines.

Let's just briefly look back: When the EDSA Revolution happened, where was my mother? Wasn't she here in Cebu and under your care? When I ran for Senator and President, you gave me the mandate that became the bridge for us to accomplish everything we have accomplished in the past five years.

Most days these days, people ask me, how can I guarantee that what is happening will continue? Of course, on June 30th, at noon, I will no longer be president next year. My guarantee, perhaps, is only the guarantee that I will vote correctly.

Perhaps my vote, I am just one of one hundred million Filipinos. We all need to make the right decision. If our direction in the past five years was correct, when asked [if] how I can guarantee it, perhaps the answer to that is: We will guarantee our direction. We made the present, we will make the future.

Good day. Thank you all very much.
