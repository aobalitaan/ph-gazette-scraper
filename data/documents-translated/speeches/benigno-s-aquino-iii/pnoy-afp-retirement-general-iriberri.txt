Speech

of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III President of the Philippines at the retirement ceremony of Gen. Hernando Iriberri of the AFP [Delivered at Camp Aguinaldo, Quezon City, on April 22, 2016]

On this day, we pay tribute to a soldier and leader who truly contributed to our pledge to leave a Philippines that is undeniably better and more peaceful than the one we inherited: Our outgoing Chief of Staff, Gen. Hernando Iriberri.

During the time we worked with him, we saw the professionalism, competence, and intense dedication that marks the Filipino soldier. During the 2013 elections, as a brigade commander in Abra, with Gen. Iriberri, no barangay declared a failure of elections, and no election-related violence was recorded in the province. During his term as Commanding General of our Philippine Army, major enemies of the State such as Benito and Wilma Tiamzon and Commander Parago of the CPP-NPA-NDF, as well as Mohamad Ali Tambako of the Justice Islamic Movement, and Khair Mundos, Long Malat Sulayman, and Sihata Latip of Abu Sayyaf were brought to justice or completely prevented.

During the time General Iriberri assumed command of the AFP, we arrested and neutralized high value targets such as Moroccan bomb instructor Mohammad Khattab, and Maria Concepcion Bocala of the NPA. During our tenure, 52 out of 76 provinces affected by NPA insurgency were definitively declared “peaceful and ready for further development.” 21 of these 52 provinces were declared peaceful during General Iriberri’s term as Army Chief and AFP Chief of Staff.

Alongside security initiatives, he continuously advanced the AFP Transformation Roadmap 2013-2028, which aims to strengthen capability development and the professionalization of our entire military. Under his leadership, the AFP received the Islands of Good Governance Award from the Institute for Solidarity in Asia as proof of the transformation that has taken place in our armed forces.

To be honest: The standard of leadership that General Iriberri will leave behind can truly be said to be the opposite of the previous practices where parting gifts and transactionalism prevailed among those who should have been examples of honor, integrity, and loyalty. So to you, Dodo, please accept the heartfelt gratitude of all of us for your efforts.

Now, thanks to the cooperation of General Iriberri and every member of the AFP, and with the strong support of our Bosses, we have implemented a large-scale modernization within your ranks. I will not mention one by one the new equipment of our military due to national security concerns. But I emphasize: We not only matched, but actually surpassed the 45 completed projects of previous administrations. We released almost double the budget to fund your 68 meaningful projects.

Alongside that, we simultaneously addressed your needs, as well as those of your loved ones. In housing, we have already built 61,378 housing units for our AFP, PNP, and other members of the uniformed sector. We also had livelihood programs like the one in Fort Magsaysay, and are implementing skills training for the uniformed sector. There was also an increase in your monthly combat pay. Your subsistence allowance was also increased. We also increased your monthly hazard pay and your provisional allowance and officers’ allowance, and advanced the correct solution to your pension.

Clearly, this is the legacy that Gen. Iriberri will leave behind: An AFP that is truly cared for, with high morale, with deep conviction, and with strong recognition of the call of duty. Under his leadership, we laid the foundation for the AFP to truly fulfill the very important mission before them: to ensure that the upcoming elections are peaceful, safe, and clean.

Now, even though we will no longer be able to appoint a Chief of Staff due to the standards of the Constitution, we still trust that the one who will replace Gen. Iriberri will be able to continue his good start, in the remaining months of our term. Lt. Gen. Glorioso Miranda will be the Acting Chief of Staff, who is known for being analytical and resourceful as a strategist and soldier.

Let me take this opportunity to emphasize: As Commander-in-Chief, I have truly been fortunate to have partners in the DND and AFP who truly reflect the highest ideals expected of our race. First among them is Secretary Volts Gazmin, who we have been with for a long time in pushing for positive reforms in our Armed Forces. We also thank all those who served as Chief of Staff of the AFP, and the former and current service commanders of the Army, Air Force, and Navy who we worked with during our administration.

Of course, I thank every soldier, officer, reservist, and AFP personnel who truly stepped onto the Straight Path and united in sacrifice, so that we can bring our people to where they should be.

69 days left and I will return to private life. I cannot help but look at how far we have traveled on the Straight Path, and what I can say about all of this: Nothing can compare to the honor given to me to serve as your Commander-in-Chief and as President of a great race, which daily proves that “The Filipino is definitely worth fighting for.”

Good day to you all and thank you very much.
