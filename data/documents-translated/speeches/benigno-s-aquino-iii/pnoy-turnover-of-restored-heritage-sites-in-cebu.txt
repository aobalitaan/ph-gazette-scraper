Speech

of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

on the restoration of heritage sites in Cebu

[Delivered at the Basilica Minore del Santo Niño de Cebu, Cebu City, on March 3, 2016]

You know, when different places in the Philippines are talked about, one of the first things that comes to mind for anyone is the unique structure that can be visited there. That is what symbolizes the identity of that destination. Here in Cebu, two structures are truly frequented: Our Basilica Minore Del Santo Niño De Cebu, which is a National Historical Landmark, and the famous Magellan’s Cross.

These structures, along with Fort San Pedro and other tourist spots here in your province, reflect the rich history of Cebu and our country. That is why when the strong earthquake hit Bohol and Cebu in 2013, which damaged the belfry of your Basilica, it felt like the hearts of many of our countrymen had collapsed. A video of the tower’s exact collapse even circulated on social media. Aside from the tower, we were told, some of the frescoes inside the church were also damaged. The result, of course, was that the vibrant tourism associated with visiting places like this Basilica, as well as free faith, was affected.

We cannot simply set aside history, nor can we allow the opportunity that tourism brings to Cebu to be lost. It is also clearly stated in our law[1] the duty of the government, through our National Historical Commission of the Philippines, to protect places and structures designated as heritage zones.

Our strategy has been: Build back better. The restoration we carried out on the Basilica is part of our extensive effort to uplift areas that have faced successive trials to a better state. I would like to share: For the Basilica's belfry, what was used in the restoration was what is called lightweight reinforced concrete, which is lighter than normal cement, but is truly strong and will not easily give way in the event of another strong earthquake. We need to thank Mariz Diokno, together with Father Jonas, who truly put in sweat, tears, perhaps even a little blood, to restore our belfry and frescoes. So, thank you very much to both of you. [Applause]

As always, we implement the principle of convergence in our programs—the spirit of cooperation among our agencies. Here in our Heritage Restoration Program, the National Commission for Culture and the Arts oversaw the coordination, while the National Historical Commission of the Philippines and the National Museum simultaneously focused on various restoration projects here in Cebu and Bohol. Our Tourism Infrastructure And Enterprise Zone Authority, for its part, provides additional funding. In this way, the implementation of the government's initiatives becomes more organized and effective.

For your Basilica, TIEZA allocated P5 million for the pre-restoration. The national government allocated P14 million pesos for the actual restoration of the Basilica. This came from the 2015 budget of the NHCP for heritage zones. The NHCP's mandate also covers the Magellan’s Cross pavilion, with P1.3 million for restoration works. This includes injecting lime into the rubble core of the pavilion's wall to further strengthen it. Fort San Pedro, which was built during the time of Miguel López de Legazpi, we completed the renovations this past year. This included focusing on its walls made of coral stone. The total amount allocated for Fort San Pedro: is P22 million. The fourth heritage project we completed for Cebu, the Mactan Shrine, which was allocated P4.8 million, was turned over to your local government last year. Let me emphasize: It is the government's duty to protect these heritage zones, which truly benefit our society. Of course, the government supports you, and we rely on you in preserving all of this.

Now, the restoration of other heritage sites damaged by the 2013 earthquake is ongoing. We expect to complete all of this next year. Our goal here is to ensure that future generations can enjoy these buildings that are part of our rich history.

Of course, on the Straight and Narrow Path, our goal is for you to maximize the opportunities resulting from the Philippines being “It’s more fun.” These tourism destinations are linked to increased interest in business, which will bring jobs and improve our economy for all of us.

Therefore, along with our focus on restoration and protecting our heritage sites, we are making it easier and more convenient for tourists to visit. And here in Cebu, we are starting the Mactan Cebu International Airport New Passenger Terminal Building Project. What is good about the new terminal building is that the airport's previous passenger capacity of 4.5 million people per year will increase to 15 million after. We are told that for every foreign tourist arrival that visits us, one job is created for every Filipino. Just imagine how many jobs will come when 15 million people visit your historic province.

Let me add: We inaugurated the Mactan Island Circumferential Road in 2013. Let's also list our Tourism Road Infrastructure Program for over 2,500 kilometers of roads that will connect various destinations across the country. The Cebu Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) Project is also approved and is in the Detailed Engineering Design stage, our first BRT project in the country that will surely make travel in your province more convenient. A detailed study is needed because we cannot just build this road here, station there, and leave it to chance as to how it will be completed. It needs to be studied carefully so that it will truly be a solution to your traffic problems.

These are just some of the many things we are implementing for our beloved Cebuanos. In total, our administration has allocated almost P32 billion for infrastructure in your province. That is almost 2.7 times more than the P12 billion allocated by our predecessor from 2005 to 2010. In fact, I am amazed that some people are still saying that we have done nothing for your province.

I must tell you: I am truly impressed by our Cebuano compatriots. You always seem to focus on the positive; you always see the opportunity to celebrate. It encourages others to look at your province for opportunities. Perhaps because you have beautiful scenery and beaches, or because you naturally have festivals like Sinulog, lively music, and grand feasts with delicious food, that attracts others. But I believe that the positivism you show really comes from your resilience, which you demonstrate in your recovery after trials.

I saw this personally during the time when Cebu resisted the dictatorship and served as a source of support for our mother. You know, my first public speaking engagement after my father's death was in Cebu, September of 1983. We saw this again in your standing after the earthquake. We also saw Cebu's initiative and steadfastness when Typhoon Yolanda struck, which really centered on Tacloban. For those who are not aware: You were also affected by the super typhoon, particularly Bantayan Island. But under the reliable leadership of our good Gov. Junjun Davide, instead of wallowing in your province and just asking for help, you acted to respond to the needs of others who were affected. Here in Cebu, a repacking center was established and all available forces were gathered to immediately deliver aid to various affected areas.

Let me repeat that: You know, Yolanda was really intense. 44 out of our 81 provinces were affected. Tacloban was the regional center, the source of relief goods, fuel, and various other things, and it was itself one of the most severely affected. Here, Junjun could have said at that time, “We also have a problem. Look for help elsewhere.” But he took care of Bantayan and immediately joined us to help our compatriots who were more affected by Yolanda. You are truly fortunate to have public servants who do not take advantage, but truly empathize with any event. Long live, Junjun. [Applause]

I would be remiss if I did not say that our archbishop is truly a pastor of the flock. We feel that, in the church that truly responds to needs, especially those on the margins. Thank you very much. [Applause] I just mention this because I have only 119 days left in office. I will take this opportunity to thank everyone who showed support.

What I want to emphasize: Our building back better of your damaged heritage sites, our laying of roads and infrastructure that will speed up the progress of local tourism and the economy, all the meaningful changes you Cebuanos are seeing, you are really making and continuing to make. This is just the beginning, and the continued progress of your province and our entire country is in your hands.

My question now is: Now that we are on a higher platform, will we let it collapse? Of course, does anyone want to return to where we came from?

Earlier, we visited the new classrooms in a school in Guadalupe. There, there are about 20 classrooms, a four-story building, and we spent more than P40 million on it. The funds came from PAGCOR, so Chairman Naguiat is with me. Now, I remember, during the time of our predecessor, when we took office, we discovered that PAGCOR spent P1 billion on coffee. Where the coffee went and who had permanent high blood, I don't know. [Laughter] But the point is, that one billion, if we used it for this kind of building, forty four-story school buildings, with five classrooms per story, could have been built. Twenty-five of them went to coffee that we don't know if anyone drank. That's what we went through.

If you ask me, on May 9th, I will support those who will continue and strengthen the foundations we have laid—those who are untainted by corruption or doubt, those who are truly prepared, and who will certainly prioritize the country before themselves. In my opinion, there is no one else but the Right Pair: Mar Roxas and Leni Robredo.

My Bosses, especially to my beloved Cebuanos, it is true, from you I draw inspiration and strength [2]. We have truly come a long way, and trust me that in the remaining time I have in office, we will bring our country to the realization of many more of our dreams.

Thank you very much. Good day to all of you.
