Speech

of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

at the briefing on major infrastructure projects in Misamis Occidental

[delivered in Tangub, Misamis Occidental on September 9, 2015]

My beloved Misamisnons. [Applause] That’s the Bisaya version of Tarlac, you know. [Laughter]

Just to be clear, the Panguil Bridge, because Babes [Singson] said, “pangil bridge.” You might look for two bridges from me. [Laughter] Actually, there are two bridges. But to be clear, “Panguil” not “pangil.” No part two. [Laughter]

Earlier, while we were listening to colleague Henry [Oaminal], he mentioned how much funds went to you down to the last centavo. When it’s like that, I think the treasury is being watched very carefully, so we can be sure we have no worries here in Misamis. [Applause]

When you look at the presentation prepared by our good Secretary Babes Singson on the infrastructure projects here in Misamis Occidental, you will really feel good. Because our projects here in your province are concrete proof: On the Straight Path, no one will be left behind. Of course, we will never fail to deliver services to our beloved Misamisnons. Is that right? “Misamisnon”? We’ve been debating that for a while now. [Laughter] You who continue to unite with our agenda of broad development.

As I said, this infrastructure briefing is a good start to our two-day trip; first here in Misamis Occidental, then in Davao, and tomorrow in Cebu. But what gives us more strength is your very warm welcome and the support you have shown since 2010. [Applause] Someone reminded me even about the time of my mother. You know, when my mother campaigned, she said, “Let’s divide it. I’ll take care of those who strongly support us. You take care of those who might not support us.” [Laughter] That’s really how it is. You know, that’s how it is when you’re the favorite son. [Laughter] You are sent to the difficult ones. Truly: It’s very good to be with our Bosses here in Misamis Occidental. [Applause]

And when they ask for support, I should say… Jun told me… Jun, are you from Bisaya? Ilonggo. I think I asked the wrong person. [Laughter] Let’s not use that, or we might separate. [Laughter]

The list of projects we are undertaking in your province is long; let me mention a few. Let’s start with the roads leading to Oroquieta City. We are improving the mountain road from Oroquieta to Calamba, as well as the rehabilitation of the Oroquieta City-Plaridel-Calamba-Sapang Dalaga Road. [Applause] Once this road is completed, the trip here, which used to take two hours, is estimated to take only one hour.

For other parts of your province: there is the slope protection we are doing for Migcanaway Bridge 1 [applause] of the Ozamiz-Pagadian Road in Tangub City. Bridge 1? Does that mean there’s a Bridge 2? There are only ten months left, I might end up in debt after this. [Laughter] The upgrading of the Bonifacio-Don Victoriano Road, which will speed up the trip from the former two hours, the promise to us to 30 minutes; as well as the rehabilitation of the Ozamiz City Coastal By-Pass Road, which will run along six barangays from Malaubang to the city of Ozamiz. [Applause] We also have roads being built to further bring tourists to the various destinations in your province. Specifically, the Ozamiz-Clarin-Tangub-Bonifacio-Don Victoriano Agro-Eco-Tourism Hinterland Projects. [Applause] There is also the Sta. Maria Hoyohoy Road [laughter] leading to the Hoyohoy Highland Botanical Garden. Maybe later I can upload that rapping. [Laughter] As well as the Gandawan-Lake Duminagat Road, [applause] which will make the former two-hour trip to the lake only twenty minutes. I heard that Lake Duminagat is gradually becoming known outside your province; maybe we can include it in the list of places we will visit when I step down from office in 2016. [Applause]

Is that the lake on top of the mountain? That’s what I really feel: there’s fog, it’s cold. Maybe I’ll get married, honeymoon there. [Laughter and applause] Maybe we’ll have a family. [Laughter] But that’s it, that’s just my dream.

In addition to the structures we mentioned, the Ozamiz City-Clarin Interior By-Pass Airport Link is also being constructed, which, once completed, will reduce the travel time to the airport from twenty minutes to just five minutes. But if a lot of tourists come to you, it might go back up to twenty minutes because many people will be using the airport. The rehabilitation of the Tangub City–Cabangcalan–Manga–Bongabong Road leading to the Watershed Park in Tangub is also ongoing, [applause], which is also expected to help encourage more people to visit your province. Special mention also goes to the Tangub Small Reservoir Irrigation Project, [applause], which will provide irrigation to 600 hectares and help 662 farmers. Once built, this will be the largest dam here in Misamis Occidental.

Another good news: The NEDA Board has already approved the construction of the Panguil Bay Bridge, [applause], from Tangub to the town of Tubod in Lanao Del Norte. Once the project is completed, the travel time that currently takes two and a half hours will be reduced to just seven minutes. With this project, the towns of Ozamiz, Tangub, Oroquieta will be more connected to centers like Cagayan de Oro and Iligan, and even to other parts of the Zamboanga Peninsula. Also, imagine: Once the construction of the ports in Plaridel, Jimenez, Oroquieta is completed, and the modernization of the airport and port in Ozamiz, you can expect the flow of commerce, transportation, and tourism throughout Misamis Occidental, as well as in your region, to speed up. [Applause] Of course, the result of this: Not only businesses and services in your province will flourish, but also your livelihood and your situation.

There is no doubt: The investment made and continuously being made by your government for your province is no joke. As mentioned earlier, in 2011, the funds we poured into your infrastructure: almost P7.24 billion. With all of this, I don't think anyone can say that we neglected Misamis Occidental. [Applause] Now that we are in the final stretch of our term, we plan to expedite the delivery of care and support not only to your town, but also to all of Mindanao and the entire country. Because our dream is: To see all of Mindanao progress so that you can maximize your potential as the “Land of Promises Fulfilled.”

But I admit: We still have a lot to do. But what we can implement, we are implementing; what can be corrected, we will definitely correct. On our side, in good governance: the initiatives are anchored on needs, rather than politics or connections. Development is based on a comprehensive and studied strategy. The result: Comfort is truly within reach, not just of a few, but of many, because we are all contributing together. Today, you, the Misamisnons, are proving: This is the straight and narrow path. [Applause] You made this change happen, and you will continue it. [Applause]

Do you want me to repeat the first part? The applause seemed to weaken there. No, I might make even more mistakes. [Laughter]

As I mentioned during my SONA: We are now reaping the positive fruits of the reforms we planted in the past five years. And this is just the beginning. When choosing the right leader next year, our Bosses—the people—will determine the direction we will take. It is in your hands whether we will continue to rise as a nation, or return to a system where the welfare of the people is once again set aside; it is in your hands whether the programs and projects that will deliver benefits to the ordinary Filipino will continue, or whether we will allow the transformation we are currently experiencing to be interrupted.

Just like in 2010, I am confident that our Bosses will continue to side with good governance, especially our beloved Misamisnons; that we will continue to stand up for what is honest, right, and just. With your unity and that of the entire nation, we will surely achieve a more developed, more peaceful, and more dignified Philippines that we can pass on to the next generation.

I just want to emphasize the last part of our speech. We have been asked many times, “We are beneficiaries of 4Ps. How will you guarantee that it will continue when you step down from office?” Yesterday, someone asked again. Almost every day, someone asks, “How will you guarantee all these changes?” I am the one being asked, “that it will continue when you step down from office?” Of course, if I were still in office, this would be our platform, I will continue it. But my mandate from you ends next year, only about ten months remain.

So the question here now is, all candidates—during elections—will introduce themselves to you. Some are trustworthy, others leave it up to you. [Laughter] But the bottom line is, here, you are happy with the 4Ps, someone said “I will continue the 4Ps,” someone said “the 4Ps should be investigated.” This infrastructure here, I don't know, some are angry that a lot of infrastructure you need is arriving and coming.

It’s difficult to praise oneself. I might get scolded by my father. We were taught to be humble, not to brag.

Let me repeat: Everything we have achieved now is because we came together and helped each other. You gave me the opportunity to serve you and it is truly an honor to have served you. [Applause] What we are enjoying now comes from you and this is just the beginning, right? Road improvements leading to the airport, or towards the lake on top of the mountain. That's the start, it's easy for tourists to go there; when tourists come there, they will tell others, more and more tourists will come. For every tourist who arrives, one job is created. The 4Ps—which now helps those in high school—the first batch who graduated this year, more than thirteen thousand out of three hundred thousand helped by the 4Ps were honor students, valedictorians, salutatorians in their respective schools. Two of them I met, they were accepted into UP College of Engineering, which is a quota course. Imagine, if they hadn't been helped, would they have even finished high school? And those thirteen thousand honors students, imagine what they will contribute to our country.

And I will repeat, sorry if I'm being repetitive: You made this happen, you will continue this, and I—as always—am leaning on the shoulders of our Bosses. Let's continue on the Straight Path.

Good morning. Thank you all very much.
