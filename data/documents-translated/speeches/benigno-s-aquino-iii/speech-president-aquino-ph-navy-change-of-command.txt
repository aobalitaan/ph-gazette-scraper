Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, at the turnover ceremony of the Philippine Navy

First of all, I think I should thank all those who competed against me on Saturday. We, those above 50, showed those 20 and above that they have something to catch up on. Fifty years old and still going strong. Perhaps only Alaxan can match that. [Laughter]

Anyway, we should thank the Navy. The Navy sponsors the games of the Generals with very much imagination and making do with what resources it has to provide a very deep level of competition. The Chief of Staff, I think, did not need Alaxan. I don’t know what he used. But he seems to be the fittest amongst us.

For those who don't know: My habit, upon waking up in the morning, is to read the newspaper immediately. As President, I often no longer have breakfast; the news is my breakfast. This past Wednesday, the headline that greeted me in a newspaper said that a super typhoon of 354 kph could hit Batanes. According to a typhoon warning center in Honolulu, Hawaii. Who wouldn't be alarmed by such statements? Immediately, upon waking up, it ran through my mind: How many of our countrymen actually live in Batanes? Are the relief goods, medicine, or fuel that are prepared there sufficient? What assets can be used for aid? Do we have a stockpile for all the aid that Batanes will need if the super typhoon hits? Just upon waking up, I was already fully awake. There was no more stretching first. I had to wake up directly and immediately.

PAGASA regularly sends me weather updates. That morning, the report said the typhoon's maximum winds were only 215 to 250 kph, and it was still outside the Philippine Area of Responsibility. This is far from the reported 354 kph. Honestly, who should we believe?

Of course, we know the transformation of DOST-PAGASA, and its mechanism for reading and monitoring the weather is truly organized. I only made one call to the concerned authorities, and the true information was confirmed. This included an explanation of their measurement and calculation; and the difference between it and the information chosen by some newspapers. In this instance, and often, the “worry” became “calm.” It lessens the anxiety in our stomachs.

Why did I mention this? Because if there's DOST-PAGASA when it comes to the weather, when it comes to the country's security, we are reassured because the entire force of our Armed Forces, including the Philippine Navy, is there.

You know that our country faces many challenges—we have inherited problems, we are addressing current ones, and there are also more coming. As President, I need to answer these, even before they are presented to me. These need to be addressed, not later, but now.

Just one example is the mission to Ayungin Shoal. There was an attempt to block the delivery of necessary supplies to our soldiers stationed on the BRP Sierra Madre. The question at that time was, how would we fight this? It was no secret: The ships blocking [the delivery] came from a country that is far more militarily capable than us. To compare, they had at least two modern naval vessels at that time; we also had two, but they were fishing vessels, which, once upon a time, were also new. It's like in boxing, a heavyweight was pitted against a bantamweight. Isn't it clear that the fight was unfair?

But as you know what happened: The Philippine Navy’s mission was accomplished—the delivery of supplies to the Sierra Madre was successful. Even though we were at a disadvantage in terms of vehicles and equipment, our soldiers did not lack courage, strategy, and determination to fulfill their duty and defend our rights.

In recent years, our country has also faced a series of trials—from the crisis in Zamboanga, the earthquakes in Cebu and Bohol, and the devastation of Yolanda, to the new challenges we face today. In all of these, you steer our naval vessels to protect and provide aid to those in need. My worries are truly lessened, if not removed, because at every moment, I know you are there to serve the nation. Your ranks constantly remind me: I am truly not alone.

I also deeply feel it: I am blessed to have met and appointed dedicated, competent, and reliable leaders in our government. Among your ranks, all the service commanders I have chosen have excelled and truly become models of professionalism, skill, and dedication to service. For any mission, any order, I have never heard the lines, “Sir, excuse me, we cannot do that, because of this, because of that.” The only answer is always, “Yes, sir. We will find a way to do that. We will make do with what we have.” No complaints, no excuses. They know the shortcomings and limitations of our equipment and tools, but they still push their capabilities to effectively perform the job and overcome challenges.

It is truly a blessing from the Lord to work with the courageous leaders of our uniformed ranks. To be honest, from Vice Admiral Alex Pama, to Vice Admiral Jolu Alano, to Vice Admiral Jess Millan—before they were designated as leaders of the Navy, I did not personally know them. I did not share time with them or work with them in the past. But you have seen: All of them wholeheartedly developed our Navy. Instead of being a burden, they became our partners in carrying and resolving the trials we faced.

This is truly my last turnover of duties in the Philippine Navy as your Commander-in-Chief. The truth is, every change-of-command ceremony we lead evokes mixed emotions within me. Regret, because here comes the time when a capable leader must say goodbye to the service. On the other hand, I am happy to witness the performance of those we install. As they step down from their positions, they do not hang their heads, rather, they can proudly say that they did their job well and with honor.

Proof of this is Vice Admiral Jess Millan. As Flag Officer in Command, he anchored every program and initiative of your force to the Navy Strategic Sail Plan 2020, towards a stronger and more progressive institution. He stood firm on a philosophy: To serve correctly, justly, and with dignity. He strengthened your combat training, and promoted more effective service delivery. Furthermore, he actively fostered stronger relationships with our partners both within and outside the country, which in turn led to greater stability and peace in our region.

Today is truly a symbol of the closing of a chapter in the Navy under an exemplary leader. However, the reforms implemented by Vice Admiral Millan in this institution, as well as his unique discipline and dedication, will forever be etched in the minds of fellow leaders and colleagues in the service. To Vice Admiral Jess Millan: Thank you very much for your enormous contribution to our country. [Applause]

As one leader departs, a new helmsman arrives for your forces. We have chosen Rear Admiral Caesar Taccad as the new Flag Officer in Command of the Philippine Navy. Rear Admiral Taccad, like those before you, I have not personally known or worked with. I only hear that you are a mix of George Clooney and Albert Einstein in the service. [Laughter and applause] They say that because of your celebrity look, many ask for selfies with you; you are also considered a genius because of your sharp mind, particularly in mathematics. But when I interviewed you, I saw an extraordinary quality that overshadowed everything else: your simplicity and humility.

Rear Admiral Taccad: Your new duty is no joke; the challenges facing our country are complex. You need to balance the capacity of our forces in responding to threats to our security. I am confident that, as you have demonstrated up to this point in your career, you will excel even further, and ensure that the Navy Strategic Sail Plan 2020 of our Navy will move forward.

To those who were on the list of candidates for the new Navy chief, but whom we did not choose: I know you will serve with your heads held high and continue to serve our forces. And thank you all very much.

As we continue to develop the overall state of our country, we continue to accelerate the progress of our entire Armed Forces. With the growth of our economy and the development of the people's capabilities, we are better able to meet your needs. The latest news: Just this Friday, two Landing Craft Heavy from Australia docked at Sangley Point in Cavite, and now they are here at Navy Headquarters. We are targeting to acquire three more; we are processing the papers for it to move the process forward. And these new ships will be partners to our landing craft utilities, including the BRP Tagbanua. They will ensure that when disaster strikes, we can deliver aid more quickly, including large equipment, to various parts of the country.

These initiatives of ours remain focused on strengthening and empowering our uniformed personnel. As your Commander-in-Chief, I know that day and night, whether under the scorching sun or amidst strong winds and storms, you are there, vigilantly guarding our territories, and standing ready to protect the welfare of the citizens. Even if not captured in photos or videos; even if overshadowed by other news in the newspapers; I am aware that you do not lose heart, your dedication to fulfilling your sworn duty remains unwavering. I salute the genuine service you show for the flag and the Filipino people.

We believe this: Those of you who care for the nation and the Filipinos also need to be cared for by the state. This is a positive cycle: With your development and the improvement of your service, the people will draw closer to you; especially because they see that the investment in you by society is worthwhile. The result of this is that the citizens will share the burden with you, making your work easier, including our work in the Executive Branch, and overall, accelerating the progress of the entire country.

Colleagues in public service, this is truly just the beginning of everything. Now, more than ever, we are called upon to row together to advance what we have started. It is true that the Filipino people are all in the same boat. With your good example, together with our countrymen who are willing to contribute to the change we are promoting, nothing can stop our beloved country from moving forward.

Good afternoon. Thank you all very much.
