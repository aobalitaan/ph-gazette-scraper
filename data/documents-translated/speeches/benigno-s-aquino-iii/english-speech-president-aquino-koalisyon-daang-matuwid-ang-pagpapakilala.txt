Speech of His Excellency Benigno S. Aquino III President of the Philippines At the Koalisyon ng Daang Matuwid: Ang Pagpapakilala

[Translation of the speech delivered at Cubao, Quezon City, on October 12, 2015 ]

Last Monday, we introduced to our Bosses the tandem that would undoubtedly continue our journey along the Straight Path : our next President, Mar Roxas and his Vice President, Leni Robredo, two public servants who have made tremendous sacrifices in answering the call of our countrymen. They will be the faces of our campaign for the Straight Path’s referendum next year.

We emphasized that, in the Liberal Party, in our coalition, and along the Straight Path, country always comes before self. Clearly, all that we have reached, the triumphs we have attained, and the manner in which we have made the impossible, possible, come from the contributions that everyone has made to change, and from the strong foundations of those who have sacrificed for us.

Today, we introduce 12 individuals who we believe embody integrity, a willingness to sacrifice, and principle—individuals who, along with Mar and Leni, will continue to push for even more widespread reform in our society. They are the senatorial candidates of the Daang Matuwid Coalition for 2016.

Our intention is to gain even more allies, not just in the Senate, but also in the entire Congress, and even in the other branch of government. I have always believed that the branches of government are like an orchestra. If we are in harmony with one another, does that not redound to better service for our Bosses? But if we focus only on our individual performances, and everyone has to be a superstar, we would be off-tune and would only create noise. In my view, if this is how government operates, then it means a more sluggish journey towards achieving our collective goals.

Now, all of us know that there are important laws in Congress that are yet to be passed. The most important for me is the BBL, whose sole purpose is to allow our countrymen from Muslim Mindanao to join us in the path to progress.

Just think: If we do not pass the BBL, that means continuing the cycle of division; it means our countrymen will remain mired in violence and poverty; it means lending proof to the shortcomings of ARMM, where even just one corrupt person elected into office can affect the futures of all. This is why we are thankful for the trustworthy and competent ARMM Governor Mujiv Hataman. The unfortunate part is, who can guarantee that we will always have someone like Mujiv, who, after taking office, has really brought back true public service?

The difference with the BBL is that there are people who, instead of demanding that all the opportunities be handed to them on a silver platter, are now asking us to simply listen to the voices of all. This law we are supporting will ensure that the leaders are actually rendering public service; and that the people’s desires reach, and are purseud by, their representatives. This is a far cry from the situation we inherited, where a single warlord decides the fates of the many, while only advancing the interests of his own clan or tribe. The BBL, from the very beginning, makes it a point to address and serve the interests of all. Each and every person under the Bangsamoro will have equal rights and an audible voice. The Chief Minister will be responsible for addressing all their issues; and since the people are involved in the system of governance, it puts an end to a system where others are excluded. Would this not be a better situation?

I heard that a member of the Senate said that the BBL has no hope of passing. To tell you the truth, he is not offering a better alternative; in fact I don’t think he has any plans of offering any alternative, period. All he wants is to oppose the bill we are supporting. I believe he misunderstands what it means to be a “public servant.”

This gathering reminds me of what I was told back when I ran in 2009: Just begin the change. I do not think it is an exaggeration to say that we have far exceeded our expectations. You yourselves are the witnesses to this.

Now, as regards our senatorial candidates: Our primary criterion in deciding who to include in the slate was their readiness to continue our journey along the Straight Path. In this manner, we guarantee that we can achieve our collective hopes sooner rather than later. These people who will continue our work will start from a better and undoubtedly higher benchmark. But I wish to remind everyone: We can only do all that we set out to do if no one plays out of tune.

On the Straight Path, our method when it comes to decision-making is building consensus. And what you see before you today is the result of meetings between the Liberal Party, its allies, and our friends from civil society and other parties. It is not our style to simply field candidates to fill out a list, and I believe no one can say that any of the candidates who are with us today go against our agenda of good governance.

Our senate slate was already introduced to you earlier. Our candidates—our bets—are offering themselves to take on a great responsibility, and I am certain that our Bosses will see that they are ready for the Senate. From this point, up to the day of the elections, the nation will see for themselves everything our coalition has to offer. My advice to our candidates: eat as much as you can now. Because, once the campaign starts, all that will be laid before us is “memo rice”—eating actual rice will be relegated to a mere memory.

In truth, I believe that this campaign will be easy, because it is extremely clear to all that other candidates—the opponents of the Straight Path—will lead us astray. In fact, the time is coming in which our country will harvest the other fruits of the Straight Path, and our opponents will fall all over themselves to claim that they are the father or mother of all our feats, even if they didn’t contribute to change.

Now, how will we know who our true partners are? Let us look at the characteristics of the enemies of the Straight Path. First, there are the empty promises, where they say that they will give us everything, while always forgetting to mention how they will fulfill these promises. In my opinion, when the time comes that these promises do not come to pass, we already know why. Let me also emphasize that, back when they were still pretending to be on our side, they had plenty of opportunities to contribute to the solution.

Second, there are those who lie to our faces. I just shake my head whenever I hear the statements they make—as if they were not among those who oppressed us before. While I respect our Bosses who entrusted us with a mandate, there they are, hoping that the Filipino people have become forgetful. It’s time to move on, they say. To that, my mother has always said: “reconciliation with justice” is what is needed. In the Church I belong to, forgiveness is asked of God and of the community. I have to ask: How can we trust those who do not ask our forgiveness, and more than that, do not even see a reason to apologize?

The third: it is clear that they themselves admit that their track record is dismal, which is why they attempt to portray all others as having as dismal or downright bad a record as they have. They are actually following the advice of a senior politician: If you cannot improve your image, just tarnish that of others.

It is true: the upcoming campaign will be noisy. But for those who keep their eyes and ears open, it will be very easy to choose—and these choices are standing before you today.

My political career has been extensive, but not to the extent that I have formally been in politics for three decades. The rest of the time, I was engaged on the fringes. I have seen how the minds of Filipinos can change when it comes to those who claim that they are serving the people. We saw this in 1986, when we put an end to a dictatorship; in 2010, when we chose to tread the Straight Path and destroy the cycle of fraud, theft, and deception; and once again in 2013, when it became all the clearer to our opponents that Filipinos no longer want the old system back.

The political climate in the country is changing: in my opinion, and in that of our bosses, the most important factor is principle. And for the LP and our entire coalition, let me emphasize: we do not only have proof, we also, and more importantly, have principle.

It is clear that, while the groups of others are being pieced together, we have a true tandem. We have a true record and a true platform. We are on the side of principle and reform. We are the Straight Path.

With Mar and Leni at its head, we have already finalized the forces that will continue, expand, and fight for our Bosses, the Filipino people. And in my remaining time in office, with the guidance of Our Lord Almighty and with you continuing to show your overwhelming support, you can count on the fact that, with full strength and resolve, I will lead the campaign for the re-election of the Straight Path, the campaign to make possible all the more what we could only dream of before, the campaign that will continue the great story of the Filipino people.

Thank you, and good day to all of you.
