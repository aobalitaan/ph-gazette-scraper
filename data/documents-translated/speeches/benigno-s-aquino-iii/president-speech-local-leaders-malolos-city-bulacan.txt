Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, at his meeting with local leaders and communities of Malolos, Bulacan

For those who may not know, the Cojuangcos, my mother’s family, started here in Malolos, Bulacan before settling in Tarlac. Our house is still there, near the Barasoain Church. I, even before, since I started working in Luisita in 1993, would travel and often traverse the NLEX alone. And that is still true until now: Every time I pass through here in Bulacan, in Pampanga, on my way to Tarlac—because of our friends, allies, relatives here, including you, my Bulacan Bosses—I really feel at home, and my trip is very safe even when I’m alone. [Applause] Thank you, and despite our heavy work, when I get to talk to you, you really lighten my heart.

When Bulacan is mentioned, the first thing that comes to mind is: Historical. Here in your midst in Malolos is the Barasoain Church, where the First Constitutional Democracy in Asia was born in 1899. [Applause] This land is also a haven for heroes, such as Marcelo H. del Pilar and Gregorio del Pilar; as well as artists like Francisco Balagtas and Nicanor Abelardo. Now that I am facing you again, I said, it is true what Bulakenyo Executive Secretary Paquito Ochoa Jr. told me: Bulacan should also be known, not only for the beauty of your places, but also for the beauty of the Bulakenyas. [Applause] You all know Valentine’s Day just passed. I’m next year. [Laughter]

A philosopher once said, whose name is George Santayana: To forget the mistakes of the past guarantees that they will be repeated. Before we traverse the Straight Path, where did we come from? I once spoke to a friend. He said to me: “Noy, I can’t forgive the one you succeeded. Almost ten years were lost to us.” That is true. Just imagine: What if, from 2001 to 2010, the government was focused on the welfare of our Bosses and not on clinging to power? What if the previous administration hadn’t allowed tons of rice to rot in warehouses due to senseless imports? What if they had stopped PAGCOR from brewing coffee, which cost billions of pesos? What if the previous administration’s mudslinging in the Laguna Lake Dredging Project had pushed through, burying us in debt of over P18 billion? Earlier, we were in the town of Hagonoy, and a child spoke. There was an inauguration of ten new classrooms there at their school. And he mentioned that he also went through a time when they studied under trees; that if they needed to use the bathroom, they would just relieve themselves by the side of the road; that their classrooms were really lacking, they were standing but during the rainy season it was as if they were bathing inside the classroom. He is now in Grade 10.

Just thinking as he spoke: What if they had focused on building those classrooms instead of squandering the money and the national treasury all over the place? How much was the school for those ten classrooms, two stories high? I was told it was P16 million. PAGCOR, as I mentioned earlier, spent P1 billion. You do the math, divide one billion by sixteen [million], that's how many classrooms could have been built. More than enough. The problem is: what we found was a shortage of 66,800 classrooms. I'll come back to that later.

We can no longer undo the past. Time, once it runs, is over. There's no stop button on a watch, and certainly no rewind. But looking back at the mistakes of the past is useful: to improve our actions in the present, and also to fix the future.

Now, in just under six years of traversing the Straight Path, it's clear how far we've come. Many of our achievements are historic. From 2010 to 2015, our average GDP growth was 6.2 percent—this is the fastest six-year economic growth in the country in almost four decades. If before, our Armed Forces only had boats and paper airplanes, now we have Weather High Endurance Cutters, Armored Personal Carriers, lead-in fighter jets, and more. Pantawid Pamilya and PhilHealth, which you probably know were used for political maneuvering before, are now truly caring for the poor. Pantawid Pamilya, which used to benefit only 790,000 households in 2010, now benefits 4.4 million households. And we will fill the 4.6 million households that need help this year. PhilHealth, which had only 51 percent coverage when we came in, the latest data shows that 92 percent of Filipinos are now PhilHealth members.

Here with you, the change is also very visible. Bulacan is now one of the most progressive provinces. [Applause] Because your local government, under the leadership of Gov. Willy Sy-Alvarado, along with Cong. Jonjon Mendoza and Cong. LV Villarica, has doubled its efforts in service, we have also doubled your province's income. From P5.23 billion in 2009, the collection of the Provincial Government and BIR reached P12.82 billion in 2014. [Applause] We have also increased our infrastructure funding by almost four times, including roads, bridges, flood control, ports, and airports here in Bulacan: From P5.01 billion from 2005 to 2010, it has reached P19.04 billion from 2011 to 2016. This includes 234 farm-to-market roads that we have completed here in Bulacan. In total, this is almost 70 kilometers, which helps more than 7,000 farmers maximize their income. For flood control and management: We completed the Angat Water Utilization and Aqueduct Improvement Project – Phase II back in July 2012. The Angat Dam and Dike Strengthening Project, which our good governor really focused on, was also signed this July and construction is now beginning. The Bulacan Bulk Water Supply Project was signed this January, and by April, the groundbreaking will take place.

These are just some of the achievements we have made on the Straight Path. But you have seen: What we once thought impossible to do, we are now able to do. It is becoming clearer: With good governance, the potential of the Filipino and the Philippines is maximized.

On our way here, we also passed by the Plaridel-San Rafael Bypass Road. I think if you've seen it, if you've been there, the road has been opened, but more importantly, Governor, factories are springing up left and right there. And you even told me earlier how high the price of land is there, because progress is already there.

Now, when May 9th arrives, the people will once again choose who will steer our country. The question is: Will we go to those who will clearly mislead us and return us to the crooked path? Will we go to those who act like a dictator and put us on a violent and dangerous course? Or perhaps, will we go to a newcomer, who is not yet sure of their destination? Or will we stay with those who have been tested in the twists and turns of service and are sure to continue our collective journey on the Straight Path?

Here with us is Mar Roxas. [Applause] For those who watched the first Presidential Debate last week, you saw that, among all of them, he is truly the one with the most “K”: Intelligence, honesty, experience, ability, and let's add, he has Korina too. [Laughter]

Mr. Mar, no longer needs to make promises, pose, or intimidate. His track record speaks for itself. While in the Senate, he pushed for the Cheaper Medicines Act. He faced the large pharmaceutical companies and fought for the right of every Filipino to affordable medicine. Just imagine: How many lives have been saved and continue to be cared for because of this initiative of Mar? You may have also noticed: As the former head of the DILG, we always call on Mar to oversee the most difficult and complicated situations in the country. From the Zamboanga crisis, the earthquakes in Cebu and Bohol, to Typhoon Yolanda—Mar was there before, during, and after the typhoon; he didn't leave the area until the situation was stable. How many of our countrymen were kept safe because of this dedication and professionalism of Mar?

What's striking about all of this: Have we ever heard him boast about these things? Despite his achievements for his fellowmen and the country, isn't he the one being ganged up on and constantly bombarded with fabricated accusations? You probably noticed: What they did to me in 2010, they are now doing to Mar. It seems they already know who their strongest opponent is.

With us today is Leni Robredo. You all know her husband, Jesse Robredo, who was truly a champion of good governance. He also taught us 'tsinelas leadership'—simple, but efficient and honest leadership. That is exactly the path Leni follows as a public servant, and that is what she will promote on the Straight Path, once she sits as Vice President. [Applause]

Alongside her work, Leni also serves as a light, and now, a pillar of the home for her family. The truth is: The decision to run was not easy for her, especially since she still has two children in school. But in the end, 'Country, before self' prevailed in Leni, and in her family. In fact, her daughter Aika, just came from Misamis Occidental yesterday, and tomorrow she will go to Pampanga. Even Tricia, and sometimes, Jillian, the youngest, are already going around the country to campaign for the Straight Path and fight for their future.

My dear Bulakenyo bosses, you all know me. I don't have the habit of flattering. Despite the hardship I bore as the heir to the problems of those who came before us, I strived to the best of my ability to serve the Filipino people honestly and fully. [Applause] In every moment of service, I remained true to you. I stand before you today, as the Father of the Nation, and ask for your greater trust and support. It is true: we can definitely continue what we have started. Let us just use our right and power to choose the right leaders. I tell you now, face to face: We are with the Coalition that will surely continue our reform agenda. Here are Mar Roxas and Leni Robredo, they will continue and expand the Straight Path, towards the realization of our dreams. [Applause]

Let me repeat: As they say in Tagalog, “To know where you are going, look at where you came from.” Our origin, for almost ten years, was led by the one I replaced. Everything we have achieved now could have been achieved ten years ago and the Filipino people could have benefited from it if the right thing had been done. [Applause] We will choose again, my countrymen. I hope we investigate: Who will truly lead us to the continuation of the change that we are already seeing? We are already feeling it, we are already benefiting from it. This is perhaps not the time to gamble, to pray that “Hopefully we get lucky and choose the right one” because we have a sure destination that will truly continue the path we are taking.

Therefore, my dear bosses, the next President of the Republic of the Philippines is none other than Mar Roxas!

Good afternoon. Thank you all very much.
