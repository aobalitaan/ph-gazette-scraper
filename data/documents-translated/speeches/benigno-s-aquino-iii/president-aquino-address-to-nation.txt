Message of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, to the Filipino people

[Delivered at the Malacañan Palace on February 6, 2015]

The path to peace is not easy. Many Filipinos have sacrificed their lives to fight those who want to continue the violence and conflict. Those who least want war are the police and soldiers, because they are the first and make the greatest sacrifice when trouble breaks out. And as President and Commander-in-Chief, I bear the responsibility for any outcome, any success, pain, or tragedy, that we may experience in seeking lasting security and peace.

On January 24, our Special Action Force conducted an operation in Mamasapano, Maguindanao. Their target was two notorious terrorists: Primarily Malaysian Zulkifli Bin Hir, alias Abu Marwan, and Basit Usman. The operation against Marwan was successful.

The cost of this success was heavy. 44 heroes of the Special Action Force sacrificed their lives. To the families of the fallen: Last Friday, I asked to be able to speak with you, and thankfully you granted me that, at a time when I could speak with you with sufficient knowledge of what happened, and suggestions for your future. And as is my responsibility to your loved ones, it is also my responsibility to you; it is my duty to ensure that the sacrifice of your relatives is properly rewarded. That remains my vow to you: In my remaining seventeen months in office, I am doing and will continue to do everything appropriate to ensure that you are in a good situation.

I am the Father of the Nation, and 44 of my children perished. They can no longer be brought back; the tragedy happened under my term; I will carry this event with me until my last days. I am responsible for them, along with the entire SAF force in this operation, including those who saved them who also put their lives in danger.

Terrorists like Marwan and Usman kill innocents without regard and without conscience, so pursuing them is inherently dangerous. This Marwan is an international terrorist, long wanted not only by us, but also by other countries. We do not expect them to be like meek sheep who will simply comply when shown a warrant.

In the operations reported to us, we always revisit the lessons or learnings they provide, especially when we lose troops. That is why I continue to ask: Is there anything else that could have been done to avoid the tragedy?

Marwan and Usman have been wanted since 2002. Many operations have been conducted to pursue them, even before we became President. This latest version of the plan has been aborted multiple times. Like you, I also want to know everything that happened on this occasion, and I have full confidence that it will come out in a thorough and impartial investigation by the Board of Inquiry. We are looking forward to its results. But as President, it is also my responsibility to identify, now, the mistakes that occurred, so that they can be corrected immediately. It is my duty to find justice as soon as possible.

Because of this, I conducted my own investigation and interviews with those involved in the operation. It is clear: The situation the troops encountered was far from what was expected in the plan.

The commander of the operation should be aware of this, especially since he has been stationed in Mindanao for a long time. He is expected to have what is called situational awareness. As commander, he knows the overall plan and the risks involved; he is the first to know if it is being implemented correctly. He should know what is happening at every moment.

In our study of what actually happened, because the situation encountered had changed significantly from the expected plan, there were at least three instances when the operation could have been aborted or postponed, or the plan drastically changed. The need to do so was further intensified because he knew there was no coordination for the expected support, and the assistance from the Armed Forces was thin, if any, due to insufficient time to prepare.

Why was there no coordination? Why was the mission continued when it had deviated so much from the original plan, and the danger facing our troops was so great? These, and many others, are the questions that trouble my mind. The former SAF commander will have the opportunity to explain these when he faces the proper authorities.

General Alan Purisima played a big part in our objective of pursuing Marwan and Usman. He contributed a lot to the long process and many operations planned for this. It is also not unknown to the public that we went through a lot. During the coup d’etat, or the attempted coup d’etat of 1987, I was confident before we were ambushed that our security personnel had sufficient capabilities. Because almost all of my companions were lost, my confidence was shaken. Alan Purisima designed, implemented, and trained us in a modified VIP protection course; it greatly helped restore my confidence. Since then, until now, we have gone through a lot together; he has been with me in the struggle against powerful interests that could harm us. During the times when I was part of the opposition, even though it was dangerous to his career to be close to me, Alan did not leave me.

Therefore, I believe you will understand why it pains me that he is leaving the service under these circumstances. I accept, effective immediately, the resignation of General Purisima. And I thank him for his long years of service before this tragedy occurred.

I assure you: We are doing everything to find out the whole truth. Any report that will be created will give us valuable lessons, to ensure that this tragedy will not happen again. There will be changes.

I am fulfilling, and will continue to fulfill, my duties. The morale of the Special Action Force troops must be restored. Their status as a fully operational unit must be returned. Although there are those who want to create discord between the AFP and PNP, we will ensure that they do not succeed; their very good relationship should not be wasted, which we are already seeing in rescue operations during calamities, or in other operations where they work together to respond to threats to our security, such as what happened in the Zamboanga siege. Let us remember, our work is not yet finished: Usman is still at large.

To the members and leadership of the MILF: From the beginning, I have considered you as brothers in the pursuit of peace. And until now, I hope that you will help find justice; all those who are guilty must be held accountable, especially if it is true that those who were wounded and unable to fight were still killed. Your action to limit the movements of the BIFF is a good first step.

Regarding Usman, I wish to state: If he is within your territory or under the custody of anyone among you, I expect you to surrender him to the authorities. If not, do everything to help in his arrest. And if this is still not possible, do not interfere with our prosecution of Usman.

Let this serve as a warning and a reminder: We will arrest Usman, whatever your decision, whoever shelters him, and wherever he hides. There should be no doubt: Our aspirations for peace and justice are shared. To those who have strayed from the path, who will attempt to obstruct our pursuit of Usman, remember this: The State is your enemy, and we will crush you.

To the members of our uniformed ranks: We are with you, in front, beside, or behind, wherever you need us; whatever is asked of you, we are ready to do it with you. I assure you: You are not alone.

Again, to the families of the fallen members of the SAF: No words can suffice to alleviate the pain of loss. To each of you, and to everyone whose life is put in danger, as the Filipino people told us when we were orphaned, I say to you now: You are not alone.

To all those who join us in seeking peace: We are determined to continue the fight. And I also want to say to those who obstruct our aspirations, especially those who use violence: Engrave this in stone, you will feel even more the sharpened and intensified strength of the united Filipino people.

Let us remain focused on our main objective: Broad and lasting peace. This is what the Special Action Force fought for in Mamasapano. This is what every decent Filipino who wants to leave a better future for the next generations continues to fight for. Through unity and cooperation, we will achieve justice, we will repay the sacrifice of our police, and we will fulfill our collective dreams.

Good evening, thank you all very much.
