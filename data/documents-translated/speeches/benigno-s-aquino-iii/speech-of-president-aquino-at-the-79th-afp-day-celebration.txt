Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino President of the Philippines On the 79th anniversary of the founding of the Armed Forces of the Philippines

Just last Monday, we faced 146 new AFP officers who underwent the Officer Candidate Course. During their graduation, we informed them of the weight of the responsibility held by a Filipino soldier. The mandate is clear for each of you: To uphold an Armed Forces that is a partner in protecting democracy; an Armed Forces that is a model of resilience in the face of challenges; an Armed Forces that is a pillar and source of inspiration for so many Filipinos. This is exactly what we are celebrating on this 79th anniversary of your organization: the principle, courage, and dedication shown by every officer, soldier, reservist, and personnel of the AFP focused on protecting the interests of our Bosses, the people.

The entire nation pays tribute to the meaningful service of our soldiers; you who are always ready to defend and help our countrymen. For example: There are the skilled members of our Air Force. During the height of the Zamboanga Crisis, they ensured that our C130s were always operational. We never heard a complaint from them; instead, every mission assigned to them was wholeheartedly addressed by the Air Force to deliver the necessary equipment to our troops in Zamboanga. There are also our soldiers guarding the West Philippine Sea, and even in other remote parts of our territory. I remember our supply operation to Ayungin for our Marines there. Our supply team was blocked, but you showed the talent and imagination of the Filipino; you completed the mission without raising tensions. With such skill, how can we not strengthen our confidence in our soldiers? In your demonstration of excellence, you proved: our military is truly dependable.

Of course: There are our peacekeepers, like those who guarded the Golan Heights, who, despite facing an impossible mission, truly demonstrated the ability and courage of the Filipino to the world. We also cannot forget the many soldiers who served as frontliners and first responders despite the numerous trials faced by our country in recent years. From the earthquakes in Bohol and Cebu, to the earlier typhoons of Sendong and Pablo; from Santi and Yolanda, and most recently Ruby, the Armed Forces did not waver; instead, you led our relief and clearing operations in affected communities, to deliver hope to them during times of tragedy.

As your Commander-in-Chief, I am truly proud to lead an Armed Forces that reflects the good qualities of our race: mutual support, compassion, and loyalty to our fellowmen and our flag. We also congratulate the 28 members of your organization whom we honored today. Through your demonstration of service, the rising quality of service of our soldiers is further highlighted.

What is the result of faithful and reliable service from our military? Your organization is key to strengthening what we call a “virtuous cycle.” Because you protect peace and stability, the government is able to plant more reforms, and investors are more encouraged to invest in our country. The result of this: the growth of industry and the improvement of our economy; this in turn creates more opportunities that deliver broader progress for our people.

We have good news for your anniversary: Senate President Franklin Drilon told us that the proposal to increase the subsistence allowance of our soldiers, police, and other members of the uniformed service from P90 to P150 was passed last night in the Senate and the House of Representatives. We are just awaiting the proposal, and as soon as it lands on our desk, we will sign it immediately.

Now that our entire Armed Forces is undergoing modernization and facing what can be called a “renaissance,” your government will do everything in its power to enable you to reach higher levels of service; deepen your capabilities; and strengthen the professionalism and integrity of your institution.

Indeed: From modern equipment such as ships, helicopters, and fighter jets; to capability development and professionalism enhancement of our soldiers through the AFP Transformation Roadmap, we have ensured that the deficiencies and needs in your ranks are addressed. We will further intensify the force-on-force training of our soldiers, along with investing in what you call “miles equipment.”

It is clear: Your government is maximizing the opportunity to strengthen your ranks and deliver the appropriate benefits to you; we are pushing reforms that bring more service to the members of the AFP, and to your families. Our housing program for you and other uniformed personnel continues to progress. Our soldiers, active or retired, are also beginning to benefit from our livelihood programs such as in Fort Magsaysay. In addition, we are streamlining the complex pension system we inherited from our uniformed personnel. The summary of all our efforts: We want to advance the Armed Forces of the Philippines to be more modern, more reliable, and admired by the whole world.

The time when the defenders of the nation had insufficient firearms is over; the time when even proper and necessary clothing could not be provided to you by the government is over; the time when buying Kevlar helmets was a source of profit and the lives of our soldiers were put at risk is over. Now, the state and the people fully support you. And to those who still want to return your institution to the old ways: Be ashamed, and be afraid. The reforms will not stop, and our efforts to ensure that the corrupt are held accountable will not stop.

And to our soldiers: I hope you will not forget that at all times, you have an obligation to do what is right and just. Do not succumb to temptation or allow yourselves to be dragged into the schemes of anyone who plans to lead us astray. 2016 is approaching, and we all know that during election season, there will really be those who will try to use you for personal gain. The mandate of the people: It is our duty to ensure peace and order during the elections. Your ranks are expected to remain on the side of the people.

Now, during this Christmas season, being away from your loved ones is also a challenge for many of you. However, I know that you also draw strength from ensuring that our countrymen can peacefully and safely celebrate Christmas. On behalf of the nation, thank you very much for your service.

In the remaining moments of our administration, you can count on us to reach even higher aspirations for your ranks and for the larger society. With the guidance and love of the Lord, we will surely overcome any challenges we encounter towards the realization of the future we aspire to. In all of this, I am confident that our Armed Forces will continue to demonstrate the excellence, discipline, and honor that is the hallmark of the Filipino soldier.

Thank you very much, happy anniversary, and Merry Christmas to all of you.
