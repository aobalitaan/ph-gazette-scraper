Speech

of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

In a meeting with local leaders and communities of Mandaue City, Cebu

[Declared at the Mandaue City Sports and Cultural Complex, Cebu, on March 3, 2016]

Every time I visit Cebu, some memories come back to me. As I recall, it was only about a month after my father was assassinated that I was sent here to your province. That was my very first speaking engagement after what happened to my father. When we arrived in Cebu, on board Philippine Air Lines at that time, the exit was at the tail of the plane. As we disembarked, there were members who were called Aviation Security Command, or AVSECOM. The same group that met my father. The PAL crew said, look down, and you'll see the AVSECOM personnel, and they said to me, “Sir, goodluck to you.” And truthfully—of course, I'm not saying I was the same as my father—but what would happen to me when I stepped down that ladder? Because, as you know, my father never set foot on our land again. He was assassinated even before reaching the ground.

Despite this, I clearly remember: I was really surprised by the feeling here with you in Cebu because it felt like Martial Law was already over, just a month after my father was assassinated. I felt in the warmth of your welcome that there was a chance for democracy to return, and the Cebuanos were already leading the way, even before People Power was thought of, you were already showing how to fight against the dictatorship. Again: Thank you very much for everything. [Applause]

Before the first EDSA People Power, my mother also came here to Cebu to call for civil disobedience, and our beloved Cebuanos truly supported her. I clearly remember, how could we protect our mother’s security with so many threats? The leader was removed, my father was assassinated, I was thinking: There is a danger that something will happen to my mother. But here in Cebu, when the EDSA Revolution arose, she was truly safe in the company of the Cebuanos. And with all of this, what I can say is: During those moments, my deep gratitude towards all of you from Cebu began. This is also the point why we are here today: To be with you again. [Applause]

Let me share this: Yesterday, upon our landing at your airport, I noticed that the road leading to your convention center was a bit dark. As well as the convention center itself, it was dark. There were lampposts, but they seemed like posts only—the important light was missing. Some, there weren’t even posts anymore. Wasn’t it controversial those overpriced lamps that were bought by the previous leadership, but now cannot be used?

To be honest, I just shake my head whenever I remember this issue. That is exactly the difference between the old system compared to our Straight Path. For them, the question of those in power was: How can I benefit from this position? When we said “If there is no corruption, there is no poverty,” where no one will be left behind, we only had one question in implementing every project and program of the government. This is the only measure: How can our Bosses, the people, especially those on the margins of society, benefit the most from the proposed project or program? If it cannot pass that measure, set it aside.

That is where our projects for Cebu originate. In infrastructure, there’s the Mactan Island Circumferential Road, which completed its concreting in December 2015. The rehabilitation of Serging Osmeña Boulevard was also completed last June of the previous year. Those underpasses in the Channel 2 Section and Viaduct Section of the Cebu South Coastal Road were completed last January. Ouano Avenue in Mandaue City, the repairs were completed as early as June 2013.

The transformation will become even clearer once the large-scale projects are completed, such as the new Passenger Terminal Building at your Mactan-Cebu International Airport. Its construction began in June 2015; upon its completion in 2019, the airport’s passenger capacity will reach an estimated 15 million passengers, up from the previous 4.5 million. [Applause] Last year, the combined number of foreign and domestic tourists who visited Cebu reached over 3.9 million. Each of our foreign and domestic tourists will spend money to go around or eat here; some will stay in hotels, or hire a tour guide. I have said this before: Every foreign tourist arrival is equivalent to one job for a Filipino. Imagine the extent of the benefits that tourism will bring if 15 million people visit your province.

My Bosses, when I reviewed everything we have done for your province, I said: The Straight Path truly showed a different level of focus and care for Cebu. For airports, ports, and mass transit, our DOTC has already allocated an additional P856.14 million. To support agriculture, we have also allocated an additional P1.61 billion for irrigation and farm-to-market roads.

For roads and bridges, flood control projects, and other infrastructure such as health facilities and school and government buildings, we have allocated almost P32 billion for Cebu from 2011 to the present. This is far from the P12 billion from 2005 to 2010 of our predecessor. [Applause] You can see what our replacement is flaunting; look at what the Straight Path has accomplished. That’s why I wonder when someone says: The Straight Path neglected Cebu. My answer: You yourselves can ask our Cebuano Bosses who neglected whom. And we will know the answer after May 9.

Now, you can see that “Good governance means good economics” is true. Here in your province, there is indeed traffic due to high car sales—the same is true in the National Capital Region—but consider this, more than 26,000 new vehicles are on the road across the country every month. It is not easy to lay down infrastructure, but the government is doing everything it can to meet your needs. Here in Cebu, there will be the first Bus Rapid Transit system in the entire Philippines. [Applause] It cannot be done haphazardly. It needs to be studied, it needs to be logical, it needs to be done correctly. The detailed engineering design is currently being done, and once the project is completed in July 2018, Cebu’s infrastructure will become even more modern.

Of course, this is accompanied by other initiatives. Earlier, we inaugurated the PAGCOR school building at Guadalupe Elementary School, and we turned over newly renovated heritage sites that will further revitalize your tourism. All of this will be linked to the further progress of your province.

Now, some are asking us: What will happen to all the good things happening in the country, now that you are stepping down from office? We will indeed face a crossroads in May. For me, in choosing a leader: Do we go with someone who acts like a dictator? Do we go with someone who, unable to answer accusations of theft, simply lashes out at our accomplishments? Do we go with someone who has no proof of their ability to continue what we have worked hard to start, and we just hope that they will continue it?

In my opinion, it is very clear who truly has the “K”: someone with ability, conscience, truthfulness in words and deeds, and of course, someone with Korina too. That is no other than Mar Roxas. [Applause]

Here in Cebu, I have been told that your IT-BPM industry is very strong. In fact, you are now the 7th IT-BPM destination in the world. Some may not know: Mar Roxas is the main reason for the entry and growth of call centers in our country; [applause] now, more than a million direct jobs have been created because of this. You know, this year, we expect the BPO sector to reach 1.3 million direct employees. This will bring $25 billion in investment to us. Mar started all of this. They say that when you are in call centers and other IT institutes, it’s like being an OFW in terms of salary but you are still in the Philippines.

Mar created this, Mar has done this before; I have no doubt that Mar will surpass this in the days to come. When there is something to address, when the nation faces a challenge, Mar Roxas, without hesitation, without drama—is first on the ground zero, first to sacrifice, quietly doing the job. To Mar Roxas alone do we entrust the leadership to continue the Straight Path. [Applause]

I just want to emphasize that: He was DILG Secretary, a storm was coming. We know Mar was there, but go to the internet as well. Count how many times Mar highlighted that he was in the affected area. I think, we all have the same memory. The important thing there is to identify the needs, ensure that the needs arrive immediately, not to promote oneself. That is important, especially now that many are saying they love you.

As for the Vice Presidency: I also present to you another contender, Leni Robredo. [Applause] When her husband Jesse passed away, Leni stood as both father and mother to her three daughters. But she was called to serve as a representative. Leni could not abandon her duty to her fellow citizens. She could have said, “I am a recent widow.” She could have said, “I still have two children in school.” She could have said, “I am first settling our family’s life.” But when the people asked, Leni did not fall short, and as a representative, she showed her competence in service, especially to those on the margins of society, those most in need. Now, a higher calling has been presented to her—and once again, she has shown her willingness to sacrifice. It is true: Leni Robredo has done a lot, has accomplished a lot, and will still do a lot, and surely there is only one word. [Applause]

Some of you may have seen our ad. I said in the ad, you know I am an expert in courtship, I only ask Junjun Davide for advice on marriage. [Laughter] And this election, campaign, you know, all kinds of courtship will happen. But there are those who are sincere and those who are not, right? And to see, there is a saying: To see where someone is going, look at where they came from.

We have an opponent, an opponent of Mar, who originally started with his popularity: an opponent of Marcos. Now that he is running for President, he tried to get the son of Marcos as Vice President. And that son hasn't said, “We made mistakes.” When he didn't get him, the one who joined him was his former opponent who supposedly defended democracy. The one who fought against the coup, is now with the one who staged the coup. Shouldn't we ask this friend of ours: Brother, where will you take us if you become President? What is this “move on”? With all this “moving on,” what remains of the principles you once held?

During this time, I remember what a close friend told me: “Noy, what I can’t forgive your predecessor for is the almost ten years that were lost to us.” It is true: Isn't it true that many of our initiatives could have been done a long time ago, if the government had only focused on the people it promised to serve, and not on clinging to power? That is precisely the mindset and culture that we changed. In our almost six years in office, we maximized and exhausted every opportunity to improve the lives of our Bosses.

Given how far we have come, should we entrust our future to “maybe”? Maybe he won’t steal. Maybe he won’t abuse us. Maybe he will continue what we have started. My question is, why should we bet on “maybe,” when we have something certain? Given the extent of the transformation being enjoyed by Cebu and the entire country, I am confident: You yourselves will say that on the Straight Path, the Cebuanos will surely not be left behind. On the Straight Path, progress is certain. And on the Straight Path—under the leadership of Mar Roxas and Leni Robredo—all of this will surely continue. [Applause]

There are only 120 days left in our term. I can’t shake from my mind that some people are telling me—and this is very true—respected people are advising me, they say to me, “Can you just not endorse anyone?” Just be practical, you won’t have any enemies. If you endorse someone, their allies are your allies. All of his opponents are your enemies. We all know how dirty politics is in this country. I think I won’t be spared from all sorts of accusations and absurdities. The important thing is for them to get their names in the papers.

I could have just thought, “Okay, maybe I can just stay on the sidelines, since there are already many people vying for attention there.” My life was peaceful. My siblings, my nieces and nephews, could benefit from the little peace we have. But I think, if I did that, I would have failed you. You already considered me the “Father of the Nation,” and then I would tell you to fend for yourselves. Or that I’ve already done my part. Or let Batman take care of you. I don’t think I was raised that way. I have a responsibility to share with you an assessment of everyone who wants to lead us to a better place.

I believe I have fulfilled my promise to you, that what I leave behind will be far better than what we inherited. And I will repeat: You made all of this happen. The direction of our future, you will still shape. And that, we need to make the right decision on May 9th. [Applause] This is our future, we have the responsibility.

My brothers and sisters, the next President of the Republic of the Philippines is none other than Mar Roxas.
