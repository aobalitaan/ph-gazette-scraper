Statement of His Excellency Benigno S. Aquino III President of the Philippines on Typhoon Lando

My fellow citizens, I am addressing you today to provide some information and reminders regarding Typhoon Lando, which is now within the Philippine Area of Responsibility.

At this point, the eye of the typhoon was observed 510 kilometers east of Baler, Aurora. According to PAGASA data, its wind speed is 130 kph with gusts of 160 kph.

Typhoon Lando is not moving freely because it is being hindered by the remnants of Typhoon Champi. There is also a high pressure area above Lando. The result: Lando’s movement out of our country will be slowed down. PAGASA estimates that intense rainfall could last from 6 to 12 hours. Due to the faster evaporation caused by El Niño, more moisture will be absorbed, which will add to the volume of rainfall. According to PAGASA, Lando’s wind speed could reach 185 kph, which means that Storm Signal Number 4 could be raised in affected areas.

Typhoon Lando is expected to make landfall in Luzon in the early hours of Sunday. The effects of Lando are expected to be felt in Regions 1, 2, 3, 4A, 4B, 5, and CAR. Based on DSWD records, we need to focus on 1.5 million families in these regions.

I urge every Filipino: Let us avoid panicking. Let us do the necessary preparations. Let us monitor the reports; understand the meanings of the warnings issued by national and local governments—from storm signals, rainfall warnings, storm surge advisories, and even evacuation notices if necessary.

You can rest assured: under the leadership of the NDRRMC and its executive director, Usec. Alex Pama, coordination among various government agencies continues, including the private sector such as our telecommunications and oil companies. We are also working with the World Food Program and other organizations.

The DSWD has already prepositioned food packs to quickly deliver aid to affected communities and evacuation centers. They are coordinating with the DPWH for the storage of relief goods. The DPWH has also prepared heavy equipment to quickly clear roads that may be blocked by debris. Our DOE and NEA are ready to act to restore electricity in areas hit by the typhoon. The DepEd has also informed all schools covered by the affected regions. The DOH is also ready with first aid supplies and necessary medicines. The DTI is monitoring the prices of goods to prevent exploitation of our countrymen.

The DILG, PNP, and BFP are actively implementing Oplan Listo. Sec. Mel Sarmiento is personally coordinating with our provincial heads and LGUs to remind them of what needs to be done during this time. Planes, ships, and other assets of our Armed Forces and Coast Guard are also on standby. If you see more police and soldiers in your area, do not be alarmed; I assure you they are there to help our first responders.

Clearly: Your government is here to ensure that we achieve our goal of zero casualties. But I must emphasize: Every LGU, community, and Filipino affected has a responsibility to cooperate in our collective action to overcome the challenges ahead. Let me also remind you: After this typhoon, it may bring relief. Because the rainfall will add to the water level of our dams, which will help mitigate the effects of El Niño.

Thank you very much, and may we all be safe.
