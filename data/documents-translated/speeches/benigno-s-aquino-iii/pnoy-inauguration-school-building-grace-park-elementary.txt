Speech

of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

during the inauguration of the two buildings

of Grace Park Elementary School

[Delivered at Grace Park Elementary School, Caloocan City, on March 30, 2016]

You know, with the warmth of your welcome from outside, I wonder what I could still say here because it seems you are already convinced. [Laughter] Then our student who spoke earlier was very good. Followed by our teacher who has been here for 13 years, and even sang! I said, “It seems like I might have to sing here too.” [Applause] But because [we are proud of the flood control], the problem of flooding here in Caloocan is supposedly no longer a problem, I won’t sing and risk testing that claim. [Laughter]

This afternoon, we once again affirm the positive results of good governance and collectively treading the Straight Path. We inaugurated two new school buildings here at Grace Park Elementary School, which will surely be a launching pad for a better future for the youth of Caloocan. I heard that Senior High School will be transferred here; its population will almost double. Your facilities, they say, can handle it and operate on a single shift; it won’t be like before. Imagine the student, a double shift. What does a double shift mean? A double shift, you can even have a triple shift in a factory. But when it comes to studying, for a child, proper sleep, proper meal times, proper study times, with rest, etc., all of that needs to be completed to complete development. When you do multiple shifts—in other places it reaches three to four. Elementary school children will start in the late afternoon, finish late at night, and go home at night. That’s not a proper policy. That’s what we are changing.

One of the buildings here was funded by PAGCOR under the leadership of the good Chairman Bong Naguiat, under the Straight Path, through the Classroom School Building Project. The aim of this program is to help us build classrooms across the country: DepEd identifies the schools that still need classrooms, PAGCOR provides the funding, and DPWH constructs the buildings. From 2011 to 2015, PAGCOR allocated P12 billion for approximately 6,500 classrooms. Perhaps we can also thank PAGCOR. [Applause]

Truly, the system and operations at PAGCOR are very different now. If we remember before, billions of pesos were simply wasted on buying coffee. I even asked, when they drink that billion-peso coffee, along with the guilt of their actions, can those people even sleep? Now, this fund is being used to raise the level of education throughout the country. So to our agencies, to the local government, and to every Boss who is joining this program: Thank you very much to all of you. [Applause]

The construction of these classrooms is just part of our extensive agenda to strengthen the education sector. If you will remember, one of the problems we inherited was a backlog of 66,800 classrooms, 61.7 million textbooks, 2.5 million chairs, as well as 145,827 teachers. Because of honest governance, we have addressed these, along with other needs brought about by the implementation of K to 12. Before we even stepped down from office, according to Bro. Armin, we had already built and funded more than 185,000 classrooms.

Of course, we cannot focus on just one sector. We also need to address the other needs of our Bosses. There's the Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program, which ensures that our youth are healthy and able to attend school. Here in Caloocan, as mentioned earlier, you were fortunate in the past: 2,141 beneficiary households in 2010.

Why are you fortunate, you might ask. We visited several provinces such as Cavite, Batangas, and Basilan. There were so many beneficiaries there. So many of them were zero. Those three provinces seemed to say that there was no one on the margins of society, that Pantawid Pamilya wasn't helping anyone when we started. Now, under our term, we searched for those who truly needed help and expanded the coverage of Pantawid Pamilya. And currently, as mentioned earlier, 31,510 households, from just two thousand previously, are benefiting from the benefits of the Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program. They are part of the 4.4 million beneficiary households of Pantawid Pamilya nationwide. According to initial studies, 7.7 million individuals have already been helped by the program to cross what is called the “poverty line.” They are no longer among our poorest compatriots and have risen, in a way.

The coverage of our PhilHealth has also expanded. Here in your area, 1.2 million individuals are now benefiting from PhilHealth because they are members. They are part of the 93 million Filipinos who are now receiving immediate care from the state. Our only concern is: What if the person who replaces us changes their mind and stops these programs? I would like to emphasize this strongly: Even during my mother's time, education was free until high school. Then we had many out-of-school youth afterwards. They asked, “Why are there so many out-of-school youth?” The reason cited: poor health, easily getting sick, unable to attend. The cited factor: just for fare, their parents cannot afford it, and they are forced to stop studying.

Of course, if you don't finish even a little, if you don't get a course from TESDA, the jobs you get are what are called “menial jobs”—you are easily replaced, the salary is low, and many are scrambling for them. It seems there is no solution or way to improve your livelihood. On the Straight Path, we are insisting that classrooms are there because there are not enough classrooms. Let's also help the family to take care of the health of their children and provide a way to support their education, that's why there is the Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program. Because there are many needs, sometimes nutrition is poor, people get sick, we strengthened PhilHealth so you have something to lean on when illness comes; we expanded it in the number of members, and we also expanded it in the number of claims processed by PhilHealth.

You know, it's true: Since 2009, when there were those suggesting I run and run as President, until now as we are nearing the end, I can say that you truly did not leave us, including my father and including my mother. When I was asked to run in 2009, we stood firm when we said yes, and we overcame all the obstacles they put in our way, all the challenges. Now, all our achievements will continue because of your trust and contribution to our reform agenda.

What's good is that we didn't add to the burden, except for what is called the Sin Tax, which aims to further improve our health services. We are helping others without increasing our burden. As Honorable Mel Sarmiento said, when he was mayor there in Samar, the PTA—Parent-Teachers’ Association—called a meeting. Apparently, the child said there, “Dad, oh no, Dad. Another contribution to the PTA.” [Laughter] Whenever there's a project, no matter how good, pull out Jess Lapid [a reference to a politician known for extracting funds]. The question we ask is: Is there anyone with sound mind who would say we should stop walking the Straight Path?

Under our term, the only thing we can be proud of: We did not steal and we only focused on the welfare of our Bosses, the people. We have come so far. Someone told me at the beginning of my term, they could not forgive those who came before us because of almost a decade that was lost to us. Imagine, what we have done could have been done by anyone who came before us. The opportunity was there if the focus was only on the welfare of the people. You know, those I replaced, their focus was on how to stay in power. They forgot the people. So we just restored the purpose of a government established with the consent of the people: to truly serve the people.

Comrades, excuse me for adding a little reminder, I was also called the Father of the Nation earlier, but we are not yet married, so I will just claim you as my children. My children and my Bosses, the message is simple: It's been a long way since 2010. In 2010, what was the ambition of the ordinary Filipino? How can I leave the Philippines? Why do I need to leave the Philippines? I don't see any hope. There's no patience that there will be a stew. No matter how much sacrifice, it seems to lead nowhere. Now, that is gradually being reversed. Our 10 million OFWs, now it's 9.4 million. Many of them have returned to the Philippines, and we have even lowered the unemployment rate. Heading here, I passed by so many factories earlier, it said there: “Immediate hiring,” “Wanted for immediate employment.” When Egay and I were young, the sign usually said “No vacancy.”

Comrades, who did this? You did. You gave me permission to lead you, a truly great race. We have reached everything we have reached because we are focused on people, not on ourselves. Not overly arrogant that they all know everything, they all have wisdom, they all have the right solution, they are the only handsome ones in the world. For me, I always repeat with our colleagues, everything that happens here, we made it happen.

Someone asked me, Sandy Javier said recently, the owner of Andok’s, a colleague of ours, went to Fairview. He said the tricycle drivers there asked him, Sandy asked, who they wanted to replace me. The drivers replied, “Couldn’t we just continue as is?” Then Sandy asked, “Why do you want to continue?” Previously, they earned P300 a day. Now, they earn P700. There is more money with people so they have more passengers. I hope that continues.

I have one more restaurant I went to recently, about two weeks ago, they said to me, “Couldn’t we have an extension, even just for three years?” To be honest, the love in my heart and in my whole family is still there. But I often reply, let’s not forget, this year is the 30th year of EDSA. It reminds us that there was a person who served two terms—in the Constitution back then, two terms were allowed—and then asked for an additional 13 years. And we cannot say that he voluntarily stepped down from office. Let’s not create a way to open the door again to a person who might not leave office and might have a successor from his own family ready. It is better to look for a real replacement who will truly continue what we have started, and because he will be starting from a higher point, we will go much further.

This is my earnest request to all of you: On May 9, we will all vote. My vote, there are no multiples. When I vote, there is only one count. When you vote, one. Whoever gets the most votes, will take office. The majority decides for all—that is our democratic system.

I implore you: Examine, examine all the candidates. I believe you will see, like my assessment, that only one truly accompanied us when we embarked on the Straight Path. Only one truly stood by us through all the hardships we faced here. Only one wasn't particularly interested, always looking for a photo opportunity. Those who have little to show need to broadcast it to the whole world. It's important, you promised to serve, so serve. If someone thanks you, that's a bonus. But fulfill your promise first.

We reached this point, you made it happen. Where will we go from here? You will decide that too. I earnestly implore you: Blood, sweat, tears, resentment – we have gone through so many things to reach this point. It is in our hands to ensure that these six years were not just a vacation. This, we can make the beginning of reaching even greater heights. I cannot do it alone. From the very beginning when we faced you, since the time my father was imprisoned, that has always been our message: Ninoy Aquino would not have done it alone, Cory Aquino would not have done it alone, without you, we are nothing. So my plea to you is: Those who will shape the future, there is no one else but us.

When I look at the children… This is the last message I want to leave with you. You know, I haven't gotten married yet. After I leave this position, maybe there will be a chance. [Laughter] But I have nephews and nieces, I look at them, of course they tell me about their lives. There's the youngest, Bimby, and the oldest, whose name is Justin, Jiggy. And it's important to me, what is the sign of success? For me, it's very simple. If the next generation, the next generation, the problems they face are not the same problems I faced or am facing. If their problems are simpler, then we have truly succeeded.

As they say, the future will come, but it is made now. It is in your hands, and I was taught to trust the Filipino people, that's why you are my Bosses. It is truly an honor to serve all of you. And a strong plea: We have achieved something, should we now fall apart when we are succeeding? I think that's wrong. We should strengthen our forces even more. We should multiply in number, because the future is truly at stake and in danger if we make a mistake on May 9th.

Good afternoon. Thank you very much to all of you.
