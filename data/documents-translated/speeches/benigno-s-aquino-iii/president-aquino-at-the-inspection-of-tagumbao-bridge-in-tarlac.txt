Speech

of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

During the inspection of Tagumbao Bridge

[Declared at Tagumbao Bridge, Gerona, Tarlac, on April 11, 2016]

Perhaps this is one of the last times I will come here as your President, so allow me to give a short report. What have we accomplished together on the Straight Path?

Earlier, the six classrooms were mentioned. I immediately said, 'Six classrooms, and you named them after Corazon C. Aquino. Out of the 185,000 classrooms we are building and have built throughout the Philippines, only six went to Gerona.' It turns out, there are 28 there. That number is part of the 1,855 classrooms we have built or are building for the province of Tarlac alone. And 28 of those are indeed at Corazon C. Aquino High School.

Regarding infrastructure, from 2005 to 2010, the allocation for Tarlac by my predecessor was P2.06 billion. We, from 2011 to 2016, for infrastructure—bridges, roads, flood control, and others—reached P13.6 billion. [Applause] That’s roughly a 600 percent increase. We have completed the two phases of TPLEX, the Paniqui-Moncada Section of Manila North Road, and the Sapang Burgos Bridge. If you are not aware, Sapang Burgos is located in Paniqui. In June, we will also begin a new large highway, called the Central Luzon Link Expressway—this goes from Tarlac City to Cabanatuan.

Throughout the country, in just under six years of our term, we have completed 18,000 kilometers of national roads and 107,000 lineal meters of bridges.

To support our farmers, P727.44 million was allocated for the construction of farm-to-market road projects, here in Tarlac, and P245 [million] for production and post-harvest facilities. We also allocated P10.59 billion for the irrigation system in Tarlac, with the largest portion being the Balog-Balog Multi-purpose Project Phase 2. You know, during my mother’s time, the Italian government offered to fund this. It was started, the government in Italy changed, their priorities changed, and it was put on hold. Now, it has been thoroughly studied, we ensured that there would be no question about the correctness of this project; we expect the Balog-Balog to be completed in about three years—it’s really big—by 2019. For flood control projects, we have allocated P1.02 billion for the rivers of Camiling, Tarlac, and Rio Chico River.

In education, I already mentioned to you how many classrooms.

Regarding health: We allocated P414.57 million to the Health Facilities Enhancement Program of the Department of Health. That went to our rural health units, barangay health stations, urban health centers, and the hospital, including the Tarlac Provincial Hospital.

Another thing I want to report to you: The Sitio Electrification Program, we listed the sitios without electricity in 2011. And the NEA reported to us that this March, or the past month, 100 percent has been completed, or here in Tarlac, 410 sitios. I met with Boy Agudo, along with other colleagues in Baguio in February, where he complained that it wasn't true. So the NEA went to him and told me, “Boy, you are now happy. All sitios have been completed. We have no debts here in Gerona.” [Applause] You can see, you might say you are weak with me. As Dennis said earlier, he asked for an ambulance, it was delivered; in February, Boy Agudo asked for the sitio, it was also completed by March, he told me. I am the only one weak with you, Boy Agudo. [Laughter]

The Tagumbao Bridge is really big. You saw how many zeros will be spent on it. You know, when I was a congressman—and let's give proper credit—one of the first I heard speak about this was [Rolly Cembrano] at that time, followed by [Jaime], followed by Dennis, who really envisioned this bridge being built.

And during that time, as your congressman, we went to two projects of the DPWH: the Pampanga River Delta and the Lower Agno. I was complaining about the floods, we were looking for a way, how can we have this bridge? The answer I received from each agency was that Tarlac was not within their scope. Pampanga said, I went to the Lower Agno project. When I went there, they told me we were not within their scope, we should go back to the Pampanga River Delta. In other words, your representative was made to run around like a ping pong ball. Right, back and forth, and nothing happened?

To be honest, I asked the DPWH. We had a PDAF at that time, worth around P40 million. I said, “Can I commit my entire PDAF to you to build this bridge?” It was that important. [Applause] We were not successful. I was told that an installment plan was not possible. They said the funds were already fully allocated.

My problem back then was, of course, during the rainy season, the water rushed, eroding the riverbank. It grew bigger and wider. The old, narrower area became expensive. Of course, when it widened, it became even more expensive. Dennis was the first to mention to me all the bridges used for lahar that we no longer use. That was the first thing we tried to do here, and a temporary one was built, I think.

Now, we had a secretary like Babes Singson. The estimate was P375,250,000, but the actual cost became P248 million. We saved [P108 million] on that project. [Applause]

To be honest, when we talk about what the Straight Path really is, Gerona is a town that is dry during the summer; during the rainy season, it has a [west] side and a center. When the people from the west side go to the town proper, the poblacion, they pass through Sta. Ignacia, pass through Tarlac City, and come here. Of course, the expenses, the length of the journey suddenly increases, adding to transportation costs, and the people are burdened—our 4Ps beneficiary mentioned this to us earlier.

How do we decide on which projects to undertake? It’s simple: What are the needs of the people? And here, we can ask: Don't the people of Gerona have the right to remain in their town whether it’s summer or rainy season? And all they need is this bridge.

We almost didn’t have anything left in our budget when we started. We worked hard on that, we grew the economy, tax revenues increased, and we gained the capacity to build this. Sorry, this was completed in 2014, and I only passed by here in 2016. But as I said, I wanted to see it because there was a time when I thought it was impossible for us to have a bridge like this.

In schools, why am I mentioning 1,800? Captain Tolentino, Boy, and other captains who were with me that time can attest to this. We were lucky to have eight to ten classrooms in the entire district. Each year, the budget can only support eight thousand classrooms throughout the Philippines. But here in Tarlac, what we will complete before we step down is 1,855, if my memory serves me right. And that’s because that’s the need of our countrymen. [Applause]

For the Pantawid Pamilya program, we used to have around 2,700 members when we first took office. Now, 39,000 are receiving state aid, just to keep their children in school and to take care of their health so that even after finishing high school, they have a new opportunity for a better job. We improved the education system. After graduation, there’s TESDA. If you want to enroll in TESDA, the placement rate—those who found a job after graduation—was 28.5 percent. Now, 72 percent of those who complete it have a job within six months. And we really want to expand this opportunity even further.

Of course, with K to 12, studies have been lengthened but children will learn more. Let's ensure that children finish their education so that they will have much better jobs. Is that right?

Now, you and I are here exposed to the sun. That's okay with me. But I am embarrassed for you—you are my Boss. My colleagues, it's very simple, everything we did in six years, could have been done before but wasn't. Before, we could be ignored, we could return to a time when those who sit in Malacañang will forget who their Boss is. What I tried to make you feel in six years is the power that comes from you, so you should really be the Boss, your needs should be the sole purpose of whoever leads. And that is what should be addressed.

My colleagues, we have another election. You've seen the competing posters. You know who we support, but I will mention it anyway: Mar Roxas, Leni Robredo, they will continue what we started on the Straight Path. [Applause] How can they continue it if they don't get the chance?

I was taught to rely on the people—that's why we always say “people power.” For a long time, I relied on your help, on your love, and this is what we have achieved. This is just the beginning. We are starting to move forward. Let's continue the Straight Path, we must elect those who will continue it and truly recognize you as the only Boss, no one else.

Let me take this opportunity, I will repeat: When I visit Gerona, truly here, even during the time I was in the opposition, no matter what threats I faced, there are so many who truly love me, we are very safe here and your support is complete. So thank you very much. [Applause]

It would be nice to say it feels like just yesterday. But it was 18 years ago when we started, when we barely knew each other. From not knowing each other, the bond grew immensely. Look at how we suddenly rally here, you are all here, your love for me is truly endless. And I, as long as I live, will strive to ensure my love for you never runs out. [Applause]

Of course, I would also like to mention Boy Agudo, because he is running for Vice Mayor. He led our captains who accompanied me as we went around to be closer to all of you. They also told us about your problems, they helped me address the issues you faced with all of us. I've known him for a long time, you might think he looks pitiful, but I'm sure he isn't mistreating anyone. Perhaps, the only advice I can give you is, I know here in Gerona, the *tupig* is already big and delicious. When you become Vice Mayor, you need to promote the products of your town. I'm already leaving here, and you haven't even given me half a *tupig*, you seem to be lacking. [Laughter] Forget about me, Boy, just don't forget the people of Gerona. [Applause]

Comrades, I apologize, I am the President but others think I am just an errand boy. Honestly, I am also given orders by everyone. And when I return home, I need to be at the airport at 5:15, already taking off back to Manila. They say it's not allowed to travel in the dark. But we still have one more place to go, in Tarlac City.

Let me repeat: I won't be able to visit you every day. I ask our candidates to reach out to those who aren't here. We can't just vote, because the future, especially of the small ones, of the next generation, is at stake here. It is in your hands where we will go: Here, to what has been proven about the meaning of the Straight Path, or to the opponents who say “maybe,” maybe they will do what is right, maybe they will accomplish something, maybe they know something? Here is where we are certain. And the name of certainty, Mar Roxas and Leni Robredo.

Good afternoon. Thank you all very much.
