Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III upon his arrival from a visit to California for the ASEAN-US Special Leaders Summit

We have now returned from a successful visit to California, for the US-ASEAN Special Leaders Summit in Sunnylands, and our working visit to the city of Los Angeles.

We attended the Special Leaders Summit at the invitation of the Honorable President Barack Obama. The discussion was substantial, particularly on the matter of innovation and digitization; deep thoughts were offered regarding its great benefit to economies, as well as the potential risks it carries. In the field of global pandemics and climate change, we shared our experience, and we emphasized our readiness to help expand the knowledge of our neighboring countries.

What is also good is this: In the matter of maritime concerns, the support of those we interviewed was clear. If before, only a few mentioned this issue, now, the position of ASEAN and the United States is clear in the joint statement released by the assembly: Security, stability, and freedom of navigation in the West Philippine Sea and South China Sea must be maintained; and any dispute should be resolved through peaceful processes, in accordance with international law.

After the summit, we went to Los Angeles. There, we met with leaders of several companies in the fields of creative services, high technology manufacturing, and infrastructure. They recognized the talent of Filipinos in these fields, which is rising in what is called the value chain. As a symbol of the companies’ interest, the chairman of Walt Disney is planning to visit the Philippines this year, and they also expressed their willingness to help further strengthen our creative industry. Western Digital also expressed its intention to expand its operations in the country. We also interviewed AECOM, which we expect to be a partner in the capacity-building of our countrymen when it comes to infrastructure.

We also addressed the Los Angeles World Affairs Council, where my father was once invited to speak. We showcased to them the successes we have achieved under the Straight Path. We were also awarded an Honorary Degree in Humane Letters by Loyola Marymount University; this is an honor, not just for me, but for my Bosses, who are with us in suppressing corruption and promoting widespread development in our country.

And as the final activity in LA, we met with the Filipino community there. We shared with them the good news happening here in the country, and we asked for their help to ensure the continuation of what we have so beautifully started. We know: Even though they are far away, their solidarity is very important in achieving the collective aspiration of every Filipino.

This may be our last trip as President. It is a great honor to represent the Philippines in various meetings—and to emphasize to our counterparts the renewed vigor of our country, and our readiness to contribute to shaping a world that is peaceful, compassionate, and enjoys widespread development. With our heads held high as we face them, we deeply feel their respect for the positive transformation we have achieved in treading the Straight Path. Truly: It is wonderful to be Filipino in this time.

Good morning. Thank you all very much.
