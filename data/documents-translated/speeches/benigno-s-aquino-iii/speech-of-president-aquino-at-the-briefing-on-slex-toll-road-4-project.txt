Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, on the commissioning of the South Luzon Expressway project and other projects in Southern Tagalog.

Good morning once again. [Applause]

Perhaps you have already heard the various versions of the rumor that circulated from Friday night into Saturday. The story was: that I collapsed in Malacañang, that I have a serious illness, while others said that I was already in the ICU. When they saw me on Saturday night and I was in good health, a new rumor emerged: that we were the ones who spread this fabrication, just to gain public sympathy. To those who believed this, our elders remind us: we gain nothing from gossip.

Truly, for our persistent critics: our situation is a catch-22. Should we trust our opponents on the straight and narrow path, especially when their desire to cause doubt and chaos, even in the public mind, has reached an extreme and unrestrained level? They are the ones creating baseless and unfounded rumors to advance their own agenda. They are also the ones who want to regain power and benefit again from corrupt practices. If we believe the rumors they are spreading, it will truly appear that we are doing nothing, and all that we have accomplished since we took office will be rendered meaningless. But I tell you the truth now, as I always do when facing our Boss, that is an obligation, and is always proven through action. And this is the truth.

It is true that in more than four and a half years of our administration, we have come a long way. It is true that we are holding accountable those who are corrupt, public trust in the government has been restored, as well as that of investors and from abroad, in our ability, of course, to progress. It is true that our economy has improved, which has given us greater capacity to deliver immediate service to our countrymen. It is true that we have eliminated the backlog in classrooms, chairs, and textbooks. It is true that more than four million households are beneficiaries of our Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program. This February, it reached 96,101 households in the province of Quezon. It is also true that the coverage rate in PhilHealth in your province is: at 84.5 percent. This means that almost 1.8 million of our countrymen in Quezon are now covered by our expanded PhilHealth benefits. These are just some of the real accomplishments we have made under our term.

And now, another true project that will truly bring comfort to many of our countrymen, not only those from Quezon, is being started. This is the South Luzon Expressway Toll Road 4 or SLEX TR 4 Project. Why did I say this will bring comfort? As you know, before I became President, I often visited Quezon, also at the invitation of our colleagues, such as the late Governor Raffy Nantes, and Secretary Procy Alcala. You know they weren't just allies, they were friends. It so happened that the invitations to go to Quezon were often on Fridays. And to estimate the length of the trip, I was told that Quezon was almost as far as Tarlac City. Quezon is at 98 km, while Tarlac reaches 107 km from the Rizal Monument. They also told me that if you travel to Tarlac in an hour and a half, Quezon can be traveled in about two and a half hours. But that's not what happens, because every time I go to Quezon, we really take five to six hours to travel.

Imagine: Every time I am invited to come here, the hosts are often already finished with dinner. [Laughter] Once, when I attended, it was Raffy Nantes' birthday party, he said, “Friend, have some soup first.” After finishing the soup, “Come on. Let's go around. My constituents have been waiting for you for a while now.” When Secretary Procy Alcala invited me. And of course I said, “‘Those from Quezon are hospitable.’ Upon my arrival, I immediately gave a speech, went around, took pictures and she said, “Friend, thank you.” And as usual, we ate at Jollibee in Batangas. [Laughter] We reached 11 pm on the road, and we asked them not to close Jollibee yet. The worst part about this is that because you asked them not to close, we ended up with a cold sandwich. That's why I asked the two of them, “Does that Queen Margaret hotel have a restaurant?” And when we became president, I tried for the first time to eat while being invited here in Quezon.

That's why when the news reached us that the extension of SLEX will finally begin, we are really grateful. You know, this was promised during the time of Mr. Marcos. I had just finished grade school. And if you look at the law made during the time of Marcos, the government built the NLEX. The government spent, he had it built. The management of this was passed on to what is called CDCP. In return, CDCP would extend the road to Carmen Rosales in Pangasinan and to Lucena in Quezon. As we all know, it is still not in Quezon.

Let me emphasize: This project was promised by Marcos, and approved during the administration of Fidel Ramos, and now, during our term, will finally commence. [Applause]

This gathering serves as proof of the private sector’s confidence in pushing for initiatives towards widespread development. Led by the South Luzon Tollway Corporation, and under the supervision of the Department of Transportation and Communications – Toll Regulatory Board, we will push forward with the SLEX TR 4 project which will greatly benefit our countrymen. Please include me in this as well. When this project is completed in 2019, even if we are no longer President, we are surely going to return and return to Quezon. And perhaps by then, we will have food waiting for us from Sec. Alcala. [Applause and laughter]

The aim of this project is to expedite transportation and spur development not only in Metro Manila but also in its neighboring towns. This project is worth P13.08 billion. More than 57.5 kilometers with four lanes will be constructed, traversing from Sto. Tomas, Batangas, to Lucena, Quezon. Of course, with a road like that, it will also be easier to complete the road going to Bicol. [Applause] Once completed, the trip from Sto. Tomas to Lucena, which currently takes four hours according to reports, will only take one hour. Even now, we are confident in the success of this initiative, especially since San Miguel Corporation and Citra Metro Manila Tollways Corporation will be spearheading it. We truly have nothing to say about Mr. Ramon Ang, who is truly contributing to the progress of our country. [Applause]

This is just SLEX TR 4 of the High Standard Highway Network we are pushing for, or the establishment of a well-maintained and continuous road transport network. But even before this is done, we have already started and completed many projects here in Southern Tagalog. Here in your province of Quezon, the rehabilitation of the Manila South Road is ongoing. This road is divided into the Tiaong-Lucena Diversion Road – Pagbilao-Sta. Elena, and the Camarines Norte Section and Pagsanjan-Tayabas – Lucena Section.

We have also completed the development of ports in San Andres, Mauban, Unisan, Gumaca, and Perez. Currently, more than 3,500 farmers are benefiting from the farm-to-market roads we are building in San Andres and Pagbilao. In addition to this, we have also improved irrigation systems in various municipalities of Quezon to increase yields and production, which generates additional income for our farmers. During our administration, the coconut industry here in Quezon has truly flourished. We introduced intercropping. In this system, your government supports the strengthening of the coconut industry. In return, of course, is the obligation to plant various seeds between the rows of coconut trees. Harvests are more frequent and, depending on what is planted, the income of each farmer who relies on coconut farming will increase significantly. Now, various coconut products are also being talked about and profited from. We found virgin coconut oil, but we also have coco water, coco nets, coco coir, and coco peat. In addition to this, we can now use the Coco Levy fund to develop our coconut farms. [Applause] Thank you to the Supreme Court for their partial judgment which has been entered.

In all of this, we uphold the right of the people of Quezon to improve your livelihoods. And there is no one with sound mind who can say that we have neglected Quezon.

Clearly: With good governance, the progress of our infrastructure is continuous. In fact, the budget for this sector in 2010, when we were not yet in charge: P165 billion. This has increased by more than three times this year, where the budget we allocated for it: P562.3 billion. In 2016, we are targeting to increase this to P787.19. This is increasing without us raising taxes.

With us today is one of the reformers who led the way to make this transformation possible, none other than Secretary Babes Singson. [Applause] Under his leadership, the image of the DPWH has truly changed: from an agency that was previously milked by the corrupt and abusive, it is now known for faithfully implementing projects that are of high quality and, if not earlier, are always on time. We are ending the old system where every time a road is built, the plan is haphazard and kickbacks come first. Now, every project is comprehensively studied, has sufficient funding, and is beneficial to the people.

Indeed, we have come a long way in our collective journey along the straight and narrow path. The transformation we are experiencing in infrastructure is also happening in various sectors of our society. But we all know that there are still those who want to return the country to the broken system we found. They take advantage of every opportunity to make us go our separate ways again. I assure our Bosses: no matter how loud the noise made by those sowing negativity, I will remain true to the mandate you have given me. In the remaining 465 days of my term, we will continue to lay out more opportunities in the country. We aspire to make the reforms permanent. And you, our Bosses, you who give life to our democracy, will ensure that whoever replaces me will further strengthen our institutions so that they are no longer used as instruments by the corrupt again.

The challenge to us is: at all times, let us maintain compassion, composure, and prioritize the welfare of others and the country, before ourselves. With our strong unity, we can be sure that our efforts will lead to a good outcome. Every success we achieve is only a foundation for further progress. As long as we are united on the side of what is right and just, what we have started well today will continue until we reach a more prosperous society.

In closing, next year we will have another election. And I have been thinking, especially with the rumors they spread, how intense our opponents really are. Why did they go that far? And perhaps, I should ask myself. And they know that I will not run next year. Why do they constantly target me to attack, attack, and attack? The answer is simple. If I have done good things and are perhaps recognized by our critics, naturally, when we endorse someone, it carries weight. So their job now is to invalidate everything we have worked for together. In that way, when they promise to do better, it seems easier. But here in Quezon, in Batangas, in Laguna, in Bicol, and many other places, Filipinos are not fooled by people who do not speak truthfully.

Good morning. Thank you all very much.
