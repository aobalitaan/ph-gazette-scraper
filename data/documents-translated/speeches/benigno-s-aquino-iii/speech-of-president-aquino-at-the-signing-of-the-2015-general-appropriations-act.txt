Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, at the signing of the 2015 General Appropriations Act

[Delivered at the Rizal Hall, Malacañan Palace, on December 23, 2014]

Today is a historic day: For the fifth time, we have passed the budget for the next year on time. This means that in every year of our service, we have fulfilled our duty, and the message is clear: things have really changed in our government, in terms of what “normal” means. Previously, budget reenactment was a tradition that created space for various forms of corruption. Now, we consistently show the Filipino people: our budget is truly the People's Budget.

What does it mean when we say the People's Budget? It’s simple: the national treasury is focused on the welfare of the citizens, especially those in the margins of society. And this is not just about pouring funds into our Bosses who need it most, we are also giving them a voice in budgeting the taxes they pay. 1,590 cities and municipalities are already part of our Bottom-up Budgeting Program. Now, the communities themselves will say what they need, they themselves will design the initiatives for it, and our government will also fund it. The funds allocated in accordance with our Bottom-up Budgeting program: P20.9 billion.

Starting last year, our budget is no longer just a collection of numbers: targets are clearly stated. For example: The Department of Public Works and Highways aims to complete the concreting and repair of all national roads by 2016, including 4,219 kilometers of roads to be constructed and repaired in 2015; the Department of Social Welfare and Development, aims to support 4.3 million families through the Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program; the Department of Tourism, aims to increase the number of our international tourist arrivals by 20 percent. In this system we call Performance Informed Budgeting, we have a basis for whether agencies are fulfilling their promises; we see where the national treasury is going. Above all, we have a clear basis for holding accountable officials who are slow to deliver services, and we see who is performing well.

When it comes to delivering services, “later” is no longer acceptable. Our mandate: deliver the benefits to our Bosses, now. We have sped up the budgeting process. We have reduced the need for a SARO; upon our signature, the agency budgets are as good as released. Likewise, we are cleaning and clarifying the process to further reduce the space for corruption. Now, agency heads need to submit requirements for reporting their budget; for those who cannot fulfill this, we have the right to sanction. In addition, we have the Transparency Seal and Open Data Philippines initiatives to increase the ability of citizens to participate in our promotion of more open and honest governance. We continue to stand firm: there is no PDAF or Priority Development Assistance Fund in our budget, which has become an instrument of plunder by some opportunists.

We have also signed the Supplemental Budget for 2014. When we responded to the people's call to remove the PDAF from our budget, we eliminated the scheme of those abusing the system. But along with that, some projects were delayed, some services were interrupted, and some debts were unpaid. Of course, we will not allow our countrymen who we have already given relief to return to suffering.

Included in the approved Supplemental Budget is more than P10 billion for Yolanda reconstruction. This includes 7.99 billion pesos allocated for the construction of permanent housing for our countrymen affected by the disaster. What I want to emphasize: This housing is being built under our principle of Build Back Better. We moved our countrymen away from dangerous areas, designed the infrastructure to be more resilient to storms, and looked for alternative livelihoods for the families who were relocated. In other words, we not only helped them recover, we also gave them opportunities in life that are far safer and more comfortable than before Yolanda arrived.

That is why we thank Secretary Butch Abad, the Department of Budget and Management, and everyone who worked together to reach this day. I am sure many of you stayed up late and made sacrifices to focus on every detail of this process. Now, at least, you can breathe a little easier. However, we also know that the work is not yet finished. That is why I continue to urge you: May you continue to contribute to our reform agenda, not only in national spending, but also in the transformation of government and of society as a whole.

We also express our deep gratitude to the entire Congress—under the leadership of Senate President Frank Drilon and House Speaker Sonny Belmonte, with the support of Senate Finance Committee Chairman Chiz Escudero and House Appropriations Committee Chair Sid Ungab—for the zeal and dedication you have shown to achieve once again on time and successfully pass the People's Budget for the year 2015, as well as the 2014 Supplemental Budget.

Our initiatives on the straight and narrow path would not advance were it not for the unity and cooperation of our Bosses—the people. I sincerely thank every Filipino citizen who, in the face of numerous trials, remains steadfast and does not hesitate to contribute to our pursuit of meaningful change in the country.

While we work together and rejoice in the victories we have already achieved, I am certain that those who once indulged in the national treasury are now saddened. Because of the righteous spending we are implementing, they are finding it difficult to line their pockets with money that does not rightfully belong to them. Well, we will add fear to the sadness they are feeling. We will not stop in our efforts to catch the corrupt, and we will make them accountable to the people.

Today, we have signed the National Budget for the year 2015. The contents of this thick book—every peso, every number, and every legal provision stated herein—symbolizes our collective desire to uplift our fellow citizens together, and to develop our country. We are only fulfilling what we promised to our Bosses: No one will be left behind on the straight and narrow path.

Let us remember: The power to deliver meaningful change in society is not held by one person, or one group—it is held by every Filipino. Now that we have come so far from where we were; now that on the straight path we are able to hold our own destiny, may we help each other even more and share strength so that the change now being experienced by the Philippines may finally become the norm.

Once again, thank you very much to all of you.
