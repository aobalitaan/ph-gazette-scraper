Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, at the inauguration of the Pili Diversion Road expansion project.

It really feels different when in Camarines Sur, with my beloved Bicolanos. In your company, where trust and support are always so strong, I truly feel I am not alone. [Applause] This is what I practiced with the Kapampangan and Ilokano earlier: Now that the day of hearts is approaching, [laughter] we hastened our visit to you here in the municipality of Pili, [laughter] because you are close to my heart. [Laughter and applause] Somehow, we understand each other. [Laughter]

There are many reasons to keep returning to your province. This is what we practiced again: There is a lot of delicious food, beautiful scenery — but the most important thing — especially the beautiful Bicolanas. [Applause] Do you agree with that? [Laughter] You know, earlier, I was listening to our good mayor, the weather is so nice today. I said, work, but I feel like I’m on vacation. It’s so cool, it’s so cold. It’s like we went to Tagaytay. This is what vacation is like when you’re President, so thank you, you brought me here. Earlier, while listening to Mayor, it really fills the heart. The Mayor’s problem isn’t that he has many problems without solutions. The Mayor’s problem is how to say all the accomplishments that were worked on in the short time given to him. Of course, at this point, some might be looking for something to be proud of. Here’s the problem, how to be proud of everything that should be proud of because time is running out? You’re really okay, Mayor. [Applause]

Another good thing here in your province is that we see the fulfillment of our projects and programs under the Straight Path. Earlier, we inspected your well-maintained San Jose Bridge. We completed its construction in July 2015. This year, we plan to expand the continuation of this road to bring even more comfort to our Bosses.

Now, we also inaugurated your expanded Pili Diversion Road, which was completed this January. According to the good Secretary Babes Singson, before this diversion road was opened, all vehicles, including cargo trucks and buses, were crammed onto your Pili-Poblacion Road. As a result, traversing the three-kilometer road would take up to twenty minutes, especially if it wasn't a fiesta. During a fiesta, it would take more than twenty minutes. Now that the construction of the diversion road is complete, the trip will only take five minutes. This is because, in addition to the road being widened, those coming from Albay, Sorsogon, and areas in Partido that don't need to go to Poblacion now have an alternative route that will benefit and provide comfort to everyone. Because of the project, the number of vehicles traversing the Pili-Poblacion Road has been reduced by almost 6,000.

You can see the comfort brought about by good governance. These new bridges and roads in Pili are just part of our extensive infrastructure development strategy. As I mentioned earlier, here in Camarines Sur, from 2011 to 2016, the funds allocated by the DPWH for infrastructure were: P15.96 billion. This is more than triple the funds allocated from 2005 to 2010, which amounted to only P4.73 billion. Before we leave that, for the entire province: P15 billion. You know what our predecessor allocated—some may have forgotten—a project in Laguna Lake, which was supposed to be deepened to increase its water holding capacity and also to improve the flow of traffic in Laguna Lake. When the project was explained to me when we took office, one of the first things explained was that they would remove the silt and mud from Laguna Lake. They would dredge it, thus expanding it. The problem was, it would be dumped on the other side of Laguna Lake. The end result was, we were just playing with mud. Our mud, we would take it and move it to the other part of the same lake. So it was still there. When I was in grade school, I was told that matter occupies volume, occupies space. In my view, the water holding capacity of the lake would not expand. That kind of project was approved by our predecessor. The cost of it was P18.7 billion. For all that the DPWH has done for you, P15 billion, there was just one project that I really couldn't see the common sense in, costing P18 billion. That's what bad governance is in my view.

Some of the projects we have completed and are completing here are the Tigaon-Goa-San Jose-Lagonoy-Guijalo Road, the Pili-Tigaon-Albay Boundary Road, and the Daang Maharlika.

Throughout the country, our predecessor allocated P165 billion for infrastructure in 2010. Now, in 2016, we have increased that to P766.5 billion. This is equivalent to almost 5 percent of our estimated Gross Domestic Product this year. With economic growth, there is also an increase in the budget for the development of our projects and programs that deliver benefits and reduce the sacrifices of our Bosses.

Therefore, regarding our target of concreting approximately 7,200 kilometers of national roads, we have already completed 6,212 kilometers of national roads. The remaining portion has already been allocated funds and is targeted for completion this year.

Aside from national roads, we have also completed 1,550 kilometers of tourism roads and 3,700 local roads, which were submitted to us by the LGUs. The agency has also replaced 12,585 lineal meters of temporary bridges across the country with permanent and more durable bridges. Here in Pili, because of the improved roads, the very popular CamSur Watersports Complex and other beautiful destinations are now more quickly accessible. So, considering all of this, the DPWH has exceeded expectations. We are truly impressed and grateful for the quality and speed of the DPWH’s work.

Another project completed here in Camarines Sur, and now being enjoyed by our countrymen: the Sabang Fishport Development Project in Calabanga. We were told that our fishermen have long been waiting for a facility like this, and it is considered the fulfillment of their dream. Once it becomes fully operational, it is estimated that around 80,000 people will immediately benefit from it.

It is clear: Good governance is good economics. With honest and good governance, the economy develops. This is also what gives the government greater capacity to empower our citizens to contribute to the further progress of the country.

Now, all our reforms can be made permanent, we can further rise and progress. The opposite could also happen. What we worked hard for in six years could be wasted; as if we just took a vacation from the crooked practices we were used to. You know the suffering this caused: decades-long infrastructure projects that were not completed, and funds being pocketed, so there are insufficient services.

On May 9, we will face a crossroads. Many suitors will come forward and present themselves for you to vote for. It is important that we choose the right candidate; not just someone who talks a lot, but someone who has already accomplished a lot. For me, there is no other, and that is the team of Mar Roxas and the true daughter of Camarines Sur, Leni Robredo. [Applause] They don't just make promises. They have already sacrificed and are working for the benefit of the majority. In their long years of public service, their names have remained untarnished; because for them, country comes before self. With Mar and Leni, we won't rely on “maybe” or “luck,” we are sure: they will continue and enhance what we have started on the Straight Path.

For the five years and eight months that I have served you, I have kept my word. I am addressing the shortcomings and problems now so that they do not worsen and be inherited by those who come after us. Our desire is: that they have a stronger foundation of development to stand on and have greater capacity to develop the entire country.

You know, before I finish, earlier as I was coming here, I saw a somewhat small billboard. It said: truck ban ordinance. I said, “Okay, good. You have a truck ban, which means there is traffic, which means commerce is running.” Is that right? So it’s just right that we widen the roads so you won’t have a serious traffic problem anymore.

This isn't my first trip to Camarines. I've been here quite often, but it's only now that I've seen that sign. Simple, explaining the law, but it really shows that we're making progress here.

Let me repeat: The one I replaced approved a project to play in the mud. You, well, you can tell us what we've accomplished in the shorter time you've given us. And because we were able to do that, we can repeat it, we can speed up the process that happened.

Don't forget, when I first took office, in July, what was left in our budget was more or less only seven percent, to run the country for half a year. We had many problems that I already mentioned to you; we were considered the “Sick man of Asia.”

You may have heard Mel Sarmiento, he was a former mayor invited to a conference. When he went to the conference, no one wanted to approach him because “From the Philippines. We’ll solicit something.” When they looked at his passport, they really scrutinized it. He was invited to the conference, and they asked him, “What is the purpose of your visit?” He replied, “I was invited by your country.” As if he had to prove it. Now, he says it's different now. Now, he's running out of business cards because of the number of people asking how to connect with him. [Applause]

During his time as mayor, it was like my time as Congressman – the constant refrain was, “There are no funds.” Now, it’s different. The issue now is often “absorptive capacity,” meaning there are funds that can’t be spent on time, it’s like being left hanging. Back then, when he was mayor, he said there were no funds. Someone would approach him, “Mayor, we have a problem.” “What’s the problem?” “Someone died, and we can’t afford a coffin.” “I don’t have funds to give you a coffin either.” Then the carpenter who was an employee at city hall approached him, “Mayor, we have extra plywood back there. I’ll take care of it.” He made a coffin. Then the person went back to Mel, thinking he would express gratitude. The person said, “Mayor, the coffin you gave is ugly.” [Laughter] “What’s wrong with it?” “It doesn’t have glass.” [Laughter] “Oh, is that so?” The carpenter who was there heard it and said, “Mayor, don’t worry about that. I’ll take care of it.” So he attached jalousie to the coffin, so when you look at the deceased, you can adjust it like this to see them fully. [Laughter] That was when he was mayor.

Now, the situation is different. Just yesterday, we said, “We still need twenty patrol vehicles because each municipality should have [one].” In Pili, there were two. There was probably an excess delivery here. [Laughter] Only twenty are missing out of more than 1,490, and there’s a second delivery coming.

If it truly reflects your experience, if you’ve really worked hard, you shouldn’t have to boast about what you’ve accomplished; you should be the witness. You yourselves should say, “Where should we go from here? Should we go back to playing in the mud, or should we continue to expand what we can do?” And all the hardships the Filipino people endured for decades – while we’re alive, we have problems, but those problems should decrease little by little. That’s really the sign that things are going well. For things to be right, it’s in your hands. So: To you, my Bicolano bosses, let’s continue our unity for the realization of our dreams or aspirations. [Laughter and applause]

Thank you very much.
