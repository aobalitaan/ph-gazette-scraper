Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, on the proclamation of the Liberal Party candidates for the 2013 Elections

[Delivered in the City of Imus, Cavite, on September 24, 2012]

Good afternoon. Please, let us all be seated.

Honorable Secretary Mar Roxas; Chairman Francis Tolentino of the MMDA; our representative who is about to become a secretary, Jun Abaya; of course, our returning governor, Ayong Maliksi; Congressman Pidi Barzaga; Congressman Roy Loyola; Congressman Erin Tañada; Congresswoman Abigail Ferriol; Mayor Manny Maliksi; incoming congressman Bambol Tolentino; our colleague Del Abaya; Commissioner Ruffy Biazon; and future senator Riza Hontiveros-Baraquel, with your help; our future vice governor, Jay Lacson; our next representative, Boy Blue Abaya; of course, and also Osboy Campaña, we also greet him; Mr. Jessie Castillo; Atty. Raffy Rodriguez; Bishop Arturo Ferriol; mayors of the cities and municipalities of Cavite; NGOs and civic organizations present; fellow workers in government; honored guests; my beloved countrymen:

Good afternoon to all of you.

Last Saturday I was in Sangley; today is Monday, and now I am here in Cavite. Perhaps by Wednesday, I will apply for citizenship here. Because upon our landing earlier, the people were really outside the road; they were really waiting for us and the welcome was so warm. So, if there are those who doubt the survey that came out where it is said that our approval rating is now higher than when we started, I think I will point them to Cavite so they can see why.

Thank you very much to all of you.

You know, even during my father's time, my mother's time, and throughout all my endeavors, I truly felt your support. And sometimes, I think to myself: At the MMDA, Francis is there. If there's a flood problem, Francis answers. If there's a traffic problem, Francis answers. Francis answers a lot of things. He's from Cavite, right? Let's look at Jun Abaya, Secretary General of our party. That means, “you are a great assistant to everyone.” Whenever there's a problem, you're the one who's called upon. Jun Abaya's name is on our list as a “very shortlist.” That means, when there's an important vacant position, Jun Abaya is likely one of those we always consider. Brother Jun, let me remind you: My father was Secretary General of our party, almost became President. I, before your batch, became Secretary General of the party, became President. Maybe you'll be the next President.

You know, truthfully, that's why I sometimes think: In the media, we are sometimes praised; often criticized—that's okay. But my spokesperson to the media, Asec. Rey Marfil is here on the right—he's also from Cavite. Perhaps the time will come when you, the people of Cavite, will say, “We haven't seen anything if not for us.” Because, even Senator Ping Lacson, after his term, there are those who insist on distorting the straight path. Perhaps it's time for Senator Lacson to speak in a new role, and we discussed this and we expect that after his term, straightening the path of these wayward ones will be expedited.

This is the part where I will flatter you to thank you.

As I was coming here, I thought: It must be wonderful to be a Caviteño in these times. We are constantly receiving good news from Cavite. First, this gathering of ours: with the caliber of the candidates of the Liberal Party and allied groups here in Cavite, led by Manong Ayong, the future of your province is becoming brighter. Perhaps, no one will question his age now; surely we all see here the vitality, strength, and energy of Manong Ayong, right?

If they challenge us to a marathon, since Congressman Abaya is on the very short list to become a secretary, we will volunteer him. We know in the party: he is tested, Manong Ayong, in service, and it is also no secret that the people of Cavite have full trust and confidence in him. When it comes to public service, when you say Ayong Maliksi, no forced persuasion is needed. From then until now, he has only focused on the development of Cavite. When he became governor here, due to his zeal, and the support of the Caviteños, he not only lowered your poverty incidence—Cavite also became one of the fastest growing provinces in the country, after his term.

Manong Ayong knows the meaning of hard work. And it's not just because of his frequent exercise and walks before sunrise. Because, we know, he grew up with a father who was a farmer and a mother who was a vegetable vendor; he himself worked hard to finish his studies. This perhaps drove him, and inspired him to focus on education in Cavite, along with the health and livelihood of his constituents. His decades of service in Cavite, and his love for your province, are truly invaluable.

Therefore, we are confident: Manong Ayong will be able to do much more for Cavite in the coming years; many more Caviteños will be given wider opportunities for development.

At this afternoon's gathering, we are also joined by our older brother, Congressman Jun Abaya. You may already be familiar with him.

I once reviewed Jun's credentials, and then I thought: it was actually easy what he went through. First of all, he graduated from Philippine Science High School; topped the entrance exam of PMA; and then, he was sent to study at the U.S. Naval Academy in Annapolis; until he received a master's degree in engineering at Cornell University. He wasn't even satisfied, so when he returned to the Philippines, he studied at Ateneo Law School, until he passed the bar and became a full-fledged lawyer. It was so easy what he went through. I almost want to confront him and ask: Kuya Jun, what do you really want to do with your life?

But joking aside, Jun's talent and dedication cannot be confined to various professions; it only shone brighter when he had the opportunity to serve the country. When we were in the House, he was among those who supported the impeachment complaint against Mrs. Arroyo. He immediately faced a crossroads in his first term: will you stand up against corruption, or will you just accept the status quo? You already know his answer to that. Even when there was an opportunity to decline, to just shrug it off and remember his district—especially since we seemed to have no chance of winning that impeachment complaint—he still clung to his principles. Jun clearly knows what is right; he clearly understands justice and good governance. He still chose to enter the fight, and risk his name and political career. I really, really admire him, truly, from the Abaya clan starting with Manong Dell and his brother Blue Boy.

These experiences, like these, reinforced my decision: we need people like Manong Ayong and Jun in government. Without hesitation, I chose this Kuya Jun as the new secretary of the DOTC—and we will have more people from Cavite, just borrowing Jun for a while—along with the selection of Mar Roxas as the new secretary of the DILG. So, I will take this opportunity to say to every Caviteño, I repeat, thank you very much for lending Jun Abaya to us; I will return him in 2016. Like you, I have full confidence in how much he can help in his new role in government.

It's really different when you're from Cavite; it's really different when you're a product of Cavite. It's wonderful to see the continued growth of your major industries, such as agriculture, fishing, and tourism. That's why we don't stop implementing programs and projects to support these industries, so that your progress continues.

Our answer: infrastructure and road projects. According to the DPWH, the Manila-Cavite Road and Bridges, which expands the roads and bridges on the Kawit-Noveleta Diversion Road section, we completed this at the end of July. Is that correct? You also have ongoing construction now: the Bangkal-GMA Bridge in Dasmariñas, which we are targeting to complete by February 2013, hopefully before my birthday; the expansion of Governor’s Drive in the City of Trece Martires which we aim to complete in March 2013; and the Daang Hari – SLEX Link Road which will connect Bacoor and SLEX, which we estimate will be completed by December 2013.

You might ask me, “Why are you planning so many roads?” Because, honestly, when we are invited to Cavite, I prepare myself, in the past, we were surely stuck in traffic going there—going there and back. I'm just visiting, you live here, a government established to serve you; a former problem should be alleviated, reduced, that's why there are many projects like these. Maybe we can get married someday [ cheers from the crowd ], maybe honeymoon in Tagaytay, hopefully there's no traffic. After this, I will look for a fortune teller to find out when that will be.

One more good news—but this is more certain: the NEDA Board approved the LRT Line 1 Cavite Extension this March. The funds we allocated for this: 60 billion pesos. We expect the bidding to be completed by April 2013, and the construction to begin in January 2014; but because the one in charge of this is the Jun Abaya we mentioned, when the time comes, a Caviteño who is also stuck in traffic, maybe it will be earlier, I won't complain if it's earlier. But I will be surprised if it takes longer. With this project, Baclaran and Bacoor will be connected, and we estimate that it will be able to carry an additional 250,000 passengers per day.

All of these initiatives will not only help improve the flow of commerce in key industries, but will also provide great relief to many motorists due to reduced traffic. Let's work together, and it is not far-fetched that we can all finish these as soon as possible.

Today, the candidates of the Liberal Party in Cavite have been proclaimed for the upcoming elections. It is important that we do not forget: we are all together and working together for the straight path. It is important that we take the same path—no rushing ahead, no pushing, no pulling—instead, we help each other.

In the coming May, Filipinos will once again face a crossroads. The question is: will you choose to deviate from the path back to a corrupt system and crooked dealings? As a Liberal, there is only one principle that will guide your term under the flag: Country, before self. From the past until now, this is our firm commitment to the Filipino people.

We have already started something wonderful, and Filipinos have been given another opportunity to hold their own destiny and future in their hands. I once again call on the people: let us side with what is right, let us choose the path of honest and good governance.

Long live our Liberal Party and its allied groups.

Before I finish, you know, I will admit to you: I am the Chairman of the party, and I feel sorry for our party president, there are some places that really seem to have been offended. You can't choose who to side with. There is discomfort and their opponent is even more uncomfortable. You will be involved in their foolishness, to be honest. But let's turn it around: I am very eager to go to Cavite because here, you really have so many good and decent compatriots. They are here on stage. But let me emphasize: Manong Ayong, during the Snap Election—that's when I first met him, but my parents knew him before that. You know, he is a few years older than me. There, Manong Ayong told my mother, he said, “There are three of us here helping you.” They were probably all mayors at that time. And they said—it was Martial Law, if you think about it. They said, “Mrs. Aquino, we will help. We will pour out everything we can do but our fight looks tilted.” You know what happened during the Snap Election. Jun Abaya—I'll just repeat—was one of the first—we were just starting to work, he was already thrown into the deep end. It's like swimming, he wasn't thrown into the shallows; he was thrown into the deep. He joined you in the impeachment; we will fight the highest official of the country who might punish us all if we lose. Manong Ayong, Jun, did they hesitate? When they promised to serve you, they did not turn their backs on their promise to make their lives convenient. Their view is clear: I am with the people who are my compatriots who hope that I will do what is right. I will not cling to the crooked and wrong just to make my life easier. That is not what was promised and, really, like all our days, we face many problems but when Manong Ayong invited me, there were no two ways about it—we went to Cavite easily even with my mother beside me now, I brought my father with me, my mother and father said, “If you make a promise, make sure it happens”; “If you guarantee someone, make sure you stand by it.” With Manong Ayong's character, there is no doubt that we will not be embarrassed in front of you.

I will just repeat to you: We have many projects, and I am not saying that they will not happen, but just think, we have the same view. When the time comes that we are already building that LRT Extension, and we have a compatriot who is a little too self-serving and the last ten meters said, “The right of way is expensive,” to Manong Ayong, I think, the two of us will convince him, “It's for the benefit of many, don't be selfish.” Isn't that right?

When he has a suggestion, it is very easy for us, we have no doubt, we will go to what is right. So, as you know, when we first met, I had thicker hair. Maybe these leftovers, like the trees we are planting, will help me stay. Give us someone who will help and not burden us.

Good afternoon. Thank you very much to everyone.
