Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, on the 29th anniversary of the establishment of the Presidential Security Group.

Good afternoon to everyone and happy anniversary to all of you.

It has been twenty-nine years now, I think if you look at Secretary Gazmin, we wouldn't know that he is our founding group commander. [Applause] Let's not challenge him to a road run, you might be left behind. It seems like only Ate Pinky is showing a little addition to her age, about one year. [Laughter]

You all know that the responsibility on the shoulders of any President is no joke. As I often say: it’s like being watched by 200 television channels, all at the same time. I need to observe and monitor everything that happens in every corner of our country. I need to respond to our 100 million Bosses, including the more than ten million who are abroad. Any tension, unrest, or tragedy abroad and within our territory, I need to focus on. I need to know what is happening and what will happen, because at every moment the welfare of our Bosses is at stake.

Problems, big or small, are brought to my attention. Of course, this is accompanied by the harshest criticisms, especially fabricated stories from those seeking attention.

You know, when I accepted the mandate to lead the country, our agenda was clear: to correct corruption, hold those who harmed the people accountable, and replace the existing status quo of dominance with one that benefits the people. There’s a saying: Do not disturb the rice bowl, or don’t interfere with others’ livelihood. The painful part is: their livelihood comes from the national treasury, and everyone suffers while only a few benefit. I will not turn a blind eye, and we will strive to correct the system we inherited. Therefore, I will disrupt many rice bowls.

I admit it: I have courage, because I do what is right, and if I lack anything, let God take care of it, especially our security. If I go to Darapanan in Maguindanao, or any place, my spirit is steadfast knowing I am accompanied by a professional and reliable Presidential Security Group. In all my activities, you are there, ready to sacrifice, ready to empathize, even if the work demands fatigue, hunger, or sleeplessness; and you are truly contributing to the advancement of widespread social transformation.

Regarding hunger, I remembered something that happened recently. We faced the families of the fallen SAF members. The first gathering was held in the early hours of that Friday. And the only thing we were able to feed our colleagues was a small hamburger for lunch, and it was already early morning. To recover from the fatigue, I invited them to eat. And when our colleagues arrived that evening, our Chief of Protocol asked, “What do you want to eat?” And their answer was, “Just something light, ma’am.” It was already early morning. So they ordered tortang talong (eggplant omelet), tortang giniling (ground meat omelet), and other kinds of torta, fried vegetable lumpia, laing, and bikol express. The place we went to was a cattle and chicken restaurant. When they realized they were very hungry, they also ordered beef and chicken. The problem was, it was already out of stock when they decided to eat.

I am only telling you this, we were in Bicutan almost all night that day, our stomachs were empty. Then, the relief came the next day, but the orders weren't yet properly placed. But we didn't hear even a little resentment. Instead, we saw smiles, and they wondered why they only ordered torta. [Laughter]

The Presidential Security Group has come a long way in almost three decades. When the PSG started, during my mother's time, when they arrived here, nothing was left to us: even the jalousies, doorknobs, and even the trees standing, were uprooted because they said they were the ones who planted them so they would take them when they left—they took everything they could carry. The weapons were also emptied by those who came before us here in Malacañan Park. My mother's security during the height of the campaign carried a clutch bag. Inside were bottles wrapped in towels to look like a bulge and something inside. For six months, even after my mother became president, some of her close-in security still carried clutch bags with the same bottles inside because there were no weapons here when they arrived. The number of your ranks was also reduced when the PSG was established under then Col. Gazmin. The troops were new. You weren't feared or respected by those who attempted coups. In at least nine coups during my mother's time, the PSG was tested, and you showed your worth. Your strength was seen and the enemy was defeated. In the end, the PSG remained strong, the enemy collapsed. [Applause]

You can see the skill of Secretary Gazmin when he was your “Group”: The PSG, he really made it “lean and mean.” You served as the president's, our country's, and even our family's support and shield. When I was ambushed in front of St. Jude, my PSG companions sacrificed their lives to save us.

Now that I am leading the country, my admiration and confidence in the Presidential Security Group has deepened, from those who make up your leadership, to your non-commissioned officers, to your ordinary members and personnel.

When I went to Zamboanga City in 2013, while in command there, and when I went to the areas of conflict, even before your commander at that time, because of the orderly administration of our former group commander, Chito Dizon, my security and that of my companions was truly ensured. Under the leadership of Commander Raul Ubando, the PSG and other units under his operational control truly excelled in Zamboanga. I might add: There came a time when we visited the Sports Stadium to show solidarity with all the evacuees. He told me, the job of our Group Commander is, “Sir, there are already many people there before we were able to screen them. We can no longer screen them. The people there are a mix. We are not sure who is there. And that sports center is within mortar range.” I said, “I need to go to these people. Do what you can. I have complete confidence in you. Let God take care of what we cannot do.” And it truly was peaceful and our group commander showed, who was only about two weeks in his post with the PSG at that time, was ready at any time and for whatever was needed.

Because of you, I was really able to fulfill my duty without thinking about my safety.

Even with the arrival of the Pope this January, despite his desire to get close to so many people, he remained safe and his visit was successful. Perhaps you are saying now: It is much easier to guard me than the Pope. And the people saw the skill of the PSG when Pope Francis visited. Now, can anyone say that the PSG cannot handle any task?

Of course, we are not complacent with all our successes. We will continue to raise the level of your competence. Because of the great role you play, I assure you that we are supporting you in all your endeavors. We will also ensure that your welfare and even that of your loved ones is in good condition.

To us, we have what is called a Central Leadership or a very strong Central Leadership, and I am at the top. I will answer for the direction, operation, success, and even the failure of any goal. You greatly help alleviate my burden, because you perform your jobs with competence, loyalty, and professionalism. I will add this as well: When Gov. Ebdane was a PG Commander, there was a time he would really catch people sleeping on the job. Now, I can say that in the more than four years we’ve been here, not once have I caught anyone sleeping at their post or on the job. It’s true: Because of the PSG, I have “peace of mind.” [Applause]

I would like to emphasize: As defenders of the nation, as members of our united AFP and PNP, the members of the PSG are truly professionals. In my daily interactions with you, I see the strong relationship between the AFP and PNP that forms my security detail and that of other units in your group. Now that there are those who aim to break the strong relationship between our AFP and PNP, my response to those sowing intrigue and negativity is only one: Look at the PSG. Look at the dedication of the soldiers and police who contribute to achieving what we aspire to as a nation. Look at my colleagues who care for the country.

We are now in the final stage of our administration, and I am confident that with the PSG with me, we can still do more towards the realization of our common goal: A Philippines that is a model of progress, a peaceful country, and a source of opportunity for our citizens.

When I first faced your entire ranks during your 25th anniversary, I said, “While you stand tall, the country is stable and progressive.” I tell you now: You are proving that the stance of our uniformed personnel is strong, united, and unmatched. You stand as the solid shield of the President and the people. And every day, you and our millions of Bosses prove that I am never alone in walking the straight and narrow path.

On your anniversary, please accept the heartfelt gratitude of the nation for all your sacrifices and efforts. It is true: I am truly proud to be your leader and commander-in-chief.

Thank you very much. Happy anniversary to all.
