Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III President of the Philippines During the distribution of assault rifles to personnel of the Armed Forces of the Philippines and the Philippine Navy

Once upon a time, some marines told me that when they were assigned to the Kalayaan Island Group, there was an instance where a country occupied a neighboring island. And the soldiers of this country allegedly placed cannons on the island they occupied. Of course, our marines did not back down. They had no cannons, so they allegedly cut down a coconut tree, painted it black, and pointed it at potential enemies—to look like cannons from a distance.

As I said, if it was discovered that those cannons weren't real, we would surely be finished. But the Filipino soldier is truly different: Even with a lack of equipment, they can still overcome any battle with strategy and courage.

In our gathering today, we are proving that the time when duty was fulfilled only through courage and strategy is over—the state truly cares for the soldiers of the nation. Under our AFP Capability Upgrade Program, we are matching your skill and courage with modern equipment.

The delivery of these M4 Caliber 5.56mm assault rifles to our uniformed personnel only symbolizes the 50,629 assault rifles that will be distributed to our Armed Forces. This July, 27,300 have already been delivered to us, and the remaining 23,329 will be delivered this coming December. This is just the first step in our extensive initiative to strengthen our uniformed personnel; we are still working doubly hard to ensure your continued progress.

What’s even better is that, due to fair bidding, we were able to acquire these new rifles at a much lower price. According to the BAC, the price should have been P63,000 per unit—which was already much cheaper than the price we found when we started our term—we were able to get them for only P38,402.13 each. If you add it all up: With an approved budget of P3.19 billion, we bought them for P1.94 billion. Our savings reached P1.2 billion. Using part of this additional fund, our Department of National Defense was able to procure an additional 12,657 units of assault rifles for the Philippine Army, worth P486.06 million. These additional rifles are expected to arrive in December. Look: We were able to buy high-caliber rifles and save money too. The result: We can further strengthen your forces.

I am truly happy with this historic success. I remember: In a video I watched when I was still in the Legislature, there was a Balikatan exercise where the front sight of our soldiers' weapons was being sprayed with WD-40 oil. What does this mean? That WD-40 oil—or, is a penetrating oil—and is used for rusty weapons that are being forced to work. The sights no longer moved, so WD-40 was needed. I really felt sorry for our soldiers, and for the rest of our uniformed personnel—even the oil used to make old equipment work, we still had to beg from our allies. That’s why, with good governance, we are addressing problems like these.

The evidence of the meaningful change brought about by the straight and narrow path is clear. You, the uniformed soldiers, are now able to ride, handle, and benefit from these—new helicopters, ships, vehicles, and other high-quality equipment, which will help you serve the people better. There is nothing fake here, no speculation, and no invention. Everything is concrete change. It comes from the creative and prudent management of the government's treasury. It comes from our fight against corrupt and traitorous officials. We have hit the bull’s eye in achieving our targets, because of our perseverance in promoting vigorous and good governance. My only request to all of you is this: Let us take good care of these new tools, so that they can be used for a long time and to serve the people.

Let us not forget: In buying this modern equipment, there are other things that we did not immediately fund. As you can see in our budget: If there was a previous need to allocate funds, surely there will be other needs to allocate funds to as well. And when we only buy equipment for a program once, funding for other programs in line will be continuous. The result: The country's situation will continue to improve. The opposite of this: If we are negligent, we will just keep buying equipment; programs that follow in line will be neglected. Let's not let that happen, right? Now that we have paved the way for this initiative for you, we also expect you to fulfill your role in the state diligently.

It can no longer be denied: The face of your government is different; and the face of your army is also different. The old days of gunfights are over, now there is quality equipment. What was once neglected is now truly being cared for. Just this Tuesday morning, one of the most wanted in the Philippines was arrested, and is now going through the proper and just process, to answer the allegations of abuse against him. We can now also say: If before, your ranks were feared as executioners, now, you are respected and trusted as true partners of the Filipino people. You are there during disasters; you are not missing during times of danger or uncertainty; you ensure that our communities sleep soundly. Likewise, you prove: When we have the Filipino people as our allies, no task is impossible. Instead of joining the chaos, they themselves will uplift their fellowmen, and all of us.

We are now in a time when we are gradually fulfilling our lofty dreams for the country. I urge you: Let us take advantage of every moment. Let us hold on to what is right; focus every opportunity to serve our Bosses—the people—honorably and well, and surely: We will finally reach the Philippines we have long dreamed of.

Thank you very much. Good morning to all of you.
