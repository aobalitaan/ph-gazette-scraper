Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III President of the Philippines For the 113th anniversary of the Philippine Coast Guard

Excellencies of the diplomatic corps; Secretary Jun Abaya; Vice Admiral Rodolfo Isorena; General Gregorio Catapang, Lieutenant General Jeffrey Delgado; Vice Admiral Jesus Millan, Fire Chief Superintendent Carlito Romero; members of the defense and naval attaches present; former commandants of the Philippine Coast Guard; awardees of the 113th Philippine Coast Guard anniversary; officers; navies; men and women of the Philippine Coast Guard; fellow workers of the government; honored guests; my beloved countrymen

Good morning.

This morning, we gather to pay tribute to the new achievements of the Philippine Coast Guard, along with recognizing the unique contributions of your members to the service. We are also here to look back and emphasize the spirit of your mission as guardians of our coastlines.

You know: 36,289 kilometers is the total length of the Philippine coastline. We are reportedly the fourth longest coastline in the world. The Exclusive Economic Zone in our country: 135,783 square kilometers. These numbers show not only the extent of the territory you need to patrol, but also the extent of your service as uniformed personnel of the government: Maintaining the security and safety of the waters of the Philippines, towards a more peaceful and more progressive nation.

The safety of our seas from any threat of destruction rests with you. You are also the ones conducting operations against smugglers, human traffickers, poachers, and other lawless elements who violate or dare to violate our country's maritime laws. Whenever there is a disaster, you serve as a lifeline for Juan and Juana dela Cruz: you wholeheartedly face the danger, just to save and distance our countrymen from misfortune.

Indeed: The display of skill by you, the Coast Guard, reflects the presence of excellent leadership, under Vice Admiral Rodolfo Isorena. Of course, there are also you personnel who wholeheartedly participate in the extensive reforms we are promoting, not only within your ranks, but also in the larger society. I congratulate the individuals and organizations we are honoring today, who have truly shown great enthusiasm in fulfilling their duties. May you continue to be an example to your colleagues in steering the Philippine Coast Guard towards a more stable and prosperous future.

Indeed, the Philippine Coast Guard is now 113 years old; and as time passes, the challenges we face also change. Due to climate change, we see that storms are becoming more frequent and stronger. We also know the complicated situation in the western part of our country. And as circumstances in the waters change, our methods must also adapt to address them. Here, we can see the excellence of our Coast Guard. We know that the problems are not one by one; we know that any complex situation cannot be addressed by a single agency alone. It is not possible for the left hand to act without the right hand knowing. Therefore, to make our actions more solid and comprehensive, I am happy to see the Coast Guard cooperating with various agencies such as the Bureau of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources so that, as much as possible, no incident can slip through that could escalate tensions in our West Philippine Sea and in our vast ocean.

It is clear: The Coast Guard is among the frontliners guarding our coastlines. Therefore, as new challenges come your way, we also need to keep pace with the development of your capabilities, so that we are not left behind. What I can say to you is: The enthusiasm and dedication you show in service are reciprocated with sufficient recognition and care from your government. That is the basic principle of the modernization of your equipment and the development of your capabilities that we are promoting.

What are some examples of this initiative? There is the aforementioned addition of 40 rubber boats and 300 aluminum boats which are now assisting you in monitoring and patrolling the sea, and in more effective search and rescue operations. The DOTC also told us that ten new 40-meter Multi-Role Responsive Vessels from Japan will sail in our waters. Here is the projected delivery schedule for these: The first vessel will arrive in October 2015, followed by two in March 2016, and two more in September of the same year. By April 2017, there will be three more, and the last two will arrive in October 2017.

And because I know the enthusiasm of the good secretary, Jun Abaya, I will not be surprised if the arrival of the ten ships is expedited. This is part of our Maritime Safety Capability Improvement Project. The loan agreement for this was signed in December 2013, amounting to P9.27 billion. This is in addition to our target of purchasing two medium helicopters which we will acquire in 2015.

Likewise, the construction of our target of 113 lighthouses has begun. We are also planning to add 109 more lighthouses once we acquire land for them, in accordance with the standards of the International Association of Lighthouse Authorities. In connection with this, we are also excited, because I heard that by April 2015, our National Coast Watch Center will be completed. This is what we expect to be the center of coordination between various agencies for more effective monitoring of our seas.

All of these initiatives are anchored in one principle: Being prepared for any problem or tension that may occur in our waters. Whether it's morning or night, rain or shine, at any time, we must be vigilant and alert to any looming threat or danger. Our citizens and you, the coast guard, must be guided by the correct policies in dealing with any situation that could cause tension. That is the capability we are developing, because we know that it has a big impact on promoting order and progress in the entire Philippines.

The direction in which we are steering the Philippines is clear: A country free from chaos and danger; a country that is progressive and peaceful, composed of Filipinos who are empowered to attain a prosperous and vibrant life, not just now, but for generations to come. Therefore, in every stroke towards the fulfillment of this mission, join me. Let us make the most of every moment and opportunity to dedicate our time, strength, and skills to our fellowmen and country, so that we can finally reach the Philippines that we have long dreamed of.

Happy anniversary to all who make up the Philippine Coast Guard, and thank you all very much.
