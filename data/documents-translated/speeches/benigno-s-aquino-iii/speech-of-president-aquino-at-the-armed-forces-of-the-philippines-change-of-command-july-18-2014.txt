Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, in honoring General Emmanuel Bautista and the turnover of leadership of the Armed Forces of the Philippines.

Vice President Jejomar Binay; Excellencies of the Diplomatic Corps; Secretary Voltaire Gazmin; Secretary Jun Abaya; Chief of Staff Lieutenant General Greg Catapang; Former Chief of Staff Emmanuel Bautista; Presidential Adviser Danilo Cortez; Presidential Adviser on Peace Teresita “Ging” Quintos-Deles; Chairperson Etta Rosales of the Commission on Human Rights; Members of the House of Representatives present; the service commanders, Lieutenant General Dodo Iriberri, Lieutenant General Jeff Delgado, Vice Admiral Jess Milan; Police Director General Alan Purisima; Vice Admiral Rodolfo Isorena; the Most Reverend Leopoldo Tumulak; Local Government Officials present; chiefs of defense present; foreign military attaches present; former chiefs of staff of the Armed Forces of the Philippines; ladies, men and women of the Armed Forces of the Philippines; fellow workers in government; honored guests; my beloved countrymen.

Good morning to all of you.

It seems like only yesterday that General Emmanuel Bautista was going up on stage to accept his new mandate as Chief of Staff of our AFP. And now that he is stepping down from his position, we truly feel a mix of joy and sadness.

On one hand, I am happy that our Armed Forces had a leader who represents true service—not only saving lives and promoting peace, but also serving as an inspiration to his men and other public servants. That is General Bautista: No airs, no arrogance or showiness, always respectful to whoever he speaks to, and truly quiet and diligent in seeking solutions to the challenges we face.

In a democracy like ours, the strength of the state truly comes from the people, which is why they should always be the ones we serve, and the fulfillment of their aspirations should always be our goal. General Bautista’s term reflected this thinking: He brought the military closer to the people—directly talked to them, learned their needs, and readily addressed them. That is also why he became known to the public as “Manny Sundalo”—he is the epitome of what people think of when they think of a Filipino soldier.

It was January 2013 when he took his oath. By February, he was immediately thrown into a crisis when the trouble in Lahad Datu occurred. Imagine, General Bautista hadn’t even warmed his seat, and he was already facing a heavy challenge. Even then, he impressed us with his ability to fulfill his duty.

During that same year, unrest occurred in the City of Zamboanga. It was another heavy fighting, especially since civilians were caught in the middle and used as human shields by the wrongdoers; the enemies were not ashamed to destroy equipment, burn houses, or fire upon those providing humanitarian assistance such as the Red Cross or firefighters. We were even more impressed with General Manny Bautista because he responded to this problem calmly and truly implemented strategic plans and decisions. This was followed by an earthquake in Bohol and Cebu. Of course, our Armed Forces were immediately on the scene to respond. And before the year ended, Yolanda arrived. Our troops were still on the frontline during this disaster: providing immediate relief, clearing roads, and showing the affected communities that they were not alone. This was also the care and dedication shown by our soldiers in responding to the devastation of Typhoon Glenda. Of course, in addition to these trials, his unit also had a role to play abroad: There are our soldiers who are part of the peacekeeping forces in the Golan Heights, and the complex issue regarding the West Philippine Sea.

Indeed, General Bautista's term was challenging. He was weighed repeatedly; his ability and dedication were tested. And now, no one can say that he did not succeed.

To those who regret Manny Sundalo's departure from the AFP and public service, do not worry. After a very long vacation—about a month, he told me—he will return to continue serving as a civilian. To you, General Bautista: Thank you very much for your service, and thank you very much for your continued contribution.

It is difficult to follow the example set by Gen. Bautista. But with the long list of medals and honors attached to the name of Lieutenant General Gregorio Pio Catapang Jr., we are confident that the improving state of our Armed Forces will continue.

During the times he was assigned to Mindanao, or when he led the 7th Infantry Division and Northern Luzon Command of the Army, until he was designated as Vice Chief of Staff of the Armed Forces, General Catapang was considered an “all-around” officer by his colleagues—responsible, reliable, and with a broad knowledge of the needs of our uniformed personnel. To Lt. General Pio Catapang: I'm not pressuring you, but as the new head of the AFP, not only our 125,000 soldiers are dependent on your leadership; even our 98 million Bosses are hoping for your honest service.

I remember when we met during the EDSA revolution, where the conflicting relationship between the Armed Forces and the Filipino people ended. You were there, you participated, and I am confident that the fervent idealism I saw in you then has not been lost. Now that you are in the highest position in the AFP, I hope you will further strengthen the relationship that Manny Sundalo started between our uniformed personnel and our citizens.

The positive transformation of our Armed Forces is clearly visible in recent years. The state has compensated your courage and sacrifice through the AFP Modernization Program, housing, special financial assistance, and improvement of the pension system. I have always mentioned in my speeches the details about the modern equipment we have acquired; so let me focus now on the total of 50,629 units of M4 caliber 5.56 mm rifles that we have purchased for the AFP. Just look: The guns that were initially priced by our BAC at 63,000 pesos each, we got for P38,402.13 to be precise. We even saved 1.2 billion pesos from this, which will also be the source of funds for us to buy additional rifles.

This is the concrete manifestation of our good governance and proper spending. Before, your equipment was few and far between, and outdated. Before, the use of your guns was estimated. Now, you are guaranteed brand new rifles, purchased from a company known for the quality of its products. You can see: this is not just promised to you, you will truly benefit from it.

We have also begun to change the meaning of modernization in the AFP. With the help of the DOST and other technology experts, our knowledge about equipment has also become more modern. Because of this, we are able to buy based on our own specifications and not just what the sellers offer us. The result of this: Your ability to protect our citizens is better.

Let me just clarify: We are not procuring new security equipment to escalate tensions in our region. Instead, we are doing this to improve our response to any challenge; we are strengthening our readiness to save the lives of our countrymen, especially since climate change brings new challenges.

To Lt. General Catapang: Now that the face of the Armed Forces has changed, may you maximize every opportunity in your leadership to further strengthen and empower our institution, in the name of meaningful service to our fellow citizens and our flag.

There are only a year and eleven months left in our administration. We have already won many battles and reforms: from holding accountable corrupt government personnel, to expanding the reach of our social services; from reviving and accelerating our economy, to creating opportunities for our citizens; from promoting long-term peace in Mindanao, to restoring our national dignity. In light of all this, you know that in promoting reform, you will have many enemies. There are still those who want to return us to the old ways of exploitation. But we have proven: as long as we are on the right path, as long as our Bosses are conducting our activities, as long as their interests prevail in our agenda—the strength given to us by the people will not disappear. Our successes will continue.

This is what has been proven by dedicated public servants like General Bautista, and this is what we expect General Catapang to continue in the coming times. No matter how difficult the trials we have gone through in the past four years, our leaders, the soldiers of the people, have shown that they are on the same side as the people. Let us further strengthen the force of the Armed Forces, and our unity on the right path. Let us focus our service on the majority, and surely the next generation of Filipinos will also benefit from it.

Good day to all. Thank you very much.
