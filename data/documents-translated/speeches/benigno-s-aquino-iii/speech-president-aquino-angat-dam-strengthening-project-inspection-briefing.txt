Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, During the Inspection and Briefing of the Angat Dam and Dike Strengthening Project (ADDSP).

Thank you for inviting me here. I almost feel like I’m on vacation with the beauty we are seeing today. It’s very fresh; maybe you could give me ten more minutes while we’re here. Because I noticed that we’ve been on the Straight Path for over five years now. And to colleague Doneng Marcos; Marcos, who I’ve known for a long time. You seem to be losing weight, Doneng. [Applause] In those five years, and despite this, our reforms throughout the country have not stopped. It cannot be denied: the government we inherited was severely flawed; the system was full of leaks; many agencies were wasting funds and choosing to do their own thing. The service that should be flowing freely to the people is being hindered, and only a few are benefiting. Problems that should have been resolved a long time ago, we are now striving to find solutions for.

When Governor Willie was listening, I saw in his eyes that our bulk water supply, which has been promised to us for a long time, seems to be finally coming true. Babes Singson told me that it will happen soon, Manong Willie. [Applause] Our good governor is very diligent and easy to talk to; he smiles when problems are brought to him, so he is easy to deal with and we don’t face only anger, resentment, and ill will. So, thank you gov, for making our jobs easier. [Applause]

The situation we found in the water sector was complex. When we entered office, there were at least—and we discovered 30—agencies dividing the administration of this sector. What’s worse is this: the institutions were fragmented; plans were haphazard; data was incomplete; and politics were rampant. If we compare it to a band: each member plays their own tune with different instruments, so the sound that comes out is chaotic and discordant; instead of music, noise is the product. The result: the service failed, and the supply of clean water in the country is still insufficient.

Therefore, upon assuming office, we immediately designated Secretary Babes Singson. He not only serves as the water czar, but he also took the initiative to become the symphony master of the water sector. Under the leadership of Sec. Babes, we formed an Inter-Agency Committee on the Water Sector, which implements what we call the Integrated Water Resources Management Policy. What this policy simply means is: we look at the totality of water needs, so that it can be used correctly. Likewise, because the various agencies have been gathered, fragmented or piecemeal initiatives are avoided; instead, a unified objective for the sector is being implemented.

Let's talk about the Angat Dam and Dike. It was built in the 1960s; it's almost my age, actually. Since then, it hasn't undergone any major rehabilitation. It seems to have been overlooked that any structure will not repair itself or become stronger on its own. What was also discovered is that the dike is close to a section of the West Valley Fault. This means it is located in areas that are inherently dangerous, especially during an earthquake, which we hope doesn't happen. Because there is no appropriate surveillance system and the stability of the structure is questionable, the Angat Dam and Dike has been labeled as “conditionally poor.”

Why is it important to address this? It's simple: the lives and livelihoods of our Bosses are at stake in any damage or destruction of the dam. According to studies, if the dam's water rushes into the communities of Bulacan and Pampanga, 3 million Filipinos will be affected. Let me repeat: 3 million Filipinos, whose livelihoods may be disrupted, or who may be injured or die. We will not allow this to happen.

The value of the Angat Dam and Dike is clear to us. It is estimated that 97 percent of MWSS service areas—including Metro Manila, and parts of Rizal, Bulacan, and Cavite—are supplied with water by it. It covers 15 million Filipinos. For irrigation: it irrigates almost 27,000 hectares of rice fields in Bulacan and Pampanga. For electricity: it generates 246 megawatts for Luzon. What's even better is that we avoid flooding because of the dam's ability to control the water it stores. In summary: water, electricity, livelihood, and safety—with the scope of service and benefits of the Angat Dam and Dike to millions of Filipinos, every peso we allocate to this project is truly worth it.

We will undertake a full rehabilitation here. Using funds of 1.08 billion pesos, our aim is to ensure the stability of the dam and dikes, so that in the event of an earthquake, even one reaching up to 7.2 magnitude—or as strong as the previous earthquake in Bohol—this structure can still withstand it. Along with this, we will install a flood forecasting and warning system worth 261 million pesos, and conduct flood control protection works worth 292 million pesos as well.

This day became possible thanks to the unity and cooperation of various organizations and agencies. We thank the DPWH, under the leadership of Sec. Babes Singson; the MWSS, and Administrator Gerry Esquivel; the province of Bulacan; the private sector, K-Water and San Miguel Corporation, and our other colleagues who are working together towards the realization of this project. Again, thank you all very much. This initiative is a truly important legacy for many of our countrymen, not just now, but also for future generations.

This rehabilitation of the Angat Dam and Dike is just a part of our broader strategy to develop the water sector. This also includes the Angat Water Utilization and Aqueduct Improvement Project Phase 2, which we completed back in 2012, and 10 months ahead of schedule. The bidding for our other projects, such as the Kaliwa Dam in Rizal, Manong Willie’s Bulacan Bulk Water Supply Project, and the Angat Water Transmission Improvement Project, is also ongoing.

The achievements we have attained under the Straight Path are truly historic. And it is clear: We never relied on looking up to the sky just to receive grace. Any project or program is carefully considered; we identify the root cause of every problem, in order to lay down the appropriate solution. Instead of haphazard and hit-or-miss approaches, all our steps are polished and calculated, so that anyone who reviews it in the future will approve of every aspect. Everything goes through a long process, follows laws and guidelines, to ensure that its benefits to the Filipino people are far greater.

My Bosses, my pledge: To bequeath a Philippines in a better state than the one we inherited. Every moment of service to the government, we strive to achieve this. I also wholeheartedly tell you: Every opportunity in service, I will be true to you.

There is less than a year left, and every Filipino will once again face a crossroads, and this is my only appeal: Choose a leader who will also be true to you. Not anyone who makes statements and empty promises; not anyone we have even a slight doubt will take advantage of or oppress us; not anyone whose ambition is always self-serving, instead of for the entire nation—but let us choose an individual whom we can confidently trust to continue the straight and narrow path. Only you, the Filipino people, will choose the path the country will take in the coming years.

This is just the beginning of our long journey. I am confident: In the coming times, concern and contribution from the citizenry will flow again; our Bosses will once again steer the country in the right direction. With the united rhythm of the Filipino, the progress of the Philippines will continue towards the broad change we aspire to.

Good day and thank you all very much.
