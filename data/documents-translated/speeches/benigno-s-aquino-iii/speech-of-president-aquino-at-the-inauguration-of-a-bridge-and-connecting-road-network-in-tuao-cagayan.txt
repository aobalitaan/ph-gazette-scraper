Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, at the inauguration of the newly constructed bridge and its connecting roads in Tuao, Cagayan

Exactly two years have passed since the passing of a beacon of reform, not just for our LGUs, but for our entire country. The loss of Secretary Jesse Robredo brought great sorrow; at the same time, it intensified the entire nation’s recognition of his accomplishments and his legacy of example.

For us who were friends of Jesse, we remember not only his good work, but also his character. This is how I remember Jesse: Always in a hurry—not just to go home to his family in Naga, but to deliver service to our citizens. For Jesse, the benefits of good governance should be received by the people, now. This is what we continue to fight for on the straight path; our stance: To reach out to the people and let them know that the government is with them.

This is what the bridge we are inaugurating today represents, which will serve as an inter-regional link for Regions 1, 2, and the Cordillera. [Applause] The inauguration today also coincides with the approaching anniversary of my father’s passing, which we commemorate with the inauguration of this bridge.

They told me, from the late Francisco Mamba Sr., that the idea to build this bridge, which would speed up the flow of transportation and commerce in your province, originated with him. That was in 1968. As we always maintain: We do not intend to pass on to our successors the needs that we can address. Indeed, Secretary Manny is a dedicated partner in this endeavor. Even before, as representative of the third district of Cagayan, he pushed for the funding of this bridge. Because of the close cooperation of the entire Mamba family with the DPWH and the local government, the bridge and its accompanying road network were completed in 2013.

The total budget for this infrastructure amounted to P599.4 million. The P145 million allocated for the fourth phase of the project came from the Disbursement Acceleration Program. We were not only able to allocate funds in a more efficient manner; because of the proper management of Secretary Babes Singson and the DPWH, we even saved P33.7 million. [Applause]

Now that this 360-linear meter permanent bridge stands, along with the 40-linear meter Malummin Bridge, the 20-linear meter Angang Bridge, and the 21.93 kilometer road network component here in Tuao, it significantly reduces travel time. They say that before, it took four to five hours to travel from Tuao to Kabugao in Apayao; now, the trip takes only about two and a half hours, so we will see Governor Bulut often. [Applause] The trip from Tuao to Conner, Apayao, which used to take two hours, has been reduced by more than half an hour. Meanwhile, the trip from the town of Rizal here in Cagayan to Tuguegarao now takes only one hour and twenty minutes, which used to take two hours. The opening of routes to various tourist destinations in your province will also boost tourism, such as the Basilica Minore of Our Lady of Piat, known as the Pilgrimage Center of the North; the Tuao Belfry, Lallayug Forest Park, and Cassily Lake. Because the routes to these places will be more accessible, you can also expect the emergence of new businesses.

We understand the value of infrastructure, not only to strengthen the local economy, but also as a reliable route during bad weather. With this bridge, travelers no longer need to wait for the water from the Chico River to recede during the rainy season. The bridge will ensure the continuous flow of transportation and commerce, rain or shine.

This project is concrete proof of the significant transformation brought about by our adherence to the straight and narrow path. If before, the welfare of the majority was set aside, while a few grew old enjoying the benefits; now, the true needs of the people are being addressed. This includes ensuring that progress reaches even far-flung provinces like Cagayan. That is why we are promoting the right projects with the right quality, at the right price, and completed on time because we have designated the right people. We are also focused on the continuous repair of our arterial and secondary roads, which are part of our national road network, the construction of permanent bridges, and the leveling of roads that will bring tourists and travelers to various parts of our country. For the proposed budget for 2015, we allocated P562.35 billion, or equivalent to more than 21 percent of the national fund, for infrastructure.

Our aspiration is: Development that benefits the majority. This is happening, and now that our economy is getting stronger, we are more eager to push for positive reforms that will bring about widespread transformation in the country. This is my father's dream; this is Secretary Robredo's dream; this is the dream of so many who stood up for the Filipino: [Applause] A society where everyone contributes to each other and the country; a society brimming with opportunities and where no one is left behind.

We have proven in the past four years that nothing is impossible for a nation walking the straight and narrow path. Perhaps, this is how we can thank those who sacrificed to get us where we are: always siding with what is right and just. And I am confident—whether I am in the scene or not—the Filipino will continue to fight for his fellow man, and will step on the path towards a brighter, fairer, and more progressive future.

Before I finish, you know, I thought of something earlier. Manny Mamba, if I'm not mistaken, was the first colleague we had here in Northern Luzon who suggested that we run for a national office. [Applause] Sometimes, Manny, when we see progress in the country, I thank you. But every time I comb my hair, I wonder, are we even friends? [Laughter and applause]

We also had Governor Bulut with us in Congress for three terms. He also supported us. In 2007, I was number 19 in Apayao. In 2010, I ran for president; I improved, I was number 4. [Laughter] But Butzy [Bulut], this is my promise to you: as Babes Singson said, whether we work together or not, I am the father of the nation, president of the entire Philippines, and we need to address where the highest need is. [Applause]

Next year, Butzy, and in addition to what has been done, P1.3 billion is allocated for Apayao. [Applause] I will not surpass that number 19 because you only need 11 bridges. [Laughter] So, you can expect 11 bridges, but let me clarify, there are still some kinks in the right of way and what is called absorptive capacity. There might be a shortage of contractors in Apayao to complete the 11 bridges, and we aspire, in cooperation with you and the local government units of Apayao, to complete this before we step down from office. [Applause]

Comrades, when we read the newspapers every day, it seems like many are already campaigning. It's as if they have forgotten that we have problems now. There are also problems with the 2016 election—it's 2016. Now, let's address the problems surrounding our people, because it is our obligation, no matter what side we are on. If we can do something for others, we can't say “somewhere down the line.” It should be, “Now!”

Good morning. Thank you very much to all of you.
