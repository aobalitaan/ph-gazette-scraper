Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, Before his attendance at the 25th ASEAN-Republic of Korea Commemorative Summit

Before Typhoon Ruby even arrived, I had already spoken with the Korean Ambassador to the Philippines, the Honorable Lee Hyuk. Early on, we informed him that our push to South Korea for the 25th Commemorative Summit of ASEAN and Korea depended on the intensity of the typhoon’s impact on us. According to the forecast, it would pass through areas previously hit by Typhoon Yolanda. Based on the latest reports we received, it appears that the problems it caused us were not as widespread and severe. In fact, some were hesitant to even suggest declaring a national state of calamity. This further gave us confidence in our goal: While responding to problems within the country, we are also focusing our attention on the Philippines’ foreign relations.

I assure you, I will continue to monitor the incoming reports; we have full trust in the members of our Cabinet who are managing the measures being undertaken by your government to ensure that the lives of our countrymen affected by Typhoon Ruby return to normal.

This morning, our flight to South Korea is confirmed to attend the said Commemorative Summit. This is a good opportunity for your government to nurture and further strengthen the Philippines-South Korea relationship.

We all know that South Korea has long been a close friend of the Philippines. This is a relationship forged by a rich history of working together for democracy, freedom, and progress. From growing the economy, strengthening national security, boosting tourism, to the close ties between our people, South Korea is truly our partner. For example: More than one million tourists from their country arrived in our country last year; the Hanjin company from their country helped us become the 4th largest shipbuilding nation in the world, according to data from the first part of 2014. When we were hit by Yolanda, South Korea was also there to extend help, such as sending their Araw Troops to our affected communities.

We look forward to speaking with President Park again. We will take this opportunity to further expand and strengthen the cooperation between the Philippines and them, and promote stability and progress that will benefit our countries.

Likewise, in the meetings during this trip, we will promote our advocacy regarding global climate change. If we are asked about our best practices in preparing for and responding to this, we will not hesitate to share our experience and knowledge on the matter.

This is now our last trip outside the Philippines this year. As always, you can count on us: We will make the most of every moment to strengthen cooperation with other countries; we will seize every opportunity to deliver more opportunities for progress to our Bosses, the Filipino people.

Truly: Step by step, we and the whole world are seeing the ability of the Philippines to achieve meaningful change. Therefore, let us continue to walk the straight and narrow path; let us continue to contribute and care for one another, and I am sure, our collective progress towards a more developed and prosperous future will continue.

Good day. Thank you all very much.
