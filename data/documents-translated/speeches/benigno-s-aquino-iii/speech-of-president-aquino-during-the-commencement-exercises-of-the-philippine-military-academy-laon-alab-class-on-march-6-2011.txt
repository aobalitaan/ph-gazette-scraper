At the culmination of the Philippine Military Academy “LAON-ALAB” class of 2011

Today is another triumph for our country. With the graduation of one hundred ninety-six cadets this morning, the Philippine Military Academy has once again proven its remarkable ability to forge loyal and courageous defenders of the nation. I would also like to pay tribute to the parents who guided the graduates today. Because of your efforts to bequeath a better future to your families, you are not only giving your children peaceful lives; you are also blessing the country with hope.

I know that the training you underwent before graduating was no easy feat. You had to pass hundreds of exams. You successfully overcame thousands of physical challenges. The limits of your strength, intelligence, and self-control were tested to shape you and make you worthy members of the sacred tradition of the Philippine Military Academy. And now that you are ready to leave the grounds of Fort del Pilar, you must be aware of the reality that there are obstacles you will now face for the first time: corruption, injustice, and poverty. You will respond in remote and impoverished areas, and you will be the only face of the government. The citizens will expect you, not only to save them from harm, but to save them from poverty. You will also meet politicians who disguise themselves, dazzle you with large sums of money, and offer you power, to entice you to stray and turn away from the straight path. You will enter a war where your only weapon is your conscience. This is no longer an exam. The training is over. In the real world, the battle is not about grades. Your career does not depend on rank or the number of medals and awards. Outside this camp, and in the eyes of the ordinary Filipino—honesty, compassion, and courage: these will be the measure of your greatness.

I have full confidence in all the members of the Class of 2011 “Lakas Tipon, Alagad ng Bayan” or LAON-ALAB, that the flame in your hearts will never fade, and the strength of your integrity will never be broken. The Presidential Saber awarded to class valedictorian Cadet First Class Angelo Edward B. Parras symbolizes that since your Commander in Chief is a civilian, the source of your power comes from your true Boss, the people. And you can rest assured that your government will remain committed to promoting reforms that promote the interests of the people alone.

Before, there was a system where theft seemed to be encouraged because nothing happened. Turning a blind eye, deaf ear, and silence seemed to be the trend. That's why those who should have been pursued by the law were coming in and out of our country, their wives vacationing elsewhere. Those who should have been enforcing the law were promoting laziness and just expanding their stomachs. This will not happen in our administration. Now, we will correct the wrongs and rectify the distorted policies. We will hold the thieves accountable, along with their accomplices. We will not tolerate anyone; we will not sanctify anyone. We have a mandate that we must fulfill. We have justice that we will not allow anyone's incompetence to trample on. I know that despite the issues involving some of your ranks, your loyalty to the flag and the people will still prevail. Do not let a few people tarnish this esteemed institution.

What are the fruits of our reforms? What does a responsible system bring? Because our crusade against corruption is serious, because we are meticulous in spending public funds, we have accomplished in just eight months what was not accomplished in almost ten years. I recently mentioned the at least 20,000 affordable housing units we are offering to personnel of the Armed Forces of the Philippines and the Philippine National Police this year. And it’s good that Vice President Jejomar Binay is here, because he is a big help in building those houses.

We also know that no matter how good the theories and tactics taught, if there are no weapons, and the morale of the soldiers is low, it is all worthless. That is why, while you uphold security and bravely fight violence and criminality, we reciprocate by focusing on your needs. The boots and weapons you will use in combat, and the sufficient salaries and benefits you will receive are proof that the administration still has full trust in the military and police, and we salute your concern for the country.

As the future leaders of our country, the fulfillment of the dreams of many of our countrymen rests on your shoulders. That is why we will appoint General Eduardo Oban as the new AFP Chief, from the PMA Class of ‘79. And we will not let General David go from public service.

I have no doubt in his ability to continue the reforms started by General Ricardo David, and further strengthen the peace programs of the Armed Forces. May he serve as an inspiration to class LAON-ALAB so that we can achieve our aspirations for our country more quickly.

Remember this day. Always ask yourselves why we are gathered here today. You are not here to enrich yourselves. I am facing you today because you chose to care for the country. That is why I hope that when someone drops a truckload of money in front of you, you can refuse it because the principle instilled in you by the PMA still prevails: courage in any fight, integrity in the face of temptation, and loyalty to the flag. We all have obligations to fulfill; we have a vow we have sworn. Let us live these with dignity, and let us bestow a peaceful and golden future upon the next generation.

And before I conclude: I also came from a batch with a “1” at the end, only it was ‘81. I noticed that there is a three-decade gap between us. After you leave this institution, in the days that follow, you will always need to make decisions. Perhaps the one who named Fort del Pilar was truly intelligent, your campus. You come and go here, with Gregorio del Pilar looking at you; and we look up to you. The greatness of Gregorio del Pilar—he chose to love the country rather than take care of his personal interests.

So in the coming days, when you doubt, just ask yourselves, are you followers of Gregorio del Pilar, looked up to by the people, or will you be like those who only ended up with amnesia?

I repeat, you will not be praised or criticized just because of the schemes of others. All of that comes from what we do.

Congratulations to the graduates, especially to their parents, and to their teachers. May we live the speech of our class valedictorian every day.

Good morning. Thank you all very much.
