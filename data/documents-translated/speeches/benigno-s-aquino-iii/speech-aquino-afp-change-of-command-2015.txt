Speech of Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, During the Turnover of Command of the Armed Forces of the Philippines

On the Straight and Narrow Path, our commitment to the Filipino people is clear: To leave behind a Philippines that is undeniably safer, more peaceful, and more progressive than the one we inherited. Today, we pay tribute to an individual who has given his heart and mind to contribute to this goal: General Gregorio Pio Catapang Jr.

Let me emphasize: What we are doing is a celebration, and not a farewell. His contributions to our Armed Forces are etched in history. That is why even as he hangs up his uniform and passes on his sword, his leadership style, his concern for our citizens and soldiers, and his way of life will always be remembered: On the Straight and Narrow Path, the soldier is disciplined, abuse is prohibited, and accuracy is essential.

I remember when I first met him during the EDSA revolution: He was one of those who sided with our people instead of defending the dictator. And throughout his career, he always carried the spirit of EDSA: Whether he was assigned to Mindanao; led the 7th Infantry Division of the Philippine Army and the Northern Luzon Command; or served as Vice Chief of Staff of the Armed Forces. It was clear to him: Our soldiers should serve no one but our flag and the people.

His long experience truly honed his skills and gave him a deep understanding of the situation of our uniformed personnel. This is why we entrusted him with the position of chief of the AFP for the past twelve months.

He did not disappoint us. In fact, he exceeded our expectations. From the implementation of our Internal Peace and Security Plan Bayanihan, to the defense of our territory, he diligently performed his duties. Because he had been assigned for a long time in areas often hit by floods, he used his experience to sharpen the disaster preparedness of our soldiers. When our peacekeepers in Golan Heights were in danger, his excellent decision-making led to what is now called “The Greatest Escape.” During the period of mourning for our brave police officers, he was one of those who ensured that the relationship between the Philippine National Police and the Armed Forces of the Philippines remained smooth. During Gen. Catapang’s term, and with the cooperation of our police and other allies in strengthening security, many high-value targets were neutralized. These included high-ranking BIFF officials Basit Usman, Mohamad Ali Tambako, and Abdulgani Esmael Pagao, as well as Commander Parago of the CPP-NPA-NDF. General Catapang, on behalf of the Filipino people, thank you very much.

Indeed, with the contribution of every member of our uniformed personnel, Gen. Catapang will leave behind an AFP that is stronger, more modern, and with integrity. This May, the Institute for Solidarity in Asia awarded the AFP with the Silver Trailblazer Award, for its successful implementation of the Performance Governance System. This recognition proves that we are not just talking about implementing reforms in our uniformed personnel. We have laid out a clear plan in our AFP Transformation Roadmap; we are providing our soldiers with sufficient capabilities and equipment under the AFP Modernization and Capability Upgrade Program; and we are ensuring that all standards are followed from the highest to the lowest levels of our Armed Forces. Above all, we are undertaking and continuing measures to address future needs and ensure that the old ways do not return.

Isn't it, just a short time ago, it was a very lofty dream for our soldiers to even see modern equipment. Now, they are not only seeing it, they are using the BRP Gregorio del Pilar and BRP Ramon Alcaraz, as well as the BRP Tagbanua, the first landing-craft utility made in the Philippines; 3 AW-109 naval helicopters, 60 field ambulances; and 1 1/4 and 2 1/2 ton troop carrier trucks. In addition, we have 36 projects worth P90.46 billion that we are scheduled to procure. These include 12 Fighter/Surface Attack/Lead-in Fighter Trainer Aircraft (FA-50) and basing support; 6 Close Air Support Aircraft; 8 Combat Utility Helicopters, 2 Long Range Patrol Aircraft and basing support; 2 C-130 Aircraft; and 2 frigates. Let me just emphasize: It’s not luck that we have the money to buy these. We are spending public funds wisely, so we are able to provide many benefits.

Aside from buying modern equipment, we also took care of decent housing for you. This March, I signed the increase in your subsistence allowance. The uniformed personnel were also among those who received the Productivity Enhancement Incentive, which I signed in May. The DBM, DOF, DND, and DILG are working together to draft a pension bill that will lay out measures to ensure that your benefits are properly and continuously funded. In this way, we can also increase your numbers, which have not changed for a long time even as our population has doubled.

It is clear: On the Straight and Narrow Path, what is due to those of you who are willing to risk your lives when disaster strikes, there is a threat of terrorism, or a contagious disease, and someone wants to take our territory, is provided. Thank you for repeatedly meeting this with even greater excellence in performing your duties. There is no doubt: In the past five years, the unity of the AFP and the people has further strengthened.

Along with our tribute to Gen. Catapang, and the celebration of the successes of the AFP, we also warmly welcome the new leader of our Armed Forces: Lt. Gen. Hernando Delfin Carmelo A. Iriberri. I heard that if you ask anyone who has worked with him on missions, training, and exercises, they will give a very good mark to our new Chief of Staff. Even when he was just a platoon leader, there were no casualties in his troops when he was in command of the operation. During the 2013 elections, his brigade was assigned to Abra. Under his supervision and that of his men, for the first time, no barangay declared a failure of elections in Abra, and no election-related violence was recorded in the province.

Lt. Gen. Iriberri, this is the challenge for you now: Continue the good work started by Gen. Catapang and those before him, and further raise the quality of service of our soldiers. Let us remember: Elections are coming up soon. It is part of your duty to ensure that the election of our countrymen to the next leader will be peaceful and clean. In this way, you will reciprocate the support and care given to you by the Filipino people, our Bosses.

Truly: In just over five years of walking the Straight and Narrow Path, we have achieved so much. We have plugged the leaks in our systems and institutions. We are holding accountable those who are corrupt in the highest offices of our government. We have improved our economy, expanded our social services, and opened up more opportunities for our people. We have restored our national dignity. Just imagine what we can achieve if we continue to choose the right path.

It is clear: The old ways where you were only asked for impossible missions have been replaced by a culture that ensures you accomplish your missions. Under a leadership that prioritizes the needs of our uniformed personnel instead of themselves; with the contribution of our non-commissioned officers; and with the unity of civilians and the military: We can give the next generation the opportunity to reach even higher dreams, in a society that is more peaceful, safer, and more capable of defending itself.

Good day to you all. Thank you very much.
