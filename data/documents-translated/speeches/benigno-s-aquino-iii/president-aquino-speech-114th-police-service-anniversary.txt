Speech of Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, on the 114th Anniversary of the Police Service

In my last SONA, you may have noticed: One of the things I highlighted was the transformation being experienced by our national police. The service provided by the state for the police is truly gaining momentum, and you are reciprocating with exceptional performance.

I mentioned Oplan Lambat-Sibat. Through this initiative, crime in the National Capital Region has been significantly reduced. What’s good is that, just two weeks ago, crime in Metro Manila was reduced again. According to the latest data: From 37 cases of murder and homicide per week in the National Capital Region from January to June 2014, it went down to 21 this July. In robbery, theft, and carnapping for the same period, from the previous 919 cases, the weekly average dropped to 340 by July. Recently, under this program, 23 people involved in gun-for-hire activities, drug trafficking, and carnapping were arrested in the city of Caloocan. Because of the positive results of Oplan Lambat-Sibat, we have also expanded its scope; we have launched it in Region 3 and 4.

Lambat-Sibat is just one part of our comprehensive strategy to suppress the bad elements in society. From 2010 to this June, almost 163,000 wanted individuals have been arrested; more than 1,000 gangs have been neutralized, and 29,294 unlicensed firearms have been confiscated nationwide. This is proof of the success of an agency with good leadership and united police officers. With your effective work, many of our countrymen have been spared from danger; many communities are experiencing peace.

This celebration further highlights the achievements of individuals and police offices from various parts of the country. There’s the example shown by Police Chief Inspector Wilfredo Sy. He led the arrest of the most wanted man, Bingbong Kapina, the perpetrator of the heinous RCBC Bank Robbery, who had been hiding from the law for 15 years in Kabuntalan, Maguindanao. He was also instrumental in the arrest of high-value target Abu Sayyaf official Khair Mundos in 2014. There’s also Police Inspector John Ryan Doceo, who led 28 anti-drug operations that resulted in the arrest of 75 people. His team neutralized two drug syndicates in Iloilo; the Braga Drug Group and the Bolivar Drug Group, along with their accomplices from the barangay and PDEA personnel.

They are just some of our awardees today. We also pay tribute and thank the units of our PNP that have been honored. You, along with our other brave police officers, are the reason for the restoring confidence of the people in our national police. On behalf of the nation, thank you very much for your dedication to service.

We also thank the exemplary leadership of Secretary Mar Roxas at the DILG; I think you will agree with his excellent stewardship of our national police, as well as the Bureau of Fire Protection and the Bureau of Jail Management and Penology. To be honest, his appointment was sudden, as a replacement for our colleague who passed away, Secretary Jesse Robredo. But it was clear: Secretary Mar continued Jesse’s good initiatives, and he truly served as a bridge to implement reforms in your ranks. Now that Secretary Mar is stepping down—to avoid politics from influencing the performance of the DILG and our PNP—we are confident that the roots of the reforms he planted are deep, and will surely flourish towards further strengthening our police force.

To Police Director General Ricardo Marquez: You are free to choose those you want to work with. However, I know you are aware of the obligation that comes with it, to ensure that they fulfill their sworn duty to our Bosses.

Despite the many problems we inherited and are addressing, we also know that many more challenges will come. It is true: We cannot bring back the past, but it is far better if we fix the present, so that we can truly improve our future. My question is: Who among you wants to work hard? I think none.

As many know, all my decisions come from and are in accordance with what is best for my Bosses—the people. As President, my job is made easier because I know I am not alone; there are many Filipinos who serve as my strength, who truly unite with our reform agenda.

I am certain: As long as you, law enforcers, do what is right, reasonable, and beneficial to the people, your burden will surely be lightened, no matter how heavy it is. In times of danger, you are the first people our countrymen turn to. You will help them with what they need to do; you will respond to those in need of help and assistance. If you have fulfilled your duty well, or even beyond expectations, you will surely earn the respect of the people. They trust that you can protect and take care of them. So when they need your contribution and help in pursuing criminals, or in resolving a case, they are confident to cooperate with you as well. Isn't it easier for everyone with this kind of relationship? Isn't everyone a winner?

It is clear: At all times, we need strong unity and adherence to the rules. We cannot each do our own thing; we cannot deviate from the law or disobey orders. At no time can compliance with orders be optional. Therefore, the accountability for disobeying orders, we prove is not optional, but guaranteed with punishment. We will not only arrest, but we will ensure that those who are guilty are imprisoned.

Imagine: If our countrymen hear about crimes involving or colluding with the police; if there are many 'kotong cops' and widespread 'hulidap,' the public’s confidence in your ranks will surely disappear. In this practice, we are also allowing them to remain silent about the crimes they witness, or even side with those who pretend to be their defenders. In our administration, we are ending the happy moments of scalawags in your agency. We are determined to cleanse your ranks, and leave only the true and honest public servants.

On the other hand, we are giving incentives to those who demonstrate excellence and dedication in service. We will promote a culture in the police force where your advancement is in your own hands. Our goal: When the people see a police officer, they know they can rely on you, and they will truly be at peace.

As we told our advisors, including Sec. Mar Roxas and Sec. Voltaire Gazmin, as well as your chiefs and leaders of related agencies: I am open to your suggestions on how to better address the needs of our uniformed ranks to effectively fulfill your missions.

I remember, there used to be a “buying of rights” in the issuance of firearms to our police. My question: “What does this mean?” Apparently, when someone retires, they will surrender their issued firearm. Whoever wants to get the gun that was returned has to buy the “rights” for it to be issued to them.

Also: Back then, to get into the service, people had to borrow money to cover all the requirements to become a police officer. We assure you: Now, there is no such system; we will not allow this practice to continue where our police force is sidelined and the police themselves are oppressed.

With the current performance of the PNP, we are becoming even more determined to continue the service and deliver benefits to your ranks. As President, I am giving you what is rightfully yours. As I mentioned in my SONA: For the first time in our history, we have achieved a 1:1 police-to-pistol ratio. Now, you are using the Crime Incident Recording System, where crimes are recorded in more detail and more efficiently. There is also the installation of hundreds of CCTV cameras in various locations in the National Capital Region where crimes often occur. With these modern systems and equipment, the movements of criminals are monitored, and our police can respond quickly.

Some examples of the modernization we are promoting include the approved procurement of a total of 2,523 patrol jeeps, 577 utility vehicles, 5,736 motorcycles, 29,266 handheld radios, and 3,328 investigative kits. In addition to strengthening the force and improving your capabilities, we are also focusing on your personal needs. We have allocated almost 25,000 housing units for qualified PNP personnel. We are also addressing measures, such as adjusting your pensions, to ensure your welfare even when you are no longer in service. We have seen that these benefits are well-deserved by those who care for the country. If you maintain high morale in service, you will surely maintain, if not exceed, the successes you have achieved.

As the election approaches, the quality of service of our police will once again be measured. My challenge and order to you: Ensure that the election of our Bosses’ next leader is fair, peaceful, and just. We have intensified our operations to dismantle Private Armed Groups, and other threats just to use the election for their self-interest. I know you will continue to do your job; that you will not favor any party or prioritize self-serving agendas over the welfare of the majority. We will not allow others to use and exploit the issues facing the country; those who would rather trample and destroy our unity to regain power. I know you will continue to take the path that is reasonable and beneficial to our Bosses.

There is less than a year left in my term. My desire when I step down is simple: to leave a police force that is truly part of a society that is more just, more peaceful, and more progressive. Your role in promoting, not only the dignity and welfare of the police, but of the entire nation, is truly important. May you have one voice, and move as one towards what we aspire for the National Police: That when the PNP is mentioned, the only thing that can be said is, this is the force that reciprocates the state’s care with service; the force that defends those who are right, and does not tolerate the bad elements; the force that serves the people with courage, strength, and sincerity.

On every occasion, promote a PNP that is always true to its fellowmen, maximizing its capabilities to promote the welfare of Filipinos, now and for generations to come.

Thank you very much. Happy anniversary to our entire police force.
