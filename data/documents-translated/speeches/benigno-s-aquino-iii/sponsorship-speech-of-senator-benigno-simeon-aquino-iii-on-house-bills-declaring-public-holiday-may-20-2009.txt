Sponsorship Speech of Honorable Benigno Simeon Aquino III Senator of the Philippines On House Bills declaring public holiday

[Released on May 20, 2009]

In presenting the House bills declaring public holidays. Senator Aquino delivered the following speech:

The Committee on Local Government is honored to present Committee Report Nos. 398, 399, 400 and 401, recommending the approval of the following local holiday bills:

We recognize the need to observe local holidays that remind citizens of the legacy of their ancestors. The memory of their achievements inspires people to work for a better future.

Each locality is separated from others by their own distinct and colorful history. This uniqueness entitles localities to celebrate key historic milestones that are a source of pride for their constituents.

The authority to declare local holidays is vested with the President, through a Presidential proclamation, or with Congress, through the enactment of a law.

Chapter 7, Book 1, Section 27 of Executive Order No. 292 (Administrative Code of 1987) states that “the President may proclaim any local special day for a particular date, group or place.”

Under the authority of the President, it is necessary to declare local holidays every year. This has led to repetitive declarations of the same holidays every year.

In contrast, Congress sets in stone the need to observe such local holidays through its power to legislate. This helps eliminate the recurring and seemingly wasteful practice of annual holiday declarations.

In view of this, we therefore seek the approval of the abovementioned bills to place these holidays permanently in the consciousness of the people and to immortalize pivotal local events that helped transform Filipino society in general.

Source: Legislative Library, House of Representatives
