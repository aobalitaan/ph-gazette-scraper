Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, on the 80th Anniversary of the Armed Forces of the Philippines

This is my last AFP Anniversary as President and Commander-in-Chief. And on this day, I cannot help but recall my promise to the people: When I step down from office, we will see a great change; let us make this a reality. We have truly come a long way, compared to where we started. It is clear where we are now. In the past few years, we have collectively changed the image of the AFP: From a force neglected after a decade of lies, fraud, and theft in government, we have strengthened your ranks to become a more modern, more prepared, and more reliable military, whose sole interest is to promote the welfare of our Bosses.

It would be good for me to share with you something that was once mentioned to me, which I think perfectly captures the point of the positive transformation that has taken place in the AFP. Apparently, due to lack of equipment, a soldier would even carry a purple backpack from a fastfood chain on sensitive operations. My thought was: Not only is it unsuitable as equipment, but that purple backpack is probably too bright for an operation in the forest. Using such a bag is like putting a bull’s eye on the soldier’s back. This was precisely the situation we changed by promoting the agenda of good governance. When we said that no one would be left behind on the Straight Path, we broke the cycle of setting aside the needs of the people, including our Armed Forces. As I emphasized when I first faced you as Commander-in-Chief in July 2010: If the needs of every soldier are not met, how can every soldier meet the needs of the people?

I can say now: We have truly come full circle. Now, wherever you look in the AFP, you will see widespread change, proof that your government and the Filipino people are behind, in front, and beside you. Let's look at the big-ticket items implemented for the modernization of your forces: The three previous administrations implemented 45 projects, with a total value of P31.75 billion. With your cooperation, we were able to match and exceed that; now, we have 65 projects completed, and the budget released for this since we took office is P56.79 billion. In addition to this is our implementation of the Medium Term Capability Development until 2017; that will amount to P83.90 billion.

Now, we have significant assets to further strengthen your lifting capability, especially during times of disaster: the additional C-130s, the three C-295 medium lift transports, the two Landing Craft Heavy from Australia, and even various troop carrier trucks. We also have modern rifles and firearms, and night fighting systems, in addition to other equipment, to more effectively carry out your missions.

For heavier operations, we have the BRP Ramon Alcaraz and BRP Gregorio Del Pilar, as well as the new combat utility and attack helicopters, your multi-purpose attack craft, and armored personnel carriers. Also during our administration, for the first time in a decade, we once again have modern and sleek fighter jets; the first two units of the 12 FA-50 fighters we purchased have already landed, and you can expect their squadron mates to arrive before the end of 2017. I already mentioned the new C-130s and Landing Craft Heavy; I just want to add that we are not yet finished expanding our forces; we also plan to acquire new frigates and strategic sealift vessels, as well as Long Range Patrol and Close Air Support Aircraft, and other equipment such as engineering equipment. We also have the US and Japan, two of our three strategic partners, with us in expanding the capability and knowledge of our AFP.

If we are talking about the AFP’s medical services, we have the new facility at Camp Bautista Station Hospital in Sulu, as well as the upgrade to the operating room of the AFP Medical Center in Quezon City. We also have new field ambulances, as well as the requirements for the First Forward Medics. We continue to focus on purchasing additional medical and dental equipment for the entire AFP.

Along with all of this, we continue to address your housing needs through the AFP-PNP Housing Program, which is 99 percent complete for Phase 2. We also increased the subsistence allowance of our soldiers, police, and other members of the uniformed service; as well as the monthly combat pay of our soldiers who are involved in operations. There are also livelihood programs for soldiers, whether active or retired. We continue our efforts to fully establish a just and fair pension system for the defenders of the people. As citizens, you have benefits that are no different from those enjoyed by our other countrymen. For example, the strengthened and expanded Philhealth; and what we want to emphasize: Your government is really working hard to deliver to you the service expected by every Filipino.

My question now is: With all of this, can anyone still say that our soldiers were neglected during the Straight Path? I believe it is very clear that we are all rising together in this chapter of our history. I personally saw how our AFP became stronger and more effective in serving and promoting order and stability, which is key to further strengthening the confidence of others to invest in Filipinos.

In every intelligence briefing and mission update shared with me, and even in the stories relayed by your leaders and commanding officers, I witnessed the sacrifices and efforts of our soldiers, who bravely, courageously, and wholeheartedly fulfilled their sworn duty. Often, I, along with the leadership of our AFP, was the only one aware of the missions you successfully accomplished, which came at the cost of blood, sweat, and even tears of your ranks.

We recognize the soldiers who demonstrated excellence alongside our police force when Zamboanga City was attacked. Through our daily monitoring of the situation in the city, we, Sec. Volts and Sec. Mar, personally saw the readiness of those on the side of the government to risk their lives to save the hostages and restore order to the city.

We are all truly impressed by the steadfastness of our Marines guarding the West Philippine Sea. They and our other soldiers are guarding the remote parts of our territory. Outside our country, we have our peacekeepers, such as those assigned to the Golan Heights, who, even when placed in dangerous positions, truly demonstrated the ingenuity, intelligence, and readiness of our race to do what is right.

We cannot fail to mention the members of our armed forces who faced numerous challenges to serve our people: those who responded to calamities such as Typhoons Sendong, Pablo, Yolanda, Santi, Ruby, Lando, and even Nona and Onyok. I would also like to give special mention to the members of the Air Force who never complained even with the high operational tempo during this trial. There are also the soldiers who deliver service and care to our remote communities; they serve as a beacon to those on the margins and those who are exploited.

I would also like to mention all the members of our uniformed personnel who contributed to ensure the safe and peaceful visit of our beloved Holy Father and the leaders of APEC and their delegations this year. Here I see the invaluable connection between the different branches of our security sector. This is also the strong relationship I witness in the daily support among the members of our Presidential Security Group.

Comrades, in the name of our Bosses, please accept our heartfelt gratitude for the invaluable service of every official, soldier, reservist, and personnel who comprise the honorable Armed Forces of the Philippines. With your exemplary performance, it is not surprising at all that the trust of our people has been renewed.

We can say that we are fortunate to have a strong and effective leadership in the DND and AFP who are truly our allies in the Straight and Narrow Path: from Sec. Volts Gazmin who was with us even before in pushing for reforms; there is also your current Chief of Staff, Gen. Hernando Iriberri and the former chiefs, and your current and former service commanders. All of them are known for their leadership skills, professionalism, and loyalty in fulfilling their duties. In every moment that could cause worry and doubt, they are there, answering every question I have, implementing any order I give, and truly sacrificing so that no fear reaches the public.

I would like to emphasize: As we enter the last six months of our administration, our Bosses have great expectations of us. Their mandate is clear: to continue serving without favoring anyone but the people; to continue reciprocating the care you have given us with service; and to continue being a source of strength and support.

In the coming year, the responsibility rests on our shoulders to ensure that the upcoming elections will be peaceful and successful. This is my last mission for you, and as your Commander-in-Chief, I am confident that you will follow only one direction: to do what is right, reasonable, and just for the good of the nation.

In my remaining 192 days in office, you can count on me to double my efforts to ensure that we achieve a Philippines that is far better than the one we inherited, a country that serves as a springboard for widespread progress. It is truly a great honor for me to serve as Commander-in-Chief of an Armed Forces that does not yield to any challenge and reflects the highest ideals of the Filipino race.

Before I conclude, I have been looking at Mount Arayat for a while now. I remembered what my grandfather used to say, that if you want to know what the weather will be like tomorrow, if you see the peak of Arayat, it means there is no rain coming. Now the peak is very clear. We hope that this is also a symbol that the future is becoming clearer and clearer, not only for the Armed Forces of the Philippines, but also for the entire Filipino people.

Good day. Thank you very much.
