Statement of His Excellency Benigno S. Aquino III President of the Philippines On the recent findings of the Mamasapano investigation

For a long time now, alternative narratives about the encounter that happened in Mamasapano have been circulating. This came out in the media: They say that our Special Action Force or SAF did not kill Marwan. Apparently, Marwan’s companions killed him; they also took his finger and delivered it to the SAF.

When this so-called alternative narrative was presented to us, and we reviewed the evidence we had, we could not simply dismiss it. This alternative version is completely the opposite of the initial account. It is our job to examine and determine the whole truth, in order to arrive at a correct and reasonable conclusion. Through this, we can also ensure that this tragedy will not happen again. That is why we immediately instructed various government agencies to also look into the angle that presents a different account.

Now, Bosses, the new evidence has arrived. I am here before you to show some of the collected photos. Please excuse us to our countrymen, especially during lunchtime. There are some unpleasant images here, but we needed to show them to clarify the issue. We chose to blur some parts for this presentation because some of the images we will see are not pleasing.

The first photo shown to me was just taken from a whole photo. It also appeared in the media. In this photo, I'm not the only one who thought that because Marwan's right hand was covered, this is where the finger was taken.

Next, this is the whole picture: You can see one of our SAF troopers, next to Marwan's body, and you can see that his fingers on his left hand are still complete.

The following photos show the same SAF trooper holding Marwan's left hand and in the act of taking his finger.

In this picture, our SAF are still present, and it can be observed that in the next photo, one of Marwan's fingers is missing.

Let's go back: What does the alternative narrative say? According to it, a companion of Marwan killed him, and this same person took the finger and gave it to the SAF. But it is clear in our presentation now: The SAF were there; it is impossible to doubt that the SAF took Marwan's finger. This also means: All other accounts regarding the alleged alternative narrative have no basis, and are meaningless.

Perhaps someone is asking: Why are these photos only surfacing now? Apparently, in the formal investigations regarding this, no one asked if there were other photos. I asked: Are there no other pictures? Isn't it common in ordinary photography during such events to always ask to be sure, it's just normal that only one photo isn't taken?

That is why we also took the initiative to ask the newspaper that released the photo. But they said they only have one photo. We also asked the SAF, in front of their new director. When I asked him, he said they were still unsure during that time, because he was really new. Once they were sure, they came back to us, and that's when we discovered there were many more photos.

I would just like to reiterate: We had the SD card containing these photos examined by the cybercrime division of the National Bureau of Investigation. The report: “Our forensic examiners concluded that the pictures are authentic and unaltered.”

Let us also remember: There was a large reward on Marwan’s head both locally and internationally. It is not surprising that many aspired to claim the reward and attempted to muddy the waters. Due to this investigation, it was given the appropriate clarification.

Now that the discussion regarding this is finished, we will proceed with our search for justice, especially for those who died. Currently, 90 individuals will face a fair and thorough process regarding the killing of the 35 Commandos of the 55th Special Action Company, which is part of the SAF.
