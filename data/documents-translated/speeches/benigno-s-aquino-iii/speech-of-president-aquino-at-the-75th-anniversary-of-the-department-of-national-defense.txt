Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, on the 75th Anniversary of the Department of National Defense.

Good morning to all of you.

As we celebrate the 75th anniversary of our Department of National Defense, we also look back on the stages that your institution has gone through in the past decades. There was a dark chapter in our history when Mr. Marcos placed the country under Martial Law. During that time, the dictator used the Department of National Defense for personal interests. He used the power of the military to remain in power, and to sow fear and terror so that no one would question his desires.

However, a time came when millions of Filipinos united and gathered in EDSA to fight the dictatorship. Here we saw how the perspective of our soldiers returned to who their true Boss was, none other than the people. Our soldiers sided with the Filipino people, and served as a key partner in the restoration of democracy. And now, we are walking the straight and narrow path, the one that once suppressed the rights of citizens, now recognizes as a defender of the welfare of all. What was once feared is now a true advocate for peace and security of our nation. It is clear: You soldiers are a pillar of the people as courageous public servants.

A large part of the success that our uniformed personnel are enjoying today is also due to the reforms being promoted by our good Secretary Voltaire Gazmin. This Sec. Volts’ service has been tested by time; whenever he faces a crossroads, he always chooses to do what is right and what is good for others. When my father was imprisoned for opposing the dictatorship, Sec. Volts was one of the few who treated my father correctly and properly. Perhaps because of this, those in power at that time were not pleased with Sec. Volts. The reward he received: He was assigned to Mindanao from 1975 to 1986.

When Sec. Volts served as head of the PSG, during the time when several coups were experienced by my mother's administration, he successfully led the defense of our family, and our newly born democracy. Let us remember: There was a real danger in those times; real guns with real bullets were held by people who seemed to easily forget the lessons of Martial Law. Despite the danger, Secretary Volts chose to protect our freedom. And now, as a civilian, he continues to pour his strength and time into promoting our agenda of reform in the Armed Forces. As an individual who came from the ranks of the military, we are confident that Secretary Volts understands the culture, as well as the shortcomings and needs that must be addressed in your sector. That is why he serves as our main advisor to implement programs that promote the interests of the Armed Forces and the people.

We also know: A large part of the duty performed by our defense is not only confined to combat and guarding the country from internal and external threats. There is also your mandate to contribute to the development of the economy and of the larger society. And in this field, I also salute your unwavering diligence and dedication.

One example of this is your help in finally completing the Basilan Circumferential Road. Imagine: it has been under construction since the year 2000. It was delayed for a long time due to peace and order issues. What is the benefit of this project? Once the construction of this road is completed, the former three-hour and 45-minute trip along the circumferential road will be only two hours. The number of residents who will benefit from this: is about 450,000 people. This includes fishermen and farmers who can now deliver their harvest to the market faster, and travelers who will have a more comfortable journey. Likewise, with a better road, our uniformed personnel can respond more quickly in times of need. Despite this, there are still those who do not want it to continue; those who benefit from chaos and lack of opportunity in the community. We will not allow them to pursue their self-serving agenda. With the help of our Armed Forces, along with our LGUs, we are targeting to complete the circumferential road next year.

This is proof that our soldiers are not only skilled in combat, but are also reliable partners in social services. It is not new to us the heroism you demonstrate: from your readiness to face danger to defend the citizens against malevolent elements, to guarding our territory, to assisting and delivering services to those affected by disasters, you are there, ready to help and support.

We congratulate our awardees today. On behalf of the nation, thank you very much for your efforts to raise the level of service to our fellow citizens and our flag. May you always serve as an example to your colleagues to further improve the fulfillment of your duties.

The time is over when those who care for the people were the ones oppressed and forgotten. Now, as you care for the citizens, the government reciprocates with appropriate support. As we focus on your welfare, our expectations for your service also increase. You can rest assured that as you excel, your leadership will also be more motivated to help you overcome the challenges of your mission, and even your daily needs. It is clear: With good governance, no one is neglected, no one is prioritized; there is only “us” moving together towards the realization of our collective aspirations.

The AFP Modernization and Capability Upgrade Program, which we have been implementing from June 2010 to October 2014, has already allocated funds totaling P41.38 billion. This is significantly higher than the combined funds allocated by the previous three administrations from 1995, which amounted to almost P32 billion. The projects we have completed have reached 46; we have also surpassed the 45 projects completed by the three administrations that preceded us. We still have more than a year and seven months remaining, so we are confident that we will be able to complete more. In 2013 alone, we completed 12 projects. These include the purchase of two modern ships, three naval helicopters, and eight combat utility helicopters. Our DND Medium Term Capability Development Program, which is covered by our Revised AFP Modernization Law, is also ongoing. Under this program, we are completing 33 projects that will run until 2017. The amount we have allocated for this: P90.86 billion.

Earlier, we witnessed the launch of the Philippine Defense Transformation Roadmap 2028. This will serve as a master plan to ensure that all our initiatives are concretely implemented. We are ensuring that our efforts will not be wasted. In this comprehensive plan, we will ensure that the roadmaps of the relevant DND agencies are aligned to achieve the complete transformation of our defense system.

Aside from providing modern equipment to our soldiers, the state also provides other services for you. These include the distribution of decent and affordable housing, the improvement of your pension system, and livelihood programs for active and retired soldiers. What we want is to provide the benefits that those who care for the country deserve.

As we celebrate your anniversary, we also commemorate the sacrifice of those who came before us; those who fought to achieve the freedom we enjoy today. Let the nation not forget: It is the obligation, not only of the soldiers, but of every Filipino, to protect our freedom. And by valuing the intellectual heritage and principles of our heroes, we can ensure that the independence emerging from their courage and dedication will not be stolen again. Through the cooperation of the citizens and the military, we can further uplift the nation towards the future we dream of. Let us reach this together, so that when the time comes that we are called by the Lord, we can proudly say, “When I leave, the condition of the country I left is far better than what I found, a society where no one is left behind in broad development.”

Before I conclude, I think we have so many things we want to do to properly serve our fellow citizens. Sometimes I wonder: Is this noticed? Is this valuable? Is this really helping? Now we have come to a moment where we call it a virtuous cycle. We have peace. Our economy has the opportunity to rise and really flourish. When there is a state, an added capacity to really serve our fellow citizens, all this cooperation speeds up our reaching what we want to achieve.

Good day to all of you. Thank you very much.
