Speech

of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

On the 119th anniversary of the Philippine Army

[Declared at Fort Bonifacio, Taguig City, on March 22, 2016]

This morning, exactly 100 days remain in our term. On the anniversary of our Philippine Army, I cannot help but recall what happened the first time I faced the Armed Forces as Commander-in-Chief; it crossed my mind then, a scene often seen on television or in movies, where, due to extreme hardship, a father says to his son, “I’m sorry, I can’t handle it.”

At the start, to be honest: With the many problems inherited by us, we would have said this to the Armed Forces many times. But on the Straight and Narrow Path, because of our cooperation, we have been able to handle so much, and we can handle much more. We can even say that we fulfilled our vow: The condition of our soldiers is undoubtedly much better now.

You yourselves, here in the Philippine Army, are witnesses to the modernization happening within your ranks. Our Commanding General already mentioned some of the large and modern assets that the Army has acquired as it walks the Straight and Narrow Path. You yourselves know what our Army is capable of in these times, because of the transformation that we have collectively pushed for. Just wait for further meaningful equipment that will be in your hands soon, as announced by your leaders and commanders.

I will not list everything, because there is a matter of national security in sharing this kind of information. What I can say is: 68 big ticket projects, amounting to P58.43 billion, have already been implemented for the entire AFP. 26 of those projects, or equivalent to P7.79 billion, went to strengthening the capability, knowledge, and readiness of our Philippine Army. Let me point this out: This AFP Modernization has not only exceeded our expectations of what could be achieved within six years; it has also exceeded what the three administrations before us actually accomplished.

Along with that, for the future, we are also implementing the Army Transformation Roadmap 2010-2028 to further strengthen the culture of effective and good governance within your ranks. Aside from the modernization of your capabilities, there are also various initiatives we have to truly protect the welfare of you who serve the people. For example: More than 61,000 units have been completed under our AFP-PNP Housing Program, and the Philippine Army is among those benefiting from this program.

There are also livelihood programs being implemented for our soldiers, active or retired, in Fort Magsaysay in Nueva Ecija, Camp Kibaritan in Bukidnon, and Camp Peralta in Capiz. For the Army, we have reported that we have also conducted 13,903 livelihood and 2,552 vocational trainings for 145,629 Army personnel and their dependents.

A total of PHP 2.35 billion has been released for the clothing and appropriate equipment of each member of the Army, while PHP 249.28 million has been disbursed for your medical and dental equipment. PHP 346.28 million has also been provided as reimbursement for the hospitalization of those in the Army and their dependents, in addition to aid for the orphans of our soldiers who lost their lives in the performance of their duty.

During our term, the monthly combat pay for our soldiers facing operations was increased. The subsistence allowance was also increased for the entire uniformed sector. As mentioned earlier: We have also signed EO 201 which covers the increase in your monthly hazard pay, and even for your provisional allowance and officers’ allowance.

This year as well, although SSL4 was not enacted into law, a portion of our approved budget is allocated for the funding source of your provisional allowance, so we were able to fully increase it for our soldiers. But I would like to reiterate our call to our Congress: Hopefully, SSL4 will continue until 2019, and this can only be done through the enactment of a reasonable proposal that will promote the welfare of all government personnel.

Under the Straight and Narrow Path, we not only focused on the welfare of our soldiers, but we also implemented widespread reforms to simultaneously address the needs of our Bosses. For example: Just last week, the NEDA report came out stating that we reached a record low in poverty incidence throughout the country since 2009. At the same time, the lowest unemployment rate in a decade was also recorded under our administration. According to the fourth quarter 2015 survey of SWS: We also registered the lowest annual average hunger rate in 11 years.

Also among the most striking examples of what we have achieved under the Straight Path is the strengthening of our Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program, which we expanded from the initial 786,000 households to support 4.4 million households currently. This year, we will finally achieve our target of 4.6 million beneficiary households. Alongside our efforts to address poverty is the good news that more than 7.7 million of our countrymen have been permanently lifted above the poverty line.

In infrastructure: We have completed and repaired more than 18,500 kilometers of national roads and 107,000 linear meters of bridges. This includes the Basilan Circumferential Road, which we visited just yesterday, and which took 16 years to concretize. Because of the convenience provided by this infrastructure, the hearts and minds of the people will be brought closer to the government and to you. Because you hold the sympathy of the ordinary Filipino, the evil plans of disruptive elements will be further hindered.

Comrades, let me emphasize: We accomplished all of this without imposing additional burdens on our countrymen. In truth, development was made possible by your shared responsibility and because we did what was right. It is true that peace and positive transformation can only be achieved if there is order and stability. It is clear: Because you fulfill your mandate, because you remain true to the motto “Serving the People, Securing the Land,” we have come so far.

To our Army, and even to our entire Armed Forces, as defenders of your fellow Filipinos, and as a legitimate force, you possess the elements to achieve what is called the “monopoly of armed power.” But this is also the very element that, if used to promote what is right and just, will lead us to a better, fairer, more peaceful future that we can bequeath to the next generation. Therefore, in the upcoming elections, the mandate of the people is clear to us: Remain on their side, do not get involved in politics, and ensure that the united voice of the nation walking the path of democracy prevails.

In fulfilling your mandate, your leaders in the service are here, led by DND Secretary Volts Gazmin, who has long pushed for the modernization of our Army, our AFP Chief of Staff, Gen. Hernando Iriberri, our Commanding General Eduardo Año, and your united leadership, who will steadfastly guide you at every moment of your service.

Now that we are here at Fort Bonifacio, I cannot help but feel personal because we have come full circle. You know, when I was very young, and I’m sure our generation will remember this: there was a TV series titled “Combat.” Because of this, I can say that I wanted to be a soldier when I grew up. There were no Playstations yet, so our games were cowboys and Indians and gunfights.

But Martial Law came, and my perspective on our soldiers really changed. Right here in Fort Bonifacio and even in Fort Magsaysay, my father was imprisoned for seven years and seven months. It was during these times that we met our future SND, who was then a guard at my father’s prison; because he treated my father well and with decency, because he was professional, Sec. Gazmin’s reward from the dictatorship was: he was exiled to Mindanao from 1975 to 1986.

If I may emphasize: The prisons where my father and many others were held were preserved as a sign that there was a period in our history where the Armed Forces were exploited and misled by those who promised to serve the people. Many of you were misled, because of the belief that you were following the Constitution in obeying the orders of the dictator. You strayed from the will of the people, and one result of this was the swelling of the NPA’s ranks from 60 people to 25,000 armed members at the end of Martial Law.

But with the guidance of my mother and even our administration, the Armed Forces truly returned to the right side where you care for the people. The citizenry, in turn, reciprocates with more solid support. The results are evident, because with the cooperation of the people, it became easier to bring to justice large-scale enemies of the State such as CPP Acting Chair Benito Tiamzon, CPP Secretary-General Wilma Tiamzon, and Eugenia Magpantay-Topacio, who is the Secretary of the Central Luzon Regional Committee of the CPP-NPA. Within our administration, with your implementation of over 1.3 million operations against enemies of the State, we have completely liberated 50 out of 76 provinces affected by NPA insurgency. Only twelve remain.

This is the image you will leave with the ordinary Filipino: an Army and Armed Forces that they can always rely on, which is a partner in promoting stability and order in communities. From this emerges the confidence that is needed to advance progress. Whenever there is a typhoon, in the face of heavy rain and strong winds, the Armed Forces and other government personnel are there, making ways to clear the roads so that aid can get through, as we witnessed during typhoons Yolanda, Ruby, Lando, and others. In such actions, you truly feel that our Bosses are behind you, and the end result is a virtuous cycle of trust, contribution, and positive change that will benefit us all.

This is the Army and AFP that we will bequeath as a departing Commander-in-Chief: An army with very high morale and fully prepared for any challenge; an army that holds the recognition and respect of the people; a professional army with strong principles.

As we journey along the Straight Path, I say: It is my honor to have served as your leader and Commander-in-Chief. One day, I will look back on this point in our history with pride: Together with the Army and the entire AFP, we did what was right and lived the truth that “The Filipino is definitely worth fighting for.”

Thank you very much to all of you. Happy anniversary to all of you as well.
