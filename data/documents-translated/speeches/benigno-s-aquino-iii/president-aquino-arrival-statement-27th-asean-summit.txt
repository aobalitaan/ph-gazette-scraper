Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III upon his arrival from a visit to Malaysia for the 27th ASEAN Summit

We return after a meaningful participation in the 27th ASEAN Summit held in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia.

Together with other leaders in Southeast Asia and our dialogue partners such as China, Japan, Korea, the United States, India, Australia, New Zealand, and the United Nations, we launched the ASEAN Community and the ASEAN Vision 2025. This outlines the direction the region will take in the coming years in terms of security, economy, and social aspects. Because of this, our engagement will be intensified and expanded at the level of what is called functional cooperation; this means that the actions resulting from ASEAN issues will be more immediate and focused. This is very important, especially in the face of issues such as disasters, terrorism, pandemics, trafficking in persons, and others.

There is no doubt: In the global community, the progress of one country is linked to the progress of others. Likewise, the challenge for each one is a challenge that must be addressed by all. Unity and cooperation are key to ensuring that no one is left behind.

We also emphasized: Without stability, there is no progress. It is the responsibility of each country to contribute to achieving stability, instead of adding to the tension in our region. The majority agreed that adherence to a fair, peaceful, and reasonable resolution in accordance with international law should be upheld. This situation is already far from what we encountered during our first attendances at the ASEAN Summit: Before, the number of those willing to contribute their voices to us was limited; now, we have many partners in our stance. Even those we used to ask for support have said that a Code of Conduct must be established.

You can see: Through good governance, we have sown the seeds of transformation. Among the sweet fruits we are reaping now is the support and trust, not only of our brothers and sisters in ASEAN, but also of the dialogue partners who attended the meeting and the entire world. Last week, we successfully hosted the APEC; now, we have been warmly welcomed at the ASEAN Summit.

Because also this is the last ASEAN Summit I attended as your president, we also used the opportunity to say goodbye to our fellow leaders in Southeast Asia. Aside from the immediate assistance during calamities, where they no longer need to be asked but arrive immediately, we also thanked them for sharing their perspectives and experiences, which helped us further strengthen our engagement with their people and governments.

In 2017, the gathering of leaders in ASEAN will be held in the Philippines. My Bosses, by that time, you will have chosen a new leader. I am confident that with the good start we have made, and with our continued journey on the Straight Path, we will once again prove our ability to shine and contribute to the promotion of broad development. Truly: With your unwavering support, we can leave the next generation with a Philippines that is more developed, stable, and respected in the global community.

Thank you very much.
