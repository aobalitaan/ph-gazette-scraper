Speech

of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

On the 2016 GOCC Dividends Day

[Delivered at the Rizal Hall, Malacañan Palace, on May 23, 2016]

My pledge to our Bosses: To leave our country in a situation that is truly far from what we found it to be. And our gathering every GOCC Dividends Day is one of the proofs of this, and it really makes us smile.

To know that we have truly fulfilled our pledge, allow me to look back at where we came from.

In our first SONA, we identified corporations that, instead of serving the people, first provided themselves with various allowances and bonuses. Back then, once you entered a GOCC, it was as if you were given the privilege to take advantage of the position.

Indeed, one of the examples we mentioned then was a GOCC: Aside from regular salaries and increases, they also received mid-year bonuses, productivity bonuses, anniversary bonuses, year-end bonuses, grocery incentives, and financial assistance. And when Christmas came, there was a Christmas bonus, and even an additional Christmas package. In that situation, their company was already losing money, yet they had so many bonuses. Most likely, we were rewarding poor performance during that time. This was the image we found of our GOCCs: Like a carabao, it was already milked, and they still wanted to slaughter it.

We did not allow this practice to continue under our term. Under the leadership of Secretary Cesar Purisima and the Department of Finance, we identified what was wrong and fixed the system. This led to the passage of a law in Congress that would promote professionalism in our GOCCs. With the help of then Congressman Jun Abaya and Senator Frank Drilon, the GOCC Governance Act of 2011 was enacted. Through this, we established the Governance Commission for GOCCs (GCG), which oversees the monitoring and improvement of the service of our corporations.

We were fortunate to have appointed Chairman Cesar Villanueva, and Commissioners Angela Ignacio and Rainier Butalid. Under their excellent leadership, we focused and returned the GOCC to its true mandate: to care for and serve our Bosses, the people. The question now is: What have we achieved?

Just a while ago, we witnessed the delivery of dividends from our GOCCs to the government. The amount remitted by our GOCCs to the national treasury this year: P27.28 billion. In addition to that is an additional P12.92 billion in other remittances from our corporations.

In total, in almost six years of our administration, P164.33 billion in dividends has been remitted by our GOCCs. This is almost double the increase compared to what the previous administration achieved, which was P84.18 billion from 2001 to 2010. Let me emphasize: Our predecessors were in power for nine and a half years.

We can see how far we have come in the service of our GOCCs. A clear example of the big change in our corporations is the APO Production Unit. Through reforms such as a computerization system and the development of the skills of its employees, the image of APO as a perpetually losing corporation has been reversed, becoming a profitable office. In fact, its sales reached only P329.14 million in 2010, soaring to P1.21 billion by 2015. Isn't that a big difference, isn't it? This year, APO has remitted P5 million in dividends.

There is also the transformation of the Philippine Reclamation Authority or PRA. If we sum up the dividends given by the PRA from its establishment in 1977 until before we took office in 2010, it was less than P1 billion. That's thirty-three years. Under our administration, from July 2010 to May 2016, the PRA remitted: P5.77 billion or more than five times the increase. This year, the PRA is among the corporations that have remitted P1 billion to the government.

Special mention also to the Government Service Insurance System, Pag-IBIG Fund, Social Security System, and PhilHealth, for further demonstrating excellence this year. Along with the increase in your income, you have also expanded the aid and benefits for Filipinos.

With the help of the contributions of our GOCCs, we are now able to fund more programs and projects under unprogrammed funds, such as, or part of, the AFP modernization; socioeconomic programs, particularly in education and health; irrigation for agriculture; as well as local and national infrastructure such as bridges and ports.

Therefore, on behalf of the Filipino people, I sincerely thank those who comprise the corporations that demonstrated excellence and showed their meaningful contribution to the country. Thank you very much to the employees and personnel of each office, up to their directors and heads managing our corporations. Honestly, you could have chosen to work in the private sector where your lives could have been more peaceful and comfortable. I know that in serving the government, you chose a smaller salary and shouldered heavier challenges to fulfill your duty. Again, thank you very much to all of you.

Now that we are reaping the positive fruits of what we have sown, how do we sustain this? Of course, in any organization, we still need to monitor the fulfillment of our duties and further improve our services. We have already begun the rationalization and reorganization of our GOCCs to ensure its effective service. This April, we have abolished 26 GOCCs due to their losses or overlapping mandates, three were approved for privatization, while one will be merged with another corporation. In addition, the GCG is also implementing the Performance Evaluation System to avoid excessive receipt of benefits by employees, and to make it truly reasonable. Our goal: To receive reasonable incentives in accordance with solid standards.

Of course, we could not allow those of you who have improved government service to be overlooked. That is why this March, we approved the Compensation and Position Classification System (CPCS) for our GOCCs. Its aim is to bring the salaries of those in the GOCC sector closer to what those in the private sector receive. While it may not fully compensate for your sacrifices, I hope you will consider it the state’s recognition of your meaningful contribution to our country. Of course, if your good performance continues, it will be very easy to suggest further aligning your salaries and benefits with those in the private sector. And regarding PEZA, I have already mentioned to Senator Drilon that we review the law in the next Congress, so that your good performance is also seen and truly recognized. They were reportedly not included in the CPCS.

With the help of our GOCCs, we have also proven: Good governance is good economics. With good governance, the economy grows. In the 1st quarter of 2016, we recorded a 6.9 percent GDP growth—the highest economic growth in all of Asia. And this growth has given us greater capacity to serve, not just a few, but the many.

Let us remember: All the successes we have achieved could have been done a long time ago, but they did not happen. Now that we are reaping the positive fruits of the reforms we have sown, the challenge and call to us is simple: Let us not stop and be content with what has been done; I am confident that you will not tire of diligently fulfilling your mandate. We have come a long way; let us ensure that what we have started becomes permanent towards reaching our loftier dreams.

Until the end, what I can say is this: It is a great honor for my administration to lead a great race that will continue to shape a more developed and comfortable future for every Filipino.

Good afternoon. Thank you all very much.
