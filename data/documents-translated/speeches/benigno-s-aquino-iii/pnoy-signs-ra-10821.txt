Speech

of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

at the ceremony for the implementation of the Children’s Emergency Relief and Protection Act (RA 10821)

[Declared at the Rizal Hall, Malacañan Palace, on May 18, 2016]

Until the final moments of our journey along the Straight Path, here we are, and your government, focused on fulfilling a vow: To bequeath a Philippines that is undeniably better than the one we inherited.

This morning, we signed the Children’s Emergency Relief and Protection Act, which began as Senate Bill No. 3034. The purpose of this law is to create a comprehensive and extensive plan to ensure the welfare of Filipino youth affected by calamities, disasters, and other unforeseen events. Because we know: In these times, the youth are the most vulnerable because they really have no defense.

Frankly, all the provisions contained in this proposal, we are already doing as part of our Disaster Risk Reduction and Management strategy. The point is: to formalize the steps and processes; to further empower the various sectors of the government, and to make their respective mandates and responsibilities clearer. Furthermore, we also want this law to be the foundation and standard for the protection of Filipino youth, regardless of who is in office.

Now, the mandate of local and national government to establish child-friendly spaces, evacuation centers, transitional shelters with facilities for the needs of youth, including pregnant women, is further strengthened. This also includes ensuring that their basic needs, such as food and clothing, as well as education and medical services, are met. It will also intensify the monitoring of the security and safety of youth. We will conduct a child-responsive training program for all responders in disaster areas. In this way, focusing on the safety of youth will also be a priority in the emergency response training of our NDRRMC agency members.

All of this points to one thing: peace of mind. What we want, wherever we are—whether it's me, in Times or in Tarlac as a private citizen, or parents who work hard every day for their families—when a disaster, calamity, or unexpected event is approaching, they can rest assured knowing that the government is there, focused on the lives and well-being of their children and the Filipino youth. In times of uncertainty, truly no one will be left behind, especially the youth who are most in need.

Of course, we recognize every individual and organization that worked together to reach this day. To our good colleagues in the House and Senate who authored and worked to pass this bill, particularly Congresswoman Susan Yap and Senator Miriam Defensor-Santiago, and Senator Bam Aquino who are its principal authors. To the government agencies, non-government organizations, and partners in the private sector; to those who make up Save the Children Philippines, led by Mr. Ned Olney, who have been caring for children for decades, not just in the Philippines but around the world. On behalf of every Filipino youth who will be cared for by this law: Thank you very much.

Our term is nearing its end. Only 43 days remain, and we will be stepping down from our positions. Despite this, as you have seen, we continue with our work. We are maximizing every opportunity to serve our Bosses, the Filipino people.

What I can say is that on June 30th, when the clock strikes twelve noon, we can peacefully step down from our positions, look back with our heads held high at the Filipino people, and say face to face: I was true to you. I fulfilled the mandate given to me by my Bosses. We can truly bequeath to the next generation a Philippines that is far better than the one we inherited; and we were able to do this because we worked together, we lifted each other up, and we prioritized our fellow citizens and country before our own self-interest.

Until the very end: It was an honor for a Noynoy Aquino to serve a great race, to my Bosses, to you, the Filipino people.

Thank you very much. Good day to all of you.
