Sponsorship Speech of Honorable Benigno Simeon Aquino III Senator of the Philippines On House Bill No. 3693

[Released on October 7, 2008]

In his presentation of House Bill No. 3693 for consideration of the Body, Senator Aquino delivered the following speech:

The committee on Local Government is honored to present Committee Report No. 106, recommending the approval without amendment on House Bill No. 3693,taking into consideration Senate Bill No. 1986, which grants the City of Malolos its own legislative district effective in the next national election.

Section 1, Article II of the Constitution states that the Philippines is a democratic and republican state, sovereignty resides in the people and all government authority emanates from them.

This measure seeks to ensure that the people of Malolos are duly represented in Congress and their needs are adequately met. As the capital of the province of Bulacan, Malolos is a major driver of growth in this thriving commercial and industrialized hub in Central Luzon. It has likewise served as the backdrop of significant milestone in our country’s political history, particularly the Malolos Convention of 1898 which led to the establishment of the First Philippine Republic by General Emilio Aguinaldo.

For many years, Malolos has been at the forefront in shaping the identity not just of Bulacan but of the entire country as well. The people of the City of Malolos, therefore, deserve their own separate congressional district to more efficiently sustain and add to these worthwhile achievements. Having their own representative in Congress will provide the citizens of Malolos with the voice with which to express their concerns and the resources with which they may preserve the gains they have already achieved as well as adequately provide for their fundamental needs.

Source: Legislative Library, House of Representatives
