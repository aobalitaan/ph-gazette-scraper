Speech

of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

at the advance meeting for the Straight Path Coalition

[Declared at the Quezon Memorial Circle, Quezon City, on May 7, 2016]

Here we are again at the Quezon Memorial Circle, where we also held an advance meeting in 2010. Thank you, and we won then, and now we are here, thank you again because on Monday, we will win again. [Applause]

This is the last day of the campaign. On Monday, we will all go to the precincts to elect new leaders. It seems destined that tomorrow, Mother’s Day will be celebrated all over the world. It is good to remember the teachings of our mothers about good manners. My own mother used to tell me: Don't swear, respect our fellow human beings. [Applause] And because I have four sisters, don't disrespect women. [Applause] I was emphasized to care for and protect them. We were taught to be fully Christian, primarily to follow the commandments of God. This includes, “Thou shalt not kill.” [Applause]

So this is how I began, because frankly, I was also thinking about it. In surveys for the Presidency, Mayor Duterte is said to be leading, which is the opposite of the good manners taught to all of us. He is also the candidate who says he will bring us real change. [Applause]

Let's go back: What will be changed? What kind of change is he referring to? He even said, “I will fix this country.” My question is, is the entire country really broken? We, in 2010, also said this. Our promise: To leave the Philippines much better than we found it.

For example, in education: The backlog we inherited, as you know, was 66,800 classrooms. We were told that the budget could only cover 48,000 throughout our term. I said, “I can’t leave office with a debt.” Now, what we have completed and funded: 185,000 classrooms nationwide. [Applause] We have addressed K to 12, and—thank you very much. I think my mother and father are smiling at what you said just now. [Applause] We have addressed K to 12, as well as old and dilapidated classrooms. The question: What change is Duterte referring to? Will he reduce the number of our classrooms?

Regarding out-of-school children, in 2008, there were 2.94 million nationwide. By 2013, under our administration, this number had already decreased to 1.2 million out-of-school youth. [Applause] This is equivalent to approximately 1.7 million young people who were once on the streets, and are now in school. Let us remember, the population grew in those five years: In terms of percentage, the previous 11.7 percent of youth aged 5 to 15 years old who were not in school, is now only 5.2 percent and we are still looking for them to return to school. [Applause] Is the change he is talking about stopping all the children who have returned to school from studying?

In health: The PhilHealth coverage we have achieved nationwide: 47 million Filipinos. That is the highest and it happened to be during an election year. This means that, if it weren't an election year, it would be lower. Now, 93 million are covered by PhilHealth. [Applause] We are striving to fill the gaps, but you can see the direction: We are moving towards 100 percent. What change does the good Mayor want here? To not make every Filipino a member?

In infrastructure: Governor Adiong of Lanao del Sur told me, as an example, that there were two towns in his province, it used to take him eight hours to travel to them: eight hours to go there, eight hours to return. And if he slept, the 24 hours would be over. Now, we have opened a road there, the former eight hours is now just an hour and a half trip. [Applause] Now, municipalities that were previously neglected can rely more on proper governance. Is the change to make it difficult again for the governor of Lanao del Sur to reach the previously inaccessible areas under his jurisdiction? Will Mayor Duterte remove this road?

For our Armed Forces: The story with us before was, due to lack of equipment, you may have seen, even a purple backpack from a fastfood chain was carried by a soldier in sensitive operations in the forest. Now, you have seen all the new equipment. We now have lead-in jet fighters—two. In 2017, meaning next year, their counterparts will arrive to form a dozen fighters for us. [Applause] Does the change mean reversing the modernization of the Armed Forces that is happening? Does it mean letting the New People’s Army do as they please with us? I know and you know, that is not right.

In our foreign relations: As we all know, China claims almost the entire South China Sea, including the part that is our West Philippine Sea. Before, if we protested in ASEAN, only Vietnam seemed to be our ally, who could not strongly oppose it for fear of damaging their economy. Now, the entire ASEAN supports our position on the rule of law. [Applause] Even Europe and of course, America, as well as Australia, and many other parts of the world, we have allies in our position. Does the change mean that these voices adding to ours will no longer remain allies, because even now, Mayor Duterte is already antagonizing them?

I believe that in their camp, there are truly those who stand for Mayor Duterte. But there are also many opportunists taking advantage to return to power. Let us not forget these few, they have been in politics for a long time. They seem to be acting to manipulate those with good intentions.

Aside from this: I think everyone would agree: The question presented to him regarding his bank accounts that were not declared in his Statement of Assets, Liabilities, and Net Worth is legitimate. [Applause] Let me just remind you: Concealing wealth from the SALN is against the Constitution. If he has nothing to hide, why doesn't he just sign a waiver of bank secrecy, so that the discussion will be over. Instead of directly answering the allegations, he retorted by asking for respect for rights that he is more than willing to deny to others. It wasn't long before he admitted that he did have money, which he supposedly used for “fun.” He said it was just a gift to him, and if that is true, our question is: Is there anyone in their right mind who would give, supposedly, hundreds of millions without anything in return? This is no small amount. What did this donor ask in return from Mr. Duterte? Everyone, I think everyone, has their own—everyone has their own way of thinking. Even those who support him, would doubt if the image that Mr. Duterte presents to us is true. Is Mayor truly without corruption?

In 2010, if you remember, when news broke that the administration we replaced was about to end, our economy improved. The change now is the opposite. As the possibility that he will be the next President supposedly increases, the value of the peso is simultaneously falling. Even our stock market, which exceeded the 8,000 level under our administration, is now reportedly weakening. Even foreign newspapers and ambassadors have protested Mr. Duterte's statements.

Whenever he is questioned or told something he doesn't like, instead of explaining or apologizing, he further insists on cursing or being rude. Doesn't he understand that every word that comes out of his mouth has an effect on the entire Philippines? [Applause] He said, with a curse, that if anyone attempts to impeach him, he will “shut down Congress.” On another occasion, he further stated: “No, no. I am Congress, I am the President.”

I did not invent these words. These came directly from his own mouth. Where will this kind of president lead us? When asked about his plans, he has nothing to say except that he will just copy. He directly said: “I could even copy, I said, the development plan of Poe and Roxas. It wasn’t their expense, it’s the government’s. Who made that? The government. Why would they begrudge it when I’m used to copying?” [Laughter]

When a person gets married, something I hope to experience in the coming days, [applause] a life of solitude happens. Our choice of a leader is like getting married, you will probably be asked if you will ask your father for permission to marry. Your father might ask you, “What will you gain from this suitor? It seems he has many relationships, are you proud of this?” He said, “He will improve your life, but he didn’t mention how.” Blood will flow, wouldn’t your father say, “Child, what are you getting into?” Regarding his statements since he ran, some say, “That’s just hyperbole, just exaggeration.” Mine is, what if his statements are literal and true? Who will stand up next? Which father will give a blessing to this kind of suitor? At the end of the discussion, the answer to the child asking for a blessing will likely be, “Daughter, what you want seems unclear. It’s Mother’s Day tomorrow. How will we explain this to your mother?”

For me, if the question is who should truly be the leader of our country: There is no doubt, it’s Mar and Leni. [Applause]

If you believe in the Straight Path, we can still improve and expand it. Mar and Leni, this is their very promise. [Applause]

They have shown true love for those on the margins of society. They have suffered, sacrificed, and continue to sacrifice for what we believe in. They know: everything valuable must be fought for. They know the importance of what is called consensus building as a pillar of our unity as a nation. Most importantly: they will continue the integrity and true service to our countrymen—not just in words, but especially in deeds.

I believe that the proper upbringing instilled in us by our parents and our religion is important. Here everything became clear; many of our party members, those in the coalition, allies, including those on the margins of society who expressed support for Mar and Leni, helped our countrymen to reflect. In the end, the truly capable, with a plan, with direction, and with integrity to bring the Philippines to the fulfillment of our dreams will prevail. [Applause]

I also believe: We were all raised correctly by our parents to live honorable lives. I believe: Most, if not all, Filipino parents want what is best for their children. We were taught good manners; therefore, children learned and lived with proper and honorable conduct.

Comrades, we have all heard what Mayor Duterte said. Mine is, if you become a dictator, only you are right, only you will be followed, no one can object. Then, like our Martial Law, as long as I don't get noticed and others think I'm unnoticed by the dictator, I am safe.

I am reminded of something my father said, a teaching of Pastor Dietrich Bonhoeffer. He said, and I need to say this in English: “First they came for the communists, but I was not a communist, so I did not speak out. Then they came for the socialists and trade unionists, but I was neither, so I did not speak out. Then they came for the Jews, but I was not a Jew, so I did not speak out. And finally, they came for me, there was no one left to speak out for me.” I just want to emphasize that if one is oppressed and you allow it, you are preparing the situation where you will be the next one to be oppressed. [Applause]

This good pastor wrote this to explain how Hitler came to power and remained in power, which caused immense suffering to so many people. The reason: Others forgot that our fates are all interconnected. May we learn the lesson of history: We are still more numerous. Let us remember: According to surveys, they are flaunting that 30 percent supposedly favor Mr. Duterte; 70 percent do not like him. [Applause] We need to unite; unity holds the key to avoiding darkness and leading our country to a bright future.

On Monday, we will be electing new leaders. On June 30th, at twelve o'clock, you will have a new President. It is an honor to be part of a peaceful transfer of power to the next administration. [Applause] May it be that on that day, we pass it on to someone who will truly lead the Philippines to the fulfillment of our dreams, in a manner that is God-fearing, just, humane, and reflects the spirit of being Filipino.

You know, at this point, I see in your faces, yellow is everywhere. My mother told me something and I remember it vividly, “You are not a candidate, don’t speak too long.” [Laughter]

Just one last plea, as Kiko said earlier, as Ralph said, by Monday, we will all be the same, my vote is the same as yours, there will be only one count. I cannot do this alone. I cannot bear to ensure that the horror does not return to our Motherland alone. I need all of you, I need you to reach out [applause], I need you to reach out to our brothers and sisters who might still be dizzy or lost. We need to give them light. Our future, as always, is in our hands. And I was taught to truly rely on the Filipino people and I am sure, again, that standing with the Filipino people will prove to be right.

Good evening to all of you. Thank you very much.
