Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III President of the Philippines During his visit to Dolores, Samar

[Applause] Here, it's embarrassing not to say a few words. But it needs to be just a few. We will be going to Borongan and there is still work to be done there.

During the height of Ruby, before it arrived, Secretary Mar Roxas was in Borongan and he also stayed here in Dolores. He was the first to report to us the damage that happened to you here.

So, we are here now to address your needs, there are hygienic kits, and the temporary shelter needs are here. Now, I have also met with the representative of the DENR—we have ongoing mangrove planting. Mangroves provide protection, so that when there are strong waves and storm surges, they also mitigate tsunamis. We hope a tsunami doesn't reach us.

Regarding the electricity, Secretary Petilla told us that Dolores has it, the lateral connections to the houses are the next step. The government will help restore the damaged homes, but we are asking—it is important that we return them to a better place.

If you live along the coast, it might not really work every time there's a small storm, it will be immediately damaged. So we need to put them, return them to a place where we are more sure that the residents are safe.

So we are asking for your help, our ambition is what we call “built back better”, right? Not just build, then when the next storm comes, it collapses again, we rebuild, the storm returns, the damage returns. What we are aiming for is to build properly, more safely so that it doesn't always break and fix, break and fix, break and fix. We want to put you in a good place once and for all.

So, of course, during Christmas, we will all pray fervently. Let us thank God that we were not too severely hit by Ruby. Perhaps, we are being given an opportunity to really improve our preparations whenever disasters like this come.

So, I repeat, we are constantly monitoring all members of our cabinet, each with their own tasks that must be done immediately, not just because it's Christmas, but because we need to reduce the suffering and sacrifice experienced by our countrymen. That is what you can expect from all of us.

So, I will not prolong this, although I would like to stay a little longer here, the roads we took were bad and our flight was delayed. We are also being waited for in Borongan now. And after that, there are still things that need to be taken care of. I am trying to catch up with those who will be on vacation for Christmas. I might need to talk to them before they go on vacation.

So, expect that even during Christmas, with the holidays here, your government remains focused on speeding up everything that needs to be done to put you not in the previous state—in a better state, rather.

So, I repeat, good morning to all of you. Thank you very much. [Applause]
