Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, in commemoration of the heroism of SAF 44

Today, we gather to honor our brave peacekeepers, from the Philippine National Police Special Action Force (SAF). Together with the nation, we recognize the wholehearted service of our awardees to the country. To our brave SAF: Thank you very much for your heroism in promoting peace.

Earlier, we witnessed the conferment of the Medal of Valor to two of our SAF troopers. This medal is the highest award in our police force, for those of us who offer extraordinary service to our fellowmen and the flag.

Present was Police Chief Inspector Gednat Garambas Tabdi, the Team Leader of the 84th Special Action Company of the SAF, in the operation where the dreaded terrorist Marwan was killed. He was the helmsman of the team in the operation, serving as navigator and pointman, directing the strategy on how to successfully carry out the mission. Even wounded, tired, and thirsty, he still skillfully led the team to meet the force of the enemy, even though they were outnumbered. This courage of Police Chief Inspector Tabdi served as the strength and inspiration of his comrades to overcome certain danger.

Another recipient of our Medal of Valor is PO2 Romeo Cumanoy Cempron, who belonged to the 55th Special Action Company. Surrounded by approximately 700 enemies, PO2 Cempron fought bravely. Until the very end, he did not surrender. When he ran out of ammunition and had no more weapons, he shielded his body from the bullets, so his comrades could escape. Although hit by many bullets, he was still able to gather his remaining strength to stand up again and serve as a shield for his comrades.

Truly, there are no words that can equal the description of their valor. Their offering of life is unique, not only for their comrades, not only for the PNP, but also for the entire country.

Likewise, we awarded the Medal of Heroism to 47 other SAF troopers, including five who survived the encounter in Mamasapano. Aside from them, 25 other survivors received a special promotion. To our awardees: You are truly the pride of our race; thank you very much for your willingness to prioritize the welfare of the country before yourselves.

We also extend our heartfelt gratitude to the families of our brave SAF 44. Despite the pain of their loss, they left behind memories that serve as a guide and inspiration to the entire national police. To the best of our government's ability, we ensure support for their beneficiaries. From the provision of financial assistance, funding for the education of orphaned children and relatives, housing, to healthcare, livelihood, and employment assistance, we are focused on helping their families move forward in returning to a normal life.

We all know: Despite the awards and aid, the loss of your loved ones can never be compensated. The wound from the tragedy that happened will not heal if, a year has passed, justice for their deaths remains elusive. The question is: Why, until now, have those who should be held accountable not yet been held accountable? We cannot help but compare this to the Boston Marathon bombing in the United States, where the perpetrators were held accountable within just one year.

Like you, I am also impatient with the slow pace of the justice system in our country. As they say: Justice delayed is justice denied. You can rest assured that, along with ensuring that the families of the SAF 44 receive support, we are relentlessly working to achieve justice. Our call to Congress: Review the PNP Law; let us identify the provisions that hinder the swift imposition of penalties on leaders who are remiss in their duties. We do not want tragedies caused only by violations of rules to be repeated. It is not reasonable for the system to continue where an ordinary individual bears heavier obligations due to the negligence of a few.

To our police: I am confident that you will make the heroism of our awardees a model and inspiration. Let us continue what they fought for. Many more challenges will test the resilience of your organization. There are still those who will take advantage of the controversies within your ranks to destroy our unity; those who will use the tragedy for their own personal agenda. I am confident that you will continue to follow the right and just path; continue to defend our Bosses.

You can rest assured: The government is behind you to improve your ability to fulfill your duties. We are strengthening the leadership of our national police not only to suppress criminality, but also to guide every member of the PNP to build a better future for their families. It is clear to the majority: There is now a government that truly reciprocates the service of those who care for the country.

In the coming years, I am confident that more and more will join our agenda of extensive reform. With the help of a national police force that is honest, has integrity, and is dependable, we will truly go far. Let us continue to show concern for one another, contribute, and serve as a monument to honorable service to the people, in the name of advancing the peace fought for by our brave Special Action Force.

Good day to you all. Thank you very much to all of you.
