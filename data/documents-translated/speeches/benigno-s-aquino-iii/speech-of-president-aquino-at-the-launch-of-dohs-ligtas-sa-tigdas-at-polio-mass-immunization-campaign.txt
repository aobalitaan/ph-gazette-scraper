Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, at the launch of the “Safe from Measles and Polio” Campaign for Mass Immunization

If you would like to check your watches, I think this might be one of my shortest speeches since we entered politics. [Laughter]

These days, these hours are for work and not for chatter, so I think I will just emphasize a few points.

An estimated 11 to 13 million people, or our young countrymen, are likely “at risk” from polio, measles, and rubella or German measles.

We have been reminding the Department of Health for a long time. And that is why we are glad that they have truly fulfilled our suggestion that instead of treating the patient, perhaps we should focus on preventing illness and not becoming a patient. Isn't that right? So, these vaccinations are to keep them away from the misfortune that can be caused by polio or even measles, especially its complications. Now, the Department of Health is really prepared and has been given sufficient funds to implement the 95% vaccination rate that we are aiming for.

As shown in the map earlier by Usec. Garin, there are areas that are already fully covered, while there are also areas where the number of vaccinated individuals is low. It even reaches below 50% in some areas. The DOH will do everything it can, but of course, we need to cooperate with all local government units. And most importantly, the parents who care for and guide their children should really take the lead to ensure that all our youth are vaccinated, and truly save them from these diseases that do not need to happen if we work together.

Please, everyone, let us support our “Ligtas sa Tigdas” (Safe from Measles) program. For those who have not yet heard of this program, let us join in reminding them of our obligation to the youth.

That is all from me. Good morning to all of you. Thank you very much.
