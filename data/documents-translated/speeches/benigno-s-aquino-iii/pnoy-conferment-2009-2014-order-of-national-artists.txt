Speech

of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

in the conferment of the 2009 and 2014 Gawad Orden ng Pambansang Alagad ng Sining

[Declared at the Malacañan Palace, Manila City, on April 14, 2016]

This afternoon, we gather to honor our new National Artists. This is the highest and most prestigious recognition for Filipinos who have offered, and continue to offer, meaningful contributions to the arts—those individuals who have demonstrated exceptional skill, not only in their respective fields, but also in shaping our national pride and identity.

This day is also a celebration of the collective success of our race. It is a symbol of Filipino excellence, including the vibrant and rich art of our country.

The frequent question is: What is the value and purpose of art? Why is time devoted to it, and for some, their whole lives? Why does it need to be preserved or enriched?

This has been a long-standing discussion; different perspectives have clashed for a long time: the so-called “art for art’s sake” and “art for man’s sake.” My view is that the value of art cannot be boxed into creation alone. Every painting, every piece of music, every work, contains a story of effort, sacrifice, and love for the field it belongs to, which yields a positive result, not only for oneself, but also for others and society.

I think we all agree that when we say National Artist, this is not a medal simply given to anyone. It means we recognize the National Artists, not only for their skill in performing or creating, but because they also represent the best qualities of our race.

Aside from being the most outstanding painters, writers, musicians, dancers, artists, and others, above all, they are good Filipinos.

Dr. Cirilo Bautista, for his unique contribution to the development of literary art and sharing it with younger generations;

Lazaro Francisco, for his classic literature that promotes nationalism and enriched the National Language;

Federico Aguilar Alcuaz, for his exceptional skill in Visual Arts, whether in painting, sculpture or mixed media;

Francisco Coching, for his comic book works that discuss Filipino culture and social issues;

Dr. Francisco Feliciano, who introduced our indigenous music, not only in Asia, but all over the world;

We also honor Alice Reyes. As a dancer, choreographer, teacher and director, she promoted and developed contemporary dance in the country;

Dr. Ramon Santos, for his unparalleled research and promotion of modern music rooted in traditional Asian music;

Jose Maria Zaragoza, for serving as the foundation of modern architecture in the country, especially in religious structures;

And Manuel Conde, through his films that reflect our customs, history, and social issues, continues to enlighten past and present generations.

To our National Artists: I know that no medal, no title, no colorful words are sufficient for your contributions to your field and to our society. Over the years, your works have become beacons of inspiration; you are models of unwavering dedication, now, then, and for generations to come. Many of you may have become experts abroad, but you still chose to return and share your knowledge with your own country. Even after reaching the peak of your profession, you did not simply settle, but instead, continuously enriched your knowledge and skills. Therefore, I stand before you today with great honor, and sincerely express my gratitude for your great contribution: To all of you, thank you very much.

Indeed: By sharing your works and talents, you serve as a bridge and inspiration for the emergence of many more talented Filipinos. Through your generosity and self-sacrifice, we maintain and further enrich our culture and identity.

It is on this principle as well that our public service is founded. We are focused not on personal interests, but on the welfare of the majority. On every occasion, we pour all our strength into promoting our beloved country.

In embodying the example shown by our honorees, and with the continued contribution of the citizenry, a more progressive and decent future is truly within our reach.

Thank you very much for maintaining the portrayal and remembering of our countrymen, who, what we have grown accustomed to does not necessarily have to be permanent, but we can continue to improve it. Thank you for maintaining the inspiration for all of us.

Good afternoon. Thank you all very much.
