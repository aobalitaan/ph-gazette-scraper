Speech

of

Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

during his meeting with local leaders and communities of Parañaque

[Delivered at Redemptorist Road, Baclaran, Parañaque City, on May 6, 2016]

I mentioned earlier, Dr. Olivares, he was training me in tennis. So I will admit, I came to a time when Parañaque had many salt farms. Why did we talk about salt farms? You know, one of the things we did on the Straight Path—Babes Singson reports to us—you had a problem with flooding. That's why the funds poured in here for the rehabilitation of the Parañaque River, including the drainage system, clearing, slope protection, amounting to approximately P1.26 billion. [Applause]

I will also report to you because I will also be saying goodbye. There are only 55 days left for me in office, then I can already shop here in Baclaran, I can already visit your different places here. I will no longer be rushing when I visit here.

In infrastructure, the budget of Parañaque, from 2005 to 2010, reached P930 million. Now, with your permission, since you elected me as your President: we have delivered P2.18 billion to Parañaque. [Applause]

Earlier, we caught up with those testifying about the 4Ps program. I was surprised, Parañaque, is not that far from Malacañang, from the DSWD. In 2010, imagine, the number of 4Ps members being helped in Parañaque was zero. It was zero in 2010. Now, we are helping 8,059 households. [Applause] This is included in the 4.6 million 4Ps beneficiaries nationwide.

In PhilHealth, the report to us in Parañaque: 609,543 are members, including 93 million nationwide. [Applause]

Here in the National Capital Region, you know, someone promised that if they sit as president, within three to six months, there will be no more crime in the Philippines. Have you heard the one who made that promise? That was a while ago, because their promise was new. They just said it will be reduced. Mar Roxas, who made the promise, already did it. You know, he started the Lambat-Sibat program. What does that mean? Gather data, for example, where are there many snatchers? The police were sent there, installing CCTV cameras and other equipment. Where there is a lot of crime, that's where we assign the police to calm the area.

The “Sibat” part there, includes something called One Time, Big Time. Many wanted individuals. Many are hiding. In one place, you have someone to arrest, you have two warrants of arrest, you only arrested one. The others would realize, “Hey, the police are hot. Let's hide somewhere else.” What One Time, Big Time did, in every place, the search warrant will be served all at once so no one can inform others that the police are hot. The result of that: 2,389 of the most wanted in the National Capital Region have been arrested, are on trial and imprisoned.

What is the effect of that? The most wanted, usually, are the leaders of their group. If the leader is gone, the tentacles are gone, they can no longer move. So that's what Mar Roxas did, he used science, he used modern equipment. The result: This April, 75 percent of the crimes recorded have decreased. It's only 25 percent compared to 2014, when the program started.

Don't worry, I am not a candidate. My mother told me, “If you are not a candidate, don't speak too long.”

Now, what is my purpose with you tonight? For about three weeks now, I have been asked, why in the surveys that are coming out, the frontrunner is the opposite of what we pushed for in the Straight Path? Some of us here have wondered: Why is this opposite frontrunner leading? Think about it, before I became President, I was asked about my health, I tried to answer fully, it went as far as what medicine you take, even my vitamins, I said what brand. Here, this candidate was asked, because they entered the hospital, just at the start of the campaign. “Can we see your medical records?” Their answer was, “Am I crazy? Will I show it to you? Is that right? Or was I the only one watching TV that day?”

They said, “You have so much money. It’s far from what you claim.” “That’s not true! I will show you my passbooks.” They showed it, even choosing which page. Isn't it that with a passbook, we can see all the transactions? It seemed like they showed something that was practically nothing. Another answer I read was, “The accusation is not in your SALN.” In the Statement of Assets, Liabilities, and Net Worth, you need to put everything there: your properties, your debts, and your net worth. He said, “What’s written there is as of this date. Well, by that date, I had already spent everything, so I didn’t need to include it.” But it seems far from his claim that he is simple and has nothing. It will take us [a long time] if we scrutinize everything he says.

Let's simplify things. Thirty years before this year, we had the EDSA Revolution where we ousted the dictator and regained our democracy. Doc, you know that. Edwin, you know that. And here in what I’m seeing, I think many of us know that. Now, he was asked on April 29, the interviewer said on Channel 7, on GMA, the questioner said, “Senator Trillanes said,”—because Senator Trillanes was asked—“What will you do if Mayor Duterte wins?” The answer from Senator Trillanes, reportedly, was, “I will impeach him. I will move to impeach him.” So Mayor Duterte was asked, “What will you do if you are impeached?” Now, some might say I am putting words in his mouth, but I had the exchange recorded to share with you.

First part: On Trillanes saying he will move to impeach him. “If they impeach me, according to Trillanes, I will close Congress. Then no one can impeach me.” Am I the only one who watched TV that day? I don’t think so.

Why does Congress have the power to impeach? In our system, power is divided into three branches of government. I, as President, Chief Executive, implement the laws. The Judiciary, on the other hand, oversees the Legislature and the Executive to ensure we follow the laws. If there is abuse, the Legislature can impeach a member of the Supreme Court, it can impeach the President. This means that power is divided, we are equal, so there is no possibility of abuse. If you remove Congress, who will check and balance? Now, can the President close Congress? The elders will tell us, “Yes, it’s possible. Marcos did that.” Back then. The Constitution was different.

Let's look at the 1987 Constitution, search for it. And I’ll bet with you, you’ll tear up the page, you won’t find any right of a co-equal branch to reduce or close another branch.

I am not a lawyer, but I was a legislator, and now I am the implementer of the law, I need to study. He, Mayor Duterte, is a lawyer, he was even a prosecutor, he was asked again on whether he would violate any law should he choose to abolish Congress once he wins. His answer was, “What do you mean by law? All of…” Then he didn’t clearly finish what he was saying. “All law, erase that when you win. If you lose, you’re finished. Every revolution that succeeds, he becomes the authority. He now becomes the authority. Look at Marcos, but for him, it was Martial Law. That’s different.”

You know, even that part, “Marcos, Martial Law, that’s different,” if we read our current Constitution, if I declare Martial Law today, two days from now, I need to go to Congress, explain why I filed Martial Law. Congress will look at the reason, if Congress doesn’t like my reason, they can lift the Martial Law. Let me add, even any citizen who opposes Martial Law can go to the Supreme Court—it’s in the Constitution—they will ask the Supreme Court if the declaration of Martial Law is correct. And the Supreme Court can say, there is no factual basis, it can be removed.

It is also stated in the Constitution that if you declare Martial Law—unlike during Marcos’ time—there is a limit, two months. Congress can extend it, Congress can shorten it. So if you close Congress, where will we go if we have Martial Law? Let’s just think about that.

There are still questions there: On whether his plan to abolish Congress is a form of dictatorship? The answer was, “No, no. I will be the Congress, I will be the President, you are truly a dictator.” The congressmen would say, ‘Why would you close us down?’” Then he swore. “Impeach, impeach you.” He swore again. “Believe that fool.”

If we are watching TV, we don't like the program, we change the channel. If this one sits [in office], let's change the channel, hopefully six years after 2016. But how about the six years leading up to 2022? Now, I was asked, “Why is he gaining ground?” I said, “It seems people like that style where only one is famous, and they all came together. Our problem is, we divided ourselves.” If thirty percent is for him, where is the 70 percent? The 70, can defeat the 30, if we are united. Is that right?

Let me admit to you, I really studied it, I had a hard time understanding. They say our country is in crisis. I was thinking, what kind of crisis do we have now? Our problem in the West Philippine Sea, when I started, we had no allies. Only Vietnam, a little bit. Now, many countries are helping us, saying that the law—international law—must be followed. We are a small country compared to our opponent. We have no weapons that match theirs. In population alone, they have 1.3 billion, we have 100 million. If they say we might be the ones causing trouble, “Are we going to cause trouble? Even in a fistfight, we have 100 million, they have 1.3 billion, just flicking ears and noses, how can we win?” Is that right?

Of course, we need to find allies, and that’s what I worked on. You are all witnesses. Many people are speaking, supporting our position that the resolution of the problem there should be done peacefully in accordance with international law. That’s why we brought it to arbitration, that’s why we are working on it in ASEAN.

This one who is leading in the surveys now, when there was a joke that many people couldn't accept, he attacked America, with whom we have a defense treaty. He attacked Australia. I even read that he said, “Let’s look at that treaty, maybe it should be removed.” It was so difficult for me to find allies, and he just kept attacking. How can we expect to be allied with those we attack? Or now, if we have no allies, if they challenge us to a nose-flicking contest, how long will we last?

My brothers and sisters, you know, you can’t take this away from me, I was ten years old when Martial Law came into effect. I slept the night before Martial Law, my father was at our house, he left for work. That night, only the bodyguard arrived, saying my father had been arrested.

Seven years, seven months he was imprisoned. Was he given a trial? It took a long time. When it came to the case, they said there was a court. The court was all military. The judge, they called them law members, were military. The president of the military commission was military. The defense lawyer was military. The prosecutor was military. When they finished deciding, of course all of that was in Marcos’ hands, and if he wasn’t pleased, you would be fired, you would resign. Who accused my father of the crimes? Marcos. When did he do it? One year before Martial Law, Doc remembers that.

My father said, “You are the Chief Executive. File a case against me, that’s your job if I committed a crime.” Marcos filed a case against my father, not immediately. He waited a year, when Martial Law was declared. Then, he was brought to a court where he appointed everyone.

After the system that existed then, who would look into whether a decision was right or wrong? Marcos himself. Marcos was the accuser. Marcos appointed the prosecutor. Marcos would look into whether the decision was correct. That’s why the term “lutong Macoy” became popular.

Now, to those who are thinking, we need someone strong with compassion. I ask you, isn't a person created? If you are a created person, of course, you cannot be perfect. If you are not perfect, can you always make a perfect decision? That seems impossible.

For me, on Monday, we will be voting. My question is very simple: We started with almost no budget left for me. If you have no budget, where will you get the funds for all our needs? We worked to have a budget, we saved, so we have something to spend. I ask you: Is it wrong that we expanded the 4Ps, from 780,000, now 4.6 million? [Applause] Is it wrong that 47 percent of PhilHealth, now 93 million? Is it wrong that we were able to build 18,000 kilometers of roads? Is it wrong that we have 107,500 lineal meters of bridges? Is it wrong that we are now called “Asia’s Rising Star” instead of the former “Asia’s Sick Man”?

When I was a congressman, the country was dividing – I’m not that old – 8,000 classrooms. If you delivered eight, ten classrooms to your district, well, you worked hard. If you delivered twelve classrooms, you were already great. Now, it will be completed by next year, the delay was only because of the National Capital Region, urban centers, the land is expensive. It’s no longer possible to build one-story classrooms, they were made three, four, five stories. What was done, is being done, will be completed by next year, 185,000 classrooms nationwide.

The one I replaced said we no longer had a classroom shortage, what they did – you here in Parañaque experienced it – shifting. There was a morning session, an afternoon session. If you were unlucky, your elementary school child had an evening session. A tired child, will they learn properly? Is that how it should be?

We, when we say we have completed something, it’s true. 185,000 classrooms, is that wrong? Importantly, where are we going? What was Paolo saying earlier about college? When we started the 4Ps, it was only up to grade school – that was all we could afford. We expanded it to grade school. Now, a study has come out, if a child finishes high school, their additional income is 40 percent. So let’s let them finish high school. Now, if they finish college, of course, they will earn even more. We didn’t say we would give you a job, then just go to St. Jude, “Hopefully I’ll get a job.” No. That was worked for.

The economy is growing. The average, 6.2 percent GDP is the largest during our term. There are new jobs. Here in front of us, we see Entertainment City. The largest one is finished, it needs workers there. Would a worker who didn’t finish grade school, who doesn’t know how to add and subtract, lacking in Mathematics, be accepted there? But we didn’t just go to Mathematics.

Let me tell you two stories: We have a fellow countryman from Pangasinan. He was invited to work in the Middle East. He went to the Middle East, then he was supposed to do accounting. Upon arriving in the Middle East, the contract was changed, he was made a domestic helper, he was maltreated. After being maltreated, he escaped. He was helped by the embassy, sent home to the Philippines, given a fare to return to Pangasinan. He was told, “There are programs for OFWs. Would you like to train?”

She told us that she was very confused. It's natural to go there, she probably incurred debt. She went to the Middle East to improve her life. Instead of improving, her situation worsened. She didn't have any debt before, now she has debt and her contract wasn't finished, how will she pay it? She told us that she was thinking of taking her own life. She now remembers that the government will provide training. She took a course at TESDA, wellness hilot and massage therapy. She was hired at a spa, a good employee, and became the spa's operations manager. After that, she established her own spas. She now owns four spas, and has three more franchises with us in Tarlac.

Let me add this: There's a woman in Pampanga who gave a testimony similar to the one earlier. She said, “I am a mother of seven children. My husband left me. My job is selling – selling.” You might understand Tagalog better than I do, I am Kapampangan. Isn't 'patinda-tinda' not permanent? Sometimes she has something to sell, sometimes she doesn't. When she has nothing to sell, she doesn't earn anything. When she has nothing to sell, there's even less hope of earning anything.

She said, “I am a member of 4Ps. Because of 4Ps, I was able to finish the studies of three of my children, they now have permanent jobs. They are regular employees. They are helping me with my four other children who are still in school.” Let's imagine, for example, that this mother approached us and said, “Please help me. I have seven children, I can't feed them.” Imagine, like me, a single person, who has a little savings, I could take some money out of my pocket to help one of them study. But here, we were able to educate, graduate three of them. And likely, those four are the new ones there.

Now, I wasn't approached to take money out of my pocket, none of us were approached by the 4Ps beneficiaries to help them. But now, we are helping 4.6 million households, and the result: 7.7 million people have been lifted out of poverty. [Applause] Let's go back: Is that wrong?

The point is: The grade school student we helped might be in high school now. We expanded the program in 2014 to also help those in high school. Now, let's continue to grow our economy, let's make Mar President, and we can help until college. Those who used to have no jobs, we gave them the ability to have the opportunity to improve their lives. I didn't stand before you in 2010 and say, “Elect me, and everyone's life will be good.” I told you then, “I guarantee, I will exhaust all opportunities for you to improve your lives.” Because my view of you is that you are my partners. You are not different from me, I will not order you around. You are my Boss, you are the ones who give me orders.

Now, let me go back. I was asked, “Why should the one who dictates to us be dominant?” That's right, it's like we're dividing ourselves. Do we want only one person to always get their way? Do we want to have no rights if we don't say “Bow. You're right”? Have we not learned from the horrors of Martial Law and are we going to give someone the opportunity to repeat it?

But what's even heavier is that Martial Law can no longer be done that way because of the Constitution. They might declare a State of Emergency, which has no guidelines in the Constitution. They can invent all sorts of hardships for us again. Can that happen? If we allow it. Where does power come from? From you. How do we show that? Through elections.

On Monday, we will vote. From now until Monday when we enter the precinct, I hope that before we make a mark on anything, we think about it. Before we even enter, I have carefully considered who is deserving. I want to be able to tell the next generation that I did not fail them. That I really thought about who would be best for them. That I really participated in improving our society. That's what I promised you, right? Give me a chance, what I will leave you with will be far better than what I found. And you will be the ones to say whether I fulfilled that or not.

If my promises come true and you see the good things happening in our country, and you see that it can become even better, please help me. My vote is the same as yours. There’s only one count for each. You will decide where we will go. [Applause]

Let me go back to Edwin, he is good. That’s for sure. The only problem is, Edwin might not be supported by those above. What will happen to Edwin? What will happen to you? How can it be right that only one person is right, only one person is good, only one is the son of God?

I really thought about it, that on the anniversary of our independence, that’s when we will face this kind of danger. I don’t want you to say that I failed you, that’s why I’m here now. I’m explaining the situation to you. But just like EDSA when we gathered and said “Enough,” just like in 2010 when we said “We will change this society,” now in 2016, we have the opportunity to make that change permanent. It’s in your hands. [Applause] And truly, I have nothing to boast about. Throughout all the challenges I faced, almost six years, how did we overcome them? I am confident that you are behind me.

Let us remain united because right now, our future is truly in danger. There is a danger that all that we have achieved will be lost if we make a U-turn and go back to the way things were. No one will benefit from that except a few. There will be oppressors again.

Good evening. Thank you all very much.
