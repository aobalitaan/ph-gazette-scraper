Speech of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

in “Straight Path: 2bContinued”

[Delivered at the Club Filipino, San Juan City, on October 5, 2015]

When we began to tread the Straight Path, we told our Bosses: You can dream again. We laid out not only our aspirations, but also the concrete steps we would take to achieve them.

After more than five years of walking this path, our Bosses themselves are saying: We not only fulfilled our words; we even achieved things that we once thought impossible. In our current tours of the provinces, we are met with testimonies from our countrymen whose lives have been changed by the programs and initiatives we have implemented.

When we visited Apayao recently, this is what Leslie Columbano, a Grade 6 student at San Isidro Norte Elementary School, shared with us: She said that before, it was discouraging to go to their school. How could it not be, they were crammed into the classroom; their roof and walls were full of holes and patched up, so they always got wet when it rained. They even had to use buckets to catch the dripping water; and her male classmates had to dig trenches to allow the water to flow.

Now, they have new classrooms. She said it's very different from the previous situation where they felt forgotten by the government. I would also add: Because of the new roads we have built in remote areas, many students no longer have difficulty going to their school. [Applause] When they arrive at school, they have new chairs and textbooks, and they don't have to endure hunger because we have provided them with assistance through our Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program. [Applause]

Rogelio Calmang, a barangay councilor in Brgy. Butao in Calanasan, Apayao, said: He said that before, because there were no proper roads, when someone in their area got sick, they would just carry the patient to the hospital. He said their journey would take two days, so often, the patient would die in the forest. Now that they have a road, their travel is easier, so there is a greater chance of saving the lives of their fellow citizens. [Applause]

As Gov. Bulut said, in the old days, they would compete for ambulances. And I looked at him. Why would they compete for ambulances? Because in the old days, the ambulance carried their hope of getting treatment to other provinces. Now, their province and their hospital are the ones being used as referrals by neighboring provinces.

Secretary Babes Singson of the DPWH explained to us that we have already reached the physical limitations in constructing new projects in Apayao. With the number of projects we are undertaking, we are reportedly running out of good contractors. Even carpenters, plumbers, and electricians are also lacking in various projects. The roads that have been opened need to settle and compact before being topped with concrete or asphalt. I just told him, “I think that’s a better problem to have – that we can’t add anything more at the moment – than doing nothing at all.”

The story of transformation in Apayao is also the story of every city, municipality, and province in the Philippines. Those of you here know the roads, hospitals, bridges, schools, and other structures that are products of the Straight Path in your area. You know the strong political will we demonstrated to pass laws such as Responsible Parenthood, Sin Tax, and K to 12. You know how we expanded the coverage of PhilHealth and Pantawid Pamilya; how we held and are holding corrupt individuals accountable; how we listen to the voices of our countrymen in funding projects and programs they need; how we closed the backlogs in classrooms, textbooks, and school seats; how we rose from being the “Sick Man of Asia” to “Asia’s Brightest Spot.” [Applause]

As I reflect on all our achievements—let me reiterate, not Noynoy’s, but ours—I cannot help but remember the people who sacrificed to get us to this point. When a dictator was the enemy of the Filipinos, my father and Tito Gerry Roxas worked together in the fight for freedom and human rights. [Applause] My mother, a simple housewife, responded to the call of the people to restore democracy. Of course, there was also Jesse Robredo, who courageously faced criticisms and powerful interests to promote the welfare of his countrymen. [Applause]

Earlier, Mar also spoke about sacrifice. Mar, you really don’t like to boast about your own accomplishments. Let me mention that in 2009, Mar also made a great sacrifice to keep the coalition intact and ensure that almost a decade of abuse of our countrymen would end. [Applause] At that time, he thought and worked for a long time to become president. But when we talked, he immediately told me and Butch Abad: He saw the people’s desire, and he would no longer run for president. There were no negotiations, no long debates, he didn’t even ask to be made vice president. He truly doesn’t prioritize personal ambition. [Applause] Wherever it benefits our countrymen, that’s where Mar Roxas will be. [Applause]

Leni, I can't help but compare her to my mother. They both suddenly lost their husbands. They were both initially considered just housewives, then called upon to lead. And just like my mother, Leni had no ambition to run. But her colleagues asked her to make a sacrifice to unite our leadership in Camarines Sur. She was obliged to run and truly served honorably. [Applause]

I have said this before and I admit it again: One of the heaviest responsibilities I had as president was facing Leni and her children when we were searching for Jesse. More than 24 hours had passed since the plane crashed, so we can say there was “less grounds for optimism.” It was truly admirable the resilience shown by Leni and her children and family during that time.

After years of effort to return their lives to normal, which became even more difficult because she needed to serve, an even greater sacrifice is now being asked of them. They know the challenges they will face. They know how complex politics is, and how difficult the things are that need to be done to succeed in the campaign. But they chose to do what is right. They proved once again that they would rather struggle than miss the opportunity to make a change that [applause] will affect the lives of many Filipinos. Thank you, Leni, and to your children. [Applause] I and the party will do everything to always make you feel that you are not alone.

Country before self: That is the mark of the Liberal Party. [Applause] All of us here today are standing on the path laid out by those who came before us, so that we may be given a better start than they had. It is not an exaggeration to say that we have not disappointed the people who sacrificed for us. We are making and continue to make the necessary sacrifices to ensure that those who come after us also have a better beginning.

Isn't it true that when we started, we only had 6.5 percent of the budget remaining? We couldn't even use all of it because it was already committed to various necessary payments. As a Kapampangan, it's a great insult to be told you're “stubborn.” [Laughter] But we exhausted every possible way to save. That's why even when we travel abroad, we really and truly economize. For example, during our State Visit to Indonesia—one of our first trips—we endured a commercial flight that only departed late at night. Regional flights, you see, finish their domestic flights first, so our flight was quarter to twelve. [Laughter] If I'm not mistaken, it was cheaper than renting. What happened was, we sat like cadets because of the thinness of the seat space. Our shoulders were perfectly straight on that trip. [Laughter] When I was looking for the seat adjustment, I couldn't find it. My companion, Cesar Purisima, told me, “Sir, we were given consideration. They’ve maximized the angle of your seat.” “So this is already maximized. We’re even worse off than those at PMA,” I said. [Laughter] But we just said, what is that compared to the suffering of our countrymen if we can't bring new opportunities?

In my cabinet, isn't the question always what other sacrifices can be made? How many times have we just relied on the understanding of friends and family we haven't seen because there's a need to address? There's no discussion about what's more important. The welfare of the people must come first. We've also gotten used to negative commentators at breakfast, insults at lunch, insults at dinner, even intrigue as a midnight snack. [Laughter and applause] Sometimes I think, we're like Manny Pacquiao who has to fight 15 rounds every day. I also wonder why I don't take advantage of the two-week vacation that is the right of any worker. What's happening now is, I can really only rest on Good Friday because no one travels anywhere in the Philippines. Every time there's a holiday, we have to protect the safety of our Bosses. Even during Christmas and New Year, we have to take care of the victims of firecrackers who don't listen. When there's a storm and most people are off, we have to watch over our countrymen who will be affected. Sometimes, others don't even know what we've been through because the problem has already been solved, like when there was a threat of the spread of the MERS Corona Virus here. Of course, we don't just need to think about Filipinos within the country. Didn't we reach a point where whenever something happens in another country, our first question is: Were any Filipinos affected there? Now, when there's tension in Libya, there's not only a declaration of Alert Level 4—which means mandatory repatriation—we also decided to write to the families of those there to convince their relatives to come home. We weren't listened to, there was a kidnapping, and we faced a new challenge on that front.

But if someone asks me: If you knew things would be this difficult, would you still choose to lead? My answer would be: A resounding “Yes.” [Applause] And I know you will join me. No matter how intense the trials, no matter how harsh the criticism, no matter how many days we have to stay up late. Because we know what we can do when we work together. We know how many we can help when we are on the right path. Let me just emphasize: Everything we've accomplished could have been done before if leaders hadn't forgotten who they were serving. [Applause] As our term is coming to an end, we can say: We promised that the people are our Boss. We did not exploit the mandate you gave us. We exhausted every opportunity to provide service and benefits. That's what we mean when we say the Liberal Party is honorable. [Applause]

At this point, I remember the story of Juan Tamad. He's waiting on a bamboo bed while waiting for guavas to fall from the tree with his mouth open. Isn't it true that if that happens, if the guava just falls into his mouth, it's likely to be overripe or rotten? Therefore, this isn't just a story about laziness, it's also about foolishness. [Laughter] The lesson is clear: If something is important to us, we must work for it. The continuation of what we started will not happen if we don't do anything.

Now that our Bosses are about to choose the next leaders, the challenge for us is to ensure that what we have started continues and even expands. Someone suggested the other day that, to prevent the transformation from being interrupted, I should run again. I have already answered why that is not possible. I will add this: The future cannot depend on just one person, on just one group. We all have limitations. We have been given time in this world, so we need to prepare those who will come after us. Because if no one continues what we have started, we might return to our horrific past.

Now, we have Mar, and we still have Leni [applause] who will surely continue the Straight Path, I am confident that they will further accelerate our progress. It may be difficult to think about, but I assure you that with the strength of my belief in these two and in our coalition, I will be far more zealous in this campaign than in my own campaign in 2010. [Applause] Gov. Marañon can deal with me if I only eat memorized speeches.

Let us not forget: It is in the game plan of the enemies of the Straight Path to diminish our achievements. Others will make statements to claim credit for what we have worked hard for. Even those who criticize the traffic, once they inaugurate the Skyway Stage 3 and other projects we are working on, just imagine if they get the chance to inaugurate it there, they are probably preparing, even now, their marker saying that they built it [laughter], and that they are the key to solving traffic, and they are also the most shameless. [Applause and laughter] Jim Paredes reminded me: Throw stones at the sky, don't get angry if you get hit.

My request to each of you here: If there is anything lacking in the party's funds, as mentioned during the meeting in NECO, let us match it with hard work. Let us not remain silent in the face of criticism; let us not tire of sharing the dreams we have achieved and are reaching. The sacrifices of those who came before us are at stake in this election. The future of one hundred million Filipinos is at stake here.

It isn't difficult what we need to do. According to past surveys, our countrymen feel and see how far we have come on the Straight Path. The testimony of our Bosses will be like a flood that will overwhelm our opponents. As the election approaches, it becomes clearer to our countrymen who truly has the ability and integrity to continue and expand what we have started. [Applause] While others have to promise the moon and the stars, we hold the concrete proof of what we have accomplished. If someone wants to take credit for our achievements, we just need to ask: What sacrifices did you make for the Straight Path? [Applause] If they say one thing and do another, our Bosses will see their true colors.

Now, we have introduced to the nation the candidates of the Straight Path. On Friday, when we present our senatorial slate to our Bosses, it will become even clearer: While others are struggling to piece together a coalition, our force is united. [Applause]

I can already see the scene at the turnover we will do after our successful campaign. [Applause] I have a very big smile, as do those who will greet me. [Laughter] Everyone I look at is also smiling, because our journey on the Straight Path has been meaningful and historic. And on that day, each one will hold the assurance that our progress will continue and accelerate. Truly: With the guidance of God, with our continued unity, and with the strength given by our Bosses, we will surely reach even higher dreams.

In closing, there's a blogger who felt sorry for me. He said the temperature in Metro Manila when he mentioned it was 40 degrees centigrade. It was so hot, and then there was my picture. I asked: Is this my fault? [Laughter] During that time, Manny Pacquiao unfortunately lost. There was another picture of me, made into a “meme.” It said when Pacquiao lost: Is this also my fault? [Laughter] I just objected when the picture of Butch Abad was released, and it said: “Butch Abad’s hair never grew back.” Butch Abad said that only a small part of the problem was why his hair didn’t grow back; only a small part was my fault. Just a reminder to our opponents who are very used to dirty politics, maybe now they want to harass our candidate Leni, and make things difficult for her children. When Jesse passed away, these children had many foster fathers. It’s getting personal if that’s where you’re headed. [Applause]

To my colleagues, we are really very enthusiastic about the upcoming election. As I told the candidates, while the campaign period hasn't started yet, eat now. [Laughter] When the campaign period arrives, I will share memorized talking points with you. Just remember the meals we had. But when we see our people supporting us, we can be sure that no matter what challenges we face, we will overcome them.

Good day. Thank you very much to all of you.
