Speech

of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

at the inauguration of the Balog-Balog Multipurpose Project Phase II

[Declared in Brgy. Sula, San Jose, Tarlac, on May 26, 2016]

Today, we mark the beginning of another project that will bring benefits to our Bosses—the Balog-Balog Multi-Purpose Project Phase 2. This is another proof of a government that truly focuses on its mandate: to serve and care for the people.

They told me that it is possible to have five harvests every two years as long as there is sufficient irrigation. As you know, we were first hit by an earthquake in 1990, which destroyed our irrigation. What remained was exhausted by Pinatubo in 1991. Here in Tarlac, we are very aware that during the summer, the Tarlac River is like a desert. And when it rains, the river rages, damaging our roads, houses, and other structures. In this situation, how can we have abundant production?

Therefore, a project like the Balog-Balog project is very important. Our goal is: to properly collect water during the rainy season, and to release it for irrigation during the dry season. This will address not only the needs of our farmers, but also the preservation of our infrastructure. You know, that is the large-scale ambition here in the Philippines, we are visited by twenty typhoons every year. So much water falls on our beloved country; if we can collect it, we can use it more correctly, and the people will truly benefit greatly.

This project was actually started during my mother's time. More than a generation has passed, and it has been started and stopped several times. I apologize if it took a long time before we reached this point. Given the large amount of funding needed for this, it is important to ensure that the quality passes, whoever examines and questions it. You can count on it: In every program and project, we ensure that it is carefully studied and meticulously funded for the benefit of the majority. We address the needs not only of the present, but also of future generations.

Currently, the construction of the North Main Canal and San Miguel Main Canal of the Balog-Balog project is ongoing. The construction of the diversion tunnel and the repair and upgrading of the Tarlac Diversion Dam have been bid out and the contract is targeted to be awarded this June. Procurement for the construction of the 105.5-meter high dam on the Upper Bulsa River will also begin in the third quarter of the year. The entire project is targeted to be completed in 2018.

Once completed, irrigation will be provided to a total of 34,410 hectares of land in Tarlac City and nine municipalities in Tarlac. The beneficiaries will include: at least 23,000 farmers directly. It is expected to increase rice production per year from 126,480 metric tons to 350,980 metric tons. That's more than double the increase.

The benefits brought by the project do not end there. Through the Balog-Balog project, flooding will be reduced, and the inconveniences caused by it. In addition, it will also help with inland fish production in the reservoir area. It also has the potential to generate 43.5 megawatts of electricity using hydropower, which will be realized with the help of a Public-Private Partnership. The good news is, it is expected to create 73,000 jobs during the construction phase, and another 30,000 jobs once our dam is operational.

Of course, this opportunity would not be possible without the trust and support of my Bosses, my beloved Tarlaqueños. So to all of you, thank you very much.

This project is just part of our extensive efforts to improve the livelihood of our Bosses. Here in Tarlac, to support agriculture and other industries, we have allocated a total of P11.3 billion for farm-to-market roads and irrigation systems.

With our pouring of funds into irrigation, from 2011 to 2014, we have already irrigated a total of 164,230 hectares of new land in the country. This has surpassed the 144,016 hectares of new land irrigated from 2001 to 2010. With its help, we have increased the average annual palay production by almost 21 percent—from 14.92 million metric tons from 2001 to 2010, to 18.05 million metric tons from 2011 to 2015.

Truly: We have come so far. Before, when there was a problem, the common refrain of those in power was: “There are no funds,” and “You’re on your own.” Now, the time of avoiding obligations is over. From infrastructure, health, education, to livelihood programs, we are delivering the services long awaited by our fellow Filipinos. What’s even better: We are able to deliver benefits to various sectors without adding to the burden of our Bosses, except for the Sin Tax.

Now, we are reaping the fruits of our reforms. With the growth of our economy brought about by good governance, we are given greater capacity to open opportunities and empower our countrymen. The challenge to everyone: Let us maximize every opportunity; let us continue to contribute so that our good beginnings become permanent and flourish.

There are only 35 days left before I step down from office. In every moment of my administration, I have been true to all of you, and I will continue to be true to the mandate you have given me. As I look back on the past six years of our administration, I can proudly recall our accomplishments, wholeheartedly face my Bosses, and say: I did everything I could to shape a future that is far better than what we inherited.

It is truly a great honor to serve the people and our beloved Philippines.

You know, in closing, I accompanied the father of our good Governor, the late Congressman Jose Yap. The first time I went here in San Jose, we rode a brand new jeep—I even remember it was a Sarao. When we left here in San Jose, because your roads back then were made of stone and many people were riding horses, the brand new Sarao started rattling. I think it was 1984.

It was good that when our mother took office, you were also, I suppose, a witness to the start of the construction of roads here and you were not neglected. It was also during her time that a foreign government, I won't mention which government because they are still helping us, offered the Balog-Balog project. We accepted it, the government there changed, and the offer was withdrawn. President Estrada arrived, and I was thinking about how to say, “Could you perhaps help us with Balog-Balog?” I was already your Congressman during those times. Before I could mention it to President Estrada, President Estrada preempted me, “That Balog-Balog, you will continue it.” So of course, all I could say was “thank you.”

President Estrada only served for two years. Mrs. Arroyo, whom I replaced, told me when we were still on good terms, “What does Tarlac need?” So I said, “Ma'am, Balog-Balog is very important to us. Perhaps you could have it studied because it is expensive.” She replied to me, “Consider it done.” But nothing was “done,” so when I had the responsibility and the opportunity, I thoroughly reviewed this proposed project because, as I said in my speech earlier, it is important that when the time comes—of course, in government, there is always competition for resources, we might be forgotten again—we made sure that all the processes we followed were correct. The investigation and study of this project were thorough, so that anyone who questions it will see that the project is reasonable and just, and will serve as a bridge to really continue it.

I would really like to apologize. We did not rush this, we did not shortcut the processes because we wanted it to continue and truly be beneficial to all our countrymen. Of course, we dream of the point where we will have five harvests every two years. We also dream of the opportunity where we will no longer be anxious during the rainy season, when the river really rages and so many lives are harmed.

Let me repeat: I believe I will no longer be able to return here as your President. I will be grateful for your support of me from the time I was a congressman until my presidency. And I hope that when we look back, we can say that we achieved a lot through our cooperation.

Good afternoon. Thank you all very much.
