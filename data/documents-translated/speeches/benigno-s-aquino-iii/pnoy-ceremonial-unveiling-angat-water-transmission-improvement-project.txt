Speech

of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

at the inauguration ceremony of the Angat Water Transmission Improvement Project

[Declared in Norzagaray, Bulacan, on May 26, 2016]

Until the last moments of our term, here we are, the flow of service continues for our Bosses, the Filipino people. This is proven by our gathering this afternoon for the unveiling of the Angat Water Transmission Improvement Project.

For almost six years on the Straight Path, you already know our style: We study problems thoroughly, so that we can identify the right solutions for them.

What problem does this project address? We conducted an assessment of the Umiray-Angat-Ipo system, particularly Tunnels 1 to 3, and Aqueducts 3 to 5. What we found: The tunnels are fragile due to age, so they need to be reinforced. The aqueducts, on the other hand, have leaks. The leakage is estimated at 2.2 cubic meters per second, or about 5 percent of the total water flow. A lot of water is being wasted here, which should be fully received by our Bosses for service. Our solution: We will build a new tunnel to repair the facilities; and the good thing is, we will do this in a way that will not disrupt the water supply to Metro Manila and its neighboring areas.

The scope of the project includes the construction of a 6.4 kilometer tunnel from Ipo Dam to Bigte Basin, or what is called Tunnel 4. This will allow for the alternating closure of Tunnels 1 to 3 and Aqueducts 3 to 5, while they are being inspected and repaired. Once completed, Tunnel 4 will have the capacity to deliver 1,600 million liters of water per day. Overall, this project will ensure a sufficient, clean, and reliable water supply from Angat Dam for an estimated 14 million residents of Metro Manila, and other fellow citizens in parts of Rizal, Bulacan, and Cavite.

On May 29, 2014, this project was approved by the NEDA Board, which is funded by the Asian Development Bank in the amount of P3.3 billion. This month, the detailed design and other pre-construction activities for its construction will begin, and the project is expected to be completed in September 2020. All of this is just part of what we call the Water Security Legacy Program, to ensure widespread, efficient, and long-term irrigation services in our country. Following the Angat Water Utilization and Aqueduct Improvement Project Phase 2, where a new aqueduct was built and leaking water in an aqueduct was patched. We also implemented the Angat Dam and Dike Strengthening Project, to reinforce the structure and make it safe from earthquakes and floods. For the Bulacan Bulk Water Supply Project, we have built water treatment plants, pumping stations, and a water reservoir to directly supply water to your beloved province. The Kaliwa Dam Project, on the other hand, will serve as an additional source of water for Metro Manila.

You can see, our projects are interconnected and anchored in one spirit: Water is the source of life and it is important to address this need of our Bosses.

We are grateful to everyone who helped us reach this day. Of course, to those who comprise the MWSS, led by Administrator Gerry Esquivel, [applause] not only for this project, but more importantly, for their hard work to establish a new face for their office. This project, meanwhile, is part of our extensive effort to further accelerate progress in Bulacan, including the National Capital Region.

There is also our pouring of funds into infrastructure: For Bulacan, from 2005 to 2010, under our predecessor, P5 billion was allocated. Under us, from 2011 to 2016, this rose to P19.04 billion—more than a triple increase. [Applause]

For agriculture, from 2011 to 2016, we allocated P2.1 billion for farm-to-market roads and irrigation.

In addition, we are also focusing on social services: In Pantawid Pamilya, the number of beneficiary households we found here in 2010 was only 5,542. Now, 68,952 households are being helped by the program.

In terms of health, under the Health Facilities Enhancement Program: We are allocating P141.83 million to improve your barangay health stations, rural health units, urban health centers, and hospitals. As for PhilHealth, 2.47 million individuals in Bulacan are already covered.

As you can see, under the Straight and Narrow Path, we have never forgotten Bulacan. [Applause]

There are only 35 days left before we step down from office. As time passes, I will never forget the successes like this one, where Filipinos truly demonstrate bayanihan (community spirit) and concern for one another, in order to achieve our collective aspirations.

In the almost six years that we have worked together on the Straight and Narrow Path, we have shown the world what united and cooperative Filipinos can achieve. Until the very end, it is a great honor for me to serve our Motherland and our great race.

Thank you very much and good morning to all of you.
