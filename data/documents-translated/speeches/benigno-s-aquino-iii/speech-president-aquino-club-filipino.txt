Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, at the gathering in Kalayaan Hall, Club Filipino.

You’re all very energized, aren’t you? [Laughter and applause] Earlier, I was mentioning our choir to Mar. If you saw the children, they looked very professional. And the song they chose, especially the prayer, is close to my heart, like being generous. I sing that when I’m really annoyed with whoever I’m talking to. It really calms me down. [Laughter] Of course, this atmosphere has added to that. It’s nice to see many of you whom I haven’t seen in a long time, or rarely see. Of course, the young ones like Karina David; [laughter] younger ones like Pidi Barzaga, over there; [laughter] and those who are young like Anton; and really many of you who were with us especially during the darkest times of the journey.

Let's go back to this hall which has truly witnessed so much that we've gone through. During the time when Joker Arroyo said we could fit in a Volkswagen Beetle, until now we still fit in Volkswagen Beetles. [Laughter and applause] Because we are understanding of others, if you want to join us, we will find a place for him in the Volkswagen Beetle. [Laughter]

We are here at Kalayaan Hall of Club Filipino, a private place that has become part of so many important events in our history. Here, my mother took her oath, to begin our achievement of the promise of EDSA. Those of us who have been with us for a long time on the Straight Path, you will also remember that here I first said: The Filipino can dream again. Now, we are here with the Liberal Party, as well as other groups such as KOMPRE, People Power Volunteers for Reform, [applause] Change Politics Movement, Urban Poor Alliance; party-lists such as Akbayan [applause], YACAP, Angkla–let's slow down this part, someone might get angry that they weren't mentioned–CoopNatco, Ateacher, ABS –that’s the party-list ah–and Append, as well as friends who are members of NUP and NPC who are also here today. [Applause] Even one of our idols, Mayor Hagedorn, is here, who has long been with the Straight Path. [Applause]

I mentioned a lot of names. You know, during the SONA some people said why they weren't mentioned. [Laughter] I can't mention everyone because this speech is a bit long and we need to listen to Mar. [Laughter] So, I apologize again to those who weren't mentioned; but even if I didn't mention you now, you are in my memory. [Laughter] Those I did mention, I will keep in my memory a little longer.

We are gathering to discuss the next chapter in our journey along the Straight Path. On Monday, I reported to you: We have really come a long way. Of course, some criticize this, as if the point is why we can't move on. My point is: We measure how far the country has traveled from point A to point B. Isn't it natural to lay out the whole truth of point A, which is where we started?

You have seen what we have achieved: We have leveled the playing field; we are holding corrupt officials accountable; we have restored the world's confidence, and our trust in ourselves and in each other. I tried to fit our achievements into a few hours, but there are just so many. Please allow me to share some more testimonies now. You know, the videos that were hastily prepared reached 46. We used 15 on the day of the SONA. But there are some that were missed which I think are very good to share with you too. Let's watch these videos:

I just want to emphasize: These are just a few examples; this is just the beginning, and we can go so much further. As I said, according to NEDA's research: Within a generation, we can reach first world status. Is there anyone here in this hall who said or thought that we could achieve, or reach first world status?

I am truly happy with the strength shown by our Bosses to fight for these achievements. At the same time, I cannot help but think: Isn't this itself—the right service that expands the opportunity to progress—what our Bosses deserve? Why did it reach a point where it still needs to be fought for? Our conclusion: All important things, if you don't fight for them, if you don't take care of them, can be lost.

Bosses, in the remaining time of my term, I have two very important obligations to you: First, I need to ensure that the 2016 elections are clean, peaceful, and remain truthful and credible. The second obligation will be clarified if I explain it using some stories.

I have already told you, some have suggested, that to ensure that our hard work is not wasted, I should stay in office longer. I explained to all of you during the SONA why I oppose this: We do not want to open the opportunity for someone to rule forever for personal interest. [Applause] This is not a theory, hasn't this happened in our history? Some also said: I should not endorse a candidate, and just be “friend to all.” [Laughter] Earlier, when Mr. Marañan said he would ask me for more, we talked about the mangoes of Guimaras, and only then would I try the mangoes of Guimaras. [Laughter] Let's continue then. That's why we are here now. I think, being “friend to all,” is probably not right either. That's personal interest, instead of the interest of the country. [Applause]

The question became: If you don't want to, and if you yourself won't continue, who should succeed you? They insisted on us: It is my obligation to find a worthy successor and continue what we started. It is my obligation to ensure that everything that has been started is not wasted. But of course, when many people told me this, in my mind at the time, I only had one vote, like every Filipino. It might be better to say: We all have an obligation here. [Applause]

There was once someone who sent a text, which at first I thought was criticizing us, but in the end, praised us. He said in the message, “It’s a thankless awful job that good people in their right minds would not take on.” [Laughter] Of course, that was a whole page of text. Why was this sent to me? He added, and this made me feel good, “I feel so bad for him; I know he’s trying so hard. Just want to make him know at least one person appreciates him now.” (This was sent to our friend.) [Applause]

The one who sent it to me at that time does not get involved in politics. His interest is environmental issues. I have never met this person but he chose to send a message to reach me.

In the search, we talked to people who could continue the Straight Path and many sectors. I interviewed three people, who in my view are partners in the Straight Path. It would have been good if those who still need training would really have the opportunity to mature and understand the true depth of leadership. In my belief, these three, if they work together, would be a very strong team. But for now, we have not succeeded. We reached an understanding; it seems we have the same goal, but not the exact way to achieve it.

I am confident that you are also aware: So much is at stake to leave it to “maybe.” Perhaps some thought: Maybe others can continue what we have started. Maybe others can hold the corrupt accountable. Maybe others can continue to uplift the economy and expand social services. Maybe others will stay on the straight and narrow path.

For me, why would we be enticed by “maybe,” when we have something certain? [Applause] Let me clarify, the certainty we are talking about, [laughter] is not the certainty of corruption in our Straight Path. So, what certainty did we talk about and are we sure of? Number 1: Certainty of competence; certainty of having no other Boss but the people, certainty of owing favors to no one, certainty of having no other interest but the nation. [Applause] In other words, we will continue the Straight Path with that certainty. [Applause]

And I believe, that person is no other than Mar Roxas. [Applause]

Let me tell you a story about Mar Roxas. [Laughter] Many of us know, he was the first to strive to bring and grow the BPO industry in the Philippines. [Applause] From 2,400 employees in the year 2000, it grew in 2014 to an industry worth P18.9 billion [applause] directly employing over a million people. [Applause] According to Greg Domingo, before I stepped down from office, it was 1.3 million. [Applause] But I think he was hiding something there to say that it exceeded the promise. [Laughter] What many don't know: Cubao was late in joining the growth of BPOs because Mar forbade his family from applying in the ecozones. [Applause] Let's emphasize: His own family, long-time businessmen, he convinced them not to participate in the initiative he started as a government official. [Applause] Here, and in many other examples, the integrity of a Mar Roxas is clear. [Applause]

From my experience, I know that whatever Mar is tasked with, he doesn't leave it until the situation is stable. Whether in Zamboanga, Bohol, or Leyte, even if communication with us was cut off due to Yolanda, I know that the tasks are in good hands, and he doesn't need to wait for orders to ensure that what needs to be done is done. Let me also remind you: Going to those places wasn't a vacation. It was a time of crisis; it was necessary to ensure that people in evacuation centers had food, and many other immediate needs were met. When we went to Zamboanga, for example, they also created—an additional benefit for Zamboanga—a new cottage industry of laundresses because some of us hoped we would only be there overnight, and didn't bring enough clothes. And there were also no stores open.

The puzzle for us is: Despite Mar's achievements, despite his sacrifice, it seems there is an industry really focused on pulling him down. In Leyte, he just wanted to follow the rules, but these people he was talking to who prefer politicking, edited a video to discredit him. [Applause] Another example: There was no road for cars yet, so Mar chose to ride a motorcycle to reach the places he needed to go; when he slipped, he was criticized for it. [Laughter] I thought to myself: Is being a decent leader's job description like this—to try to do good, but all the opponent sees is bad? What many don't know, when it's night and the media is asleep, Mar himself drives and goes around the communities to see what else needs to be done. [Applause]

We in the Liberal Party, especially our generation, have been emphasized by those who came before us and we are not comfortable boasting about our own achievements; if you notice, our focus is always “We,” unlike others who constantly say “I did this, and I did that.” Perhaps, many people are also unaware of Mar's achievements and true character. That's just the fate of those focused on real change, instead of photo-ops and putting faces and names on novelty items. [Applause] For me: The good deeds really happened, even if they weren't flaunted. And in our choice, I, like you, no longer consider those who are obviously self-serving, and contrary to the straight path.

Bosses, when I first campaigned, when I went around Tarlac, when I ran as congressman, there were so many places that seemed unchanged since the sixties, during my father's time as governor. 'Where is the progress?' Why is it like this? This is probably the effect of what is called traditional politics: There were efforts made to keep our countrymen impoverished, so they would be the ones people run to—for food, medical treatment, to weddings, baptisms, or wakes. This is the debt of gratitude they will collect during elections, to continue the suffering of the majority, and enrich the few.

Therefore, in the next election, who should we support? The one who will continue the Straight Path and break the system of patronage, or the one who is looking for a way to return us to the cycle of corruption and poverty? Let's examine the candidate's achievements; it would be even better if there is a long experience as a basis for the review, because that's where we will see a lot of evidence of his honesty and competence as a partner in the Straight Path. It is clear who among the choices is true and worthy of being our next leader. And if his numbers are low now, it means we need to work harder to introduce him. [Applause] We are being called again to prove that “The Filipino is worth fighting for.” [Applause] We all know: What is truly important, must be worked for, must be fought for.

My Bosses, I submit to you today: In my opinion, the one who has shown brilliance and integrity, the ripe and fully prepared to continue the Straight Path: it is none other than Mar Roxas. [Applause]

Good day. Thank you very much.
