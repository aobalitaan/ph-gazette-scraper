Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, at the Inauguration of the Iloilo Convention Center.

There are many reasons for our beloved Ilonggos to celebrate today. Earlier, we had the ceremonial launch of the Iloilo Business Park and the inauguration of the Richmonde Hotel. Now, we are launching your magnificent Iloilo Convention Center. These projects will serve as a springboard for the further development of Iloilo as a center of commerce and tourism in Western Visayas. The benefit it brings: Wider opportunities for many of our countrymen. The massive transformation of Iloilo is clearly visible: from a sluggish province before, it is truly accelerating in progress and ready to compete with other developed provinces like Cebu. It’s a bit sad though, with Iloilo’s progress now, when I return to Tarlac, they might say or ask me if I’m from Iloilo rather than Tarlac. [Laughter] My answer will be, it’s really hard to refuse the “Big Man of the Senate.” [Applause]

We are here at a structure that proves the positive results of good governance—the Iloilo Convention Center. This is truly something the Ilonggos can be proud of. Aside from its design inspired by your Dinagyang and Paraw Festival, its world-class quality is also impressive. That’s why we are doubling our efforts so that, before long, it will be known and be a source of pride throughout the Philippines.

We would like to thank, first of all, Megaworld Corporation, led by Dr. Andrew Tan, for their significant contribution to the realization of this project: 1.7 hectares were donated by Dr. Tan. [Applause] A big thank you also to our Big Man in the Senate, and your fellow Ilonggo—Senate President Franklin Drilon [applause] who is truly very enthusiastic about Iloilo and neighboring provinces. Architect Coscolluela, I heard, donated his services for the design of this structure. [Applause] Earlier, Senate President told me, “You know, the land was donated, free, from Dr. Tan. The design, free, from Architect Coscolluela. Jose Mari Chan’s performances earlier were also free.” [Laughter and Applause] The problem though, someone filed a case here, even the case and insult were free. [Laughter] Let me clarify: This convention center is just one of the meaningful projects of our good Manong Frank. There’s also the Iloilo River Esplanade that you frequent, the Stage 2 of the Jalaur River Multi-Purpose Project that we have broken ground on, as well as many roads, bridges, and infrastructure that he strived to fund and implement, including the housing for our informal settlers. Truly: In the legacy he will leave in Iloilo, you will see the difference between the Straight Path and the Crooked Path.

This Iloilo Convention Center is a concrete example that on the Straight Path, the people’s money goes only to the people. The cost of this project: P713.31 million. It aims to be a center for conferences and gatherings, not only in your city, province, and region, but also in neighboring regions. The good news is: It is scheduled to be used for large gatherings such as the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation until October. I heard there are already confirmed bookings and inquiries here in the coming months until next year.

According to Mon Jimenez, there are 38 weeks remaining in our term, and now 30 are already reserved. Maybe with a little more effort, every month—maybe next, every week—we will fill this Convention Center. It hasn’t even started, it’s almost fully booked. We can already see, even now, that the benefits of this building are worth it, and it is certain that it will bring even greater benefits.

This is the pillar of our infrastructure strategy in provinces like Iloilo. Through better roads and world-class structures, more tourists will be encouraged to visit. This development of tourism directly creates jobs. And with the greater ability to improve themselves, citizens also contribute to the development of the economy and society. It’s a cycle of empowering the country, the community, and our Bosses—the people.

In the past five years, we have seen the positive transformation of our country. We achieved this because we united. In 2010, we chose the straight, honest, and reasonable governance. Clearly: This positive change we have achieved, you made it and are continuing to make it.

In the coming year, we will once again face a crossroads. I see this as a referendum on all the successes we are now enjoying. What is a referendum? The question is simple: Do you like what we have achieved during our term, or do we return to the old system where a few exploit the suffering of our countrymen? Do you think we have fulfilled or even surpassed the promised change when we were just starting? As far as I'm concerned, if the answer is yes, it is only right to continue what we have started and choose the one who will undoubtedly continue on the Straight Path. [Applause]

I am confident that, just as in 2010, the vast majority of our Ilonggo countrymen, if not all, will side with what is right and reasonable. We all know the standard-bearer of the Straight Path, none other than Mar Roxas [applause]—like you, also a son of Panay Island.

In the remaining time of our term, we will continue to exert all our efforts to alleviate the suffering of others, and to accelerate the benefits for the people. Every road and bridge we build, every business that opens and expands, every building we construct, we want to bring lasting relief to more Filipinos. We are focusing our programs not on the dictates of politics or whims, but where they are truly needed.

My challenge and call to each of you: Now and in the coming years, let us continue and further enrich the reforms we have planted; and let us proudly bequeath them to the next generation.

I practiced a little Ilonggo again earlier. If it’s correct, it’s thanks to Jed Mabilog. If it’s wrong, it’s thanks to Jun Delantar [laughter]: Let us continue to help each other along the straight path so that we can achieve the progress we have long dreamed of. [Applause]

My countrymen, I will repeat it just so that when we look at the Convention Center, let us remember one thing: This happened, many people helped; you who helped me in 2007 and 2010, Manong Frank who initiated it, Andrew Tan who donated the land, Architect Coscolluela who designed this building. We all helped each other, and we achieved something. Now you have the ability to truly compete in the field of what is called MCE—meetings, conventions, exhibits—these activities. The vision, like Dr. Tan’s, saw a part of Metro Manila called Libis back then. I admit, I am old enough to have seen this place as grasslands. It was really quite remote. His concept: a township. Everything is there: the children’s school, the parents’ workplace, housing for everyone, even entertainment. That’s what is envisioned for our old airport here. It didn’t work out due to the needs, and perhaps in time, maybe even 5 years from now, you will complain to our officials, “Why is it so congested here? Why are there so many people here?” The point is this: Our destination is in our hands. We have the freedom to decide where we want to go. Let us use that right decision, especially in the coming year.

Good day. Thank you all very much.
