Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III upon his arrival from his visit to Japan

After four packed days, we have returned from a successful State Visit to Japan. There, we truly saw the fruit of a nation’s collective effort to succeed against any challenge. In truth, in recent decades, we have seen that despite facing their own challenges, Japan has extended assistance to us beyond their obligation. Their support has been significant and continuous in various sectors of our society, including disaster preparedness, security, and peace in Muslim Mindanao.

In the activities during this trip, we expressed our heartfelt gratitude for all the assistance they have provided and continue to provide; we thanked not only their leaders, Emperor Akihito and Prime Minister Shinzo Abe, but also their business associations, and media organizations that we met. In addressing their National Diet, which is the equivalent of our congress, we emphasized our admiration and gratitude for their country, along with clarifying our positions on security issues. We also invited them to further strengthen relations in trade and the labor sector.

It was a great honor to be invited to speak at the National Diet, and we were truly pleased with their warm and positive reception to our words. But as we always emphasize, any honor or praise they have extended is truly more deserving of you, my Bosses. Anything we have achieved is a result not only of my individual effort, but, and more importantly, of the contribution of our entire nation as we traverse the Straight and Narrow Path.

Also stemming from this contribution are the souvenirs we brought home from Japan. The good thing here is that whether in low-tech or high-tech industries, we are creating opportunities for every level of Filipino worker. Let's look at this: Last Wednesday, eleven Japanese companies signed letters of intent to open or expand operations in our country. What is truly pleasing is that these industries that have pledged their investments are clearly higher up in the value chain: From renewable energy in the form of electric tricycles, to printers, smart glasses, and other digital media products; there are also high-level medical devices such as aortic catheters and invitro diagnostics and hemodialysis treatment devices. By bringing these products to the Philippines, it is clear that the world's recognition of Filipino intelligence and talent is expanding. Some of them have even expressed their intention to make our country their operational center in ASEAN. In total, these eleven companies' pledged additional investments have the potential to reach over P13.5 billion, which could bring 30,761 jobs.

This is even better because we know that when the product created is higher in the value chain, the wages are also higher and the comfort and dignity of life can be enjoyed more quickly for the majority of Filipinos. Our call, especially to the youth, is to study hard to truly make the most of the opportunities that will open up with the imminent entry of these investments. I would just like to emphasize that these 11 companies have already signed letters of intent; in addition to them, there are many companies we have spoken with who have also expressed interest, and are studying how they will expand or begin to invest in our country.

An example of this is the company Uniqlo, which currently has 23 stores in the Philippines. They repeatedly said that they really want to expand their operations and are already accelerating the opening of an additional 200 outlets in our country. In our business meeting, we invited them to also establish a factory for their products here in the Philippines. They said they still have processes they need to complete. They need allied factories to make that happen. Fortunately, we also had with us at the gathering the supplier of fifty percent of their fabrics and other fibers. When I mentioned our suggestion to Uniqlo, they thought that it might be good to also establish factories of these Uniqlo suppliers in the Philippines. They said they would immediately meet with their friend who owns Uniqlo to expedite the study and look at the possibility, or expedite the possibility of making us a manufacturing hub as soon as possible.

Adding to the good news: the various agreements signed between Japan and the Philippines, particularly in the sectors of health, maritime safety, and trade. In my discussion with Prime Minister Abe, he also said they are willing to provide a concessional loan to fund three large infrastructure projects. This concessional loan is a loan with a very low interest rate and is given by a friend to his fellow friend. This shows a desire to truly help rather than be a burden. The amount they are willing to lend to us is estimated at P136.9 billion.

We also signed a Joint Declaration on Strengthened Strategic Partnership with the Prime Minister, which truly deepens the relationship between our countries in many sectors, including security. This elevation of our relationship with Japan is timely in the face of threats to stability in the West Philippine Sea. What is important is: We see that the principles of Japan and the Philippines align regarding respect for the rights of each country, freedom of navigation in international waters, and the rule of law and peaceful relations to address any misunderstandings. With the help of Japan, attention is being drawn by more and more countries to the situation in the Asian seas.

The Philippines is truly now recognized by the world: Our positions are respected, we are flooded with investors, and our achievements in raising the standard of living in our country are admired. In truth, in our interactions with the Japanese, they asked us repeatedly: How can I ensure that the transformation we are enjoying now will continue? This extensive and meaningful social service, this economic upliftment and opening of opportunities, accountability for the corrupt, the restoration of our national dignity—how can we ensure that these are not wasted, stopped, or returned to the previous situation when we step down from office?

And as always, my response: I did not create the change alone; it is the result of the contribution of every decent Filipino. So, Bosses, you made the change, so you will also maintain and continue it. My job: To ensure the positive results of reform and good governance. I know you see this, so I am confident that you yourselves will ensure that we do not U-turn back to corruption. I am confident that we can raise the level of politics, from the squabbling of personalities to a true examination of issues, principles, and character, to see what kind of leadership can continue to deliver benefits to all of you.

Bosses, in Japan, we saw the result of their philosophy of constantly forging oneself and society, to reach the highest it can reach; I saw what a culture can achieve that truly does everything to create the best product, service, or process. I have full confidence: We also have this character; self-assessment is also in our race; the continuous correction of what is crooked and the correction of mistakes; and the pouring of abilities to reach the highest we can reach. May it be that we are now in and will continue to be in a time when we are admired by other races, and they ask us how we achieved the transformation of our society. The response is: We united, we worked hard, and we trusted in God; we took the Straight Path, and here we remain, towards the fulfillment of our collective dreams.

Thank you very much. Good evening to all of you.
