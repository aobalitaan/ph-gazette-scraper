Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, at the PAGCOR Christmas Offering 2015

Truly, the Filipino Christmas is about family. Here we are again, a family coming together and contributing to illuminate and color the Christmas of our countrymen who are in greater need.

From 2010 to 2012, PAGCOR allocated a total of P42.25 million to provide gift packs to our countrymen. More than 14,000 individuals benefited from this, including orphans, out-of-school youths, street children, and our fellow countrymen with illnesses.

In 2013 and 2014, we decided to postpone the holding of the PAGCOR Christmas Offering, to give way to the provision of gift packs to our brothers and sisters affected by Typhoon Yolanda. A total of P12 million was allocated for this, in addition to the P2 billion allocated for the construction of classrooms in the affected areas.

Now, we are with our beloved senior citizens who I will be with for five years, the hardworking members of the Tricycle Operators and Drivers Associations, and our diligent street sweepers here in Quezon City. [Applause] Gift packs have also been given to some youth and their families from the National Children’s Hospital. This week, as part of the PAGCOR Christmas Offering, there will also be a gathering with the brave barangay health workers from Pasay, Parañaque, and Manila. In total, approximately 9,500 are beneficiaries of our program this year, which was allocated P25.35 million. Earlier, we also presented a check worth P2 billion, for the “Straight Path to the Classroom” School Building Project.

This gathering is just proof that on the Straight Path, no one is left behind. We recognize and value the hard work and contribution of everyone—whether you are a health worker caring for and revitalizing the health of your fellow citizens, a tricycle driver who delivers our countrymen to their destinations day and night, or a street sweeper who ensures that our streets and waterways are clean, to keep our communities safe from pollution and floods.

This is also a time for gratitude. So, to every Boss who works hard for their family, and who are also our partners in promoting development throughout the country: Thank you very much to all of you. You are still my strength, and you are still the ones making change happen. I hope that, in some way, this small offering from your government can reciprocate your sincere sacrifice and dedication to your respective jobs.

I would also like to thank those who comprise the Philippine Amusement and Gaming Corporation, led by Chairman Bong Naguiat. In just over five years, you have transformed PAGCOR from a former agency riddled with corruption, into an agency that is a source of service to the Filipino people. I also thank you all from the bottom of my heart.

In the end, the Filipino people remain one family. In just a short period, we are already able to fix, beautify, and strengthen our country, which we consider our home. We are reaching our dream Philippines, where if you persevere, you are sure to have something to cook in a stew. All of this is because of you; because of the daily unity and cooperation of everyone, to develop not only ourselves and our loved ones, but also the entire nation.

In the coming year, it is once again in the hands of every Filipino whether we can continue the repair and strengthening of our home, or if it will be demolished back to its old weak foundation and decaying condition. I emphasize: You, our Bosses, hold the fate of our beloved country in your hands; you hold the future of your children, your loved ones, and your fellow citizens. I trust that, with the guidance and light of the Lord, we will truly continue what we have started.

To all of us: A happy and prosperous Christmas. Let me repeat: Thank you very much to PAGCOR, from the old days when the earnings were thrown away on expensive coffee, now we are really able to exchange it for schools and help for everyone. Thank you also to our leaders in the city of Quezon, which I will return to in 199 days. Just 199 days, and I will return home on Times [street]. We are close to West Avenue, there are many places to eat, [applause] and on Quezon Avenue. I hope Bistek doesn't raise all the real estate tax. But we can rest assured that Quezon City is truly more progressive with the efficient administration of our leaders here.

Good day. Thank you very much to all of you.
