Speech

of

The Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III

President of the Philippines

At the time capsule-laying and briefing at Panglao Airport and the ceremonial lighting of 2,684 sitios in Region VII under the Sitio Electrification Program

[Declared in Brgy. Lourdes, Panglao, Bohol, on March 2, 2016]

Our commitment to the Straight and Narrow Path: In progress, no one will be left behind. As we go around the country, we identify the needs of the community, and immediately address them. Bohol is one of those we focused on after a strong earthquake shook your province. But we saw: The Boholanos did not give up. With your cooperation, your province has returned to normal life. I commend your strength and resilience. [2] [Applause]

When we say Bohol, the first thing that comes to mind: Chocolate Hills. Included there are the famous tarsiers, and rivers and beaches visited for various water sports. Therefore, tourism is one of your main industries, which is why we are focusing on its development.

You waited a long time to have a new airport, so one of our priorities here in Bohol was the construction of your new Panglao Airport, which will replace your limited and small Tagbilaran Airport.

Just now, when we landed, we really felt how small the Tagbilaran Airport is. It felt like we were using reverse thrust and fully applying the brakes, and I was looking around, “It looks like this runway is about to end.” [Laughter] “Is this where it ends for us?” [Laughter] But for me, that was just a temporary inconvenience. I know that when the weather is bad or there is a disrupted airplane, everyone arriving will have to wait; next time. If just one airplane lands and cannot take off, all flights are over. That should really stop.

We couldn't just easily expand the Tagbilaran Airport, and that was one of the points of contention. Could we just add to the airport to speed up its construction? The other side said that it was already full; it would be difficult to acquire land on both sides to enlarge it. And if we did enlarge it, right in the middle of Tagbilaran, it would really be difficult if we enlarged it further. So when we looked at Panglao, one of the issues in my mind was whether your limestone foundation could support this airport. And that's what delayed the project. Our experts, including PHIVOLCS, investigated and proved that it really could. So next year, you will have a new airport. I will probably be a passenger on the new airplanes at your new airport.

I also told you that when an airplane is stalled, subsequent flights are cancelled. So your tourists, which number around half a million now, the [Department of] Tourism promises 1.7 million once this airport is finished.

Now, why is the number of tourists so important? For every foreign tourist arrival that comes to us, it is equivalent to a job for a Filipino. And it is estimated that each one spends at least a thousand dollars per visit to our country. In other words, not only the airline will earn, the resort owner will earn, the one selling food for the resort to serve will earn, the tourist guide who doesn't need to study extensively will earn, the one making woven handicrafts will earn. In other words, it will help a lot in boosting your economy. It was also mentioned earlier by the second spokesperson regarding our beneficiaries, they are still looking for additional sources of income. This is one of the fastest things we can do to increase the opportunity for you to maximize the chance to improve your lives.

Panglao Airport is just one of our infrastructure projects here in Bohol. For your public works, from 2011 to 2016, we allocated P10.45 billion. With this, we repaired roads and bridges, school buildings, health facilities, and flood control projects. We also repaired a large part of the Panglao Island Circumferential Road, which will attract more tourists to your island, and improve the flow of services and commerce in your province. This is separate from the budget allocated by the DOTC for the construction and repair of airports and ports—from 2011 to 2016, in the DOTC budget, P8.5 billion was allocated to Bohol. Imagine, a total of almost P19 billion has been allocated to support infrastructure and tourism here in Bohol.

You have seen, all our projects are interconnected; all are focused on one goal: To raise the standard of living of our Bosses. We are focusing on tourism to create more jobs and expand opportunities. To further strengthen tourism, of course, we will need electricity. And we didn't just wait for this to happen; we made it a reality. Today, we witnessed the ceremonial lighting of 2,684 sitios here in Central Visayas under the Sitio Electrification Program led by the DOE, the NEA, and made possible with the help of our electric cooperatives. Thank you very much to them all.

Let me tell a story: Before we took office, our predecessor said that all barangays across the country already had electricity. When I took office, it was explained to me that when a barangay hall is lit, it is said that the entire barangay is lit. [Laughter] What about the other sitios that are plunged into darkness? And how many barangay captains would agree to, “Okay, connect all the houses here to the barangay hall. I’ll pay for your electricity?” [Laughter] Now, we are ending the time when we seemed to have gotten used to a culture of lies, fraud, and theft.

In 2011, we conducted an inventory to determine how many sitios in the country still lacked electricity. Based on the list, 32,441 sitios were still unenergized. What’s worse, it was estimated that almost one million pesos would be needed to connect each sitio to the grid. Let’s multiply that: 32,441 times P1 million each, and it would require P32.4 billion to energize the identified sitios. My question to you is, if you were in my position in 2011, of course, the first thing that came to my mind was, “Where would we get that much money?”

Now, we could have just turned a blind eye and shrugged off the problem, saying, “My predecessor said there’s no problem with that,” so I didn’t bother to address it. They said it was already resolved. But we faced the problem, we looked for solutions, because we knew the value of electricity to the livelihood of our countrymen. Through the proper process and honest management, the previous estimate of one million per sitio—through the cooperation of the DOE, NEA, and the cooperatives—we were able to lower it from P1 million to almost P600,000 per sitio on average. Therefore, the government saved P400,000 per sitio. It’s clear: On the Straight and Narrow Path, funds are not pocketed; any savings we achieve go directly to other programs that will provide immediate benefits to our Bosses.

The good news: Here in Bohol, we have been reported that 100 percent of all your sitios are now energized. [Applause] Throughout the country, 32,026 sitios, or 98.72 percent of the total target, have already been energized. Before the end of March, we will have completed everything identified in our inventory. That March is the current one, not 2017.

This allocation of funds for tourism, job creation, and bringing light to the homes of every Filipino family, are just some of the things we are doing to ensure that no one is left behind on the Straight and Narrow Path.

Here in Bohol, we have the Bohol Fabrication Laboratory—FabLab, for short—and several Shared Service Facilities to support some of your industries, as explained to us earlier. With the help of the FabLab, you can design and produce prototypes of your products using laser cutters and 3D printers. Our National Police also helped there. There is also a Shared Service Facility for loom weavers, like the Tubigon Loom-weavers Multi-Purpose Cooperative. As one of our beneficiaries shared earlier, with the help of this SSF, his income, which used to be P700 per week, is now P5,400—almost eight times the increase in his income. [Applause] That’s why the roasted chicken, and them, are greeted by their children during visits.

Along with this are social services that we continue to expand the scope of for our Bosses. In education: As you know, we cleared the inherited backlog in classrooms, school seats and textbooks, and we strengthened our Basic Education System so that our students can compete with others internationally. We have also expanded the coverage of our Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program: Here in Bohol alone, where I was surprised to learn, only 531 households used to benefit from Pantawid Pamilya, now, there are 59,222 beneficiaries of this program. [Applause] You do the math: 59,222 now versus 500 plus back then. What happened to that difference? Maybe Batman will take care of it. [Laughter] They are now among the 4.4 million households assisted by Pantawid Pamilya nationwide. In PhilHealth, 1.2 million Boholanos are now covered by the program; they are part of the 93 million Filipinos who are already benefiting from the benefits offered by PhilHealth.

With all these successes, is there anyone in their right mind who would say that we neglected Bohol? Is there anyone who would say that we should stop pursuing the Straight Path?

In May, our Bosses will once again choose the next leader of the country. We will once again face a crossroads: Do we continue the reforms, or do we return to the crooked system? Will we disregard everything we have started and entrust the future to uncertainty; will we side with what is certain, with people of integrity and capable of leading our country? If public service, no drama, and work for the people are the basis, it is clear who should be chosen: No one else but Mar Roxas and Leni Robredo. [Applause]

We have come so far: What were once thought impossible, we not only made possible, but we are accomplishing one by one. This is just the beginning. With your support and trust, we can overcome any challenge, and we can achieve any height of dreams.

You know, I had a photo taken earlier at the end of this airport. It’s just a reminder that we were able to accomplish this rather ambitious undertaking. When I started as your President, I had two thoughts in my mind. The first: A friend told me that he could not forgive the person I replaced, because for almost ten years that he was in power, opportunities were lost to us.

What does that mean? If we were able to build this airport in, more or less, six years, he could have built it in those nine-and-a-half years. If he had built it, how many jobs would have been created because of tourism? How many farmers could have gotten better prices? Everything we have done, there is no reason why it couldn't have been done before. But perhaps we are different. I, I focus on the welfare of my Bosses.

And of course, the second thing, as you remember, I’ve said this many times when we started: We were waiting for Friday night, because on Friday night there was a timeout. What was the timeout? Every day, from Monday to Friday in the early evening, you would discover bigger and bigger problems. They created it, we tried to block it then, we didn't succeed, we were the ones fixing the problems passed on to us. And you remember, I was left with only 7 percent of the budget to take care of all the problems he left behind.

After the earthquake, we also slept here for a night when there were still many aftershocks. I slept in a tent on the grass. The cot they gave me was beautiful, a folding one. The metal frame was straight at the back. [Laughter] That's why it was comfortable to sleep on, I was supported even when it was shaking. When I woke up, of course it was on the grass, it would be embarrassing to face anyone without washing my face and brushing my teeth. I made do with a small bottle of water to feel what everyone was going through. The lack of electricity there, I also felt that. It was actually good that I went to the garden, there was a call of nature, so to speak. [Laughter] We are also human. But it was good that we experienced that so we could expedite everything that needs to be done. You are witnesses: When we went around, the church that collapsed, the municipality that was almost gone, the market that fell… How can we fix all of this? But you are the witnesses. We have already done this. [Applause]

Comrades, earlier you were thanking me, our colleagues. I think it's the other way around, we should be thanking you. You gave us the opportunity to do this. Now, we will return again, will this continue? You are the Boss. If you, new TV viewers, go for the one with the most beautiful promises that are broken, we might get lost. If there are those who claim too much and we believe them; “Okay, let's give them the benefit of the doubt.” We might get lost.

That's why I will return here. Why would we leave where we have already seen the proof? We are now on solid ground. The promises are gone. They said they promised before, did we fulfill them or not? If you see that the trust and support you gave to all these people were not wasted, your hands will say “We will continue or not.”

Come on, let's welcome a more peaceful and prosperous tomorrow. [3]

Thank you very much to everyone.
