Today, we witness another historic milestone in our country: the enactment of the P3.002 trillion National Budget for 2016. Through the cooperation of the Executive, Congress, and various other institutions, we have achieved another record. This is the sixth consecutive year that the People’s Budget has been passed on time. [Applause] This means that from the time we assumed office until the final stage of our term, we have not failed to pass our budget on time. Therefore, to all those who led and participated in the creation of this budget, a heartfelt thank you to all of you.

If you will recall, when we assumed office, the National Budget for 2010 was already in use, and we simply inherited the proposed budget for 2011. That was the biggest mistake we could correct in the 2011 budget. Imagine this: Of the P1.54 trillion total spending for the entire year of 2010, only P100 billion, or 6.5 percent, was freely available for us to use in the remaining six months of the year. The question is: Where did the money go?

Under our predecessors, portions or the entirety of the National Budget were always reenacted. In 2007, for example, it was approved almost in April. Isn't it reasonable that, since the budget was reenacted, the funds for the months that had already passed and were spent should also be removed from the budget? But what’s worse: The items that were already spent on, such as salaries, Maintenance and other Operating Expenses, and others in the past three months, were still in the budget. Why were those items that were already paid for still there? Where did the excess allocations go?

We stopped this practice. We removed the mechanisms that facilitated exploitation and the squandering of public funds. We promoted the proper process. We also know: When the budget is passed late, the delivery of services is delayed, and the suffering of our countrymen is prolonged.

Now, we have forged a mechanism that will ensure the honest and effective implementation of the targets of our agencies. As I said: If the Executive's proposed budget is reasonable, the dialogue with Congress will be smooth, it will be passed quickly, and the benefits will be delivered to our countrymen, our Bosses, immediately.

Our goal is clear: Inclusive growth. By allocating a reasonable fund to our agencies and programs, our countrymen are given the opportunity to keep pace with the country's economic progress. We promote thorough consultation so that the people themselves can design the projects that we need to fund.

We all know that the opportunities that open up will only be maximized if Filipinos have sufficient capacity to take advantage of the benefits it brings. That is why we continue to pour funds into social services that empower our Bosses.

In the coming year, DepEd will still have the highest budget. The allocated fund for it: P436.5 billion. After we have addressed the backlogs in classrooms, books, chairs, and teachers that we inherited, we are now addressing the needs of the future. This includes the construction of 47,553 classrooms, the purchase of 103.2 million new textbooks, and the hiring of 79,691 positions for teaching and non-teaching personnel.

We will also continue to accelerate our Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program, for which we have allocated P62.7 billion. Its target for assistance in the coming year: 4.6 million beneficiary households, including more than 218,000 Filipino families who were not included in Listahanan 1 in 2008-2009. This also includes poor indigenous peoples and families on the streets. We really do not hesitate to pour funds into such programs, especially now that we are already seeing positive results. According to the initial study of the DSWD, 1.5 million households or the equivalent of 7.5 million Filipinos have been lifted above the so-called poverty line due to Pantawid Pamilya.

The budget we allocated to the Department of Public Works and Highways: P400.4 billion. This will support the completion of all national roads and the construction of flood control projects to mitigate the damage caused by flooding. If this year, 4 percent of our GDP was allocated to infrastructure, next year we aim to allocate 5 percent of our GDP to this sector.

As preparation for the effects of climate change and in support of the “Build Back Better” program, we have allocated P38.9 billion for the National Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Fund. Part of this will be allocated to our rehabilitation program, including the Yolanda Comprehensive Rehabilitation and Recovery Plan. There is also the construction of additional evacuation centers and the assessment of the quality of public buildings to ensure the safety of our countrymen affected by disasters.

Clearly: The entire budget, especially infrastructure, is formulated with a thorough study of problems and their corresponding appropriate solutions. Every road, bridge, port, airport, school, farm-to-market road, and other infrastructure built in various parts of the country is part of our strategy towards broad development. No one, while with their hand on the Bible, could say that we have neglected their city or province.

Truly: We allocate funds where there is a need. What is good: We deliver immediate benefits without burdening the Filipino people. During our term, we have not added any new taxes, except for the Sin Tax Reform.

It would not have been possible to celebrate today without the individuals and institutions who worked hard to build the National Budget for 2016. We are grateful to everyone who comprises the Department of Budget and Management, led by Secretary Butch Abad. [Applause] You meticulously reviewed every page and detail of this book, and ensured that not a single centavo is wasted.

We also thank the cooperation of our Congress—led by Senate President Frank Drilon and House Speaker Sonny Belmonte; [applause] Senate Finance Committee Chair Loren Legarda, the kind Vice Chair Cynthia Villar, House Appropriations Committee Chair Sid Ungab, and House Majority Floor Leader Boyet Gonzales [applause]—for the time and dedication you poured into passing the People's Budget for 2016.

Above all, thank you very much to our Bosses, for your continued contribution to good governance, towards the greater advancement of our country. It has not changed: You are the ones who made and continue to make change happen.

This continuous enactment of the National Budget on time and through a proper mechanism is just a part of the transformation happening on the Straight Path. As our term nears its end, we are equipping the next administration to continue and further advance the change happening in our society. The details in the pages of this book are clear and organized to address the needs of the coming year. Our desire: Let us not pass on the problems we inherited to others.

We have truly come so far compared to where we started. With our continued unity, I know we can reach even further. Our administration has laid the foundation for our reforms, and I hope that those who come after us will nurture the widespread progress we are enjoying. The challenge and call to each one: Let us continue to walk the Straight Path, fight for what is right and just, and wholeheartedly contribute to a future where no one is left behind.

Good morning. Thank you very much.
