Speech of the Honorable Benigno S. Aquino III, President of the Philippines, for the 189th graduates of the Philippine Merchant Marine Academy

Secretary Mar Roxas; Secretary Patricia Licuanan; Commodore Richard Ritual; Captain Gaudencio Morales; Governor Hermogenes Ebdane Jr.; Representative Jun Omar Ebdane; Mayor Peter Lim; Director General Emmanuel Joel Villanueva of the TESDA; former Representative Risa Hontiveros-Baraquel; Vice Admiral Alexander Pama of the FOIC; Vice Admiral Edmund Tan, Commandant, Philippine Coast Guard; fellow workers in government; parents and esteemed graduates; officers, faculty members of PMMA; the PMMA Corps of Midshipmen; honored guests; ladies and gentlemen; my beloved countrymen:

Truly, it is a beautiful day for all of you.

As a nation surrounded by water, it is not surprising that being a seafarer is in our blood. Just imagine: out of more than one million three hundred thousand seafarers around the world, about three hundred and fifty thousand of them are our fellow Filipinos. This means that one in every four seafarers in the world is Juan dela Cruz. Our people's diligence is often described as 'kayod marino' – enduring the heat of the sun, facing gigantic waves, and working enthusiastically, even far from their loved ones.

This is the exceptional quality that our seafarers continue to demonstrate. Today, 241 new products of the Philippine Merchant Marine Academy will be added to the ranks of our hardworking seafarers. We congratulate our graduates on this success. Congratulations, because the easy stages of your life are over; the practice and four years of maturation in school are finished. The voyage into real society begins; the time to face the waves of challenges begins; it is time to set sail to reap the fulfillment of your dreams.

We also congratulate your parents who serve as your guide and inspiration in your efforts; your teachers who tirelessly strive to impart excellence and knowledge to you, and the PMMA which continues to mold competent, reliable and high-caliber merchant marines. For a long time, the products of this institution have proven that Filipinos can succeed, not only here in the Philippines but all over the world.

The great contribution of our seafarers to our country is undeniable. Just this 2011, the total remittances of our seafarers reached 4.34 billion dollars – this is 21.58 percent of the total 20.12 billion dollars in remittances of our overseas Filipino workers. Along with the hard work of our seafarers to provide for their families, our economy is also being strengthened.

In your travels to different countries, you express and demonstrate the inherent talent, diligence, and dedication of the brown race to the whole world. Whether engineer, captain, cook, or ordinary employee; you truly give life to the ships. You maintain the peaceful and orderly flow of commerce on the seas.

Just imagine if you weren't in service: how many international commercial activities would be hampered? How many products and services would not be delivered to different countries? Perhaps, some of you think your job is pleasant and easy—you even get free rides on luxurious ships and are able to travel to different parts of the world. But we all know that isn't the whole story. Along with your voyages comes facing the dangers and calamities inherent in your work. You probably hear about Filipinos being captured by pirates, as well as seafarers being put in precarious situations. Aside from these, you also endure the longing for your families left behind.

We are aware of these sacrifices and efforts, so the government is also working doubly hard to protect your welfare. DOLE-POEA is strictly implementing Memorandum Circular No. 2 Series of 2010. This ensures that our seafarers undergo extensive training. This is preparation for facing any malevolent elements they may encounter during their voyages.

In connection with this, all students at PMMA undergo training with the Navy in the field of security. By providing them with modern equipment and seafarers, we are giving them sufficient capabilities and skills to properly perform their duties. POEA and DFA are also working together to monitor incidents of piracy in the country. We have instructed them to ensure the safety and protection of our seafarers—the coordination for their release and recovery, and the care for their families.

We won't look too far either. We received a report from PMMA to the Local Water Utilities Administration, or LWUA, regarding the low quality and lack of water supply here in your area. We will allocate five million pesos for the repair of the water supply system in the affected municipality, including PMMA. It wouldn't be right for those who have trained to dock and sail the seas to lack a water supply, would it?

In the short time that we have steered the country towards good governance, the Philippines that our seafarers encounter and find upon their return is very different. Now, we are suppressing corruption in order to implement more that will lift more people out of poverty. We are ensuring that all sacrifices, every drop of sweat, and the sacrifices you offer to the service are worth it. My only request is that we do not lose heart or tire of fulfilling our duty. No matter how strong the waves of challenges are, no matter how many storms pour down, as long as we work together, we are sure to go far.

To our graduates, I rely on your cooperation in treading the straight and narrow path. Whether you become part of the Navy, the Coast Guard, or international seafarers, we are confident that you will uphold the Filipino race. As scholars of the nation, it is your duty to consider not only your own welfare and that of your families, but also that of the larger society. Together with the Filipino people, we will continue to sail towards true progress, justice, and prosperity in our country.

Thank you very much. Good day to all.
