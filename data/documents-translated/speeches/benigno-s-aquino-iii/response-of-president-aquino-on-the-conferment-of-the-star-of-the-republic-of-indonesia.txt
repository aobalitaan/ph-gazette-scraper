Response of His Excellency Benigno S. Aquino III President of the Philippines Upon being conferred with the Bintang Republik Indonesia Adipurna

[Delivered in Bali, Indonesia, on October 10, 2014]

It is a distinct privilege to receive the Bintang Republik Indonesia Adipurna . It humbles me to receive the highest honor from Indonesia, and I extend my gratitude to His Excellency President Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono and to the people of Indonesia for bestowing this upon me.

When I visited your country in 2011, I spoke of the many words shared by the Bahasa and the Filipino languages: There is your word for love, “tsinta,” which sounds very similar to the Filipino word for love, “sinta”; there is our shared word for child, “anak.” More recently, I was told that we also have the same word for “return,” “balik,” which I am very glad to have done today in your beautiful country.

Indeed, whether one looks at our languages, our histories, or our cultures, one can only find overwhelming evidence of the bonds that have tied our countries together through the years. It is no surprise, then, that our relations have always been characterized by solidarity and, above that, the warmth of enduring friendships. In 1963, following the eruption of Mt. Gunung Agung in Bali, the Philippines extended a hand to Indonesia: seven C-47 aircraft flew from our country, brought paramedics, and delivered relief goods and medical supplies to aid those affected.

In our time of calamity, Indonesia in turn demonstrated true solidarity. Your generous assistance in the aftermath of Typhoon Haiyan earned the gratitude of the entire Filipino people. In an ever-shrinking globe, we are all beginning to see how, more and more, our fortunes are linked with those of our neighbors; the response of Indonesia, together with the international community, in such a critical time, has instilled confidence in me that while the emerging challenges of the modern world may be formidable, we, as one region and one globe, can surmount them together.

This award I receive today represents the rich, historical relations between our countries; on top of that, however, it is also a recognition of the boundless potential of our partnership, and of our continuing task of exploring areas of cooperation and increasing our synergies in every possible manner, all for the mutual benefit of our peoples. It is for these reasons that the Philippines remains committed to working towards strengthening and expanding our partnership with Indonesia.

As His Excellency President Yudhoyono concludes two successful terms as President, he leaves behind a more vibrant Indonesia, one that has truly taken its rightful place as an influential voice and as a leader in the world stage. It is a testament to the exceptional leadership of President Yudhoyono, who has nurtured in so many countries a feeling that Indonesia is a reliable and constant partner.

Mr. President, you have not only been a friend but a big brother to so many nations. I can only assume that, after leading Indonesia for ten years, you look forward to a break from the pressures and problems of office. But given your sterling leadership, I also wonder if you will be even busier after your term with matters within and beyond the borders of Indonesia. Nonetheless, it is my hope that you will find the time to relax, and perhaps even visit the Philippines for vacation.

Today, Mr. President, I assure you that, moving forward, the Philippines remains fully committed to enhancing the cooperation we have forged; to deepening our friendship with our neighbors, with whom we share common beliefs, ideals, and aspirations for a more prosperous global community.

Again, terimah kasi banyak for the award. I wish Your Excellency good health, continued success, and some well-deserved relaxation after so many years of public service, and I wish the great nation of Indonesia continued peace, progress, and prosperity.

Thank you, and good day.
