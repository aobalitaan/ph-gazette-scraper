THE PRESIDENT’S SPEECH

I feel extremely happy on this occasion in which the friendship of the Philippines and Spain is sealed in a document signed by the hand of men, but in which the high designs of Providence shine. This act of ratification is nothing more than a diplomatic formality to strengthen the friendship that has existed between the two countries since time immemorial. Neither the vicissitudes of time nor the contingencies of history have diminished the strong relations of the spirit, which have sunk their roots in the consciousness of the two peoples, since the Spanish captain set foot on the land of our ancestors. And that is because the currents of tradition are alive and unalterable. It is a matter of pride for me to be able to give, on behalf of this Government, official sanction to the pact that was agreed upon last year, very different and more noble than other analogous pacts, because blood was not necessary in it. I would call it a pact of souls, because it is conditioned, more than in what is written on paper, in the noble impulses of the heart.

Mr. Minister: tell your Government that the Filipinos recognize the beneficial work of Spain, whose customs, religion and culture have left indelible marks on the structure of our nationality. Such is the reason and origin of this pact. May God grant that the two peoples always meet on the path traced for them by Destiny, consolidating more and more their spiritual and material ties, and that, by carrying out what the paper says, they work, more than for the benefit of their own interests, for the interest of the well-being and peace of all peoples!
