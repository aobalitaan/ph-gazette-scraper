TRUE FILIPINO LANGUAGE

The President said that we should set aside the previous misunderstanding regarding what the general term should be and move towards the true development and cultivation of the true Filipino language.

First of all, I extend a heartfelt welcome to each and every one of you.

I congratulate you on demonstrating admirable enthusiasm in uniting to celebrate National Language Week.

This coming together of our leaders in Congress – Representatives and Senators – in a cultural and linguistic organization is a wonderful event. And that is the Balagtas Association.

For me, this is a complete unity of all Filipinos, regardless of their native language, in the name of “Filipino” which is our national language.

We have set aside the previous misunderstanding regarding what the general term should be, and we are moving towards the true development and cultivation of the true “Filipino” language where all our languages will be combined. Perhaps through this, we may also achieve complete success in endeavors related to economic, social, educational and cultural advancement.

Therefore, I request and suggest that we enact a law implementing what the Constitution mandates regarding the Filipino language. I am referring to the establishment of the National Language Commission, as stated and ordered by Article XIV Section 9 of the Constitution. You can count on my unwavering support for all your efforts regarding this important matter.

The action on this matter has long been awaited by our countrymen.

Let us consider that on this occasion, we have already made a decision and align it not only with the celebration of Language Week but also with the celebration of the Balagtas Bicentennial Year and the start of the Decade of the Centennials of Filipino Nationalism, Nationhood and The Philippine Revolutionary Movement.

With such a step, these moments will be historic and great.

I have great hope that the full blossoming of our language will not be delayed any further.

This is a unique honor for your leadership, which I am sure our countrymen will support unconditionally in all parts of the country.

One more small request: If possible, let us start speaking our own language in our respective fields.

The entire nation is watching us, especially the youth.

Therefore, let us begin this historic undertaking. Thank you very much.

Source: Presidential Museum and Library

Aquino, C. C. (1988). Speeches of President Corazon C. Aquino: June – December 1988. [Manila: Office of the President of the Philippines].
