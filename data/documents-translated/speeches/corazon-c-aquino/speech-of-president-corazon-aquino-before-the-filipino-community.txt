President Aquino, in an audience with the Filipino community, paves the way for a wider interaction of overseas Filipinos in national development as she urges them to help and rely on one another in the country’s efforts in building the nation. While she realizes that “red tape” in government is one of their complaints in coming home, the President asserts that her government is doing its best to address their problems.

I came here to pave the way for broader engagement of Filipinos abroad for the progress of our country. Not just for foreigners, but for our fellow Filipinos, wherever they may be, we should hope to participate and unite to develop our nation.

To this end, we have strived to implement changes within our government to avoid delays in serving our countrymen. It is not hidden from me that in recent years, “red tape” in the government has been a reason for the loss of your interest in returning to the Philippines. Although these changes may not yet be sufficient, we continue to undertake everything necessary for the betterment of our nation despite the current lack of our resources.

It is the duty of your government to serve and protect the citizenry, but our desired aspiration for the progress of our nation will be realized through our cooperation and unity.

Thank you very much.

Source : Presidential Museum and Library
