Ang advertising ay isang napakalakas na puwersa sa modernong lipunan. Ito ay hindi lamang tungkol sa pagbebenta ng mga produkto at serbisyo; ito ay tungkol din sa paghubog ng mga opinyon, pag-impluwensya sa pag-uugali, at pagtatakda ng mga trend. Sa pamamagitan ng mga creative na kampanya at estratehikong paglalagay, ang advertising ay may kakayahang abutin ang malawak na audience at mag-iwan ng pangmatagalang impresyon. Ito ay isang mahalagang bahagi ng ekonomiya, na nagtataguyod ng kompetisyon at nagbibigay ng impormasyon sa mga konsyumer. Gayunpaman, ang kapangyarihan nito ay dapat gamitin nang responsable, na may pagsasaalang-alang sa etika at katotohanan. Ang misleading advertising ay maaaring makapinsala sa mga konsyumer at makasira sa tiwala ng publiko. Therefore, regulation and self-regulation are crucial to ensure that advertising serves the public interest. Ang epektibong advertising ay nakakaapekto sa emosyon ng mga tao, nagpapakita ng mga aspirasyon, at nag-aalok ng mga solusyon sa kanilang mga pangangailangan. It is a complex and dynamic field that continues to evolve with technological advancements and changing consumer preferences. Sa huli, ang advertising ay isang repleksyon ng ating kultura at isang mahalagang bahagi ng ating pang-araw-araw na buhay.

President Aquino encourages the advertisers to use radio, print, and tv to bring the message of democracy to every household through jingles, plugs and subliminals.

First, I want to know how you spell “pray” – with an “e” or with an “a”?

Seriously, what we have here is the lifeblood of press freedom. If you believe in the advantages of a free press, then help the press – be prompt in paying for your ads.

The life and integrity of the press depend on it.

I congratulate the new members.

We have changed for the better and that is certainly good for our country. For better and not for worse we have to link hands in this transition period that demands the very best from all of us Filipinos.

For better and not for worse, you in advertising will expend the amount of P2 Billion in 1988. This is because the economy is expanding and all indicators point to what Filipino businessmen themselves are stating as a boom year. As the economy expands, so does advertising. The happy result is more goods, more services, and certainly more jobs to a deserving citizenry.

For better and certainly not for worse, you in advertising have an obligation to bring the message of democracy to every household. I do not ask you to wage frontal war on our common enemies on the left and right by name. But I do ask you to nudge democracy forward through your jingles, your plugs, your subliminals. For if our restored democracy does not succeed, all of us will go under.

You in advertising have unusual power. You have dominion in print, radio and tv. Use this dominion wisely. When the final word is written on EDS A and on my administration, let it be said that you in advertising delivered best what you deliver most – the goods.

Thank you.
