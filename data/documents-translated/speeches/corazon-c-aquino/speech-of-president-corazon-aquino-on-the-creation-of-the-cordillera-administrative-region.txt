AUTONOMY FOR THE CORDILLERA

Ang Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) ay binubuo ng anim (6) na probinsya, dalawang (2) lungsod, at pitumpu’t pitong (77) munisipalidad. Ito ay tahanan ng iba’t ibang ethno-linguistic groups, bawat isa ay mayaman sa kanilang kultura, tradisyon, at paraan ng pamumuhay. Sa loob ng maraming dekada, ang mga mamamayan ng Cordillera ay naghangad ng mas malawak na awtonomiya upang mapangalagaan at mapaunlad ang kanilang natatanging pagkakakilanlan at mapabuti ang kanilang kalagayan.

The pursuit of regional autonomy for the Cordillera has been a long-standing aspiration of its people. This desire stems from the recognition of their unique cultural heritage, socio-economic needs, and the limitations of a centralized governance structure in addressing their specific concerns.

Ang pagtatatag ng isang autonomous region para sa Cordillera ay nakabatay sa prinsipyo ng self-determination at paggalang sa karapatan ng mga katutubo na pamahalaan ang kanilang sarili. Ito ay hindi nangangahulugan ng paghihiwalay mula sa Pilipinas, kundi isang pagbibigay ng mas malawak na kapangyarihan at responsibilidad sa rehiyon upang magdesisyon sa mga bagay na may direktang epekto sa kanilang buhay.

Establishing an autonomous region for the Cordillera is anchored on the principles of self-determination and respect for the indigenous peoples’ right to govern themselves. This does not signify separation from the Philippines, but rather the granting of greater powers and responsibilities to the region to decide on matters that directly affect their lives.

Ang kasalukuyang panukalang batas para sa paglikha ng Cordillera Autonomous Region (CAR) ay naglalayong magbigay ng isang komprehensibo at inklusibong balangkas para sa pamamahala sa rehiyon. Ito ay naglalaman ng mga probisyon para sa pagpapalakas ng kapangyarihan ng regional government, pagprotekta sa mga karapatan ng mga katutubo, at pagpapabuti ng serbisyo publiko.

The current proposed legislation for the creation of the Cordillera Autonomous Region (CAR) aims to provide a comprehensive and inclusive framework for regional governance. It contains provisions for strengthening the powers of the regional government, protecting the rights of indigenous peoples, and improving public services.

Ang pag-apruba ng panukalang batas na ito ay isang mahalagang hakbang tungo sa pagkamit ng pangmatagalang kapayapaan, kaunlaran, at katarungan sa Cordillera. Ito ay magbibigay daan sa mga mamamayan ng rehiyon na kontrolin ang kanilang sariling kapalaran at bumuo ng isang mas magandang kinabukasan para sa kanilang mga anak.

The approval of this proposed legislation is a crucial step towards achieving lasting peace, development, and justice in the Cordillera. It will empower the people of the region to take control of their own destiny and build a better future for their children.

Granting autonomy to the Cordillera does not, in any sense, subdivide the territory of the Philippines. The aim of autonomy is the strengthening of the whole by means of strengthening of its parts. By the special attention the Cordillera will give to itself, the State becomes empowered to pursue its objective of freedom and prosperity.

I have signed this day the Executive Order creating the Cordillera Administrative Region. This is meant to pave the way for the autonomy of this region that Congress, pursuant to the Constitution, shall enact.

The intense discussions and even the tragic events that attended the formulation of this order both underscore the importance of granting autonomy and the equal importance of reaffirming the overriding duty and recognition we all owe to the one democratic State that rules all our lands.

Autonomy within the State, as the State understands it, can mean no derogation of its territorial integrity or its essential authority over all who were and shall be born under the protection of its arms, the security of its laws, and in the dignity conferred by its great charter of freedom.

This first measure of autonomy, and all other measures that shall follow upon it, cannot in any sovereign sense subdivide the territory of our State. On the contrary, the aim of autonomy is the further empowerment of the whole, by the strengthening of its parts. By the special attention that an autonomous unit of the State will give itself, we expect the State to be, overall, further empowered to pursue its timeless objectives; prosperity; and honor abroad to the one Philippines.

Long live the Republic.

Long live freedom.
