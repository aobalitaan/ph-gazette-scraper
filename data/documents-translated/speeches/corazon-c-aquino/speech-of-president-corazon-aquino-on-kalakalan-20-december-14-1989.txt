Kalakalan 20 December 14, 1989

THE IMPETUS TO RAPID DEVELOPMENT

The President congratulates the authors of R.A. 6810 or Kalakalan 20, a law which gives exemptions and benefits to Countryside and Barangay Business Enterprises. This law proves that the country’s elected representatives are sensitive to the needs of the people and are willing to respond to those needs.

I have signed into law the Kalakalan 20 bill.

As Republic Act No. 6810, the law establishes the Magna Carta for Countryside and Barangay Business Enterprises, providing them exemptions from fettering rules and regulations and granting them incentives and benefits. The singular purpose of this signal legislation is to release the energies of our people to achieve rapid development of our rural areas.

I congratulate the authors of this bill as well as the Congress itself for this innovative product of their collective efforts. This proves, once more, that our elected representatives are attuned to the needs of our people and are ready to respond creatively to those needs.

In these critical times of a national emergency, the sustained recovery and growth of our economy is crucial. This law will provide the needed impetus to accomplish this goal.

Again, heartfelt congratulations. Let us now proceed to harness the vast strength and capability of our people to surmount all difficulties and obstacles remaining in our way to a better life for all, where everybody works for the common good, where justice promotes development and prosperity will be justly shared.

Thank you and good day.
