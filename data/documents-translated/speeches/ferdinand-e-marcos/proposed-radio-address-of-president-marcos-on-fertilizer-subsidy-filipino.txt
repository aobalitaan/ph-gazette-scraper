Proposed Radio Address of the Honorable Ferdinand E. Marcos, President of the Philippines, Regarding Fertilizer Subsidies

My dear countrymen, especially the rice, corn, vegetable, and nipa farmers and other food crop growers.

The farmers should not be worried or agitated about the removal of the fertilizer subsidy because it will be done gradually or within two to three years, with the aim of increasing the support price for agricultural products in line with the additional cost of fertilizer.

And during this period of removing the fertilizer subsidy, small farmers who grow food crops will be assisted by the Ministry of Agriculture to increase the use of natural organic fertilizers and what is called azolla, which provides sufficient nitrogen needed by crops at a small cost.

I would also like to inform you that the 18% increase in the price of fertilizer on July 23, 1981 is already included in the increase in the price of palay (unhusked rice) to P1.55 per kilo from the previous price of P1.45 per kilo.

And to address this situation, I am directing the National Food Authority to purchase palay from farmers at the said price of P1.55 per kilo.

Furthermore, I still order that the NFA’s purchases be increased from 10 million cavans during the last harvest to 20 million cavans starting this September and to utilize village organizations as agents in purchasing to ensure buying from the farmers.

I reiterate that farmers should not worry because no further increases in the price of fertilizer will be made this year, and there will also be no increase in the price of palay and rice. I am directing the National Food Authority to maintain the current price of rice at P2.85 per kilo.

Thank you.
