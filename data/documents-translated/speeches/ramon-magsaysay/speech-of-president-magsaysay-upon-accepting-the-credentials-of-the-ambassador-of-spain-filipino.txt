Speech of the Honorable Ramon Magsaysay, President of the Philippines, Upon Receiving the Credentials of the Ambassador of Spain

With all our hearts, we welcome you to our country. You will find in the Philippines the former aspirations and feelings of our nation that was occupied by Spain for more than 300 years. Those same feelings and aspirations are what we use as a shield in fighting communism, just as you fought that same problem under the leadership of your valiant general, Francisco Franco.

I am aware that you will settle here as you would settle in your own country, because our customs are similar to yours due to the fact that Spain colonized us for more than 300 years. Therefore, our aspirations and feelings are not very different.

In the dark days of our history as a race, Spain left in our hearts and feelings an immeasurable strength that we are now using to fight communism.

I gladly offer high praise to Spain for its steadfast fight against communism, which we are also doing here.

I reiterate our warm welcome to our country and I greet Gen. Franco.

Source : Presidential Museum and Library

Magsaysay, R. (1954). Upon the President’s acceptance of the credentials of the Ambassador of Spain, June 15, 1954. Official Gazette of the Republic of the Philippines, 50(6) , 2431-2432.
