Address of His Excellency Ramon Magsaysay President of the Philippines In Response to the Pakistan Minister’s Address

[ Released on February 9, 1955 ]

MR. MINISTER:

I am pleased to extend to you a cordial welcome and to receive from you the letters of credence accrediting you as the first Envoy Extraordinary and Minister Plenipotentiary from Her Majesty, the Queen, on behalf of the Government of Pakistan to the Republic of the Philippines.

I note with gratification the kind words you have just said concerning my country and people. You have stressed the need for greater collaboration between our two peoples and this feeling, I assure you, Mr. Minister, is deeply shared by the Filipinos and their government.

It is also with keen interest that I have noted the determination of your Government to shoulder the burden of responsibility for the maintenance of peace in South East Asia as evinced by your prompt ratification of the Manila Pact. It is with the same deep concern for peace and security in this area that we have signed and ratified this Treaty. Permit me to assure you, Mr. Minister, that your efforts in this direction will have the constant support of my Government.

I take this opportunity to ask you to convey to Her Majesty, the Queen, and to His Excellency Mohammad Ali, the head of Your Government, our grateful thanks for these greetings as well as our best wishes for a long and happy life to Her Majesty, the Queen, and to his Excellency the Prime Minister and for the happiness of the people and Government of Pakistan.

Source: Presidential Museum and Library
