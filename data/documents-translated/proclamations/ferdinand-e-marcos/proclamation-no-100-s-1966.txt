MALACAÑANG RESIDENCE OF THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 100

REVOKING PROCLAMATION NO. 20 DATED MARCH 12, 1966, WHICH RESERVED A PARCEL OF LAND IN MALATE, CITY OF MANILA, FOR PHILIPPINE CULTURAL CENTER SITE PURPOSES AND RESERVING ANOTHER PARCEL OF LAND OF THE PUBLIC DOMAIN FOR THE SAID CENTER.

By virtue of the powers vested in me by law, I, FERDINAND E. MARCOS, President of the Philippines, do hereby revoke Proclamation No. 20 dated March 12, 1966, which reserved for Philippine Cultural Center site purposes a parcel of land of the public domain situated in the District of Malate , City of Manila, and reserve another parcel of land situated in the District of Malate , City of Manila, for said Center, which is more particularly described as follows:

For the Cultural Center of the Philippines Swo-40880

A PARCEL OF LAND (as shown on plan Swo-40880), situated in the District of Malate , City of Manila. Bounded on the NE., along line 1-2 by Roxas Boulevard ; on the SE., along line 2-3 by Public Land (Reclaimed area) and along line 3-4 by Manila Bay; on the SW., along line 4-5 by Manila Bay; on the NW., along line 5-6 by Manila Bay; and along line 6-1 by Lot 4 of plan Swo-18580. Beginning at a point marked “1” on plan, being N. 73 deg . 40′ W., 392.21 m. from C.B.M. 2, Manila Cadastre,

beginning ; containing an area of TWO HUNDRED FORTY FIVE THOUSAND SIX HUNDRED NINETY (245,690) Square Meters, more or less. All points referred to are indicated on the plan and marked on the ground as follows: points 1 and 6 by Old Corners; and the rest by Stakes; bearings true; date of the survey, August 8-11, 1966.

The area covered by Proclamation No. 20 dated March 12, 1966, is hereby reverted as Naval reservation.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done in the City of Manila, this 10th day of September, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and sixty-six.

(SGD.) FERDINAND E. MARCOS President of the Philippines

By the President:

(SGD.) RAFAEL M. SALAS Executive Secretary
