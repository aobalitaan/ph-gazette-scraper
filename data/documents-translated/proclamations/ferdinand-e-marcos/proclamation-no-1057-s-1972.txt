TANGGAPAN NG PANGULO NG PILIPINAS (OFFICE OF THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES)

PROCLAMATION NO. 1057

DECLARING THURSDAY, AUGUST 3, 1972, AS A SPECIAL PUBLIC HOLIDAY IN THE ISLANDS OF NEGROS AND SIQUIJOR AND THE CITIES THEREIN.

WHEREAS, Thursday, August 3, 1972, marks the thirtieth anniversary of the start of the resistance movement in Negros during World War II at a place now called Heroes Park on a hill in Barrio Buenavista, Municipality of Himamaylan, Province of Negros Occidental, Islands of Negros, where a brave band of resistance fighters fired the first shot against the invaders;

WHEREAS, to the people of the Islands of Negros and Siquijor comprising the Seventh Military District during World War II, this date is symbolic of the valiant and courageous spirit of the resistance; and

WHEREAS, this is the kind of spirit that our younger generation must imbibe and an example of courage that they should emulate;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, ALEJANDRO MELCHOR, Executive Secretary, by order of His Excellency, FERDINAND E. MARCOS, President of the Philippines, do hereby declare Thursday, August 3, 1972, as a special public holiday in the Islands of Negros and Siquijor and the cities therein.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done in the City of Manila, this 3rd day of August, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and seventy-two.

(SGD.) ALEJANDRO MELCHOR Executive Secretary

By the Executive Secretary: (SGD.) ROBERTO V. REYES Assistant Executive Secretary
