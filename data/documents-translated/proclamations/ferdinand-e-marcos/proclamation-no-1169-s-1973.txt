MALACAÑANG Manila

PROCLAMATION NO. 1169

DECLARING SATURDAY, JULY 28, 1973, AS A SPECIAL PUBLIC HOLIDAY.

WHEREAS, the period of voting in the referendum set for July 27, 1973, has been extended to July 28, 1973, under Presidential Decree No. 254 dated July 24, 1973, and

WHEREAS, there is a necessity to give the people the fullest opportunity to express their views on the issues to be presented to them in the referendum;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, FERDINAND E. MARCOS, President of the Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by law, do hereby declare Saturday, July 28, 1973, as a special public holiday.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done in the City of Manila, this 27th day of July, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and seventy-three.

(SGD.) FERDINAND E. MARCOS President Republic of the Philippines

By the President:

(SGD.) ROBERTO V. REYES Assistant Executive Secretary
