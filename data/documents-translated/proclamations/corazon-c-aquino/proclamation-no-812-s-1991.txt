MALACAÑANG MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 812

RESERVING FOR RESETTLEMENT SITE PURPOSES OF THE MOUNT PINATUBO VICTIMS A CERTAIN PORTION OF CLARK AIR BASE MILITARY RESERVATION LOCATED IN THE MUNICIPALITY OF CAPAS, PROVINCE OF TARLAC, ISLAND OF LUZON.

In consideration of the urgent need to assist the families affected by the eruption of Mt. Pinatubo by providing them with suitable resettlement areas and livelihood opportunities conducive to their economic recovery and upon recommendation of the Secretary of Environment and Natural Resources and the Presidential Task Force created under Memorandum Order No. 369 dated June 26, 1991, and by the powers vested in me by law, I, CORAZON C. AQUINO, President of the Philippines, do hereby reserve for resettlement and livelihood purposes, subject to private rights, if any there be, and to future classification and survey, a parcel of land segregated from the site occupied by the Clark Air Base Military Reservation situated in the Municipality of Capas, Province of Tarlac, Island of Luzon, which is more particularly described, to wit:

A PARCEL OF LAND, (being a portion of Zone “B” Clark Air Base Reservation as shown on the attached sketch plan), located at Barangay Calangitan, Municipality of Capas, Province of Tarlac. Bounded on all sides by portions of the reservation. Beginning on a point marked “1” on the sketch plan, being N. 68 deg. 08′ W., 3723.45 meters from MBM No. 1, Cut-Cut 11, Capas Cadastre, Capas, Tarlac;

beginning, containing an area of ONE MILLION TWO HUNDRED TWENTY FIVE THOUSAND AND NINETEEN (1,225,019) Square Meters, more or less.

The Presidential Task Force created under Memorandum Order No. 369 dated June 26, 1991 is empowered to issue implementing guidelines in accordance with the purposes of this Proclamation.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

DONE in the City of Manila, this 11th day of October, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and ninety-one.

(Sgd.) CORAZON C. AQUINO President of the Philippines

By the President:

(Sgd.) FRANKLIN M. DRILON Executive Secretary
