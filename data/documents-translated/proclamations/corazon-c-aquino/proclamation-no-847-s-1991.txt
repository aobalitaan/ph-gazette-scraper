MALACAÑANG Manila

PROCLAMATION NO. 847

FURTHER AMENDING PROCLAMATION NO. 823, DATED OCTOBER 30, 1991, AS AMENDED, WHICH DECLARED A STATE OF PUBLIC CALAMITY IN CERTAIN PROVINCES AND CITIES IN REGIONS I AND II AND THE CORDILLERA ADMINISTRATIVE REGION WHICH SUFFERED FROM THE EFFECTS OF TYPHOON “TRINING” TO INCLUDE THEREIN THE PROVINCES OF NUEVA VIZCAYA AND MOUNTAIN PROVINCE

WHEREAS, additional disaster reports reveals that in the Province of Nueva Vizcaya, several bridges were washed out and roads leading to or passing Dalton Pass and those going to Kayapa were rendered impassable due to land slides and road blocks;

WHEREAS, Mountain Provinces was totally isolated due to massive landslides and roadslips, power lines were knocked down, rice and lands along river banks were flooded and school buildings/residential houses were blown off;

WHEREAS, to facilitate the normalization of conditions in the typhoon-stricken areas, the National Disaster Coordinating Council recommends a state of public calamity in the two provinces;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, CORAZON C. AQUINO, President of the Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by law, do hereby further amend Proclamation No. 823, dated October 30, 1991, as amended, which declared a state of public calamity in certain provinces and cities in Regions I, II and the Cordillera Administrative Region which suffered from the effects of typhoon “Trining” to include therein the province of Nueva Vizcaya and Mountain Province which were also hit by the typhoon “Trining”.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done in the City of Manila, this 27th day of December, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and ninety one.

(Sgd.) CORAZON C. AQUINO

By the President: (Sgd.) FRANKLIN M. DRILON Executive Secretary
