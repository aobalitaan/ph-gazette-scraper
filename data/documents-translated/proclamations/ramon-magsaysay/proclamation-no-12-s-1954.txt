PROCLAMATION NO. 12

DECLARING THE PERIOD FROM MARCH 29 TO APRIL 4 OF EACH YEAR AS NATIONAL LANGUAGE WEEK

WHEREAS, the Institute of National Language was created and laws were enacted in accordance with the mandate of the Constitution to take steps towards the development and strengthening of a national language based on one of the existing native languages;

WHEREAS, the national language, Filipino, was proclaimed by virtue of Commonwealth Act No. 570 as one of the official languages of the Philippines effective July 4, 1946; and

WHEREAS, it is desired to launch with vigor the participation of all citizens in giving effect to the aforementioned constitutional mandate;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, Ramon Magsaysay, President of the Philippines, upon the recommendation of the Institute of National Language, do hereby proclaim the period from March 29 to April 4 of each year as National Language Week, which period includes the birthdate of Francisco Baltazar, the renowned author of “Florante at Laura.” I call upon all public and private schools, colleges and universities, and other cultural agencies in the Philippines to celebrate National Language Week in an appropriate manner and to demonstrate their fervent desire to strengthen more effective measures for the propagation of the national language, Filipino.

This proclamation shall supersede Proclamation No. 35 dated March 26, 1946.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done in the City of Manila, this 26th day of March, in the year of our Lord, one thousand nine hundred and fifty-four, and the eighth of Philippine Independence.

RAMON MAGSAYSAY President of the Philippines

By the President:
FRED RUIZ CASTRO Executive Secretary
