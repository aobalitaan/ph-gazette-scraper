MALACANAN PALACE
MANILA

BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES
PROCLAMATION NO. 1101

DECLARING 10 FEBRUARY 2021 AS A DAY OF NATIONAL MOURNING
OVER THE PASSING OF MANLILIKHA NG BAYAN YABING MASALON
DULO .

WHEREAS, Yabing Masalon Dulo of the Blaan community in Polomotok, South
Cotabato was declared as Manlilikha ng Bayan under Proclamation No. 126 (s. 2017) in
recognition of her commitment to the safeguarding and promotion of the Blaan Mabal Tabih
(ikat weaving) tradition;

WHEREAS, the death of Manfilikha ng Bayan Yabing Masalon Dulo is a great loss
to our nation, and it is appropriate to honor her outstanding commitment to the preservation
of the Biaan cultural and artistic heritage; and

WHEREAS, Section 27(k), Chapter 1, Rule 3 of the Implementing Rules and
Regulations of Republic Act No. 8491 or the “Flag and Heraldic Code of the Philippines”
provides that the Philippine flag shall be flown at half-mast as a sign of mourning on all
buildings and places where it is displayed, on the day of interment of recipients of national
orders and decorations;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, RODRIGO ROA DUTERTE, President of the Philippines, by
virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution and existing laws, do hereby declare
10 February 2021 as a Day of National Mourning over the passing ‘of Maniilikha ng Bayan
Yabing Masalon Dulo.

All government agencies and instrumentalities, including government-owned or
-controlled corporations, and state universities and colleges, are hereby directed to fly the
Philippine flag at half-mast from sunrise to sunset on all government buildings and
institutions on 10 February 2021.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, | have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the
Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

DONE, in the City of Manila, this 10thday of February _, in the year of Our Lord,

Two Thousand and Twenty One.
