MALACANAN PALACE
MANILA

BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES
PROCLAMATION NO. 1401

DECLARING THE MONTH OF SEPTEMBER OF EVERY YEAR AS “PHILIPPINE
BAMBOO MONTH”

WHEREAS, Section 12, Article XII of the 1987 Constitution provides that the
State shall promote the preferential use of Filipino labor, domestic materials and locally
produced goods, and adopt measures that help make them competitive;

WHEREAS, many farmers and fisherfolk in the country have long depended on
the bamboo plant for their livelihood, particularly in rural areas where the young
bamboo shoots are eaten and the mature bamboo is the ubiquitous construction
material;

WHEREAS, Executive Order No. 879 (s. 2010) created the Philippine Bamboo
Industry Development Council (PBIDC) to promote the product development of
bamboo and enhance market access to bamboo products, for the purpose of
sustaining and strengthening the bamboo industry;

WHEREAS, bamboo has been identified by the Department of Trade and
Industry as one of the eight (8) priority industry clusters;

WHEREAS, parts of the bamboo plant are now being used not only for the
nourishment of people in rural areas and the construction of simple infrastructure, but
also in producing world-class Filipino furniture and handicrafts renowned in all parts of
the globe; and

WHEREAS, there is a need to instill in the national consciousness of the Filipino
people the importance of the bamboo plant and its products;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, RODRIGO ROA DUTERTE, President of the Republic
of the Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution and existing
laws, do hereby declare the month of September of every year as “Philippine
Bamboo Month.”

The PBIDC shall lead and promote the observance of the Philippine Bamboo
Month, and identify the programs, projects and activities for the yearly celebration
thereof.

All other agencies and instrumentalities of the National Government, including
government-owned or -controlled corporations and state universities and colleges, are
