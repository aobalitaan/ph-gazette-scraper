MALACANAN PALACE
MANILA

BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES
PROCLAMATION NO. 1300

DECLARING 10-11 FEBRUARY 2022 AS BAYANIHAN, BAKUNAHAN
NATIONAL COVID-19 VACCINATION DAYS III

WHEREAS, the Bayanihan, Bakunahan National Vaccination Days on 29
November 2021 to 01 December 2021, and 15 to 17 December 2021 yielded a total
inoculation of 17,435,629 individuals;

WHEREAS, as of 08 February 2022, a total of 66,965,435 individuals or
75.02% of the target population have at least one dose, 60,451,838 individuals or
67.73% of the target population have been fully vaccinated, while 8,736,442
individuals have their booster doses; and

WHEREAS, the Bayanihan, Bakunahan National Vaccination Days | and II
became successful campaigns due to the whole-of-society and whole-of-government
approach employed by the National Government, where agencies, local government
units (LGUs), stakeholders, partners, and civil society groups, through the Bayanihan
spirit, were able to continuously mobilize communities and peoples, and relentlessly
provide essential vaccination services to them;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, RODRIGO ROA DUTERTE, President of the Republic
of the Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution and existing
laws, do hereby declare 10 to 11 February 2022 as “Bayanihan, Bakunahan National
COVID-19 Vaccination Days III.”

For this purpose, the Department of Health (DOH) and the Department of the
Interior and Local Government (DILG), in coordination with the National Task Force
Against COVID-19 (NTF), shall lead the observance of the Bayanihan, Bakunahan
National COVID-19 Vaccination Days III, and the implementation of programs,
activities and campaigns relative thereto.

The Department of Education, Department of Social Welfare and Development,
Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE), Department of Information and
Communications Technology, Department of Tourism, Department of Trade and
Industry, Department of National Defense, Department of Transportation, and their
attached agencies, together with the Metropolitan Manila Development Authority,
Presidential Communications Operations Office, Philippine Information Agency,
Technical Education and Skills Development Authority, and Commission on Higher
Education, as well as other relevant agencies, government-owned or -controlled
corporations, state universities and colleges, and LGUs are hereby directed, and
medical, nursing and allied health associations, faith-based, civil societies and non-

government organizations, and the private sector, are hereby encouraged to actively
participate and provide the necessary support and assistance to the DOH, the DILG
and the NTF for the effective implementation of this Proclamation.

The LGUs shall mobilize all resources, stakeholders, health human resource,
and constituents within their areas of jurisdiction to achieve the objectives of the
National COVID-19 Vaccination Days.

All health workers, including doctors, nurses, midwives, pharmacists, dentists
and medical technologists, are called upon to extend their full support and cooperation
in providing COVID-19 vaccination during the two-day campaign.

Consistent with existing government policies and regulations, employees and
workers of the government and the private sector, who will be vaccinated during the
aforesaid period, shall not be considered absent from their work, provided that they
present proof of vaccination to their respective employers, subject to rules that may
be issued by the DOLE and the Civil Service Commission.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, | have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of
the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

DONE, in the City of Manila, this 10th dayof February , in the
year of Our Lord, Two Thousand and Twenty-Two.
