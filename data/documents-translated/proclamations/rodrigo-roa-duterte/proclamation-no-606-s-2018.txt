MALACANAN PALACE
MANILA

BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES
PROCLAMATION NO. 606

DECLARING RAYMUNDO “RYAN” CAYABYAB, AMELIA LAPENA-BONIFACIO,
LAURO “LARRY” ALCALA (POSTHUMOUS), RESIL B. MOJARES, RAMON
L. MUZONES (POSTHUMOUS), ERIC DE GUIA AND FRANCISCO MANOSA
AS NATIONAL ARTISTS FOR 2018

WHEREAS, the 1987 Constitution of the Republic of the Philippines provides for
the patronage of arts and letters by the State;

WHEREAS, the Order of National Artists was established under Proclamation
No. 1001 (s. 1972) to give appropriate recognition and prestige to Filipinos who have
distinguished themselves and made outstanding contributions to Philippine arts and
letters;

WHEREAS, the Order of National Artists is the highest national recognition
conferred upon Filipinos who have made distinct contributions to the development and
promotion of Philippine arts and culture;

WHEREAS, the Order of National Artists is jointly administered by the National
Commission for Culture and the Arts (NCCA) and the Cultural Center of the Philippines
(CCP), by virtue of Proclamation No. 1144 (s. 1973) and Republic Act No. 7356 or the
“Law Creating the National Commission for Culture and the Arts”:

WHEREAS, Executive Order (EO) No. 236 (s. 2003), as amended by EO No.
435 (s. 2005), provides that the NCCA and the CCP shall advise the President on the
conferment of the Order of National Artists;

WHEREAS, the works and achievements of Ryan Cayabyab as a composer,
arranger, and musical director reflect a perspective of music that extols the exuberance
of life and human happiness, thus capturing the very essence of the Filipino soul:

WHEREAS, as a playwright, theater director, painter, teacher, set and puppet
designer, Amelia Lapefia-Bonifacio’s achievements have sparked today’s renaissance of
writing for children and young adults, and reanimated the world of theater and literature
for Filipino children;

WHEREAS, the works of Larry Alcala, consisting of entertaining and humorous
images of everyday Fitipino life which have been widely circulated for decades, has
served as a mirror of Filipino society, exposing society’s ills while also projecting the best
in our people and country;

WHEREAS, the works and achievements of Resil B. Mojares as a teacher,

scholar, essayist, fictionist, and historian brought about the promotion of regional
literature and history, as well as the formation of Cebuano and national identity;

WHEREAS, as a Hiligaynon poet, essayist, short story writer, critic, grammarian,
editor, lexicographer, and novelist, Ramon Muzones extended with remarkable versatility
and inventiveness the scope and style of the Hiligaynon novel, and has deeply enriched
Hiligaynon literature;

WHEREAS, Eric de Guia (Kidlat Tahimik) epitomized the possibilities of
alternative modes of filmmaking and the artisanal form of production and pioneered the
postcolonial essay film that calls on Filipinos to actively live out their independence from
western imperialism;

WHEREAS, Francisco T. Mafiosa passionately created original Filipino forms
and spaces with intricate and refined details and developed a legacy of Philippine
architecture that has greatly enhanced the Filipino identity;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, RODRIGO ROA DUTERTE, President of the Philippines,
by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution and existing laws, upon the joint
recommendation of the National Commission for Culture and the Arts and the Cultural
Center of the Philippines, do hereby declare Raymundo “Ryan” Cayabyab, Amelia
Lapefia-Bonifacio, Lauro “Larry” Alcala (posthumous), Resil B. Mojares, Ramon L.
Muzones (posthumous), Eric De Guia and Francisco Mafiosa, as National Artists, with
the privileges and emoluments attached thereto.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, | have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of
the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

DONE in the City of Manila, this 24thday of October in the year of Our Lord,
Two Thousand and Eighteen.
