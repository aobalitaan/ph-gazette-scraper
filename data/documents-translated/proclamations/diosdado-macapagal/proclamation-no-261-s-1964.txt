MALACAÑANG

RESIDENCE OF THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 261

CALLING THE CONGRESS OF THE PHILIPPINES TO A THIRD SPECIAL SESSION

WHEREAS, the public interest requires that the Congress of the Philippines be convened in a second special session in order to consider urgent legislative measures which failed of final enactment during the last special session as well as other vital subjects of legislation.

NOW, THEREFORE, I, Diosdado Macapagal, President of the Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution, do hereby call the Congress of the Philippines to a second special session commencing on June 26, 1964, and ending on July 3, 1964, to consider the following subjects:

Public Works Act

An Act to Develop the Rice and Corn Industries Towards the Level of National Self-Sufficiency and Long Range Stability, Providing Funds Therefor, and Other Purposes.

An Act to Amend Section Four of Republic Act Numbered Eleven Hundred and Eighty, Entitled “An Act to Regulate the Retail Business,” and for Other Purposes.

An Act to Amend Certain Sections of the Tariff and Customs Code of the Philippines.

An Act Changing the Date of Philippine Independence Day from July Four to June Twelve, Further Amending for the Purpose Section Twenty-Nine of the Revised Administrative Code.

An Act to Revise Republic Act Numbered Eighteen Hundred Twenty-Eight Authorizing the Development, Exploitation and Utilization of the Mineral Deposits in the Surigao Reservation, as Amended by Republic Act Numbered Two Thousand Seventy-Seven.

An Act to Amend Republic Act Numbered Three Thousand Five Hundred and Thirty-Eight Entitled “An Act to Exempt the Ford Foundation and its Grants from the payment of Gift, Franchise, Specific, Percentage, Real Property, and all other Taxes, Duties, and Fees and to Exempt Foreign Personnel Engaged in the Ford Foundation Program from the Payment of Income Tax” by extending to the Rockefeller Foundation Agricultural Development Council, Inc., and their Foreign Personnel Engaged in their Respective Programs Similar Exemptions”.

An Act Amending Subsection II of Section Four of Republic Act Numbered Thirty-One Thirty-Seven so as to Require as a Conditions for the Release from the Bureau of Customs of any Imported Materials and/or Supplies used in the Manufacture of Embrodidery and Apparel for exempt the Filing of a Cash Deposit or the Equivalent in Socio-Economic Bonds Equal to all Duties and Taxes and Other Charges Due Thereon which shall be Refunded Under Certain Conditions.

An Act Creating the National Commission on Culture.

Such other measures as may be submitted.

If Congress so desires, it may continue in session for a longer period than that herein fixed, provided it shall not exceed thirty (30) days.

All persons entitled to sit as members of the Congress of the Philippines are requestd to take notice of this proclamation.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done in the City of Manila, this 25th day of June, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and sixty-four.

DIOSDADO MACAPAGAL President of the Philippines

By the President: CALIXTO O. ZALDIVAR Acting Executive Secretary
