MALACANAN PALACE
MANILA

BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES
PROCLAMATION NO. 429

NAMING AND RENAMING THE FIVE (5) PHILIPPINE NATIONAL
POLICE (PNP) CAMPS AND REAL PROPERTIES

WHEREAS, Section 25 of Republic Act (RA) No. 6975, or the “Department of
the Interior and Local Government Act of 1990,” as amended by RA No. 8551, or the
“Philippine National Police Reform and Reorganization Act of 1998,” provides that the
Philippine National Police (PNP) shall be composed of a national office, regional
offices, provincial offices, district offices, city or municipal stations;

WHEREAS, real properties were donated to the PNP, through the Police
Regional Office (PRO) 5, in the Municipality of Pasacao, Province of Camarines Sur
and the City of Ligao, Province of Albay;

WHEREAS, the Camarines Sur Police Provincial Office and the 504t
Maneuver Company Regional Mobile Force Battalion 5 are PNP offices respectively
located in the City of Naga, Province of Camarines Sur, and the Municipality of Pilar,
Province of Sorsogon;

WHEREAS, Camp Efigenio C Navarro, also known as the PRO MIMAROPA
Headquarters, serves as one of the PNP regional offices in the City of Calapan,
Province of Oriental Mindoro;

WHEREAS, pursuant to Section 2 of RA No. 10086, or the “Strengthening
Peoples’ Nationalism Through Philippine History Act,” the State shall provide means to
strengthen people’s nationalism, love of country, and respect for its heroes and pride
for the people’s accomplishments by reinforcing the importance of national and local
history in daily life with the end in view of raising social consciousness;

WHEREAS, the National Historical Commission of the Philippines “Revised
Guidelines on the Naming and Renaming of Streets, Public Schools, Plazas, Building,
Bridges and Other Public Structures” (NHCP Guidelines) provides that all public places
may be named or renamed by the President, through a Proclamation, or by Congress,
through legislation;

WHEREAS, in accordance with Item Ill, Sub-items 1 and 2 of the NHCP
Guidelines, proposed names of public places must have historical and cultural
significance and must contribute to the development of national pride through the good
example exhibited by the name being used, and be appropriate in terms of historical
value and significance to the place to be named or renamed;

THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES

WHEREAS, it is fitting to give honor to former servicemen who have shown
patriotism, courage, and dedication in serving the provinces and municipalities to
which they are assigned, and distinguished themselves in their service to the nation,
by way of naming and renaming PNP facilities in their honor;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, FERDINAND R. MARCOS, JR., President of ‘the
Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution and existing laws,
do hereby name and rename the following PNP Camps and real properties:

a. Naming the Donated Lot to the PNP in Pasacao, Camarines Sur as “Camp
Brigadier General Ludovico Padilla Arejola;”

b. Naming the Donated Lot to PRO 5 as “Camp Captain Salvador Jaucian Del
Rosario, Sr;”

c: Naming the Camarines Sur Police Provincial Office as “Camp Colonel Juan
Querubin Miranda;”

d. Naming the 504" Maneuver Company Regional Mobile Force Battalion 5 as
“Camp Police Captain Max Jim Ramirez Tria;” and

6: Renaming Camp Efigenio C Navarro, PRO MIMAROPA Headquarters, as
“Camp Brigadier General Efigenio C Navarro.”

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, | have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of
the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

DONE, in the City of Manila, this 20th day of December _, in the year of
Our Lord, Two Thousand and Twenty-Three.
