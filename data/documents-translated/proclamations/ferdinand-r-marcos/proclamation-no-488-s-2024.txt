MALACANAN PALACE
MANILA

BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES

PROCLAMATION NO. 488

DECLARING THE MONTH OF MARCH OF EVERY YEAR AS
“NATIONAL ENGINEERING MONTH”

WHEREAS, Section 17, Article II of the Constitution provides that the State
shall give priority to education, science and technology, arts, culture, and sports to
foster patriotism and nationalism, accelerate social progress, and promote total human
liberation and development;

WHEREAS, the United Nations Education, Scientific and Cultural Organization
designated March 4 of every year as “World Engineering Day for Sustainable
Development’ to celebrate engineering and the contribution of engineers for a better
and sustainable world;

WHEREAS, the celebration of the National Engineering Month will encourage
the youth to pursue a career in Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics,
increase public awareness on the value of engineers, engineering technologists and
technicians in nation-building and development, ensure the sufficiency of engineering
and technological professionals in the country, and celebrate the unique role of
engineers in the implementation of new technologies and the application of science
and technology;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, FERDINAND R. MARCOS, JR., President of the
Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution and existing laws,
do hereby declare the month of March of every year as “National Engineering
Month.”

For this purpose, the Professional Regulatory Boards of Engineering, including
Aeronautical Engineering, Agricultural and Biosystems Engineering, Chemical
Engineering, Civil Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Electronic Engineering,
Geodetic Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Metallurgical Engineering, Mining
Engineering, Sanitary Engineering, under the Professional Regulation Commission,
shall lead, coordinate, and supervise the observance of National Engineering Month,
and identify the programs, projects and activities for the yearly celebration thereof.

All other national government agencies and instrumentalities, including
government-owned or -controlled corporations, government financial institutions, and
