MALACANAN PALACE
MANILA

BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES

PROCLAMATION NO. 1006

DECLARING THE REGULAR HOLIDAYS AND SPECIAL (NON-WORKING) DAYS
FOR THE YEAR 2026

WHEREAS, Republic Act (RA) No. 9492 dated 24 July 2007 amended Section
26, Chapter 7, Book | of Executive Order (EO) No. 292, also known as the
Administrative Code of 1987, by declaring certain days (specific or movable) as special
or regular holidays;

WHEREAS, on 17 February 2026 (Tuesday), Chinese nationals all over the
world will celebrate Spring Festival, popularly known as the Chinese New Year, which is
one of the most revered and festive events celebrated not only in China but also in the
Philippines, and such date may be declared as a special (non-working) day without
detriment to public interest;

WHEREAS, on 25 February 2026 (Wednesday), the country will commemorate
the 40 Anniversary of the EDSA People Power Revolution which ushered political,
social, and economic reforms in the country, thus, may be declared as a special
(working) day;

WHEREAS, Black Saturday, which falls on 04 April 2026, has been traditionally
declared a special (non-working) day throughout the country, the observance of Holy
Week being one of the most cherished traditions of our predominantly Catholic people;

WHEREAS, RA No. 9256 dated 25 February 2004 declared August 21 of every
year as national non-working holiday to commemorate the death anniversary of former
Senator Benigno S. Aquino Jr.;

WHEREAS, RA No. 10966 dated 28 December 2017 provides that December 8
of every year shall be celebrated as a special non-working holiday in the entire country
to commemorate the Feast of the Immaculate Conception of Mary;

WHEREAS, declaring 02 November 2026 (Monday) and 24 December 2026
(Thursday) as additional special (non-working) days throughout the country will
strengthen family ties by providing more time for the traditional All Saints’ Day, All Souls’
Day activities, and Christmas Day activities, as well as promote domestic tourism;

WHEREAS, RA No. 9177 dated 13 November 2002 declared the first day of
Shawwal, the tenth month of the Islamic calendar, as a regular holiday throughout the
country for the observance of Ejdul Fitr; and

WHEREAS, RA No. 9849 dated 11 December 2009 provides that the tenth day
of Zhul Hijja be declared as a national holiday in observance of Eidul Adha;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, FERDINAND R. MARCOS, JR.., by virtue of the powers
vested in me by the Constitution as President of the Philippines, do hereby declare:

SECTION 1. The following regular holidays and special days for the year 2026
shall be observed in the country:

A. Regular Holidays

New Year's Day - 1 January (Thursday)

Maundy Thursday - 2 April

Good Friday - 3. April

Araw ng Kagitingan - 9 April (Thursday)

Labor Day - 1 May (Friday)

Independence Day - 12 June (Friday)

National Heroes Day - 31. August (Last Monday of
August)

Bonifacio Day - 30 November (Monday)

Christmas Day - 25 December (Friday)

Rizal Day - 30 December (Wednesday)

B. Special (Non-Working) Days

Ninoy Aquino Day - 21 August (Friday)

All Saints’ Day - 1. November (Sunday)

Feast of the Immaculate Conception of Mary - 8 December (Tuesday)

Last Day of the Year - 31 December (Thursday)

C. Special (Working) Day

EDSA People Power Revolution Anniversary - 25 February (Wednesday)

D. Additional Special (Non-Working) Days

Chinese New Year - 17 February (Tuesday)

Black Saturday - 4 April

All Souls’ Day - 2 November (Monday)

Christmas Eve - 24 December (Thursday)

SECTION 2. The proclamations declaring national holidays for the observance
of Ejdul Fitr and Eidul Adha shall hereafter be issued after the approximate dates of the
Islamic holidays have been determined in accordance with the Islamic calendar (Hijra)
or the lunar calendar, or upon Islamic astronomical calculations, whichever is possible
or convenient. To this end, the National Commission on Muslim Filipinos shall

recommend to the Office of the President the actual dates on which these holidays shall
respectively fall.

SECTION 3. The Department of Labor and Employment shall promulgate the
implementing guidelines for this Proclamation.

SECTION 4. This Proclamation shall take effect immediately.

SECTION 5. This Proclamation shall be published in a newspaper of general
circulation.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, | have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of
the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done in the City of Manila, this 3rd day ofSeptember in the year of Our Lord,
Two Thousand and Twenty Five.
