MALACAÑANG Manila

PROCLAMATION NO. 397

DECLARING NOVEMBER 7, 2000 AND EVERY YEAR THEREAFTER AS A SPECIAL WORKING HOLIDAY FOR METROPOLITAN MANILA

WHEREAS, Metropolitan Manila or the National Capital Region (NCR) has been an important economic generator in the Philippines, with some parts of the region now part of the international urban economy;

WHEREAS, Metropolitan Manila is home and workplace to more than ten million Filipinos, making it the 18 th largest metropolis in the world;

WHEREAS, Metropolitan Manila remains as the seat of national governance and retains its dominance in economic activity, in availability of better health and educational facilities, as well as in cultural and recreational amenities;

WHEREAS, the public’s appreciation of the vital role of Metropolitan Manila in influencing the country’s development can be further enhanced through the commemoration of the date of the integration of its component cities and municipalities.

WHEREAS, 07 November 2000 marks the 25 th Foundation Day Anniversary of Metropolitan Manila.

NOW, THEREFORE, I, JOSEPH EJERCITO ESTRADA, President of the Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by law, do hereby declare November 7, 2000 and every year thereafter as a special working holiday in Metropolitan Manila.

To ensure effective compliance to this declaration, the Metropolitan Manila Development Authority (MMDA), the component Local Government Units of Metro Manila, the NCR offices of national and other government agencies implementing programs and projects in Metro Manila (with the MMDA as Lead Agency) are tasked to support and actively participate in the preparation and implementation of appropriate activities to commemorate the Metropolitan Manila Day.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done in the City of Manila, this 16 th day of October, in the year of Our Lord, two thousand.

(Sgd.) JOSEPH EJERCITO ESTRADA

By the President: (Sgd.) RONALDO B. ZAMORA Executive Secretary
