IN THE NAME OF THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES

PROCLAMATION NO. 174

DECLARING THE YEAR 2002 AS THE CENTENNIAL YEAR OF THE NATIONAL LABOR MOVEMENT OF THE PHILIPPINES

WHEREAS, the struggle of the Filipino worker for independence, justice and equality inspired the creation of the first national labor movement through the Union Obrero Democratica on February 2, 1902;

WHEREAS, after a century, it is fitting and proper that the nation commemorate the creation of the Union Obrera Democratica and the role of the labor movement and labor unions in nation-building and in the advancement of nationalism;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, GLORIA MACAPAGAL-ARROYO, President of the Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by law, do hereby declare the period from May 1, 2002 to April 30, 2003 as “Centennial of the National Labor Movement of the Philippines”.

Section 1. Implementing Agency. The Secretary of the Department of Labor and Employment is hereby directed to create, administer, and coordinate the implementation of the program for the year-long celebration from May 1, 2002 to April 30, 2003.

Section 2. Initial Funding. The amount of three million pesos (P3,000,000.00) shall be drawn from the “President’s Contingent Fund” (PCF) to implement the provisions of this Proclamation.

SECTION 3. Effectivity. This declaration shall take effect immediately.

DONE in the City of Manila, this 12th day of April, in the year of our Lord, two thousand and two.

By authority of the President:
(Sgd.) ALBERTO G. ROMULO
Executive Secretary
