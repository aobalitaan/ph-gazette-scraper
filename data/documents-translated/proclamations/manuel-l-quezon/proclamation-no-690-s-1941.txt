MALACAÑAN PALACE MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 690

RESERVING FOR SCHOOL PURPOSES A PARCEL OF THE PUBLIC DOMAIN SITUATED IN THE BARRIO OF NAUCONG DIOT, MUNICIPALITY OF CATAIÑGAN, PROVINCE OF MASBATE, ISLAND OF MASBATE.

Upon the recommendation of the Secretary of Agriculture and Commerce and pursuant to the provisions of section eighty-three of Commonwealth Act Numbered One hundred and forty-ore, as amended, I hereby withdraw from sale or settlement and reserve for school purposes, under the administration of the Director of Education, subject to private rights, if any there be, the following parcel of the public domain, situated in the barrio of Naucong Diot, municipality of Cataingan, Province of Masbate, Island of Masbate, and particularly described in Bureau of Lands plan Psu-108002, to wit:

Beginning at point marked 1 on Bureau of Lands plan Psu-108002, S. 57° 19′ E. 1,678.42 m., more or less, from B. L. B. M. No. 2. barrio of Placer, municipality of Cataiñgan. Masbate, thence S. 40° 29′ W. 128.10 m. to point 2; N 38 40′ W. 154.73 m. to point 3; N. 56° 43′ E. 132.90 m. to point 4; S. 36° 46′ E. 113.04 m. to point 5; S. 36° 29′ E. 4.69 m. to point 6; S. 40° 29′ W. 2.64 m. to point 1, point of beginning.

Contining an area of seventeen thousand seven hundred forty-three square meters (17,743), more or less.

Point 3, G. I. S. in “X” on tree; point 5, old G. I. S. in “X” on tree; and the rest are P. L. S. concrete monuments.

Bounded on the northeast by properties of Calle Esperanza; on the southeast, by Calle Lecoiones; on the southwest, by proposed Calle Pedrano; and on the northwest, by proposed Calle Bansoy.

Bearings true. Declination 1° 34’ E. Point are marked on Bureau of Lands plan Psu-108002

Surveyed: January 22, 1939.

Approved: July 25, 1939.

In witness whereof, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Commonwealth of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done at the City of Manila, this seventh day of March, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and forty-one, and of the Commonwealth of the Philippines, the sixth.

MANUEL L. QUEZON President of the Philippines

By the President: JORGE B. VARGAS Secretary to the President
