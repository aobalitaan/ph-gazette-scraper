MALACAÑAN PALACE MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 201

CALLING THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY INTO A SPECIAL SESSION FOR A PERIOD OF ONE DAY ON SEPTEMBER 9, 1937

By virtue of the powers conferred upon me by the provisions of Article VI, section three, paragraph three, of the Constitution of the Philippines, I, Manuel L. Quezon, President of the Philippines, hereby call the National Assembly into a special session for a period of one day on Thursday, September ninth, nineteen hundred and thirty-seven, for the purpose of considering the enactment of a bill to provide for the restoration and payment of all annuities due to persons retired under, and pursuant to, the terms and provisions of the “Teachers’ Retirement Act,” “Health Retirement Act,” and “Constabulary Retirement Act.”

In witness whereof, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Commonwealth of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done at the City of Manila, this eighth day of September, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and thirty-seven, and of the Commonwealth of the Philippines, the second.

MANUEL L. QUEZON President of the Philippines

By the President: ELPIDIO QUIRINO Secretary of the Interior
