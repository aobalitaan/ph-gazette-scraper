MALACAÑAN PALACE MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 532

REVOKING PROCLAMATION NO. 488. DATED DECEMBER 2, 1333, PROCLAIMING THE EXISTENCE OF PUBLIC CALAMITY IN THE PROVINCE OF CAPIZ.

It appearing that the conditions now obtaining in the Province of Capiz are not such as those which prevailed during and immediately after the typhoon which passed through and devastated the Province of Capiz on November 19, 1939, and which caused the existence of a public calamity in the in the aid province to be proclaimed under the provisions of Act No. 4164, I, Manuel L. Quezon, President of the Philippines, do hereby give notice that no public calamity now exists in sufficient degree to warrant the regulation of prices of articles of prime necessity and, therefore, I do hereby proclaim such emergency to be ended, effective this date.

Proclamation No. 488, dated December 2, 1939, is hereby revoked.

In witness whereof, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Commonwealth of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done at the City of Manila, this first day of April, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and forty, and of the Commonwealth of the Philippines, the fifth.

(Sgd.) MANUEL L. QUEZON President of the Philippines

By the President: (Sgd.) JORGE B. VARGAS Secretary to the President
