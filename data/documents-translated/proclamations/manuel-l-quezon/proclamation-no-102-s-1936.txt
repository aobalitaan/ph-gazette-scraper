PROCLAMATION NO. 102

EXTENDING THE PERIOD OF SPECIAL SESSION TO OCTOBER 30, 1936

Whereas, by Proclamation Numbered Ninety-five issued on October seventeenth, nineteen hundred and thirty-six, the National Assembly was called in special session for a period of six cays commencing on Monday, October nineteenth, for the purpose of considering and passing such measures as may be submitted to it by the President of the Philippines;

Whereas, by Proclamation Numbered Ninety-eight issued on October twenty-fourth, nineteen hundred and thirty-six, the said period of special session was extended to the twenty-eighth day of October, nineteen hundred and thirty-six; and

Whereas, in order to dispose of important and urgent legislation now pending consideration by the National Assembly, it is found to further extend the period of said special session;

Now, therefore, pursuant to the powers conferred upon me by the Constitution and by existing laws, I, Manuel L. Quezon, President of the Philippines, hereby extend the period of special session of the National Assembly, duly called in Proclamation Numbered Ninety-five, to the thirtieth day of October, nineteen hundred and thirty-six.

In witness whereof, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Commonwealth of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done at the City of Manila, this twenty-eighth day of October, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and thirty-six and of the Commonwealth of the Philippines, the first.

[SEAL]

President of the Philippines

By the President:

Secretary of the Interior
