MALACAÑAN PALACE MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 234

RESERVING FOR SCHOOL PURPOSES A PARCEL. OF THE PUBLIC DOMAIN SITUATED IN THE BARRIO OF CAMIGUIN, MUNICIPALITY OF CALAYAN, PROVINCE OF CAGAYAN, ISLAND OF CAMIGUIN.

Upon the recommendation of the Secretary of Agriculture and Commerce and pursuant to the provisions of section eighty-three of Commonwealth Act Numbered One hundred and forty-one, I hereby withdraw from sale or settlement and reserve for school purposes, under the administration of the Director of Education, subject to private rights, if any there be, the following-described parcel of the public domain, situated in the barrio of Camiguin, municipality of Calayan, Province of Cagayan, Island of Camiguin, and particularly described in Bureau of Lands plan Mr-384, to wit:

Beginning at a point marked “1” on Bureau of Lands plan Mr-384, N. 22° 08′ E. 409.51 m., more or less, from triangulation station Sta. Sap (U. S. C. & G. S.), barrio of Camiguin, municipality of Calayan, thence S. 10° 11′ E. 33.22 m. to point 2; S. 86° 40′ W. 92.87 m. to point 3; N. 8° 22′ W. 129.88 m. to point 4; N. 71° 51′ E. 49.62 m. to point 5; S. 31° 16′ E. 89.30 m. to point 6; S, 22° 31′ E. 31.96 m. to point 1, point of beginning.

Containing an area of 1.0637 hectares.

Points 3 and 4, galvanized iron, spikes on crosses on trees; and the rest are B. L. concrete munuments, 15 by 60 cm.

Bounded on the northeast, by properties of Crispiniano Tugade, Pancrasio Miguel, and Lazaro Delgado; on the south, by property of Francisco Reconoce; on the south­west, by China Sea; and on the northwest, by property of Valentin Abad.

Bearings true. Declination, 0° 40′ E.

Points referred to are marked on Bureau of Lands plan Mr-384.

Surveyed: February 24, 1937.

Approved: August 19, 1937.

In witness whereof, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Commonwealth of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done at the City of Manila, this twenty-third day of December, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and thirty-seven, and of the Commonwealth of the Philippines, the third.

MANUEL L. QUEZON President of the Philippines

By the President: ELPIDIO QUIRINO Secretary of the Interior
