{"translation":"Unofficial translation of the Official Gazette for advertising purposes.\n\nREPUBLIC ACT NO. 6728\n\nAN ACT PROVIDING FOR GOVERNMENT ASSISTANCE TO STUDENTS AND TEACHERS IN PRIVATE EDUCATION, AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR\n\nSECTION 1. Title. – This Act shall be known as the “Private Education Student and Teachers Assistance Act.”\n\nSECTION 2. Policy Declaration. – It is declared policy of the State, consistent with the mandate of the Constitution, to promote and make accessible to all Filipino citizens quality education. The State also recognizes from hence the complementary roles of public and private educational institutions in the educational system and the significant contribution and potential contribution of private schools to education. For these purposes, the State shall provide mechanisms to improve the quality of private education through the maximization of existing resources of private education, recognizing the responsibility of government to provide basic elementary and secondary education with priority to its function of providing for higher education.\n\nSECTION 3. Basis for Assistance. – The assistance programs shall be in accordance with established criteria which shall involve, among others, tuition fees charged by schools, the socio-economic needs of each region, the general performance of schools, the academic qualifications and financial needs of students, as well as the geographic scope and population size of students.\n\nIn addition to the foregoing criteria, within a reasonable period to be set by the Assistance Board, the student granted assistance under the Private Education Student Financial Assistance Program shall be enrolled in schools with programs that have accreditation or are undergoing accreditation in accordance with the assurance of the Federation of Accrediting Agencies of the Philippines, such as The Philippine Association of Accredited Schools, Colleges and Universities; Association of Christian Schools and Colleges Accrediting Agency; and the Philippine Association of Colleges and Universities Commission on Accreditation.\n\nPriority shall be given to students whose family income does not exceed thirty-six thousand pesos (PHP36,000.00) or the amount determined by the Board, as interpreted herein.\n\nFor purposes of this Act, assistance programs for private education students beyond secondary education shall also be extended to students of community colleges and to students of non-degree programs including vocational and technical courses. The implementation of the program shall encourage students to pursue tertiary education in the same region where their families reside.\n\nAssistance programs under this Act shall be granted only to students who are citizens of the Philippines.\n\nSECTION 4. Forms of Assistance. — Assistance to private education shall consist of:\n\n(1) Tuition fee supplement for students in private high school, including students in vocational and technical courses;\n\n(2) High School Textbook Assistance Fund: Provided, That students in public schools shall be provided with a comprehensive textbook program under the Secondary Education Development Program (SEDP);\n\n(3) Expansion of the existing Educational Service Contracting (ESC) Scheme;\n\n(4) The voucher system of the Private Education Student Financial Assistance Program (PESFA);\n\n(5) Grants of scholarships to students graduating as valedictorian and salutatorian from secondary schools;\n\n(6) Tuition fee supplements to students in private colleges and universities;\n\n(7) Educational Loan Fund; and\n\n(8) Fund for the Improvement of College Faculty.\n\nSECTION 5. Tuition Fee Supplement for Private High School Students. — (1) Financial assistance for the tuition of students in private high schools shall be provided by the government through a voucher system in accordance with the following:\n\n(a) For students enrolled in schools charging less than one thousand five hundred pesos (PHP1,500.00) per year for tuition and other fees during the school year 1988–1989 or the equivalent amount in subsequent years as periodically determined by the Assistance Board: The Government shall provide them with a voucher equivalent to two hundred ninety pesos (PHP290.00): Provided, That the student paid in the 1989–1990 school year tuition and other fees equivalent to the tuition and other fees of the preceding academic year: Provided, further, That the Government shall pay the vouchers to the concerned schools within sixty (60) days from the closing of the registration period: Provided, also, That the student’s family resides in the same city or province where the high school is located unless the student was admitted to the school in the preceding academic year.\n\n(b) For students enrolled in schools charging more than one thousand five hundred pesos (PHP1,500.00) per year for tuition and other fees during the school year 1988–1989 or the equivalent amount in subsequent years as periodically determined by the Assistance Board, no tuition assistance shall be provided by the Government: Provided, however, That the concerned schools may increase tuition fees in accordance with Section 10 herein.\n\n(2) The assistance under paragraph (1), subparagraphs (a) and (b), shall be granted and the tuition fees under subparagraph (c) may be increased, on the condition that seventy percent (70%) of the amount allocated for tuition or of the tuition increase shall go to the payment of salaries, wages, allowances, and other benefits of teaching and non-teaching personnel excluding administrators who are the major stockholders of the school, and may be used to cover increases in accordance with existing collective bargaining agreements or implemented during the time this Act was approved and made effective: Provided, That government grants shall not be directly used for the salaries of teachers of non-secular subjects. At least twenty percent (20%) shall go to the improvement or modernization of buildings, furniture, libraries, laboratories, gymnasiums, and similar facilities and to the payment of other operating expenses thereof. For this purpose, the school shall maintain a separate record of accounts for all assistance received from the government, any tuition increase, and the detailed disposition and use thereof, and such record shall be open for periodic inspection in accordance with the rules set by the Assistance Board, during business hours, by the faculty, the non-teaching staff, the students of the concerned school, the Department of Education, Culture and Sports and other concerned government agencies.\n\nSECTION 6. High School Textbook Assistance Fund. — A High School Textbook Assistance Fund shall be established in the Department of Education, Culture and Sports (DECS) to provide assistance to each student in private schools charging less than one thousand five hundred pesos (PHP1,500.00) per year in 1988–1989 or the equivalent amount in subsequent years as periodically determined by the Assistance Board, exclusively for the purchase of high school textbooks, as a means of promoting the implementation of the Secondary Education Development Program: Provided, That such fund shall not be used to purchase books that promote or suppress sectarian interests: Provided, also, That such textbooks shall be included in the list approved by the Department of Education, Culture and Sports.\n\nSECTION 7. Expansion of the Existing Educational Service Contracting (ESC) Scheme. — (a) The Department of Education, Culture and Sports shall continue to enter into contracts with private schools where the Government shall shoulder the tuition and other fees of students exceeding the capacity of public high schools under this program. It shall fulfill all existing obligations before entering into new obligations.\n\n(b) The Department shall also be required to enter into contracts with private schools in communities where there are no public high schools, and thus the Department shall shoulder the tuition and other fees of students enrolling in such private schools. The number of such schools assisted by the program shall be increased each year until all schools in this category are assisted within four years from the promulgation of this Act.\n\n(c) The amount of assistance to be provided by the Government under this Section shall not exceed the amount allocated as the cost per student in public high schools.\n\n(d) The Department shall fully pay the corresponding amount to the participating schools not later than the end of the school year, unless the delay is due to the participating schools.\n\n(e) The allocation of assistance shall be distributed to the fourteen (14) regions in proportion to the total population, and also to the population of high school age for the first school year: Provided, That starting school year 1990, an equalization program shall be implemented by the Assistance Board.\n\nSECTION 8. Assistance to First Year College Students. — (a) Private Education Student Financial Assistance Program (PESFA) Voucher System. The existing Private Education Student Financial Assistance Program (PESFA) Voucher System covering degree and vocational/technical courses shall be expanded to at least ten percent (10%) for school year 1989, fifteen percent (15%) for school year 1990, twenty percent (20%) for school year 1991, twenty-five percent (25%) for school year 1992 and thereafter, of all incoming first year students to benefit from full or partial scholarship, and allowances. Such financial assistance shall be granted to deserving and underprivileged students, to be selected according to family income, geographic location, and results of competitive examinations to be given by the Department of Education, Culture and Sports to students in all secondary schools. The program shall be equitably allocated to provinces and cities in accordance with regional and national plans for priority courses as determined by the Department of Education, Culture and Sports in coordination with the National Economic Development Authority (NEDA). The priority courses shall be submitted to Congress at the start of this program and any changes thereto periodically.\n\nFor purposes of this Act, an underprivileged student shall refer to a student whose annual total income, if any, and the combined annual total income of his/her parents does not exceed thirty-six thousand pesos (PHP36,000.00).\n\n(b) Waiver of Tuition Fees. — Private colleges and universities shall grant full or half waiver of tuition fees for five percent (5%) of all incoming first year students, including valedictorians and salutatorians of both public and private high schools charging less than one thousand five hundred pesos (PHP1,500.00) per student per year starting school year 1988–1989, or the equivalent amount in subsequent years as determined by the Assistance Board: Provided, That said valedictorians and salutatorians meet the entrance examinations and retention requirements of the concerned schools. For this purpose, the tuition fee for first year entrants in all private schools and colleges may be set by the school itself, after appropriate consultation with the parents, students and alumni of the school. For this purpose, the audited financial statement shall be open to authorized representatives of these sectors.\n\n(c) Allowances for Valedictorians. — Under rules and regulations that may be promulgated by the Assistance Board, the valedictorians identified under subparagraph (b) above, in addition to the tuition waivers provided by the concerned schools, may receive such allowances from the government as are granted to those receiving from PESFA, provided they enroll in priority courses.\n\n(d) Allowances for Other Honor Graduates. — Should the graduating class consist of more than two hundred fifty (250) students, all salutatorians and honorable mentions graduating therefrom may also receive allowances granted to the valedictorian under the preceding paragraph.\n\nSECTION 9. Additional Assistance to Students in Private Colleges and Universities. — Tuition fee supplement for students beyond the first year in private colleges and universities in programs of priority courses as determined by the Department of Education, Culture and Sports shall be provided by the government through a voucher system in the following manner:\n\n(a) For students reenrolling in priority programs in schools, charging an amount of eighty pesos (PHP80.00) per unit or less per unit or such amount in subsequent years as determined by the Assistance Board: The government shall provide the student with a voucher equivalent to the tuition increase: Provided, That all schools in this category shall not be allowed to increase their fees by more than twelve pesos (PHP12.00) per unit, both in priority and non-priority courses; and Provided, That such assistance shall be given only to students who completed one academic year as of June 1989 in priority programs and shall not apply to future college students and to current college students who transfer outside of their region.\n\n(b) For students in schools charging an amount of more than eighty pesos (PHP80.00) per unit or such amount in subsequent years as determined by the Assistance Board: The Government shall not provide assistance, and the schools concerned may set their own tuition rates, in accordance with Section 10 herein: Provided, That they shall provide a full or half waiver of tuition fees to five percent (5%) of all their students.\n\n(c) Schools with accredited programs charging a tuition rate of less than eighty pesos (PHP80.00) per unit or the equivalent amount in subsequent years as determined from time to time by the Assistance Board may continue to set tuition rates, in accordance with Section 10 herein, and students beyond the first year in their accredited priority courses may receive a voucher equivalent to the tuition increase in accordance with paragraph (a) herein.\n\n(d) Government assistance and tuition increases described in this Section shall be subject to the same conditions set forth in Section 5 (2).\n\nSECTION 10. Consultation. — In any proposed increase in tuition fees, appropriate consultations shall be conducted by the school administration with the concerned organized associations of parents and teachers and faculty organizations in secondary schools, and with the student government, alumni and faculty organizations in colleges. For this purpose, the audited financial statement shall be open to authorized representatives of these sectors. All efforts shall be exerted to reconcile any disagreement. In case of disagreement, the alumni association of the school or any impartial group of their choice shall serve as mediator.\n\nSECTION 11. Educational Loan Fund. — (a) “Study Now, Pay Later” Plan. There is hereby created a special fund to be known as the Student Loan Fund to be administered by the Department of Education, Culture and Sports, or through delegation of the Department, by the Student Loan Fund Authority created under Republic Act No. 6014 as hereby re-established in accordance with the provisions of the same Republic Act to be used to finance educational loans to cover tuition and other school fees and educational expenses for books, living expenses, and board and lodging.\n\n(b) Amounts covering tuition and other school fees shall be payable directly to the concerned school.\n\n(c) Any loan under this Section shall be repaid by the student-borrower upon completion of the course or profession financed by the loan, but after two (2) years from the time he/she obtains employment: Provided, however, That an interest rate not exceeding twelve percent per annum shall be added to the balance thereof.\n\n(d) Social Security Fund. The Social Security System shall open educational loans with low interest rates for its members and for private educational institutions for school buildings and/or improvement of their premises and facilities.\n\nSECTION 12. Limitations. — The right of any student to avail of benefits under this Act shall not apply:\n\n(a) When he/she fails for one (1) school year in the majority of his/her academic subjects enrolled in, unless the cause of such failure is a justifiable reason beyond his/her control; and\n\n(b) When he/she enrolls for the first time, or transfers, outside of the region where he/she resides unless the course he/she wishes to take is a priority course as determined by the Department of Education, Culture and Sports and is not offered in any private school in his/her region.\n\nSECTION 13. Fund for the Improvement of College Faculty. — For the purpose of improving the quality of instruction in higher education, there is hereby established in the Department of Education, Culture and Sports a Fund for the Improvement of College Faculty to provide scholarships for graduate degrees and non-degree workshops or seminars for faculty members in private colleges and universities: Provided, That the faculty member receiving such scholarship shall render three (3) years of reciprocal service for each year of scholarship received. The scholarship shall be in priority courses as determined by the Department of Education, Culture and Sports in coordination with the National Economic Development Authority (NEDA) and shall not be granted to promote or suppress sectarian interests.\n\nSECTION 14. Program Administration/Rules and Regulations. — The Assistance Board shall be responsible for the policy guidance and direction, monitoring and evaluation of new and implemented programs, and the promulgation of rules and regulations, while the Department of Education, Culture and Sports shall be responsible for the day-to-day administration and the accreditation of qualified government or private entity for its implementation.\n\nThe Assistance Board (SAC), hereinafter referred to as the Board, shall be chaired by the Secretary of Education, Culture and Sports, with representatives from the NEDA, DBM, DOST, and representatives from organized national associations of teachers, students, and school administrators as members. The last three (3) members shall be appointed by the President upon recommendation of their respective sectors for a term of four (4) years.\n\nThe Board shall meet, from time to time, as necessary, to assess the effectiveness of the programs and to ensure that the schools, colleges, and universities where the assisted students enroll continue to provide quality education. For this purpose, the Board shall establish standards, including the state of accreditation, to ensure which schools, colleges, and universities shall continue to receive government-assisted students under this Act.\n\nSECTION 15. Appropriations. — (a) The appropriations of the Department of Education, Culture and Sports authorized in the General Appropriations Act for Fiscal Year 1989, Republic Act No. 6688, for A.7.d.
