[ REPUBLIC ACT NO. 11969]

AN ACT CONVERTING THE BATAAN PENINSULA
STATE UNIVERSITY-BAGAC EXTENSION CAMPUS
IN THE MUNICIPALITY OF BAGAC, PROVINCE OF
BATAAN, INTO A REGULAR CAMPUS OF THE
BATAAN PENINSULA STATE UNIVERSITY, TO BE
KNOWN AS THE “BATAAN PENINSULA STATE
UNIVERSITY-BAGAC CAMPUS”, AND APPROPRIATING
FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the
Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the
“Bataan Peninsula State University-Bagac Campus Act”.

iS)

SEC. 2. Conversion of BPSU-Bagac Campus into a Regular
Campus. —The Bagac Extension Campus of the Bataan Peninsula
State University (BPSU) located in the Municipality of Bagac,
Province of Bataan, is hereby converted into a regular campus,
to be known as the BPSU-Bagac Campus.

SEC. 8. General Mandate. — The BPSU-Bagac Campus
shall primarily provide short-term, technical-vocational,
undergraduate, and graduate courses within its areas of
competency and specialization, pursuant to the mandate of the
BPSU, and according to its capability, as the Board of Regents,
hereinafter referred to as the Board, may deem necessary to
respond to the human resource development needs of the Province
of Bataan and the Central Luzon Region. It shall be mandated to
undertake research and extension services and to provide
progressive leadership in these areas.

SEC. 4, Supervision and Management of BPSU-Bagac
Campus.—The BPSU-Bagac Campus shall be headed by a Campus
Administrator, to be appointed by the Board subject to the
guidelines, qualifications, and standards set by the Board, and
upon the recommendation of a search committee duly constituted
for the purpose of selecting the Campus Administrator.

SEC. 5. Compliance with the Requirements of the
Commission on Higher Education. ~The BPSU shall ensure the
compliance of the BPSU-Bagac Campus with the policies,
standards, and guidelines of the Commission on Higher Education
(CHED) for the operation of regular campuses, including those
related to the offering of courses and the introduction of new or
revised programs prior to its conversion as a regular campus.

SEC. 6. Management of Personnel, Assets and Records of
the Campus. — All assets, both real and personal, personnel, and
records of the BPSU-Bagac Campus, as well as its liabilities or
obligations, shall be titled under or named after the BPSU.

oo

All parcels of land belonging to the government that shall
be occupied by the BPSU-Bagac Campus shall be the property of
the BPSU, and shall be titled under its name: Provided, That
should the campus cease to exist or be abolished or should such
aforementioned parcels of land be no longer needed by the BPSU,
the same shall revert to the concerned local government unit or
to the Republic of the Philippines, as the case may be.

SEC. 7. Suppletory Application. —The provisions of Republic
Act No. 8292, otherwise known as the “Higher Education
Modernization Act of 1997”, shall form part of this Act.

SEC. 8. Appropriations. — The amount necessary to carry
out the provisions of this Act, including those required to fund
the plantilla positions of teaching and non-teaching personnel of
the BPSU-Bagac Campus, shall be included in the annual General
Appropriations Act (GAA).

The Municipality of Bagac shall continue to provide funding
assistance for the operations of the BPSU-Bagac Campus until
such time that the amount necessary for its continued operation
and maintenance shall be included in the annual GAA.

SEC. 9. Implementing Rules and Regulations. — Within
sixty (60) days after the effectivity of this Act, the Board shall,
in consultation with the CHED, issue the rules and regulations
to fully implement the provisions of this Act.

SEC. 10. Separability Clause. —If, for any reason, any part
or provision of this Act is declared invalid or unconstitutional,
the remaining parts or provisions not affected shall remain in
full force and effect.

SEC. 11. Repealing Clause. — All other laws, presidential
decrees, executive orders, rules and regulations contrary to or
inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are repealed or
modified accordingly.

SEC. 12. Effectivity. —'lhis Act shall take effect fifteen (15)
days after its publication in the Official Gazette orin a newspaper
of general circulation.
