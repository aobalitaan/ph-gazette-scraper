AN ACT INCREASING THE BED CAPACITY OF TEODORO B.
GALAGAR DISTRICT HOSPITAL IN THE MUNICIPALITY
OF JAGNA, PROVINCE OF BOHOL, FROM SEVENTY-FIVE
(75) TO ONE HUNDRED (100) BEDS, AND APPROPRIATING
FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippines in
Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. The authorized bed capacity of the Teodoro B.
Galagar District Hospital in the Municipality of Jagna, Province of
Bohol, shall be increased from seventy-five (75) to one hundred (100)
beds. Any future increase in bed capacity shall be consistent with the
hospital development plan that shall be prepared by the Chief of Hospital
of the Teodoro B. Galagar District Hospital, and submitted to the
Department of Health.

Suc. 2. The existing professional health care services and
facilities of the Teodoro B. Galagar District Hospital shall also be
upgraded to conform with and be commensurate to the increase in bed.
capacity as provided for in Section 1 hereof.

Sic. 3.-The existing workforce of the Teodoro B. Galagar
District Hospital, which. includes doctors, nurses, and support
personnel, shall be increased correspondingly.

eC, 4, The Provincial Government of Bohol shall provide
the necessary funds to cover the maintenance and other operating
expenses of the Teodoro B. Galagar District Hospital. It shall also
appropriate funds for the additional human resource complement
to support the increase in bed capacity and the corresponding upgrading
of its service facilities and professional health care services.

Sec. 5. The Secretary of Health shall include in the
Department’s subsidy program, support for the capital outlay
requirements of the Teodoro B. Galagar District Hospital.

Sec. 6. If any provision or part of this Act is declared
unconstitutional, the remaining parts or provisions not affected shail
remain in full force and effect.

Sec. 7. All laws, executive orders, issuances, and rules
and regulations inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or
modified accordingly.

Sec. 8. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after
its publication in the Official Gazetie or in a newspaper of
general circulation.
