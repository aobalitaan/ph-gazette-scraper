[ REPUBLIC ACT NO.12212 ]

AN ACT INCREASING THE BED CAPACITY OF THE WAO
DISTRICT HOSPITAL IN THE MUNICIPALITY OF WAO,
PROVINCE OF LANAO DEL SUR, FROM FIFTY (50) TO
ONE HUNDRED (100) BEDS, AND APPROPRIATING
FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippines in
Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. The authorized bed capacity of the Wao District
Hospital in the Municipality of Wao, Province of Lanao del Sur, is
hereby increased from fifty (50) to one hundred (100) beds. Any future
increase in bed capacity shall be consistent with the hospital
development plan that shall be prepared by the Chief of Hospital of
the Wao District Hospital, and submitted to the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao ~ Ministry of Health.

 

SEC. 2. The existing professional and healthcare services and
facilities of the Wao District Hospital are hereby upgraded to conform
with and be commensurate to the increase in bed capacity as mentioned
in Section 1 hereof.

SEC. 3. The existing workforce of the Wao District Hospital,
which includes doctors, nurses, and support personnel, shall be
increased correspondingly.

SEC. 4. The Bangsamoro Autonomous Government shall provide
the necessary funds to cover the maintenance and other operating
expenses of the Wao District Hospital. It shall also appropriate funds
for the additional human resource complement to support the increase
in bed capacity and the corresponding upgrading of its service facilities
and professional healthcare services.

SEC, 5. The Secretary of Health shall include in the
Department’s subsidy program, support for the capital outlay
requirements of the Wao District Hospital.

SEC. 6. Ifany provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional,
the remaining parts or provisions not affected shall remain in full
force and effect.

SEC. 7. Alllaws, decrees, executive orders, issuances, and rules
and regulations contrary to or inconsistent with the provisions of this

Act are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

SEC. 8, This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after
its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of
general circulation.

Approved,
