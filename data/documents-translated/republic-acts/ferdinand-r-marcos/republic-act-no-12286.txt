[ REPUBLIC ACT No.1.2286 ]

AN ACT CONVERTING THE GUIMARAS STATE UNIVERSITY
MOSQUEDA AND BATERNA EXTENSION CAMPUSES
LOCATED IN THE MUNICIPALITIES OF JORDAN AND
SAN LORENZO, RESPECTIVELY, BOTH IN THE
PROVINCE OF GUIMARAS, INTO REGULAR CAMPUSES
AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippines in
Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as
the “Guimaras State University (GSU)-Mosqueda and Baterna
Campuses Act”.

SEC, 2. Conversion into Regular Campuses. — The
GSU-Mosqueda Extension Campus located in the Municipality of

Jordan and the GSU-Baterna Extension Campus located in
the Municipality of San Lorenzo, both in the Province of
Guimaras, are converted into regular campuses of the GSU.
They shall be known respectively as the “GSU-Mosqueda Campus”
and the “GSU-Baterna Campus”.

SEC. 3. General Mandate. -The GSU-Mosqueda Campus and
the GSU-Baterna Campus shall offer undergraduate and graduate
courses within their areas of competency and specialization, pursuant
to the mandate of the GSU, and according to their capabilities as the
Board of Regents, hereinafter referred to as the Board, may deem
necessary for the accomplishment of its objectives, and those which
respond to the human resource development needs of the Province of
Guimaras and the Western Visayas Region.

The GSU-Mosqueda Campus and the GSU-Baterna Campus are
also mandated to undertake research and extension services in support
of the socio-economic development of the said province and the
concerned region and to provide progressive leadership in these areas.

SEC. 4. Supervision and Management. —The GSU-Mosqueda
Campus and the GSU-Baterna Campus shall each be headed by a
Campus Director, to be appointed by the Board, subject to its
guidelines, qualifications, and standards, and upon the
recommendation of a Search Committee duly constituted for the
purpose of selecting the Campus Director.

SEC. 5. Compliance with the Requirements of the Commission
on Higher Education (CHED). —The GSU shall ensure compliance of
the GSU-Mosqueda Campus and the GSU=Baterna Campus with the
policies, standards, and guidelines of the CHED for the operation ofa
regular campus, including those related to the offering of courses and
the introduction of new or revised programs prior to the conversion.

SEC. 6. Management of Personnel, Assets, and Records. ~ All
assets, both real and personal, personnel, and records of the
GSU-Mosqueda Campus and the GSU-Baterna Campus, as well as
their liabilities or obligations, shall be transferred to the GSU.
The positions, rights, and security of tenure of faculty members
and personnel therein employed under existing laws prior to the
conversion into regular campuses shall be respected.

*

All parcels of land belonging to the government occupied by the
GSU-Mosqueda Campus and the GSU-Baterna Campus shall be
transferred to the GSU: Provided, That should the said campuses or
the GSU cease to exist or be abolished or should such aforementioned
parcels of land be no longer needed by the GSU, the same shall revert
to the local government units concerned or to the Republic of the
Philippines, as the case may be.

SEC. 7. Suppletory Application. ~The provisions of Republic
Act No. 8292, otherwise known as the “Higher Education
Modernization Act of 1997”, shall form part of this Act.

SEc. 8. Appropriations. — The amount necessary to carry out
the provisions of this Act shall be included in the annual General
Appropriations Act.

SEC. 9. Implementing Rules and Regulations. — Within
sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this Act, the Board shall,
in consultation with the CHED, issue rules and regulations to
implement its provisions.

SEC. 10. Separability Clause. — If, for any reason, any part

or provision of this Act is declared invalid or unconstitutional,

'the remaining parts or provisions not affected shall remain in
full force and effect.

Sec. 11. Repealing Clause. ~ All other laws, presidential
decrees, executive orders, rules, and regulations contrary to
or inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are repealed or
modified accordingly.

SEC. 12. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15)
days after its complete publication in the Official Gazette or

in a newspaper of general circulation.
