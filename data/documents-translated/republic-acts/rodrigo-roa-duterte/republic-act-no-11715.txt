[REPUBLIC ACT No. 11719]

AN ACT CONVERTING THE COTABATO SANITARIUM IN
THE MUNICIPALITY OF SULTAN KUDARAT,
PROVINCE OF MAGUINDANAO INTO A GENERAL
HOSPITAL, TO BE KNOWN AS THE COTABATO
SANITARIUM AND GENERAL HOSPITAL, AND
APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the
Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. The Cotabato Sanitarium in the Municipality
of Sultan Kudarat, Province of Maguindanao, is hereby converted
into a general hospital, to be known as the Cotabato Sanitarium
and General Hospital (CSGH), which shall be under the direct
control and supervision of the Department of Health DOH).

SEC. 2. The bed capacity of the CSGH shall be increased
from forty (40) to two hundred fifty (250) beds. Any future increase
in bed capacity shall be consistent with the hospital development
plan which shall be prepared by the DOH.

SEC. 3. The existing professional health care services and
facilities of the CSGH shall also be upgraded to conform with and
be commensurate to the increase in bed capacity as provided for
in Section 2 hereof.

Sc. 4. The existing workforce of the CSGH, which
includes doctors, nurses, and support personnel, shall also be
increased correspondingly. The Secretary of the DOH shall, after
consultation with the Chief of the CSGH, and in coordination
with the Secretary of the Department of Budget and Management
and the Commissioner of the Civil Service Commission, determine
the additional new positions to be created and filled up.

Sec. 5. The Secretary of Health shall immediately include
in the DOH’s program the implementation of this Act, the funding
of which shall be included in the annual General
Appropriations Act.

Sec, 6. If any provision or part of this Act is declared
unconstitutional, the remaining parts or provisions not affected
shall remain in full force and effect.

SEc. 7. All laws, executive orders, issuances, rules and
regulations inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or
modified accordingly.

SEc. 8. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after
its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of
general circulation.
