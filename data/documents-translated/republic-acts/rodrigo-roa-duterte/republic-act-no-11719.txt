AN ACT CONVERTING THE BICOL REGIONAL TRAINING
AND TEACHING HOSPITAL IN THE MUNICIPALITY
OF DARAGA, PROVINCE OF ALBAY INTO A
GENERAL AND SUBSPECIALTY HOSPITAL, TO BE
KNOWN AS THE BICOL REGIONAL HOSPITAL
AND MEDICAL CENTER, AND APPROPRIATING
FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the
Philippines in Congress assembled: ,

SECTION 1. The Bicol Regional Training and Teaching
Hospital located in the Municipality of Daraga, Province of Albay
is hereby converted into a general and subspecialty hospital to be
known as the Bicol Regional Hospital and Medical Center
(BRHMC).

SEC. 2. The bed capacity of the BRHMC shall be increased
from two hundred fifty (250) to eight hundred (800) beds. Any
future increase in bed capacity shall be consistent with its hospital
development plan which shall be prepared by the Department of
Health DOH).

SEc. 3. The existing professional health care services and
facilities of the BRHMC are also upgraded to conform with and be
commensurate to the increase in bed capacity as provided for in
Section 2 hereof.

Src. 4. The existing workforce of the BRHMC, which
includes doctors, nurses, and support personnel, shall also be
increased correspondingly. The Secretary of the DOH shall, after
consultation with the Chief of the BRHMC, and in coordination
with the Secretary of the Department of Budget and Management
and the Commissioner of the Civil Service Commission, determine
the additional new positions to be created and filled up.

SEc. 5. The Secretary of the DOH shall immediately
include in the Department’s program the implementation of
this Act, the funding of which shall be included in the annual
General Appropriations Act.

Ssc. 6. If any provision or part of this Act is declared
unconstitutional, the remaining parts or provisions not affected
shall remain in full force and effect.

Src. 7. All laws, executive orders, issuances, rules and
regulations inconsistent with this Act are hereby modified
accordingly.

SEc. 8. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after
its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of
general circulation.
