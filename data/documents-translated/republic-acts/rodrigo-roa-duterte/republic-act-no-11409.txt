AN ACT DECLARING PINSAL FALLS IN THE
MUNICIPALITY OF SANTA MARIA, PROVINCE
OF ILOCOS SUR A TOURIST DESTINATION,
PROVIDING FOR ITS DEVELOPMENT AND
APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the
Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. The Pinsal Falls in the Municipality of
Santa Maria, Province of Ilocos Sur, is hereby declared a
tourist destination. As such, its development shall be
prioritized by the Department of Tourism (DOT) and shall be
subject to the rules and regulations governing the development of
tourist destinations.

SEC. 3. Within one (1) year after the approval of this Act,
the DOT, in coordination with the concerned local government
units and agencies of the government, shall prepare a tourism
development plan involving the construction, installation, and
maintenance of appropriate infrastructure and facilities that
shall encourage tourism in the area: Provided, That said
development plan shall ensure the preservation and conservation
of the natural beauty and resources of the area: Provided, further,
That any activities and developments to be carried out in the
area should conform with the concept of ecotourism as provided
in the national ecotourism strategy jointly issued by the
Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR)
and the DOT.

SEC. 4. The DOT and its concerned attached agencies
shall take immediate steps to implement the tourism development
plan that will suit the natural characteristics of Pinsal Falls,
and shall incorporate the same in its tourism development
program for the ensuing calendar year. It shall also include
Pinsal Falls in all its national, regional and local promotion
programs.

Sec. 5. The Secretary of the DOT shall include in the
Department's program the implementation of this Act, the
funding of which shall be included in the annual General
Appropriations Act and from internally generated funds of
the DOT.

SEC. 6. Within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this

Act, the Secretary of the DOT, in consultation with the local
‘government of Santa Maria, shall promulgate the necessary
rules and regulations for the proper implementation of this Act.

SEC. 7. All laws, rules, regulations, proclamations,
executive orders or parts thereof inconsistent with this Act
are hereby amended accordingly,

 

SEC. 8. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after
its publication in the Official Gazette or in any newspaper of
general circulation.

Approved,
