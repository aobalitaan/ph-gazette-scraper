AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE SARANGANI SPORTS
TRAINING CENTER IN THE MUNICIPALITY
OF ALABEL, PROVINCE OF SARANGANI AND
APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the
Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. There shall be established in the Municipality
of Alabel, Province of Sarangani, a sports training center to be
known as the “Sarangani Sports Training Center”, which shall
be constituted as the primary sports development and training
center for the Province of Sarangani.

SEC. 2. The Provincial Government of Sarangani and the
Municipal Government of Alabel, in coordination with the
Philippine Sports Commission (PSC), shall take the necessary
steps to upgrade, improve, and modernize existing facilities,
equipment and personnel and integrate them into the newly
created sports training center.

The Provincial Government of Sarangani and the Municipal
Government of Alabel, in coordination with the PSC, shall create
the necessary training programs, appropriate tournaments and
competitions, as well as the system by which the facilities shall
be maintained and managed in order to accomplish the objectives
and purposes of this Act.

SEC. 3. The construction, establishment and operation of
the Sarangani Sports Training Center shall be funded by the
Provincial Government of Sarangani and the Municipal
Government of Alabel, and such funds as may be granted by
the PSC and by the Office of the President. In addition, grants
and endowments may be obtained from local or foreign sources
intended for sports development.

Sec. 4. The Chairman of the PSC shall immediately
include in the Commission’s program the necessary funding
requirements of the Sarangani Sports Training Center.

Src. 5. The PSC, in coordination with the Provincial
Government of Sarangani, the Municipal Government of Alabel,
and other concerned stakeholders, shall promulgate the necessary
rules and regulations for the implementation of this Act within
ninety (90) days after its enactment into law.

SEC. 6. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after
its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of
general circulation.
