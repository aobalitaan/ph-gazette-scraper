[REPUBLIC ACT No. 11082]

AN ACT UPGRADING THE SOUTHERN ISABELA
GENERAL HOSPITAL IN SANTIAGO CITY,
PROVINCE OF ISABELA INTO A TERTIARY
GENERAL HOSPITAL TO BE KNOWN AS THE
SOUTHERN ISABELA MEDICAL CENTER AND
APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the
Philippines in Congress assembled:

gucrion 1. The Southern {sabela General Hospital in.
Santiago City, Province of Isabela is hereby upgraded into a
tertiary general hospital to be known as the Southern Isabela
Medical Center (SIMC) which shall be under the direct
technical and administrative supervision of the Department

of Health (DOH).

Src. 2. The authorized bed capacity of the SIMC is
hereby increased from fifty (50) to three hundred fifty
(350) beds.

The Secretary of the DOH shall implement the increase
in bed capacity of the SIMC over a period of five (5) years from
the approval of this Act.

Sec. 3. The existing service capability, professional health
care services and health human resource of the SIMC shall be
upgraded and increased to conform with and be commensurate
to the increase in authorized bed capacity as herein provided.

The Secretary of the Department of Budget and
Management (DBM) shall create regular plantilla positions for
health personnel required of a medical center, in consultation
with the DOH, with the corresponding increases in the
maintenance and other operating expenses (MOOEF) and
personal services (PS) for the SIMC.

Sec. 4. The Secretary of Health shall immediately
include in the Department’s programs the implementation of
this Act, the funding of which shall be included in the annual
General Appropriations Act,

SBC. 5. Within ninety (90) days from the approval of
this Act, the Secretary of the DOH, in consultation with the
Secretary of the DBM, shall promulgate the necessary rules
and regulations to implement the provisions of this Act.

SEC. 6. Republic Act No. 3138, establishing the Southern
Isabela Emergency Hospital, and Republic Act No. 8338,
upgrading it to Southern Isabela General Hospital, are hereby
repealed. All other laws, decrees, orders, issuances, rules and
regulations and parts thereof which are inconsistent with
the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed, amended or
modified accordingly.

   

SEC. 7. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after
its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of
general circulation.
