[REPUBLIC Act No. 11221)

AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE CITY PROSECUTION
SERVICE IN THE CITY OF NAVOTAS, AMENDING
FOR THE PURPOSE SECTION 55 OF REPUBLIC ACT
NO. 9387, OTHERWISE KNOWN AS THE “CHARTER
OF THE CITY OF NAVOTAS”

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the
Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. Section 55 of Republic Act No. 9387,
otherwise known as the “Charter of the City of Navotas”,
is hereby amended to read as follows:

“Sec. 55. The Office of the City Prosecution
Service and Public Schools Division. —

“(a) There shall be established in the City,
a prosecution service to be headed by a city prosecutor
and such number of assistant city prosecutors as

may be necessary, who shall form part of the
Department of Justice (DOJ) and be under the
supervision and control of the Secretary of
Justice, and whose qualifications, manner of
appointment and rank, salary and benefits shall
be governed by existing laws covering prosecutors
in the DOJ.

“b) The city prosecutor shall handle the
prosecution of cases and perform such other
functions as provided by law and its implementing
rules and regulations. The existing City Prosecution
Service in the City of Malabon shall continue
to function pursuant to existing laws under
the supervision of the city prosecutor.

“The Secretary of Justice shall always
ensure the adequacy and the quality of prosecution
service in the City of Navotas and, for this
purpose, shall, in the absence, lack or insufficiency
in the number of assistant city prosecutors as
provided hereinabove, designate from among the
assistant provincial prosecutors a sufficient
number to perform and discharge the functions
of the City Prosecution Service.

“Likewise, the public schools division for the
cities of Malabon and Navotas shall continue
to administer public education in both local
government units.”

Src. 2. The Department of Justice and the Department
of Budget and Management are hereby directed to implement
this Act.

Src. 3. All laws, decrees, executive orders, rules and
regulations which are inconsistent with this Act are hereby
amended, repealekor modified accordingly.

SEc. 4. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its
publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of general
circulation.
