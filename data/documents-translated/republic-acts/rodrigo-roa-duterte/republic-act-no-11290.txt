AN ACT INCREASING THE BED CAPACITY OF THE
MARIANO MARCOS MEMORIAL HOSPITAL AND
MEDICAL CENTER (MMMH&MC), BATAC CITY,
ILOCOS NORTE FROM TWO HUNDRED (200) BEDS
TO SEVEN HUNDRED (700) BEDS, UPGRADING ITS
SERVICES, FACILITIES, AND PROFESSIONAL
HEALTH CARE, AUTHORIZING THE INCREASE OF
ITS MEDICAL AND NON-MEDICAL PERSONNEL AND
APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the
Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. Increase in Authorized Capacity. - The
authorized bed capacity of the Mariano Marcos Memorial
Hospital and Medical Center (MMMH&MC), Batac City, Ilocos
Norte is hereby upgraded from two hundred (200) to seven
hundred (700) beds. The increase in bed capacity of
MMMH&MC shall be implemented consistent with the five
(5)-year development plan of the hospital.

SEC. 2. Upgrade of Existing Service Facilities. — The
existing service facilities and the professional health services
of the MMMH&MC are hereby upgraded to conform with and
be commensurate to the bed capacity increase as provided for
in Section 1 hereof.

SEC. 3. Upgrade of Existing Professional Health Care
Services. — The existing workforce and personnel, medical and
non-medical, of the MMMH&MC shall also be increased. The
Department of Health (DOH), in coordination with the
Department of Budget and Management (DBM) and the Civil
Service Commission (CSC), shall determine the additional
plantilla positions to be created and filled up upon prior
consultation with the MMMH&MC.

SEC. 4. Appropriation. — The Secretary of Health shall
immediately include in the Department’s program the
implementation of this Act, the funding of which shall be
included in the annual General Appropriations Act (GAA).

SEC. 5. Separability Clause. — If any provision or part
of this Act is declared unconstitutional the remaining parts
or provisions not affected shall remain in full force and effect.

SEC. 6. Repealing Clause. - All other laws, decrees,
orders, issuances, rules and regulations, or parts thereof which
are inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby
repealed, amended or modified accordingly.

SEC. 7. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect fifteen
(15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or in a

newspaper of general circulation.
