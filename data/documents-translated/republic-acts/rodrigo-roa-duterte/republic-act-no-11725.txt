[ REPUBLIC ACT No. 11725]

AN ACT CONVERTING THE DON JOSE S. MONFORT
MEDICAL CENTER EXTENSION HOSPITAL IN THE
MUNICIPALITY OF BAROTAC NUEVO, PROVINCE OF
ILOILO, INTO A TERTIARY HOSPITAL TO BE KNOWN
AS THE DON JOSE S. MONFORT MEDICAL CENTER,
AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the
Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. The Don Jose S. Monfort Medical Center
Extension Hospital in the Municipality of Barotac Nuevo, Province
of Iloilo, is hereby converted into a tertiary hospital to be known
as the Don Jose S. Monfort Medical Center, which shall be under
the direct control and supervision of the Department of Health
(OH).

Src. 2. The authorized bed capacity of the Don Jose S.
Monfort Medical Center shall be increased from one hundred (100)
to three hundred (300) beds. Any future increase in bed capacity
shall be consistent with the hospital development plan that shall
be prepared by the Chief of Hospital of the Don Jose S. Monfort
Medical Center and submitted to the DOH.

Sec. 3. The existing professional health care services,
_ equipment, and facilities of the Don Jose S. Monfort Medical Center
shall also be upgraded to conform with and be commensurate to
the increase in bed capacity as provided for in Section 2 hereof.

SEC. 4. The existing workforce of the Don Jose S. Monfort
Medical Center, which includes doctors, nurses and support
personnel, shall be increased correspondingly.

SEc. 5. The Secretary of the DOH shall, after consultation
with the Chief of Hospital of the Don Jose S. Monfort Medical
Center, and in coordination with the Secretary of the Department
of Budget and Management and the Chairperson of the Civil
Service Commission, determine the additional new positions to
be created and filled up.

SEc. 6. The Secretary of the DOH shall immediately
include in the Department’s programs the implementation of this
Act, the funding of which shall be included in the annual General
Appropriations Act.

Src. 7. All laws, decrees, executive orders, and other
presidential issuances which are inconsistent with this Act are
hereby repealed, amended or modified accordingly.

SEc. 8. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its
publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of general

     

circulation.
