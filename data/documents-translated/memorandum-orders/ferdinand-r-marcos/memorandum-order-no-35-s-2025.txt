MEMORANDUM ORDER NO. 35

FURTHER AMENDING MEMORANDUM ORDER NO. 109 (S. 2000),
AS AMENDED, ENTITLED, “ESTABLISHING A PROVIDENT FUND
FOR THE OFFICE OF THE PRESIDENT PROPER PERSONNEL”

WHEREAS, Memorandum Order (MO) No. 109 (s. 2000) authorized the
establishment of the Office of the President (OP) Proper Provident Fund to provide
supplementary benefits to and improve the quality of life, work and general welfare of
the member-employees of the OP Proper;

WHEREAS, MO No. 16 (s. 2011) amended MO No. 109 by reconstituting the
membership of the Board of Trustees of the OP Proper Provident Fund to improve the
efficiency and effectiveness of managing the affairs, business, and properties of the
Fund; and

WHEREAS, it is imperative to expand the membership coverage of the OP
Proper Provident Fund in recognition of the invaluable services of government
employees who work under the OP Proper and play a pivotal role in fulfilling the critical
mandates of the OP;

NOW, THEREFORE, |, FERDINAND R. MARCOS, JR., President of the
Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution and existing laws,
do hereby order:

Section 1. Amendment to MO No. 109. Section 2 of MO No. 109 is hereby
amended to read as follows:

Sec. 2. Membership — All permanent and regular officials and employees
of the OP-Proper, as well as employees whose salaries are charged
against lump-sum funds, are eligible to be members of the OP Provident
Fund: Provided, that, membership thereof shall continue until the
member retires, resigns or is separated from the service for cause, or
upon transfer to another office, or upon his death: Provided, further,
that, seconded civilian personnel to the OP Proper from other
government agencies, shall be eligible for membership in the OP
Proper Provident Fund, subject to the following:

1. Such seconded personnel may join and remit their

contributions to the OP Proper Provident Fund on a voluntary
basis;

2. Membership in the OP Proper Provident Fund shall continue
only during the period of their secondment to the OP Proper,
including renewals or extensions thereof;

3. The seconded personnel is not a member of any Provident Fund
or its equivalent in his/her parent agency; and

4. Such seconded personnel shall be subject to the same benefits,
qualifications, disqualifications, and other conditions imposed
on all members of the OP Proper Provident Fund.

Section 2. Implementing Guidelines. The Board of Trustees shall issue rules
and regulations necessary to effectively implement this Order.

Section 3. Repeal. MO No. 109 is hereby amended. All other orders, rules and
regulations and issuances or parts thereof, which are inconsistent with the provisions
of this Order, are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

Section 4. Effectivity. This Order shall take effect immediately.

DONE, in the City of Manila, this 24th day of March , in the
year of our Lord, Two Thousand and Twenty-Five.
