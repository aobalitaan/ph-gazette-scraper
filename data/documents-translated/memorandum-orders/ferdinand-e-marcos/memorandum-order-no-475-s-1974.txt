TANGGAPAN NG PANGULO NG PILIPINAS (OFFICE OF THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES)

MEMORANDUM ORDER NO. 475

CONSTITUTING THE PHILIPPINE PANEL OF DELEGATE TO THE 8TH SESSION OF THE FAO INTERGOVERNMENTAL GROUP ON HARD FIBERS, JANUARY 20-25, 1975, IN MANILA.

WHEREAS, it will be recalled that due to the Philippine request for a containing review of the world’s hard fiber market in 1955, the FAO Intergovernmental Group in Hard Fibers was finally established in 1966;

WHEREAS, even as abaca has always been one of the important items discussed at the Group’s session since its inception in 1966, it failed to sustain any international cooperation and commitment of assistance from any of the FAO member counties;

WHEREAS, the awakening of the world to the crisis of abaca fortunately came in the wake of the establishment of the New Society in the Philippines;

WHEREAS, come January 20-25, 1975, the Philippines will host the 8th Session of the FAO Intergovernmental Group on Hard Fibers in Manila;

WHEREAS, the forthcoming Session, the first ever held in Asia and the second only outside of Rome, would serve as an appropriate and opportune venue through which the Philippine government could solidify domestic efforts to put the international affairs of abaca and of the other commercial fibers, including their by-products, in their proper perspective.
