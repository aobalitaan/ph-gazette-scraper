Office of the President of the Philippines

MEMORANDUM ORDER NO. 73

AMENDING MEMORANDUM ORDER NO. 102. SERIES OF 1964, BY PRESCRIBING A NEW UNIFORM FOR LADY EMPLOYEES IN THE OFFICE OF THE PRESIDENT.

Memorandum Order No. 102 dated September 11, 1964, requiring all lady employees in the Office of the President to wear the uniform therein prescribed during attendance in office, is hereby amended so as to prescribe a new uniform.

The new uniform-shall be in the shift style with jacket as shown in the attached sketch with the following specifications: the basic dress shall be in tetoron-polynosic fabric, plain pink; the jacket shall be of the same material in white with pink polka dots, with hem line 4 inches from waist line, open front with plain white lining material, and 3/4 length sleeves without lining.

The exemption from wearing the prescribed uniform previously granted to lady lawyers is hereby withdrawn. All concerned shall be in uniform not later than two weeks from the date of this Order. Newly appointed lady employees shall be in the prescribed uniform not later than 15 days from the date of assumption of office.

Materials for three suits each will be initially furnished by this Office free of charge to all lady employees in the office roster on June 30, 1967. Lady employees appointed thereafter shall procure their own materials .at their expense.

By authority of the President: (Sgd.) RAFAEL M. SALAS Executive Secretary

Manila, August 11, 1967

Reference: New Uniforms for Ladies
