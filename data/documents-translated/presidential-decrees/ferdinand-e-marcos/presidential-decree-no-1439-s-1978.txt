CREATING ADDITIONAL BRANCHES OF THE COURTS OF FIRST INSTANCE AND CITY COURTS, AND NEW AND ADDITIONAL JUVENILE AND DOMESTIC RELATIONS COURTS, IN CERTAIN PROVINCES AND CITIES.

WHEREAS, the rapid growth and population and the consequent rise in the number of cases have increased the caseloads of the Courts of First Instance and City Courts in certain provinces and cities to such an extent that they are not able to expeditiously dispose of the many cases filed with said courts;

WHEREAS, such growth of population has also given rise to the numerous juvenile and domestic relations problems in certain cities and provinces;

WHEREAS, in order to make justice available promptly and expeditiously, it is necessary to create additional sales where needed, to restructure existing courts, and to create new or additional Juvenile and Domestic Relations Courts in areas where juvenile and domestic problems are concentrated;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, FERDINAND E. MARCOS, President of the Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution, do hereby decree, order and make as part of the law of the land the following:

Section 5. Special Provisions on Juvenile and Domestic Relations Courts.

(a) In provinces and cities where there are no Juvenile and Domestic Relations Courts, the Supreme Court, when so warranted, shall designate and authorize any branch of the Court of First Instance therein to function as Juvenile and Domestic Relations Court.

(b) The Juvenile and Domestic Relations Courts stationed in the Cities of Manila, Quezon, Caloocan and Pasay shall have jurisdiction over all cases within the entire Metro Manila area and the Supreme Court shall define their respective territorial jurisdiction.

(c) The existing Criminal, Juvenile and Domestic Relations Court of Dumaguete City is converted into a regular Juvenile and Domestic Relations Court with jurisdiction over all cases in the province of Negros Oriental and the Cities of Dumaguete, Bais and Canlaon and the incumbent presiding judge of the former court as now converted shall continue as judge without the need of a new appointment. The pending criminal cases of the former court which do not pertain to the jurisdiction of Juvenile and Domestic Relations Courts shall be transferred to the Regular City Courts.

Section 6. Court Personnel Equipment And Facilities. The additional courts herein created shall each be provided with the necessary personnel, equipment and facilities to enable them to function as soon as the judges therein are appointed.

Section 7. Transitory Provisions.

(a) Upon appointment of a judge or judges to a newly created branch or branches of a Court of First Instance or City Courts, all pending cases in the branches pertaining to one administrative area in which the trial has not commenced shall be equitably distributed among all said branches in accordance with such rules and regulations as the Supreme Court shall prescribe: PROVIDED, HOWEVER, That all cases pending before any existing branch, the trial of which has commenced, shall remain therein until final disposition.

(b) Upon the organization of the Juvenile and Domestic Relations Courts herein created, the Supreme Court shall cause all cases and proceedings pending before any court which are properly cognizable by the Juvenile and Domestic Relations Courts to be transferred thereto.

Section 8. Appropriations. The sums of P19,000,000.00 for Courts of First Instance, P2,000,000.00 for City Courts and P3,000,000.00 for Juvenile and Domestic Relations Courts or so much thereof as may be necessary to carry out the purposes of this Decree are hereby authorized to be appropriated. The appropriation for the succeeding years shall be included in the Annual General Appropriations Decree.

Section 9. Repealing Clause. All laws, decrees and orders of any part thereof which are inconsistent with the provisions of this Decree are hereby repealed and/or modified accordingly.

Section 10. Effectivity. This Decree shall take effect immediately.
