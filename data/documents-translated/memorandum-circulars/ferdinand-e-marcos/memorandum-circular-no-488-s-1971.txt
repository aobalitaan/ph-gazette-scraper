MEMORANDUM CIRCULAR NO. 488

ORDERING ALL GOVERNMENT OFFICES TO HOLD PROGRAMS IN CELEBRATION OF NATIONAL LANGUAGE WEEK

Proclamation No. 186, series of 1955, provides that the annual celebration of National Language Week is from August 13-19.

The National Language is an important component of true patriotic spirit, aside from being necessary for the direct, easy and practical means of communication of the government and the common citizens, in addition to being the lightest instrument that can be used in the social relations of the largest part of the nation.

Because of this, from now on and until the time when this type of celebration is no longer needed because the Filipino Language has reached its peak, all heads of departments, bureaus, offices, and other branches of the national and local government, together with corporations owned or controlled by the government, are ordered to hold programs in the Filipino Language for at least one hour, on any day included in the week of celebration. Any topic related to true Filipino freedom and independence may be discussed in the Filipino Language. The leaders and personnel of the government, whether legislative, judicial or executive branches, should properly lead in holding simple programs in their respective offices.

This Order shall take effect during this year’s Language Week, August 13-19, 1971.

By order of the President: (SIGNED) ALEJANDRO MELCHOR Executive Secretary

Manila, July 29, 1971
