MEMORANDUM CIRCULAR NO. 443

ORDERING GOVERNMENT OFFICES TO HOLD PROGRAMS IN COMMEMORATION OF THE 183RD BIRTHDAY OF FRANCISCO (BALAGTAS) BALTAZAR, ON APRIL 2, 1971

Francisco Baltazar, more popularly known as Balagtas, author of the immortal Florante at Laura, is one of the nation’s heroes. Considered a pioneer of the Propaganda Movement, he depicted in his poetry the characteristics of his country and encapsulated therein his Four Revolts: against a corrupt government, ostentatious religion, the bad customs of society, and the low quality of literature, all of which remain relevant to the present day.

THEREFORE, all heads of departments, bureaus, offices and other branches of the national and local governments, as well as government-owned or controlled corporations, are hereby ordered to hold a literary-musical program in Filipino, even for just thirty (30) minutes, on April 2, in commemoration of the 183rd birthday of Francisco (Balagtas) Baltazar, in a manner that emphasizes the lessons contained in his work.

By order of the President:

Manila, March 4, 1971
