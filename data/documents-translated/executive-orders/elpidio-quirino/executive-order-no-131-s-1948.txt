MALACAÑANG MANILA

EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 131

ORGANIZING THE SITIO OF BAYONG OF THE BARRIO OF LUNGSUDAAN, MUNICIPALITY OF SIERRA-BULLONES, PROVINCE OF BOHOL, INTO AN INDEPENDENT BARRIO UNDER THE NAME OF BAYONG

Upon the recommendation of the Secretary of the Interior, and pursuant to the provisions of section sixty-eight of the Revised Administrative Code, the sitio of Bayong of the barrio of Lungsudaan, municipality of Sierra-Bullones, Province of Bohol, is hereby segregated from said barrio and organized into an independent barrio under the name of Bayong. The barrio of Bayong as herein constituted shall consist of the area included in the following boundaries:

From point 1, which is a Balete tree about 120 meters southwest of the intersection of the barrio road connecting the sitio, now barrio, of Bayong with the provincial road and a creek without a name emptying into the Abaca creek, as shown in the sketch map of the proposed barrio of Bayong and its mother barrio Lungsudaan, Sierra-Bullones, Bohol Province, prepared and submitted February 10, 1947 by the district engineer of Bohol, in a straight line due N. 32°E. to point 2, which is a Gakit tree on the south bank of the Abaca creek; thence southeastward meandering and following the course of this creek to point 3, which is a Pili tree located somewhere east of the east bank of the most south-easterly portion of this creek; thence S. 36°W. in a straight line to point 4, which is a Tawoto tree; thence N. 61°W. on Canlomain hill in a straight line to point 5, which is a Tugas tree; thence N. 15°E. in a straight line to the point of beginning.

Done in the City of Manila, this 21st day of April, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and forty-eight, and of the Independence of the Philippines, the second.

(Sgd.) ELPIDIO QUIRINO President of the Philippines

By the President:

(Sgd.) EMILIO ABELLO Executive Secretary
