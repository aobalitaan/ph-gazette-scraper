BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES

EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 187

DIRECTING ALL DEPARTMENTS, BUREAUS, OFFICES AND OTHER BRANCHES OF THE GOVERNMENT TO USE THE FILIPINO LANGUAGE AS MUCH AS POSSIBLE DURING NATIONAL LANGUAGE WEEK AND THEREAFTER, IN ALL OFFICIAL GOVERNMENT COMMUNICATION AND TRANSACTIONS

WHEREAS, the development and propagation of a national Philippine language as provided for in the Constitution and Commonwealth Act No. 570 is one of the primary objectives of this administration; and

WHEREAS, our national language, now accepted and recognized by all as “Pilipino,” is one of the essential components of nationalism that can lead our country to greater progress, stability and unity;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, FERDINAND E. MARCOS, President of the Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by law and in furtherance of the objectives of the Constitution and Commonwealth Act No. 570, do hereby order and declare that the Filipino language shall be used as much as possible, in all departments, bureaus, offices and other branches of the government during National Language Week and also thereafter, in all government communication and transactions.

DONE in the City of Manila, this 6th day of August, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and sixty-nine.

(SGD) FERDINAND E. MARCOS
President of the Philippines

By the President:

(SGD) ERNESTO M. MACEDA
Executive Secretary

Executive Order No. 187, August 6, 1969
