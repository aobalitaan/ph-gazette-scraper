MALACANAN PALACE
MANILA

BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES

EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 63

REVOKING THE POLICY OF GRANTING CAREER EXECUTIVE
SERVICE RANK TO GRADUATES OF THE NATIONAL DEFENSE
COLLEGE OF THE PHILIPPINES

WHEREAS, Section 2, Article IX-B of the Constitution provides that
appointments in the civil service shall be made only according to merit and fitness to
be determined, as far as practicable, and except to positions which are
policy-determining, primarily confidential, or highly technical, by competitive
examination;

WHEREAS, Presidential Decree No. 1 (s. 1972) mandated the Career
Executive Service Board to promulgate rules, standards and procedures on the
selection, classification, compensation and career development of members of the
Career Executive Service;

WHEREAS, Section 8, Chapter II, Book V of Executive Order (EO) No. 292 or
the “Administrative Code of 1987” states that the entrance to the third level (Career
Executive Service [CES]) positions shall be prescribed by the Career to Executive
Service Board (CESB);

WHEREAS, EO No. 696 (s. 1981), as amended by EO Nos. 771 (s. 1982) and
145 (s. 2021), provides that graduates of the Master in National Security Administration
(MNSA) program of the National Defense College of the Philippines (NDCP) who are
appointed to CES positions shall be granted CES rank that is commensurate to their
respective CES positions, subject to the requirements to be prescribed and mandated
by the CESB;

WHEREAS, pursuant to Memorandum Circular (MC) No. 35 (s. 2023), on 22
February 2024, the CESB recommended to revoke the policy of granting CES rank to
the graduates of MNSA Program of the NDCP to synchronize the standards and
procedures for the conferment of CES rank; and

WHEREAS, Section 17, Article VII of the Constitution vests in the President the

power of control over all Executive departments, bureaus and offices, and the mandate
to ensure the faithful execution of laws;

THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES

NOW, THEREFORE, I, FERDINAND R. MARCOS, JR., President of the
Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution and existing laws,
do hereby order:

Section 1. Revocation. The policy granting CES eligibility and rank to
graduates of the MNSA program of the NDCP under EO No. 145, is hereby revoked.
As may be necessary, the CESB, in coordination with concerned government
agencies, shall formulate and issue guidelines for the effective implementation of this

Order.

Section 2. Repeal. Sections 1 and 2 of EO No.145 are hereby repealed. All
other orders, rules and regulations, and other issuances or parts thereof which are
inconsistent with the provisions of this Order are hereby repealed or modified
accordingly.

Section 3. Effectivity. This Order shall take effect immediately upon its
publication in the Official Gazette or a newspaper of general circulation.

DONE, in the City of Manila, this 2nd day of July , in the
year of our Lord, Two Thousand and Twenty-Four.

By the President:
