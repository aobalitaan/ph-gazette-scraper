H. No. 9361

Republic of the Philippines Congress of the Philippines Metro Manila

Second Regular Session

Begun and held in Metro Manila, on Monday, the twenty-sixth day of July, nineteen hundred and ninety-three.

[REPUBLIC ACT NO. 7724]

AN ACT DECLARING OCTOBER NINETEEN OF EVERY YEAR A SPECIAL NONWORKING PUBLIC HOLIDAY IN THE CITY OF BACOLOD TO BE KNOWN AS “BACOLOD CITY CHARTER DAY”

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. October Nineteen is the Charter Day Anniversary of the City of Bacolod and its celebration is highlighted with the staging of the now famous Maskara Festival.

SEC. 2. October Nineteen of every year is hereby declared a special nonworking public holiday in the City of Bacolod.

SEC 3. This Act shall take effect upon its approval.

Approved,

(Sgd.) EDGARDO J. ANGARA President of the Senate

(Sgd.) JOSE DE VENECIA, JR. Speaker of the House of Representatives

This Act which originated in the House of Representatives was finally passed by the House of Representatives and the Senate on August 3, 1993 and March 3, 1994, respectively.

(Sgd.) EDGARDO E. TUMANGAN Secretary of the Senate

(Sgd.) CAMILO L. SABIO Secretary General House of Representatives

Approved: 19 MAY 1994

(Sgd.) FIDEL V. RAMOS President of the Philippines
