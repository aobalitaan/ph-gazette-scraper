H. No. 8443

Republic of the Philippines Congress of the Philippines Metro Manila

Second Regular Session

Begun and held in Metro Manila, on Monday, the twenty-sixth day of July, nineteen hundred and ninety-three.

[REPUBLIC ACT NO. 7794]

AN ACT RENAMING THE LIGAO TECHNCAL VOCATIONAL HIGH SCHOOL TO LIGAO NATIONAL HIGH SCHOOL IN THE MUNICIPALITY OF LIGAO, PROVINCE OF ALBAY, AND APPROPRIATING FUND THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. The Ligao Technical-Vocational High School in the Municipality of Ligao, Province of Albay, is hereby renamed as Ligao National High School.

SEC. 2. The amount necessary for the implementation of this Act shall be charged to the appropriations of the Department of Education, Culture and Sports. Thereafter, such sums as may be deemed necessary for its implementation and maintenance shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act.

SEC. 3. This Act shall take effect immediately upon its approval.

Approved,

(Sgd.) EDGARDO J. ANGARA President of the Senate

(Sgd.) JOSE C. DE VENECIA, JR. Speaker of the House of Representatives

This Act which originated in the House of Representatives was finally passed by the House of Representatives and the Senate on August 11, 1993 and April 28, 1994, respectively.

Approved:

FIDEL V. RAMOS President of the Philippines

Lapsed into the law without the signature of the President on August 8, 1994, in accordance with Article VI, Section 27 (1) of the Constitution.
