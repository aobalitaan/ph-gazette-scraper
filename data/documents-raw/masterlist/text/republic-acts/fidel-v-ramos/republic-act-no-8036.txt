Republic of the Philippines Congress of the Philippines Metro Manila

Begun and held in Metro Manila, on Monday, the twenty-fifth day of July, nineteen hundred and ninety-four.

[REPUBLIC ACT NO. 8036]

AN ACT SEPARATING THE GODOD HIGH SCHOOL ANNEX-BALAKAN NATIONAL HIGH SCHOOL IN THE MUNICIPALITY OF GODOD, PROVINCE OF ZAMBOANGA DEL NORTE, FROM THE BALAKAN NATIONAL HIGH SCHOOL IN THE MUNICIPALITY OF SALUG, AND CONVERTING IT INTO AN INDEPENDENT NATIONAL HIGH SCHOOL TO BE KNOWN AS THE GODOD NATIONAL HIGH SCHOOL, AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. The Godod High School Annex-Balakan National High School in the Municipality of Godod, Province of Zamboanga del Norte, is hereby separated from the Balakan National High School in the Municipality of Salug and converted into an independent national high school to be known as the Godod National High School.

SEC. 2. The Department of Education, Culture and Sports shall issue such rules and regulations as may be necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act.

SEC. 3 All personnel, assets, liabilities, and records of the Godod High School (Annex) are hereby transferred to and absorbed by the Godod National High School.

SEC. 4 The amount necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act shall be included in the General Appropriations Act of the year following its enactment into law and thereafter.

SEC. 5. This Act shall take effect upon its approval.

Approved,

(Sgd.) EDGARDO J. ANGARA President of the Senate

(Sgd.) JOSE DE VENECIA, JR. Speaker of the House of Representatives

This Act which originated in the House of Representatives was finally passed by the House of Representatives and the Senate on January 16, 1995 and February 15, 1995, respectively.

(Sgd.) EDGARDO E. TUMANGAN Secretary of the Senate

(Sgd.) CAMILO L. SABIO Secretary General House of Representatives

Approved:

FIDEL V. RAMOS President of the Philippines

Lapsed into the law on JUN 05 1995 without the signature of the President, in accordance with Article VI, Section 27 (1) of the Constitution.
