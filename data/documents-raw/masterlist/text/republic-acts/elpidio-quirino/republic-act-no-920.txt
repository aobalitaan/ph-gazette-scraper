SEC. 3. Any provision of existing law to the contrary not withstanding, all funds appropriated under the provisions of this Act shall, when released, be carried in the account books of the treasurers as national disbursing funds and all the projects herein provided are classified as national projects unless such funds are appropriated specifically as aid to provinces, chartered cities and municipalities, as the case may be.

SEC. 4. All funds herein authorized shall be released by the Secretary of Public Works and Communications subject to the following conditions: under Title A to I of section one, and under sub-sections ( a ) and ( b ) of section two of this Act the itemized lists of projects are divided into Groups I and II. In releasing funds and initiating public works, projects in Group I shall have precedence over those in Group II. If projects in either group cannot be implemented fully due to insufficiency of available cash in the National Treasury, the sequence of release of project funds shall be according to the order of the titles as they appear in section one, and to the order of the subsections as they appear in section two of this Act: Provided , that no release of funds for any project or projects under any group falling under any title or subtitle of section one, and falling under sub-section of section two of this Act, shall be made if the aggregate sum appropriated for all projects under said group is not covered by sufficient cash in the National Treasury.SEC. 3. Any provision of existing law to the contrary notwithstanding, all funds appropriated under the provisions of this Act shall, when released, be carried in the account books of the treasurers as national disbursing funds and all the projects herein provided are classified as national projects unless such funds are appropriated specifically as aid to provinces, chartered cities and municipalities, as the case may be.

SEC. 5. Forty-five days before the next general election no laborer shall be employed nor money spent even if actually released or remaining unexpended at the beginning of this period, except for:

( a ) Work awarded by contract; and

( b ) Payment for the usual cost of preparation of working drawings, specifications, bill of materials, estimates, and other procedures preliminary to actual construction, including the purchase of materials and equipment, and all incidental expenses connected therewith.

SEC. 6. The sums herein appropriated, subject to the limitations set forth in sections five and six hereof, shall be released only upon certification by the Secretary of Finance, the Budget Commissioner and the Auditor General to the President of the Philippines that there are sufficient funds in the National Treasury derived from current revenues in excess of the authorized appropriations for the ordinary operation of the National Government.

SEC. 7. All provincial engineering surcharges on projects provided in sub-title ( e ) of Title B hereof shall not exceed three per cent, and on all other projects, shall not exceed six per cent.

SEC. 8. Any provision of law to the contrary notwithstanding, the requirements of prior publication for bids and the usual procedure of awarding to the lowest acceptable bidder any work to be undertaken with funds appropriated under this Act, shall apply to all school buildings the estimated cost of which exceeds five thousand pesos: Provided, however , That in places where the members of the Parents-Teachers Association (PTA) are willing to render free labor for such work, said requirements shall not apply: Provided, further , That if the funds appropriated for any school project is found insufficient to meet the estimated requirement of the engineer, the money shall be used to pay only the cost of materials for said project if and when the members of the Parents-Teachers Association decide to furnish the necessary labor free of charge for such work or contribute to pay the equivalent cost thereof.

SEC. 9. Before initiating any public works the district, city or project engineer concerned shall prepare a program of work including among other things the total cost of materials and of the labor required, the anticipated duration of the work in men-days, the margin of contingencies above his estimates if the work is to be undertaken by administration, and in addition, the contractor’s profit as he deems reasonable if the work is to be undertaken by contract. In no case, however, shall this margin of contingencies be more than ten per cent if the work is undertaken by administration, nor fifteen per cent if undertaken by contract. This provision shall not be construed as allowing the engineer a total margin of contingencies of more than twenty-five percent cent. He shall, however, be allowed to submit a revised program of work before the completion of the project if, due to unforeseen fluctuation in price of labor and materials or other circumstances, it becomes evident that he cannot successfully accomplish the work under his original program. The Auditor General or his authorized representative shall have access to the program of work or other data required in this section.

SEC. 10. Any appropriation herein authorized for the construction of buildings shall be understood as to include the acquisition or purchase of sites, if such acquisition or purchase is necessary for the prosecution of the project.

SEC. 11. No appropriation herein authorized shall be used to pay overdraft previously incurred nor made available for the purchase of automobiles or station wagons.

SEC. 12. Any violation of sections four, five seven, eight, nine, ten, or eleven hereof committed by a district, city or project engineer shall, after an administrative investigation thereof, be punished by suspension or removal from office.

SEC. 13. This Act shall take effect upon its approval.
