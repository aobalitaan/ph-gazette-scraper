OFFICE OF THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES

MEMORANDUM CIRCULAR NO. 289

URGING ATTENDANCE IN THE TWELFTH ANNUAL CONVENTION OF THE ASSOCIATION OF GOVERNMENT INTERNAL AUDITORS, INC. (AGIA).

The Association of Government Internal Auditors, Inc. (AGIA) will hold its 12th Annual Convention in Legaspi City on November 25-25, 1969, on the theme “The Role of Internal Auditing in Public Administration.”

The convention aims to project the importance of internal auditing in public administration as a means of promoting efficiency and economy in the administration of government operations.

Heads of departments and chiefs of bureaus and offices, including government-owned or controlled corporation, are hereby authorized to allow the attendance of the chiefs of their internal auditing unit, as well as those who are members of the AGIA, to attend the convention-seminar on official business, that is, with expenses for transportation, subsistence, lodging and convention fees, chargeable against the appropriations of their respective offices, subject to the availability of funds and the usual accounting and auditing requirements.

The delegates shall submit to their respective agency heads a report on their attendance and participation in the convention-seminar.

By authority of the President: (SGD.) ERNESTO M. MACEDA Executive Secretary

Manila, September 25, 1969

Source : Presidential Management Staff
