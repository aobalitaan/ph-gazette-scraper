PROCLAMATION NO. 88

DESIGNATING PART OF THE REGAN BARRACKS, DARAGA, ALBAY, FEDERAL RESERVATION, AS PHILIPPINE ARMY POST

Whereas, Regan Barracks, Daraga, Albay, former United States Army Reservation, except a portion called Sanburn Field, has been turned over to the control of the Government of the Philippine Islands to be administered for the benefit of the inhabitants thereof;

Whereas, this camp is now occupied by the Trade School, Normal School, and High School of Albay Province;

Whereas, the Bureau of Education has limited use and does not need all the premises inside the Reservation;

Whereas, the unused portion of the total area can be made available for the education of the military citizens of the nation without interrupting the school activities; and

Whereas, the barracks left therein by the United States Army are not used for the educational activities of the Bureau of Education;

Now, therefore, I, Manuel L. Quezon, President of the Philippines, do hereby declare that the area and all the United States Army buildings therein, east side of the Albay-Daraga Road, shall be available for the Philippine Army not later than November tenth, nineteen hundred and thirty-six; the area mentioned herein to be jointly used for field activities of the Philippine Army and the Bureau of Education.

In witness whereof, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Commonwealth of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done at the City of Manila, this twenty-second day of September, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and thirty-six and of the Commonwealth of the Philippines, the first.

[SEAL]

President of the Philippines

By the President:

Secretary of the Interior
