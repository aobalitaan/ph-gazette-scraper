PROCLAMATION NO. 95

CALLING THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY TO A SPECIAL SESSION FOR A PERIOD OF SIX DAYS BEGINNING OCTOBER 19, 1936

By virtue of the powers conferred upon me by the provisions of Article VI, section three, paragraph three, of the Constitution of the Philippines, and in order that the National Assembly may consider and pass such relief measures as may be necessary on account of the recent typhoons, provide for a definite solution of the problems confronting the administration and operation of the various pension funds of the Government, including the establishment of a general pension system, and to consider other important measures which were left without final action during the last regular session, I, Manuel L. Quezon, President of the Philippines, hereby call the National Assembly into special session, for a period of six days, commencing on Monday, October nineteenth, nineteen hundred and thirty-six, at ten o’clock in the forenoon, for the purpose of considering and approving the following:

In witness whereof, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Commonwealth of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done at the City of Manila, this seventeenth day of October, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and thirty-six and of the Commonwealth of the Philippines, the first.

[SEAL]

President of the Philippines

By the President:

Secretary of the Interior
