MALACAÑAN PALACE MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 705

RESERVING FOR MILITARY PURPOSES A PARCEL OF THE PUBLIC DOMAIN SITUATED IN THE CITY OF CEBU

Upon the recommendation of the Secretary of Agriculture and Commerce and pursuant to the provisions of s eighty-three of Commonwealth Act Numbered One hundred and forty-one, as amended, I hereby withdraw from sale or settlement and reserve for military purposes, under the administration of the Chief of Staff, Philippine Army, subject to private rights, if any there be, the following parcel of the public domain, situated in the City of Cebu, Island of Cebu, and particularly described in Bureau of Lands plan 16833, to wit:

Beginning at point marked 1 on Bureau of Lands plan Swo-16833, N. 48° 00′ E. 894.10 m., more or less, from B. L. L. M. No. 1, City of Cebu, thence N. 61° 41′ W. 35.00 m. to point 2; N. 61° 41′ W. 40.00 m. to point 3; X. 2$- 19′ E. 125.00 m. to point 4; S. 61° 41′ E. 75.00 m. to point 5; S. 28° 19′ W. 87.72 m. to point 6; S. 28° 19′ W. 37.2S m. to point 1, point of beginning.

Containing an area of nine thousand three hundred seventy-five square meters (9,375), more or less.

All corners are B. L. concrete monuments.

Bounded on the northeast by proposed estero; on the southeast, by Quezon Boulevard; on the southwest, by proposed street; and on the northwest, by street (no name).

Bearings true. Declination 1° 30′ E.

Points referred to are marked on Bureau of Lands plan Swo-16833.

Surveyed: May 4, 14, 15, 16 and 18, 1938.

Approved: February 7, 1941.

In witness whereof, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Commonwealth of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done at the City of Baguio, this fifth day of May, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and forty-one, and of the Commonwealth of the Philippines, the sixth.

MANUEL L. QUEZON President of the Philippines

By the President: JORGE B. VARGAS Secretary to the President
