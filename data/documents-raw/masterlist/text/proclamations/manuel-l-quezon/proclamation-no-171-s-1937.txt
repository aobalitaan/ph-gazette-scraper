MALACAÑAN PALACE MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 171

RESERVING FOR SCHOOL PURPOSES A PARCEL OF THE PUBLIC DOMAIN SITUATED IN THE BARRIO OF BARANGOBONG, MUNICIPALITY OF VILLASIS, PROVINCE OF PANGASINAN, ISLAND OF LUZON.

Upon the recommendation of the Secretary of Agriculture and Commerce and pursuant to the provisions of section eighty-three of Commonwealth Act Numbered One hundred and forty-one, I hereby withdraw from sale or settlement and reserve for school purposes, under the administration of the Director of Education, subject to private rights, if any there be, the following-described parcel of the public domain, situated in the barrio of Barangobong, municipality of Villasis, Province of Pangasinan, Island of Luzon, and particularly described in Bureau of Lands plan Mr—380, to wit:

Beginning at a point marked “1” on plan Mr-380, S. 47° 05′ E. 1,183.43 m., more or less, from B. M. No. 13, Villasis cadastre, thence S. 78° 34′ W. 56.22 m. to point 2; N. 11° 46′ W. 111.25 m. to point 3; N. 77° 00′ E. 38.84 m. to point 4; N. 13° 01′ W. 115.50 m. to point 5; N. 78° 51′ E. 29.42 m. to point 6; S. 13° 58′ E. 11.48 m. to point 7; S. 9° 08′ E. 216.33 m. to point 1, point of beginning.

Containing .an area of 9,633 square meters.

Points 3, 6, and 7, stakes.

Bounded on the northeast, by lots Nos. 4545 and 4607; on the southeast, by a road; on the southwest, by lots Nos. 6143 and 4604; and on the northwest, by lots Nos. 4604 and 6136.

Bearings true. Declination, 0° 40′ E.

Points referred to marked on plan Mr-380.

Surveyed: During 1912.

Approved: April 28, 1937.

Note.—This is lot No. 4606 of Villasis cadastre No. 10, case No. 4.

All corners not otherwise described are P. L. S./B. L. concrete monuments, 15 by 60 cm.

In witness whereof, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Commonwealth of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done at the City of Washington, D. C. (for the City of Manila), this twenty-seventh day of June, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and thirty-seven, and of the Commonwealth of the Philippines, the second.

MANUEL L. QUEZON President of the Philippines

By the President: ELPIDIO QUIRINO Secretary of the Interior
