MALACAÑAN PALACE MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 767

DECLARING SATURDAY, NOVEMBER 15, 1941, A SPECIAL PUBLIC HOLIDAY

In commemoration of the day (November fifteenth, nineteen hundred and thirty-five) that saw the birth of our Commonwealth Government, it is but fitting and proper that the sixth anniversary of the inauguration thereof be celebrated and that said day be declared a special public holiday so that the spirit of the occasion may be preserved in the memory of our people.

Now, therefore, I, Manuel L. Quezon, President of the Philippines, in pursuance of the provisions of section thirty of the Revised Administrative Code, do hereby proclaim Saturday, November fifteenth, nineteen hundred and forty-one, a special public holiday.

In witness whereof, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Commonwealth of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done at the City of Manila, this twenty-second day of October, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and forty-one, and of the Commonwealth of the Philippines, the sixth.

MANUEL L. QUEZON President of the Philippines

By the President: JORGE B. VARGAS Secretary to the President

Source: University of the Philippines, College of Law Library
