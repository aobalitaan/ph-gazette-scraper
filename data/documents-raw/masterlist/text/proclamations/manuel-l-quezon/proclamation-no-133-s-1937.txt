PROCLAMATION NO. 133

SETTING ASIDE JULY 16, 1937, AS A DAY FOR THE INAUGURATION OF THE CITY OF ILOILO, AND DECLARING IT A SPECIAL PUBLIC HOLIDAY FOR THE PROVINCE OF ILOILO.

By virtue of the powers conferred upon me by law, I, Manuel L. Quezon, President of the Philippines, do hereby set aside July sixteenth, nineteen hundred and thirty-seven, for the inauguration of the City of Iloilo, created by Commonwealth Act Numbered Fifty-seven, as amended by Commonwealth Act Numbered One hundred fifty-eight.

In order to provide full opportunity for the people of Iloilo to participate in the celebration of the inauguration of the said city, so that the day may be fittingly observed, July sixteen, nineteen hundred and thirty-seven, is hereby declared a special public holiday for the Province of Iloilo.

In witness whereof, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Commonwealth of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done at the City of Manila, this twenty-third day of January, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and thirty-seven, and of the Commonwealth of the Philippines, the second.

[SEAL]

President of the Philippines

By the President:

Secretary of the Interior
