MALACAÑAN PALACE MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 463

RESERVING FOR SCHOOL PURPOSES THREE PARCELS OF THE PUBLIC DOMAIN SITUATED IN THE BARRIOS OF BARCELONA, ANIBUNGAN, AND SAN ROQUE, MUNICIPALITY OF LINGIG, PROVINCE OF SURIGAO, ISLAND OF MINDANAO.

Upon the recommendation of the Secretary of Agriculture and Commerce and pursuant to the provisions of section eighty-three of Commonwsalth Act Numbered One hundred and forty-one, as amended, I hereby withdraw from sale or settlement and reserve for school purposes, under the administration of the Director of Education, subject to private rights, if any there be, the following parcels of the public domain, situated in the barrios of Barcelona, Anibungan, and San Roque, municipality of Lingig, Province of Surigao, Island of Mindanao, and particularly described in Bureau of Lands plans Mr-277, Mr-277-A, and Mr-277-B, to wit:

Mr-277, lot No. 2 (Barcelona School site). —Beginning at a point marked 1 on Bureau of Lands plain Mr-277, which is identical with B. L. L. M. No. 2, municipality of Lingig, thence N. 86° 39′ E. 106.86 m. to point 2; S. 21° 20′ W. 92.37 m. to point 3; S. 82° 09′ W. 5403 m. to point 4; N. 12° 55′ W. 35.96 m. to point 5; N. 12° 28′ W. 53.38 m. to point 1, point of beginning.

Containing an area of 0.6897 hectare.

Points 2, 3, 4, and 5, B. L. concrete monuments; points S and 4, on shore of Cateel Bay.

Bounded on the north and east by street; on the south, by Cateel Bay; and on the west, by street

Bearings true. Declination, 1° 44′ E.

Points referred to are marked en Bureau of Lands plan Mr-277, sheet No. 2.

Surveyed: August 14, 1931.

Approved: August 26, 1932.

Mr-277 —A (Anibungan School site).— Beginning at a point marked 1 on Bureau of Lands plan Mr-277-A, N. 6° 34′ E. 4,823 m., more or less, from B. L. L. M. No. 1, municipality of Lingig, thence N. 44° 12′ E. 75.52 m. to point 2; S. 35″ 28′ E. 38.27 m. to point 3; S. 2° 09′ E. 100.41 m. to point 4; S. 19° 48′ W. 32.84 m. to point 5; N. 40′ 33′ W. 11.50 m. to point 6; N. 31° 05′ W. 116.21 m. to point 1, point of beginning.

Containing an area of 0.6975 hectare.

Points 1, 2, 4, and 6, B. L. concrete monuments; points 3 and 5, crosses on trees 1 m. above ground; points 1 and 2, on bank of Anibungan Creek; point 5, on bank of creek (no name).

Bounded on the northeast by property claimed by Dalmacio Galingan; on the east and southeast, by property claimed by Dalmacio Galingan; on the southwest, by creek (no name) and property claimed by Guillermo Daliap; and on the northwest, by Anibungan Creek.

Bearings true. Declination, 1° 25′ E.

Points referred to are marked on Bureau of Lands plan Mr-277-A.

Surveyed: August 24 and 25, 1932.

Approved: January 24, 1933.

Mt-277 —B (San Roque School site). —Beginning at a point marked 1 on Bureau of Lands plan Mr-277-B, N. 12° 08′ W. 5,389.25 m., more or less, from B. L. B. M. No. 1, barrio of San Roque, municipality of Lingig, thence N. 27° 42′ E. 21.00 m. to point 2; N. 58° 23′ W. 23.46 m. to point 3; N. 30° 45′ E. 63.16 m. to point 4; S. 53° 22′ E. 30.67 m. to point 5; S. 8° 32′ E. 46.86 m. to point 6; N. 84° 58′ E. 43.44 m. to point 7; S. 15° 48′ W. 47.36 m. to point 8; S. 75° 27′ W. 32.96 m. to point 9; N. 60° 29′ W. 59.89 m. to point 1, point of beginning.

Containing an area of 0.6174 hectare.

Points 1, 2, 3, 5, 7, and 9, B. L. concrete monuments; points 4 and 6, crosses on trees; point 2, stone; points 7, 8, and 9, on bank of Bagang Creek.

Bounded on the northeast by properties claimed by Gregorio Rosada and Francisco Gonzales; on the southeast, by Bagang Creek; on the southwest, by street (no name) and property claimed by the Roman Catholic Chapel; and on the northwest, by street (no name).

Bearings true. Declination, 1° 54′ E.

Points referred to are marked on Bureau of Lands plan Mr-277-B.

Surveyed: August 27, 1932.

Approved: June 30, 1933.

In witness whereof, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Commonwealth of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done at the City of Manila, this ninth day of October, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and thirty-nine, and of the Commonwealth of the Philippines, the fourth.

MANUEL L. QUEZON President of the Philippines

By the President: JORGE B. VARGAS Secretary to the President
