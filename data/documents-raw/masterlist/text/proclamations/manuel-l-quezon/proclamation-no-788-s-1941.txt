MALACAÑAN PALACE MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 788

CALLING THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY IN SPECIAL SESSION

Whereas by resolution of the Congress of the United States a state of war has been declared to exist between the United States and the Imperial Japanese Government; and

Whereas the public interest requires that the National Assembly be convened in special session to adopt such measures as may be necessary to meet the existing emergency;

Now, therefore, I, Manuel L. Quezon, President of the Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution, do hereby call the National Assembly in special session commencing on the eleventh day of December, nineteen hundred and forty-one, at four o’clock in the afternoon, for the purpose of considering such measures as may be submitted from time to time by the President.

All persons entitled to act as members of the Second National Assembly are required to take notice of this Proclamation.

In witness whereof, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Commonwealth of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done at the City of Manila, this tenth day of December, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and forty-one, and of the Commonwealth of the Philippines, the seventh.

MANUEL L. QUEZON President of the Philippines

By the President: JORGE B. VARGAS Secretary to the President

Source: University of the Philippines, College of Law Library
