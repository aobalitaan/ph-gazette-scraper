MALACAÑANG MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 823

DECLARING A STATE OF PUBLIC CALAMITY IN CERTAIN PROVINCES AND CITIES IN REGIONS I, II AND THE CORDILLERA ADMINISTRATIVE REGION WHICH SUFFERED FROM THE EFFECTS OF TYPHOON “TRINING.”

WHEREAS, super typhoon which code name “Trining” wrought destruction in the country, particularly Northern Luzon;

WHEREAS, initial reports revealed that 15,222 families or 84,732 persons were affected, 7,628 houses were damaged, including agricultural crops and infrastructure valued at P25,171,675.00 and P5,800,000.00, respectively; and

WHEREAS, in order to cushion the adverse impact of the typhoon, the National Disaster Coordinating Council recommends the declaration of a state of public calamity in the affected areas.

NOW, THEREFORE, I, CORAZON C. AQUINO, President of the Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by law, do hereby declare a state of public calamity in the following provinces and cities:

CAR

All departments and other government agencies concerned are hereby directed to implement and execute relief and rehabilitation work in accordance with existing operational plans and the directive and order issued in connection with calamities.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done in the City of Manila, this 30th day of October, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and ninety-one.

(Sgd.) CORAZON C. AQUINO

By the President: (Sgd.) FRANKLIN M. DRILON Executive Secretary
