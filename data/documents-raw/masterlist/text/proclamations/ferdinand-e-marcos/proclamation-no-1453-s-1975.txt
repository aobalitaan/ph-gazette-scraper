MALACAÑANG Manila

PROCLAMATION NO. 1453

EXCLUDING FROM THE OPERATION OF PROCLAMATION NO. 423 DATED JULY 12, 1957, WHICH ESTABLISHED THE FORT ANDRES BONIFACIO MILITARY RESERVATION SITUATED IN THE MUNICIPALITIES OF PASIG, TAGUIG AND PARAÑAQUE, PROVINCE OF RIZAL AND PASAY CITY, A CERTAIN PORTION OF THE LAND EMBRACED THEREIN SITUATED IN THE MUNICIPALITY OF MAKATI, PROVINCE OF RIZAL AND RESERVING THE SAME AS SITE OF THE NATIONAL POLICE COMMISSION UNDER THE ADMINISTRATION OF THE OFFICE OF THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES.

By virtue of the powers vested in me by law, I, FERDINAND E. MARCOS, President of the Philippines, do hereby exclude from the operation of Proclamation No. 423 dated July 12, 1957, which established the Fort Andres Bonifacio Military Reservation, situated in the Municipalities of Pasig, Taguig and Parañaque, Province of Rizal and Pasay City, a certain portion of land embraced therein situated in the Municipality of Makati, Province of Rizal and reserve the same as site of the National Police Commission under the Administration of the Office of the President of the Philippines, subject to private rights, if any there be, and to the condition that the area shall automatically revert to military control when no longer needed for the purpose stated, which portion is more particularly described as follows:

Proposed Site of the NATIONAL POLICE COMMISSION (NAPOLCOM)

A portion of Parcel 4, PSU 2031 situated in the Municipality of Makati, Province of Rizal, bounded on the North, East, South, and West by the remaining portion of Parcel 4, PSU 2031.

Beginning at a point marked “1” on the attached sketch plan being N 61 degrees 37 E 6,584.01 m. from BLLM I Maricaban, Pasay,

beginning containing an area of EIGHTY SIX THOUSAND FOUR HUNDRED SIXTY NINE POINT TWENTY EIGHT (86,469.28) square meters.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done in the City of Manila this 30th day of June in the year of Our Lord nineteen hundred and seventy-five.

(SGD.) FERDINAND E. MARCOS President Republic of the Philippines

By the President

(SGD.) ALEJANDRO MELCHOR Executive Secretary
