MALACAÑANG RESIDENCE OF THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 424

EXCLUDING FROM THE OPERATION OF PROCLAMATION NO. 254, DATED AUGUST 5, 1929 WHICH ESTABLISHED THE PHILIPPINES CONSTABULARY RESERVATION, OTHERWISE KNOWN AS THE “CAMP ALLEN” SITUATED IN THE CITY OF BAGUIO, ISLAND OF LUZON, A CERTAIN PORTION OF THE LAND EMBRACED THEREIN AND RESERVING THE SAME FOR HEADQUARTERS SITE FOR THE USE OF THE USAFIP, NL.

Upon recommendation of the Secretary of Agriculture and Natural Resources and pursuant to the authority vested in me by law, I, FERDINAND E. MARCOS, President of the Philippines, do hereby exclude from the operation of Proclamation No. 254, dated August 5, 1929, which established the Philippine Constabulary Reservation, otherwise known as the “Camp Allen” situated in the City of Baguio, Island of Luzon, a certain portion of the land embraced therein and reserve the same for headquarters site purposes of the United States Army Forces in the Philippines, North Luzon (USAFIP, NL) subject to private rights, if any thereby and to future survey which portion is more particularly described as follows:

Proposed USAFIP, NL. Headquarters Site

A parcel of land (the proposed USAFIP, NL. Headquarters Site, being a portion of Lot 10 of Rs-104 (Camp Allen). Situated in the Res. Sec. “B” City of Baguio. Bounded on the S., along line 1-2, by Kayang Street; on the W., and N., along lines 2-3-4, by Remaining Portion of Lot 10 of Rs-404 (Camp Allen); and on the E., along line 4-1 by Lot 28 of plan Bcs-56-Amd.-2 (Market Site). Beginning at a point marked “1” on the attached Sketch Plan, being S., 66 deg. 02 min. E., 302.53 meters from Across Baguio Townsite,

beginning; containing an approximate area if three thousand (3,000) Square Meters.

NOTE: All date are approximate and subject to change based on future surveys.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done in the City of Manila, this 27th day of June, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and sixty-eight.

(Sgd.) FERDINAND E. MARCOS President of the Philippines

By the President:

(Sgd.) RAFAEL M. SALAS Executive Secretary
