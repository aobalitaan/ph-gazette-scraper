TANGGAPAN NG PANGULO NG PILIPINAS (OFFICE OF THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES)

PROCLAMATION NO. 853

DECLARING MONDAY, MAY 17, 1971, AS A SPECIAL PUBLIC HOLIDAY IN ILAGAN, ISABELA.

WHEREAS, Monday, May 17, 1971, marks the centenary of the birth of the late Don Pascual Paguirigan, outstanding former governor and representative of the province of Isabela; and

WHEREAS, the people of Ilagan, the town of his birth should draw inspiration from Don Pascual’s humble beginnings, perseverance and dedication to the service of his constituents, for which he was fondly called the “Grand Old Man of Isabela”;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, ALEJANDRO MELCHOR, Executive Secretary, by order of His Excellency, FERDINAND E. MARCOS, President of the Philippines, do hereby declare May 17, 1971, as a special public holiday in Ilagan, Isabela, to enable the residents of that town to celebrate the centenary of the birth of the late Don Pascual Paguirigan with appropriate ceremonies.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done in the City of Manila, this 14th day of May, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and seventy-one.

(SGD.) ALEJANDRO MELCHOR Executive Secretary

By the Executive Secretary: (SGD.) ROBERTO V. REYES Acting Assistant Executive Secretary
