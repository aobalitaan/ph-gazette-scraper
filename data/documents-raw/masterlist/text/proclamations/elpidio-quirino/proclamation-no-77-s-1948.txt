MALACAÑAN PALACE MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 77

EXTENDING FOR ONE DAY THE PERIOD OF THE SPECIAL SESSION OF THE CONGRESS OF THE PHILIPPINES AUTHORIZED UNDER PROCLAMATION NO. 71, DATED JUNE 6. 1948.

WHEREAS, the special session of the Congress of the Philippines authorized under Proclamation No. 71 will expire at midnight of June 24, 1948;

WHEREAS, the public interest requires that this special session be extended to consider and finish urgent legislative measures;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, Elpidio Quirino, President of the Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution, do hereby extend for one day, June 25, 1948, the period of the special session of the Congress of the Philippines authorized under Proclamation No. 71, dated June 6, 1948, for the purpose of considering pending bills and other urgent legislation as the President may submit to it.

All persons entitled to sit as members of the Congress of the Philippines are requested to take notice of this proclamation.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done in the City of Manila, this 24th day of June, in the year of Our Lord, nineteen hundred and forty-eight, and of the Independence of the Philippines, the second.

ELPIDIO QUIRINO President of the Philippines

By the President: EMILIO ABELLO Executive Secretary
