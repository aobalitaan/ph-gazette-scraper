MALACAÑAN PALACE MANILA

PROCLAMATION NO. 900

DECLARING TUESDAY, 4 NOVEMBER 2014, AS A SPECIAL (NON-WORKING) DAY IN THE CITY OF SAN CARLOS, NEGROS OCCIDENTAL

WHEREAS, Tuesday, 4 November 2014, marks the annual festival of the City of San Carlos, during which activities are held in thanksgiving for the many blessings the City has received;

WHEREAS, the National Historical Commission of the Philippines has recommended the declaration of 4 November 2014 as a special day for the City, citing it as the date the City was re-named to San Carlos, from its former name “Nabinkalan,” by its then politico-military governor Don Emilio Nuñez Saravia;

WHEREAS, it is but fitting and proper that the people of the City of San Carlos, Negros Occidental be given full opportunity to celebrate and participate in the occasion with appropriate ceremonies.

NOW, THEREFORE, I, PAQUITO N. OCHOA JR., Executive Secretary, by authority of His Excellency, BENIGNO S. AQUINO III, do hereby declare Tuesday, 4 November 2014, as a special (non-working) day in the City of San Carlos, Negros Occidental.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the seal of the Republic of the Philippines to be affixed.

Done in the City of Manila, this 4th day of November, in the year of Our Lord, Two Thousand and Fourteen.

By authority of the President:

(Sgd.) PAQUITO N. OCHOA JR. Executive Secretary
