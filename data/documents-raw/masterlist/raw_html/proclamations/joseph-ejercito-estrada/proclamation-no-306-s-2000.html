Proclamation No. 306, s. 2000

Signed on May 6, 2000

MALACAÑANG
MANILA

BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES

PROCLAMATION NO. 306

RESERVING CERTAIN PARCELS OF LAND OF THE PUBLIC DOMAIN SITUATED IN MIAG-AO, ILOILO AS SCHOOL SITE OF THE UNIVERSITY OF THE PHILIPPINES (U.P.) VISAYAS AND GRANTING OWNERSHIP OF THE SAID AREA IN FAVOR OF THE U.P. VISAYAS

WHEREAS, Presidential Decree (P.D.) No. 1200, otherwise known as the Five-Year Development Plan (1978-1982) directs that: “. . . State Colleges and Universities, particularly the University of Philippines system, will take a more dynamic role in upgrading higher education to promote the Philippines as an center of learning. As an apex of tertiary education in the Philippines, the facilities and programs of U.P. allied fields of agriculture, business, economics and the natural and physical sciences will be improved. while an integrated University of the Philippines campus in Iloilo based on food and marine sciences will be expanded.”

WHEREAS, in the same Five-Year Philippine Development Plan, Region VI has been designated as “the site of the University of the Philippines in the Visayas with emphasis on sciences”;

WHEREAS, Section 1 of Executive Order No. 628, series of 1980, mandates that “[U.P. Visayas] may acquire, subject to pertinent laws and to budgetary constraints, land for its main campus in Miag-ao, Iloilo, and such other land and water resources as may be suitable for the academic, research, and education purposes of the U.P. in the Visayas.”

NOW, THEREFORE, upon the recommendation of the Secretary of Environment and Natural Resources and by virtue of the powers vested in me by law, I, JOSEPH EJERCITO ESTRADA, President of the Philippines, do hereby withdraw from sale and settlement and reserve certain parcels of land of the public domain, situated in the Municipality of Miag-ao, Iloilo, as school site of the University of the Philippines, Visayas, containing an aggregate area of TWO MILLION EIGHT HUNDRED EIGHT THOUSAND ONE HUNDRED FORTY SEVEN (2,808,147) square meters, and granting ownership of the said area in favor of the U.P. Visayas, subject to private rights, if any there be, which are more particularly described as follows:
